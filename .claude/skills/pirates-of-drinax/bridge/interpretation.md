Broad GM-craft for THIS world. **Not a reskin table.** Read on every interpretation, NPC moment, and Random Event.
This is how the oracle's bare results are read *true to the setting* — and how NPCs act to win within it.

## Reading the oracle here
- **Genre = space-opera piracy on a contested frontier** — swashbuckling, opportunistic, and lethal. Read Meaning/Fate
  results with colour: technicolour court drama at Drinax, an invincible-seeming Imperium, exotic honour-bound Aslan,
  weird isolated worlds (*Dune* meets *Firefly* with pirate slang). Lean dramatically into the romance of the pirate
  life — the score, the chase, the crew, the dream of a crown — but **its brutality always has a price**.
- **The Reach is a sandbox, not rails.** Three pressures are always live and colour every ambiguous result: the
  ihatei tide, Imperial Heat, and Drinax's own decay. A quiet result still happens *against* that ticking backdrop.
- **Canon first.** When a result touches a known world/faction/NPC, read it through their canon wants (below /
  `setting-canon.md`), not a generic gloss. When canon is silent, a Fate Question decides and is written back.
- **Player ≠ PC knowledge.** The adventures are spoiler-gated. A fact you (GM) know but the crew haven't earned is
  only *potential and may be wrong* — never steer an interpretation toward un-earned knowledge.

## NPCs in this world (so NPC-behaviour interpretations come out setting-true and competent — they act to win)
- **Third Imperium** — the lazy giant; holds the line cheaply, cultivates buffer clients (often via GeDeCo). Indifferent
  to Drinax; does **not** recognise its marque (privateer = pirate). Tolerates low-level piracy until a megacorp or
  escorted convoy bleeds → a Navy sortie sweeps the Borderlands for months. Plays bureaucratic, overconfident, patient.
- **Aslan Hierate** — *not a monolith*: dozens of feuding clans, each with its own navy, reacting fast under an honour
  culture where **territory = SOC**. Bombed Drinax in 885 and will crush a risen Drinax again. Raid Aslan shipping →
  relentless honour vendetta; raid *Imperial* shipping → Aslan quietly pleased. Run them through the honour ladder + the
  reaction table (`../references/setting/factions.md`); males are proud and quick to violence over face.
- **Aslan ihatei** — landless second-sons who must *win their own land*, flocking to the border to conquer. A swarm a
  charismatic warlord could point at Drinax/Asim. The campaign's standing existential threat — a clock, not a mood.
- **Florian League** — TL15 via Ancient tech, ultra-conservative, transactional; uses overwhelming force only if the
  League itself is threatened. Treats route pirates as a border-control problem. Cold, alien, isolationist.
- **Vargr corsairs** — freelance raiders, no polity, no side; opportunists who want easy prizes and the freedom of the
  lanes. Loyalty follows charisma and success.
- **Sindalian remnants & independent worlds** — *the prizes*: individually weak ex-Drinax vassals (Paal/Pourne/Hilfer)
  that would rather negotiate into the Imperium/Hierate as equals than be ruled again. They remember Drinax's grandeur
  *and* its tyranny; play their pride, fear, and self-interest.
- **Theev pirates** — a vital, dangerous *service hub* (fence, repair, recruit), never a true ally. They steer Navy heat
  to other systems and keep one iron rule: never raid near Theev. Mercenary, watchful, deniable.
- **GeDeCo** — the Reach's most widespread non-state actor; believes Drinax's fall is inevitable and builds for the
  Imperium's quiet anti-Aslan ends. Sometimes a patron, often a rival empire-builder for the same worlds, always a leak
  risk. Plays the long corporate game; smiles while it undercuts you.
- **The Drinax court** — Oleb (bluster masking a cunning ex-pirate statesman), Rao (subtle empire-builder, won't truly
  marry a pirate), Harrick (uncanny rebuilt heir, a succession bomb), Wrax (wants the Harrier and a real fleet; a
  standing obstacle). Court scenes are Shakespearean: ambition, masks, and leverage. Full wants: `setting-canon.md`,
  `../references/setting/personalities-and-factions.md`.

**Rule for NPC interpretations:** roll their competence; never pick the convenient-for-the-PC option. Captains, navies,
and rivals run the *actual* force listed and fight to win or flee to survive. A repetitive pirate is a dead one — let
exposure, heat, and the clocks find a careless crew.

## Good-GM advice for this campaign
- **Where the stakes sit:** the **ship** (the Harrier is life and livelihood), the **crew** (loyalty and morale),
  **reputation & world Attitudes** (the empire game), **Heat** (Imperial + Aslan response clocks), the **marque**
  (legitimacy that can be revoked), and the **throne** (the long dream). Put one of these on the line in every scene.
- **Rhythm:** a few sandbox turns, then a patron job or scripted episode, then court time / consequences — not raid
  after raid. Vary hunting grounds; repetition raises Heat and dulls the drama.
- **Piracy ↔ empire tension** is the engine of the whole campaign: pirate income buys the power to rule, but every raid
  risks the standing needed to rule. When the crew choose loot over goodwill (or vice-versa), make the *other* track
  pay (`subsystems.md`, `../references/campaign/empire-reputation.md`).

## Maximal honest consequence (genre-mapped — harshness scales to the fiction; honesty never relaxes)
- A lost boarding fight → **capture, maiming, or death**; prisoners spaced or ransomed.
- A botched raid → a **crippled Harrier**, a bounty on the crew, a hunted ship.
- Brutality and atrocity → worlds turn **Hostile**; the empire game slips away.
- Greed and noise → the **Imperial Navy** comes; Heat rises into a hunt.
- Betraying an ally → **lost reputation and Fighting Strength**.
- *Skill changes HOW the crew survive, never WHETHER danger comes.*

**Reward earned safety:** when the crew scout, pick soft targets, win with Tactics/Persuade, or talk a prize into
surrender — let honest dice spare them, and don't claw it back. Good piracy is patient.
