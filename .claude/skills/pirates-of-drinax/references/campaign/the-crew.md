## The premise: why this crew exists
- The Travellers are handed a **letter of marque** by **King Oleb XVI** of Drinax and ordered to "cut a swathe across the Trojan Reach" — unite the old vassal worlds under Drinax by piracy, diplomacy and force (PoD2 p.3).
- They are **given a 'free' ship**, the *Harrier*-class commerce raider (originally *V'Hurg*). **No mortgage, no ship-shares spent on the hull** — but it remains the **property of the King**, who expects a **tithe** and obedience to his plan (PoD2 p.7).
- They need not be bloodthirsty rogues: loyal Drinaxi warriors, job-seekers, or adventurers with a grudge against the Imperium or the Aslan Hierate all fit. The bar is **daring + skill** to crew a raider (PoD2 p.3).
- **Why take the offer?** Pick at least one per PC — it drives play (see `../setting/personalities-and-factions.md` for Oleb): a shot at real wealth/a ship of their own; loyalty to Drinax's restoration; a grudge (Imperial or Aslan) the marque lets them act on; nowhere else to go (fugitive/exile); the thrill. Oleb is a charming dreamer who will spend the crew freely — that tension is fuel.

## How to make a PoD crew
Run chargen **as a group, term-by-term in parallel** so the Connections rule binds the crew together (mechanics: `../rules/character-creation.md`). PoD adds origins, career emphasis, and the ship rule below.

### 1. Origins (replace the Core Life Events table; modify starting chars) — PoD2 p.4–6
Each origin gives characteristic mods, suggested careers, and its **own 1D Life Events table** (use it in place of Core p.44). Full event tables are in the source (PoD2 p.4–7); summarized hooks here.

| Origin | Char mods | Suggested careers | Flavour / event hooks |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Drinax (Floating Palace)** | STR −1, END −1, EDU +1, SOC +1 | Noble, Scholar, Navy, Entertainer | Court-bred noble of the decaying citadel; events: court Contacts, arranged marriages, stolen inheritance, antique weapon (rapier/laser pistol), bastard of Oleb, psionics. |
| **Drinax (Wasteland / Vespexer)** | END +2, EDU −1, SOC −1 | Army, Citizen(colonist), Drifter, Rogue, Scout | Surface tribesman who "kneels to no-one"; events: exile/Enemy, Aslan-relic disease (END−1), torn hazard suit, prophesied *Hlax Kur Eaisa* messiah, Syoisuis assassin-clan chest. |
| **Asim** | END +1, EDU −1 | Army, Citizen(colonist), Drifter, Entertainer, Marine | Low-tech tithe-world peasant; events: Foundation spy conspiracy, **Aslan slavers** (enslaved into Navy/Army/Scout, escape on 8+), Aslan Contacts. |
| **Elsewhere in the Reach** | (use Core, or pick) | any | Lawless central subsectors, Theev pirates, old vassal worlds — pirates come from anywhere. |
| **The Imperium** | (use Core) | any | Spinward Marches drifter stranded on Drinax / fugitive in the Outrim Void. |
| **The Hierate (Aslan)** | use Aslan rules (Core p.64; PoD2 p.64) | — | *Ihatei* in good standing, outlaws or outcasts; Oleb will hire them. Run honour rules (`../setting/factions.md`). |
| **Vargr corsair** | use Vargr rules (Core p.51) | Rogue, Drifter | A few Vargr raiders work the Reach; one might drift coreward to Drinax. |

### 2. Recommended characteristic priorities
No special PoD array — roll 2D×6 per Core. For a working raider crew, **favour high DEX and INT** (almost every shipboard skill check keys off them: Pilot/Gunner = DEX; Astrogation/Engineer/Electronics/Tactics = INT/EDU). Give the **face/captain** the best SOC; give **boarders** STR + END. A crew can carry one or two low scores if roles are spread (see solo guidance for a single PC).

### 3. Careers that fit a privateer (PoD2 p.7)
The book's "obvious careers" for prospective privateers: **Agent, Navy, Marine, Rogue, Merchant, Drifter** — plus Drinax's surplus of **Nobility**. Detailed skill tables and which roll gives which skill are in `../rules/character-creation.md` ("Best careers for a pirate crew"); the short version:

| Career (assignment) | Gives the crew | Covers roles |
|---|---|---|
| **Rogue (Pirate)** | Pilot, Astrogation, Gunner, Engineer, Vacc Suit, Melee; weapons + Ship Shares | the archetype — pilot/gunner/engineer/boarder |
| **Navy** (Engineer/Gunner, Flight, Line) | Engineer, Gunner, Pilot, Electronics, Tactics(naval), Mechanic | engineer, gunner, pilot, sensor ops, captain |
| **Marine** (Star Marine, Ground Assault) | Vacc Suit, Gun Combat, Melee, Tactics, Heavy Weapons, Gunner | boarders/marine, gunner |
| **Scout** (Courier, Surveyor, Explorer) | Pilot, Astrogation, Navigation, Electronics, Recon, Survival, JoT | pilot, astrogator, sensor ops, navigator |
| **Merchant** (Free Trader, Broker) | Pilot, Broker, Streetwise, Persuade, Steward, Gunner | face/broker, fence/quartermaster, pilot, steward |
| **Agent** (Intelligence, Corporate) | Deception, Streetwise, Investigate, Recon, Electronics, Persuade | face, scout-the-target, sensor/comms |
| **Drifter** (Scavenger) | Pilot(small craft), Astrogation, Mechanic, Vacc Suit (auto-qualify) | fallback engineer/pilot; always available |
| **Scholar** (Physician) | Medic, Science | **medic** (the role most crews forget) |
| **Noble** (Drinax origin) | SOC, Admin, Diplomat, Leadership, Advocate | captain/face; court access on Drinax |

### 4. Connections — bind the crew (Core p.19; details in `../rules/character-creation.md`)
During parallel chargen, any event one Traveller rolls **may involve another** (only one need have rolled it). Each connection = **both** gain one free skill (max two free skills per Traveller, each with a different crewmate; can't raise above 3; no JoT). Use connections to explain **why they already crew together**: served on the same Navy ship, did a stretch in the same prison, grew up in the same Vespexer tribe, were the same merchant's crew before the engine died over Drinax. Note resulting **Contacts/Allies/Rivals/Enemies** — these seed mythic-gm Lists.

### 5. Group skills package — fill the gaps (Core p.50)
After everyone finishes, pick **one** package collectively to patch role holes. For this campaign the best fits are **Starship** (Astrogation, Electronics, Engineer, Gunner, Mechanic, Medic, Pilot, Tactics(naval)) or **Traveller** (adds Gun Combat, Pilot, Gunner, Medic, Stealth, Persuade); **Criminal**/**Mercenary** suit a rougher band. Take items in turn until assigned — prioritize **Medic, Engineer, Astrogation, Gunner** if missing.

### 6. Starting gear & credits (Core p.46–47; gear table `../rules/equipment.md`)
- **Cash:** mustering-out Benefit rolls (Cash column **max 3 rolls total** across all careers; Gambler = DM+1). After mustering out, **buy personal gear up to Cr10000** each.
- **Other benefits** give weapons (≤Cr3000), blades (≤Cr1000), armour (≤Cr10000/TL12), augments, TAS, Contacts/Allies.
- **Pirate kit to prioritize:** a **vacc suit** (boarding actions happen in vacuum; `../rules/equipment.md`), a **snub pistol** (Cr150, Zero-G, won't breach a hull) or **cutlass** (Cr200, opens a vacc suit, won't wreck systems), **cloth/flak** armour, a **comm/tool/medikit** per specialty. Leftover cash → keep, trade goods, or pool.
- **Ammo/upkeep reality:** the Harrier's maintenance is **Cr26,579/month** plus the King's **tithe** — the crew needs income from day one (see `../rules/trade-commerce.md`, `../rules/piracy-raiding.md`).

### 7. The ship — GIFTED, not bought (PoD2 p.7; full stats `../setting/the-harrier.md`)
- The campaign **gifts the *Harrier*** (TL15, 200t, Jump-2, Thrust-6, particle barbette). **No mortgage, no ship-shares spent on the hull.**
- Travellers **do not receive ships from Benefit rolls** here: any "ship" benefit becomes **D3 Ship Shares** instead, **spent to UPGRADE/repair the battered Harrier** (PoD2 p.7, p.12), not to buy a hull. (Contrast Core, where one PC keeps a ship and others take a pension.)
- She is loaned, watched, and **recognizable as a raider** — running her openly invites suspicion (`../setting/the-harrier.md`).

## Crew ROLES the Harrier needs
The stat-block roster is **12** (Captain, Pilot, Astrogator, Engineer, Maintenance, Gunner×2, Medic, Marines×3); **absolute minimum is 2** (a pilot/astrogator + an engineer) — but short-handed means constant breakdown/defect exposure (`../setting/the-harrier.md`). A PC band of 4–6 covers the essentials by **doubling up**. Map each role to the skill that runs it, then make sure every essential role has at least a level **1** somewhere aboard.

| Role | Primary skill (level to aim for) | Backup skill | Notes / who doubles up |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain** | Leadership 1+ (Tactics(naval) for fights) | SOC/Persuade | Commands; gives Leadership bonus actions. Often also the Face. |
| **Pilot** | Pilot(spacecraft) 1–2 (DEX) | Pilot(small craft) | Flies the strike pass + evasion; the most-used skill in space combat. |
| **Astrogator** | Astrogation 1+ (EDU) | Navigation | Plots jumps; a botched jump misjumps. Pilot often doubles here. |
| **Engineer(s)** | Engineer(j-drive/m-drive/power) 1–2 (INT) | Mechanic | Keeps drives/power alive; runs damage control. The Harrier *wants* 2 (engineer + maintenance/drive-chief). Hardest gap to cover solo. |
| **Gunner(s)** | Gunner(turret) 1–2 (DEX) | Gunner(ortillery) | Two stations (beam + missile). Marines/anyone can man the second turret. |
| **Sensor ops / Comms** | Electronics(sensors) 1+ (INT/EDU) | Electronics(comms) | Detect, lock, ID targets, jam, run comms. Pilot or astrogator usually doubles on a small crew. |
| **Medic** | Medic 1+ (EDU) | — | No medical bay aboard — usually the steward/cook part-time (`../setting/the-harrier.md`). The role crews most often forget. |
| **Boarders / Marine** | Gun Combat 1–2 + Vacc Suit 1 | Melee(blade), Tactics | Take prizes; fight in vacuum. Need Vacc Suit 1 to wear a boarding suit (`../rules/equipment.md`). |
| **Face / Broker** | Persuade or Broker 1–2 (SOC/INT) | Streetwise, Deception, Advocate, Diplomat | Sells loot, fences cargo, talks down a port, negotiates with warlords/Aslan. The captain often is this. |
| **Quartermaster / Steward** | Steward / Broker / Mechanic | — | Runs cargo, passengers, supply; manages the tithe ledger. Optional but handy. |

Doubling rules of thumb for 4–6 PCs:
- **Pilot also does Astrogation + Sensors** (one bridge officer can cover flying, jumping, and detection across scenes).
- **Captain also is the Face** (SOC/Leadership/Persuade on one PC).
- **A Marine also mans the 2nd turret** (Gunner from Marine career).
- **An Engineer also does Mechanic/Electronics** for repairs.
- **The Medic also does Steward/Broker** (the book's part-time-medic model).
- **Cover these at level 1 minimum or expect trouble:** Pilot, Astrogation, Engineer, Gunner, Medic, Electronics, plus one Face skill.

## Solo play: one PC + the rest as NPCs
A single Traveller cannot crew a strike raider; PoD assumes a band. For solo on the **mythic-gm** engine, run **one full PC** (the one you build deeply) and treat the **rest of the crew as NPCs** you control as a unit.

1. **Build your PC + a small NPC crew.** Use a pregen below (fastest) or build 1 PC fully and grab 3–5 pregens as crew. Ensure the **role-coverage check** passes across the whole band (every essential role at level 1+).
2. **Make the PC the role you most want to play** (the Captain/Face is the classic solo lens because it touches diplomacy, command and the empire game; a Pilot lens centres dogfights and raids). Other roles still get rolled — the PC just isn't *piloting and gunning and engineering* all at once.
3. **Run crew NPCs as helpers, not protagonists.** Mechanically, each NPC simply **uses its own skill** when its station acts: the gunner rolls Gunner, the engineer rolls Engineer (with the Harrier task DMs in `../setting/the-harrier.md`). You decide their actions; mythic-gm only adjudicates the **uncertain or contested** ones.
4. **Voice them via mythic-gm.** Give each NPC a one-line WANT + a quirk (the pregens below ship with these). When a crew NPC's opinion, loyalty, or off-screen action is in doubt, ask a **Fate Question** (set odds by their disposition) or roll a **mythic-gm random event / NPC action** — don't simply have them agree. Loyalty matters: a privateer crew can mutiny, skim the tithe, or cut a side deal. Track each NPC's attitude alongside the empire/reputation tracker (`empire-reputation.md`).
5. **Resolve crew actions in bulk when you can.** For routine watches, jumps, and maintenance, make ONE check for the responsible station rather than rolling every NPC; reserve individual rolls for combat stations and dramatic moments. This keeps a 6-person crew playable solo.
6. **Helping/assisting:** a second crew NPC can **assist** the lead (Core teamwork: +1 if the helper has the skill at the task level). Use this to make the band feel like a crew without doubling your dice.
7. **NPCs can die or leave.** Honour outcomes (no rescues). Replace lost specialists by recruiting at port, promoting (low berths carry spares), or covering the role yourself — a real campaign pressure, especially for Engineer and Medic.

## Quick role-coverage check
Before play, confirm the whole band (PC + NPCs) has **at least level 1** in each: **Pilot · Astrogation · Engineer · Gunner · Electronics(sensors) · Medic · one Gun Combat/Melee for boarding · one Face skill (Persuade/Broker/Diplomat).** If any is missing, patch it with the **group skills package** (Starship/Traveller) or recruit at a starport. Engineer and Medic are the holes that hurt most.
