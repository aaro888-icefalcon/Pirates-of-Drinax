## The long game (premise)
King Oleb's plan: use piracy/raiding to make Imperial–Aslan trade through the Reach so unsafe that the **cheapest option** for the great powers is to **recognise a resurgent Kingdom of Drinax** as protector of that trade — rather than swat the upstarts and restore the status quo. Two things must rise together (Companion: How to Build an Empire):
1. **Threat to trade** — enough disruption that protection is *needed*.
2. **Power of Drinax** — enough ships + allied worlds that co-opting Drinax beats crushing it, and that the kingdom is a **hard target** for Aslan clans.

Drinax is the last outpost of the **Sindalian Empire** that once ruled the whole Trojan Reach; every Reach system sits in its "spiritual gravity well." Reminding worlds of that grandeur boosts recruitment. The crew win worlds over by **shifting Attitudes toward Haven**, then **recruiting** them into the Kingdom at the Finale. The campaign ends with a **blockade**, **negotiations** with the Imperium and Hierate, and a final **Aslan assault** (PoD1 ch.13 *Finale*). Players may instead build **their own** pirate empire — same tools, the ruler of Drinax is just removed from the equation.

**Two doctrines** (each adventure's outcome leans one way; record which the crew use):
- **Subtle / saviour:** hide who is behind the raids; let Drinax pose as protector and win admiration. Long-term payoff, fragile if exposed. Best money tactic when blatant; best political tactic is *deniable threat → offer protection*.
- **Overt / intimidation:** "join us and stay loyal, or we cut your trade and shoot up your cities." Fast, but *loyalty is not born of fear* — expect rebellion at any sign of weakness, and worse negotiating terms with outsiders. The **Norse/Danegeld model** is the ideal: raid until rulers pay to be left alone, then trade or raid elsewhere — threat earns money in one place while raids earn it in another. (Companion: Getting Money)

## The two heat tracks vs. this track (don't confuse them)
Three separate gauges run in parallel. **This file owns only world Attitude + the empire endgame.** The other two live in piracy-raiding.md; reference them, don't re-roll them here.

| Track | What it measures | Scale | Lives in |
|---|---|---|---|
| **World Attitude** | one *world's* relationship with the crew/Drinax | Hostile…Haven | **this file** |
| **Standing** (Imperium / Hierate) | a *great power's* tolerance of the crew's piracy | +20…−40+ | piracy-raiding.md "Heat 1" |
| **PRI** (Piracy Response Indicator) | regional law-enforcement reaction to recent raids | numeric, decays | piracy-raiding.md "Heat 2" |

Interactions worth remembering: at Standing **20+ (Ally)** *all* that power's ports jump to Friendly (+SOC 2 in its space); at Standing **−40+ (Enemy of the State)** any port not already Friendly+ **drops one Attitude level**. So great-power Standing can move many world Attitudes at once. Otherwise Attitude is shifted world-by-world (below).

## World ATTITUDE scale (the spine)
Every port/world has an Attitude toward the crew (and, by extension, toward joining Drinax). Seven steps (PoD1: A Pirate's Life; Finale uses the same ladder for recruitment):

| Attitude | Meaning at the port | Recruitment DM |
|---|---|---|
| **Haven** | Part of the crew's organisation; SDBs + locals **defend** the crew. (Start: only **Drinax**.) | **+6** |
| **Friendly** | Willing accomplice; helps every way short of open battle. (Start: only **Theev**.) | **+4** |
| **Tolerant** | Will look the other way on minor illegality (ignore cargo origins, unexplained damage). | **+2** |
| **Neutral** | Takes the crew's money, offers no protection; turns on them if they bring trouble. | **+0** |
| **Suspicious** | Law-abiding, suspects the crew are pirates; searches/detains; everything harder. | **−2** |
| **Unfriendly** | Won't tolerate pirates; landing = arrest; lingering → SDBs chase them off. | **−4** |
| **Hostile** | Fired on at arrival; faces on wanted holograms; ship logged sector-wide. | **will NOT join** |

**Starting Attitudes (default seeding):** Drinax = **Haven**; Theev = **Friendly**; all Aslan ports = **Unfriendly**; every other port by **Law Level** — `2- Tolerant · 3-5 Neutral · 6-9 Suspicious · 10-11 Unfriendly · 12+ Hostile`. Override with the Appendix 2 starting Attitudes for listed worlds (below). Full per-Attitude *port mechanics* (Fence %, Risk Arrest/Spies, Protection) are in piracy-raiding.md "Fencing plunder & friendly ports."

## Shifting Attitude (how the crew move a world)
Attitude moves **one step at a time**, earned in fiction — never a single bypass roll. Tie every shift to a deed, a scene, or money spent. Drivers (PoD1: Building Alliances / Building Relationships; Companion: Factions; patron rewards throughout):

| Lever | Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Complete a Patron mission** for that world | +1 step (often more — many patrons grant a specific shift) | The main engine. Many adventures specify the exact jump (e.g. "moves two steps toward Haven"). |
| **Diplomacy / aid** (visit, donate money or facilities, deliver needed goods/medicine) | +1 step | Visiting **allied worlds** (Suspicious+) and doing good moves them toward Haven. |
| **Invest in the port** (e.g. **MCr10 in starport facilities** → Haven, per Kteiroa) | +1 step or more | Money buys goodwill; scale to the world. Bribing a small port (e.g. Exe) outright can flip it. |
| **Defeat a common enemy** (raiders harrying that world, the world's rival, an Aslan threat) | +1 step | Bringing raiders to justice "brings [a world] closer to alliance with Drinax." |
| **Trade / reliable supply** (become the supplier of a needed good) | +1 step over time | Build a habit of dependence; underpins the "saviour" doctrine. |
| **Intimidation** (deniable threat → offer protection; protection racket) | +1 step *or* sets up alliance | Overt doctrine. Works, but caps loyalty and worsens later negotiations. |
| **Donate a captured ship / equipment** to the world | +1 step | Useful sink for un-fenceable prize hulls (see piracy-raiding.md). |
| **Factions** (Companion ch.3): cultivate Assets/Factions on a world, then advance them into power | indirect — friendly factions give **DM+** to the world's later Attitude-shift / recruitment | Slow, powerful. A friendly Starport Workers' Union etc. lobbies its government toward Drinax. Faction work *supports* a shift; it does not replace Traveller involvement. |

**Downward shifts:**
- **Raiding that world** (being identified attacking its traffic, shooting up its settlements, atrocities there) drops its Attitude — usually 1 step, more for atrocity.
- **Great-power retaliation:** Standing **−40+** drops every non-Friendly port one level (above).
- **Betrayal / broken promises** to a world's leaders.

**Adjudication:** treat each step-shift as the *reward line* of a scene the crew actually played. If they want to push a shift directly (no mission handy), run it as a Faction/Asset interaction (Companion): Average (8+) Leadership/Diplomat/Persuade with situational DMs; a single success = one step, and only if the action is fictionally proportionate. Repeated dice-spam on the same world without new fiction should fail.

## Piracy ↔ empire tension (the central trade-off)
The engine of the whole campaign. The crew need pirate income (ships, money, leverage) but every raid risks the standing they need to *rule*. Hold both ideas in tension every session:

- **Raiding a world hurts standing there, but can help elsewhere.** Hitting World A's shipping lowers A's Attitude and may raise Standing with the power that benefits — and lets you pose as A's *protector* later if no one knows it was you. Hitting the *other* great power's ships is a **Heroic Deed** (+1D Standing with the pleased power).
- **Exposure is the killer.** A distinctive ship (the **Harrier auto-counts** on watch lists) repeatedly seen raiding then docking at Drinax, plus crew carousing at Oleb's court, will reveal Drinax's hand. Mitigate: a **less distinctive ship / clean tender** for raids, raid only in distant systems with the Harrier, leave no witnesses (the hard-nosed path — alienates allies if discovered).
- **Income that does NOT cost standing:** legitimate trade; "legitimate" tariffs/fees once Drinax can pose as a state; protection (the deniable-threat model); industry; selling to factions in wars the crew helped start.
- **The Drinax tithe:** Oleb demands **10% of all piracy proceeds**. Refusing forfeits the letter of marque and makes an enemy of Drinax (your only starting Haven). (See piracy-raiding.md "Division of the Spoils.")
- **Rule of thumb for the engine:** when the crew choose loot over goodwill (or vice versa), make the *other* track pay. A big atrocity that empties a hold should also cost an Attitude step on that world and a Standing hit — surface both.

## Royal Policy (Government / Security / Technology)
At the Finale, the reborn Kingdom adopts **three policies**, each rated **0–3**. Policy decides **which worlds will join** (a world recruits more easily when Drinax's policy matches its Profile). Set late-campaign; warn players choices have empire-wide consequences. (PoD1 *Finale*: Shaping Royal Policy.)

**Government — how centralised:**
| Rating | Name | Appeals to |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | First Among Equals | iconoclastic/isolationist worlds, corporations, religious dictatorships, balkanised worlds |
| 1 | Parliament (equal representation) | low-population worlds, democracies, bureaucracies |
| 2 | Feudal (dukedoms) | high-population, autocratic/centralised worlds (feudal technocracies, dictatorships, oligarchies) |
| 3 | Autocracy (king decides) | only worlds already strongly aligned to Drinax |

**Security — tolerance of piracy vs. centralised navy:**
| Rating | Name | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | A Pirate King | piracy tolerated/encouraged; Kingdom = a big criminal enterprise |
| 1 | Low Security | piracy outlawed but patchily enforced; mutual-defence pacts; little real change |
| 2 | High Security | crackdown on piracy, ship-building, stronger anti-Aslan alliances; Star Guard revived |
| 3 | A Warrior King | all resources → defence; mighty armada; current pirates swear fealty or die |

**Technology — sharing Drinax's hoarded TL15 knowledge:**
| Rating | Name | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Keep it | Drinax hoards its advantage |
| 1 | Limited (factories/students) | builds high-tech factories on member worlds; Tower opens to deserving students |
| 2 | For a price | limited tech transfer, esp. to worlds about to advance a TL |
| 3 | Uplift | tech uplift is the Kingdom's primary goal; push toward TL16+ |

**Starting Policy ratings** depend on who rules Drinax (if the crew control the throne, set freely). One attempt to nudge ratings: Persuade or Diplomat (or **Carouse** with Oleb) — apply the **Effect** to move ratings up/down.
| Ruler | Gov | Sec | Tech |
|---|---|---|---|
| King Oleb | 3 | 1 | 0 |
| Prince Harrick | 0 | 2 | 2 |
| Princess Rao | 2 | 2 | 1 |

## World Political Profile & Policy DMs
Each world has a **Political Profile**: up to three ratings (0–3) for its *desired* Government, Security, Technology (the **Policy** column in Appendix 2). Compare Drinax's policy to the world's profile, digit by digit:

- **Match** on a digit → **DM +1**.
- **Mismatch** → **DM −1 per point of difference**.
- **`-` (null)** in the profile → that world doesn't care → **DM +0**.
- **`X`** in the profile → no grounds for negotiation on that axis → **DM −3**.

**Worked example** (PoD1): Drinax policy **122**, world profile **32-** → Government −2 (2-pt gap), Security +1 (match), Technology +0 (null) → **Policy DM −1** total.

This **Policy DM** feeds the recruitment roll below, alongside Attitude and any "Other" DMs.

## The recruitment roll (the endgame mechanic)
At the Finale, for each prospective world, the crew make a **Recruitment roll** to bring it into the Kingdom. Pre-compute DMs at end of session; roll dramatically next session. (PoD1: Initial Recruitment Rolls.)

**Roll 2D, target 8+,** sum these DMs:
1. **Attitude DM** (table above): Haven +6 / Friendly +4 / Tolerant +2 / Neutral 0 / Suspicious −2 / Unfriendly −4. **Hostile worlds never join.**
2. **Policy DM** (profile vs. Drinax policy, above).
3. **Other DM** — campaign rewards, existing relationships, bribery, telepathic coercion, GM's call.

**Telepathic Coercion (Zhodani):** if *Blood of the Star Dragon* left Drinax with secret Zhodani support, roll **1D**; the crew may apply **DM+2** to that many recruitment attempts (psionic tampering).

**Lost Worlds:** any world **destroyed** during the campaign (WMDs from *Treasure of Sindal*, the punitive fleet in *Game of Sun and Shadow*, Aslan conquest) is removed from the list and contributes **no Fighting Strength**.

**Aslan worlds:** the few Aslan systems on the table are *very* unlikely to recruit, but a clan might switch sides.

**Collecting the stragglers (second pass):** worlds that decline get a **2D-week** window for a second roll. A crew member must **visit in person** and either have an existing relationship with a government member *or* pass a GM-set challenge. Second roll = as the first, with two changes:
- The crew may **justify the policy** with Diplomat or Advocate: if Policy DM is −1, **Difficult (10+)**; −2, **Very Difficult (12+)**; −3 or worse, **Formidable (14+)**. Success offsets the penalty.
- **Proximity:** no Kingdom worlds within 3 parsecs → **DM−1**; all systems within 3 parsecs already loyal → **DM+2**.
- Crews who began in *Honour Among Thieves* get **DM+1** to recruitment rolls.

## Fighting Strength (military contribution)
A semi-abstract measure of Drinax's ability to **block/harass the trade route**, in **dice**. Not raw tonnage — five 400t corsairs beat one 2,000t cruiser for hit-and-run blockade work. (PoD1: Determining Fighting Strength.)

**From the fleet:** **+1 FS per 500 tons** of armed ships (round up). **Any single ship counts as at most 1,000t** (max +2 FS per hull). Only count ships that could fight (an unarmed freighter or a Scout with one beam laser does not).

**From recruited systems:** each contributes the **Fighting Strength** in its Appendix 2 row (not its true navy — big rich worlds hold most ships back; tiny worlds commit everything). Profile meanings:
| FS | What it represents |
|---|---|
| 0 | No ships at all (uninhabited / barbarous / taboo). |
| 1 | A handful of battered old ships; low-tech or tiny outposts. |
| 2 | A few armed traders / a third-hand escort / a lot of pirates. Average backwater. |
| 3 | Many traders+pirates, or a real planetary militia. Above-average world. |
| 4 | A pirate stronghold (Theev) or one of the few worlds with a real navy. |
| 5+ | Massive military presence. |

**From allies elsewhere** (logistical, rarely open war): e.g. **Ahroay'if clan +6 FS**, **GeDeCo +10 FS**.

**The blockade (Finale, abbreviated):** the trade route has a **jump-3 route**, a **jump-2 route**, and **Backwaters** (Appendix 4 target numbers). Block **either** main route to succeed; blocking more (or Backwaters) buys negotiating leverage. Procedure per system: assign FS dice → optional Tactics(naval) check → roll the dice; **beat the system's target number** = Blockaded; **discard dice that roll odd** (1/3/5 = attrition/return home), Leadership check halves losses. DMs: recruited Kingdom system **+1**; sabotage enemy defences **+2**; starport agents **+2**; leaving a system off the list **−1 (one gap) / −3 (two gaps)** to all other rolls (and hurts negotiations). Full procedure in PoD1 p.252–254.

## Unlisted worlds (profiling on the fly)
For worlds the crew cultivated that aren't on the table, build a Profile (Appendix 3):

**Government** (by gov code) → preferred Gov rating: 0/1 None/Corp →0; 2/4 Democracy →1; 3 Oligarchy →2; 5 Feudal Tech →2; 6 Captive →as ruler (or 2 if Drinax backs a rebellion); 7 Balkanised →0 or 2; 8/9 Bureaucracy →1; A Charismatic Dictator →2; B Non-Charismatic →2; C Charismatic Oligarchy →2; D Religious Dictatorship →0 (unless promised state-religion status). **DM+1** to preferred Gov if the world was loyal to / long-associated with old Drinax.

**Security:** start from **Law Level** — 0-2→0, 3-5→1, 6+→2. **DM+2** if within six parsecs of the Aslan border.

**Technology:** repressive/superstitious →0-1; low-tech →1; average trading →1-2; high-tech/weird →3 (unless their power rests on a tech edge over neighbours).

**Fighting Strength:** assign 0-5+ per the FS table above, by the world's actual ships.

## Milestones to the throne & victory conditions
Rough progression the engine can track as a clock (PoD1: Building Alliances; *Finale*):
1. **Survival & a foothold** — keep the letter of marque (pay the tithe), secure Havens beyond Drinax (each Haven is a concrete win), build a fleet.
2. **A power base** — enough Tolerant+/Friendly worlds and FS that Drinax can pose as a legitimate state and run "legitimate" raids/levies.
3. **Allies in place** — Factions advanced into power on key worlds; patrons across subsectors owe favours.
4. **The Declaration (Finale)** — a trigger event (Oleb's funeral, a coronation, Rao's wedding, a conference, or news of an Aslan attack) gathers the Reach; recruit worlds (rolls above).
5. **Show of force** — mount the **blockade** of the jump-2 *or* jump-3 route (or another impressive show: seize a key world, war games, wipe out Theev) to prove Drinax must be reckoned with.
6. **Negotiation** — drag the **Imperium** and **Hierate** to the table; trade blockade concessions, trade levies, pacts, and bases for recognition (Appendix 4 negotiation tables; difficulty set by Standing).
7. **The first test** — repel a splinter-Aslan assault on Drinax (the final battle).

**Kingdom size → reward tier** (Title/Gift/Credits per Traveller; Reward table PoD1 p.251):
- **1-5 systems** (a handful in Tlaiowaha): **one** reward roll.
- **6-15 systems** (most non-Aslan Tlaiowaha + some Borderlands/Sindalian Main): **two** reward rolls.
- **16+ systems** (a new Reach power): **three** reward rolls — Oleb can style himself **Emperor of Sindal**.

**Victory / Defeat (the final battle):** no middle ground. **Win** → the Kingdom of Drinax is reborn and endures; the crew retire as nobles/admirals; a marriage offer (Rao/Harrick) may still stand. **Lose** → everyone dies: the Floating Palace is destroyed, the crew die in battle or are executed as *hostis sophonti generis*; the letter of marque is worthless.

## What the growing Kingdom unlocks
- **More Havens/Friendly ports** → safe repair, refit, fencing, recruiting, and **Protection** (port defences fight for the crew).
- **Fighting Strength** → ability to mount the blockade and survive the Aslan assault.
- **Legitimacy** → "legitimate" tariffs/fees, overt raids reframed as tax-collection, doctored watch lists, retroactive pardon of crimes committed within Kingdom borders at the Declaration.
- **Negotiating leverage** → blockade scope and FS set how good a treaty (levies, recognition, pacts) the crew can extract.
- **Standing 20+ (Ally)** with a power flips all its ports Friendly and grants SOC+2 in its space.
- **Titles & rewards** scaling with Kingdom size (above).

## Threats to the nascent empire (scale response to growth)
The bigger the crew/Kingdom get, the harder the powers push back. Surface these as the empire track climbs:
- **Imperium/Hierate find out Drinax is behind the piracy** — *inevitable* eventually; the Kingdom must have enough protection by then that recognition is cheaper than war.
- **Standing collapse** — at −21 to −40 (Infamy) patrols hunt the crew; at −40+ (Enemy of the State) assassins + warships, and every non-Friendly port drops a level. (piracy-raiding.md.)
- **PRI spikes / task forces** — repeated raids in one area summon Q-ships and naval task forces (2D×500t). (piracy-raiding.md.)
- **The Imperium ignores small polities** — two-three worlds calling themselves a "Foederate" get a Christmas card, not respect; only a **trade-strangling blockade** forces real negotiation. Leaving blockade gaps weakens leverage (−1/−3 to rolls and worse terms).
- **Aslan ihatei pressure** — masterless second-sons gather near the border (e.g. Kteiroa); a charismatic warlord could launch an invasion. A worry that grows as the Kingdom looks like worthwhile territory.
- **The final Aslan assault** — a splinter clan tries to crush the upstart humans the moment the Kingdom is declared.
- **Internal fragility** — empires built on fear rebel at any weakness; broken treaty promises trigger Fighting-Strength losses; refusing Oleb's tithe makes Drinax an enemy; rival pirate councils (Theev) and court factions (Lord Wrax, the Vespexers) maneuver against the crew.

## Per-adventure "Empire / Reputation effects" — how to apply
Every adventure file ends with an **`## Empire / Reputation effects`** section (Authoring Spec). It tells you the *outcomes* that move this track. To apply one:
1. **Read the stated shift** — most are explicit ("World moves N steps toward Haven," "becomes a Haven/Friendly," "Standing −1D with both empires," "World destroyed → Lost World").
2. **Resolve it as the reward line of what the crew actually did** — apply the step(s) only if earned; partial/failed outcomes give less.
3. **Update the tracker** — change the world's Attitude (and note the cause), adjust Standing/PRI per piracy-raiding.md if the adventure says so, mark Lost Worlds, add allied FS.
4. **Surface the trade-off** — if the win came via raiding/atrocity, also apply the *cost* on the other track (Attitude down on the victim world, Standing hit). Never give a free positive.
5. **Cascade** — a Standing crossing 20+ or −40+ re-rates that power's ports; recompute affected recruitment DMs.

Examples of stated effects from canon: *Honour Among Thieves* (bring the raiders to justice → Torpol & Clarke move toward Drinax); Acrid/Argona, Falcon, Pandora, Cordan, Umemii, Arunisiir patrons each grant a specific shift toward Haven/Friendly; Byrni's regent → Byrni joins if Oleb proves worthy; Exe → flips for a few thousand credits in bribes.
