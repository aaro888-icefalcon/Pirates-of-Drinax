# The Prodigal Outcast

> Use this when: the exiled Aslan **Kasiyl** calls in his debt and recruits the crew to kidnap a young *ihatei* (**Hwehalokharl**), then march into the deep Hierate to extort a confession from the boy's father — the judge **Lokharl** — and clear Kasiyl's name. A heist that detonates a clan war. Cross-ref `setting/factions.md` (Aslan clans, honour, reactions, Standing), `setting/personalities-and-factions.md` (Kasiyl, the Syoisuis, clan stances), `setting/ships-of-the-reach.md` (Aslan hulls), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Hierate Standing, Clan Standings), `worlds/00_world-index.md` (Hierate UWPs).

**Logline:** Kasiyl's "simple plan" — grab a noble's son, force the father to recant the judgement that exiled him, go home with honour — is in truth a wedge the crew drive into the most dangerous fault line in Aslan politics. The confession they extract proves the keepers of Aslan honour took a bribe to ruin their own ally's vassals, and selling, hiding, or shouting that secret restarts a clan war and reshapes which clans become Drinax's friends or executioners.

## At a glance
- **Where:** Starts at **Drinax** (Tlaiowaha 0603). Phase 1 (the hunt/kidnap) tracks **Hwehalokharl** through wherever the crew sent the *ihatei* swarm in *Ihatei!* (Kteiroa 0505, or a target world like Fantasy 0808 / Hilfer 0804 / Akoaft). Phase 2 (the confession) goes deep into the Hierate: **Tlaiowaha** (the clan stronghold + Anchorage, clan-capital hex 0308), with Lokharl's courier interceptable en route to **Suiaoir** (Nora'a 0308). Resolution at **Stohyus** (Ranib 0805, the Ahroay'if golden world) and/or **Hkakh** (Ranib 0807, where Fyohierl restarts her war); Yerlyaruiwo stronghold at **Iysewekh** (Ranib 0600).
- **Threat:** Crew are **outmatched** the instant they enter Hierate core space (Tlaiowaha = the main Aslan naval base in the sector; piracy there is "absolute suicide" — spinal meson guns and fusion cannon). This is a **heist / infiltration / intrigue** adventure with set-piece ship interceptions, not a war of attrition. The lethal scenes are clan-agent retaliation and Yerlyaruiwo pursuit, not fleet battles.
- **Length:** Long, three-act. No single hard clock, but Kasiyl proposes an **8-week execution deadline** on Hwehalokharl to pressure Lokharl, and the search/intercept legs eat weeks. Failure, partial success, and lucrative betrayal are all reachable endings.
- **Prerequisites:** **Kasiyl must already be a known NPC** — ideally recruited in *Ihatei!* (Adventure 3), where he extracted a **future favour** that comes due *here*. If the crew never took Kasiyl, the referee can introduce him fresh (he turns up at their door with a beaten enemy of theirs as a "gift," then asks for repayment). Hierate Standing typically **−5** (pirate) going in. Whatever the crew decided in *Ihatei!* sets where Hwehalokharl is now.
- **Arc slot:** Mid-campaign Aslan-facing intrigue. It is the **payoff of the Kasiyl thread** and the **moment the crew acquire (or burn) lasting friends inside the Hierate** — friends they will badly need at the Finale's clan negotiations. Directly seeds **Blood of the Star Dragon** (next chapter): Princess Rao is quietly behind Kasiyl's scheme, and Prince Harrick's interference here foreshadows his palace-coup arc.

## Player-Facing (the hook)
*Safe to narrate.*

- **Kasiyl recruits the crew.** The beggar-prince who has haunted Oleb's court has found a way to reclaim his birthright. He is an Aslan male — bred to lead and fight, hopeless with money or logistics — so he hires the crew clumsily: he drops a **beaten, blooded enemy of the crew** (pick one from a PC's background or the campaign) at their feet, declares he has done them a favour, and asks one in return. (If the crew have no handy enemy, he offers something else valuable but unexpected — or it is simply the favour he is owed from *Ihatei!*.)
- **The brief (the Quarry):** Kasiyl wants to speak with a young *ihatei* named **Hwehalokharl**, second-son of the *earleatrais* (Aslan war-judge) **Lokharl**, who recently came of age and is questing for territory in the Trojan Reach. Contacts place him arriving at the **Kteiroa** camp six weeks ago. **Hwehalokharl must be taken alive.** Kasiyl is cagey about why; pressed, he admits the boy is "the key to restoring my honour" and that he needs him to reach the boy's father, who wronged him. He has no ship — the crew do. He hints he can **richly reward** them.
- **The wrong Kasiyl claims:** he was exiled for **murder**. During a **War of Assassins** (a ritual where two warring clans may legally kill each other, victory to first authenticated blood) between his clan, the **Ahroay'if**, and the minor **Tokohfealr** clan, Kasiyl killed a Tokohfealr noble. The judge **Lokharl** ruled the kill **illegal** — Kasiyl had used a *wye'oiheasarl* plasma streamer (a PGMP-class battlefield weapon, supposedly barred from civilian assassination) — so instead of a clean win it counted as murder, and Kasiyl was exiled. Kasiyl has **always believed Lokharl broke his sacred vow of neutrality and was bribed/biased against him.** Now Lokharl's own second-son is of age and abroad: capture the boy, leverage the father into recanting, return home with honour. "A simple plan."
- **Playing Kasiyl to the table (referee guidance, surfaces in fiction):** he reveals as little of himself as possible; his past stays sealed; he meditates to suppress his emotions; he uses humans' instinctive fear of Aslan to his advantage. **He must not become the crew's de-facto leader** — it starts as his revenge plan and should pass into the crew's hands the moment they realise he has bitten off far more than he can chew.
- **The Exile & the Princess (seed):** if the crew spend time on Drinax, one may glimpse **Princess Rao** in deep conversation with Kasiyl. *(That this whole scene is partly her doing is GM-Only — below.)*

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned. This is "The Truth of the Knife."*

- **The hornet's nest is bigger than Kasiyl knows.** Lokharl's ruling was **not** a simple judicial call and **not** mere bias against Kasiyl. It was a **bought verdict in a multi-clan conspiracy**, and the crew are unwittingly about to expose it.
- **The conspiracy, clan by clan:**
  - The **Ahroay'if** (Kasiyl's clan) were once a minor, ferociously warlike clan — among the first across the Great Rift — who became **vassals of the wealthy Tlaiowaha** and prospered as their mercenaries. **It was the Ahroay'if who bombed and destroyed the old Kingdom of Drinax** when its kings threatened Tlaiowaha trade routes.
  - Centuries of **Imperial trade** made both clans rich, fat, and lazy. A **warlike faction within the Ahroay'if** (Kasiyl's faction, fronted by his aunt) decided to put that wealth into **new wars of conquest** — building warships, training troops.
  - This **alarmed the Tlaiowaha**, whose fortune rides on trade routes running through Ahroay'if space. If their vassals grew too strong, they might renegotiate — or break free. The Tlaiowaha wanted the Ahroay'if **kept in their place.**
  - Their chance came when the Ahroay'if attacked the minor **Tokohfealr** clan and Kasiyl used a questionably-legal weapon. The legality ruling fell to an *earleatrais* of the **Yerlyaruiwo** — the traditionalist "keepers of Aslan honour," highly respected. **The Tlaiowaha bribed the Yerlyaruiwo** to have their judge (**Lokharl**) rule against the Tlaiowaha's *own* troublesome vassals, the Ahroay'if.
  - It worked. Shamed by the Tokohfealr "defeat," the Ahroay'if war faction collapsed; Kasiyl was exiled; Tlaiowaha trade was secure; the Yerlyaruiwo's reputation stayed intact — **so long as no one ever exposes the bribe.**
- **So Kasiyl's exile had nothing to do with his impulsiveness or honour.** It was sealed because merchants hundreds of parsecs away feared an Ahroay'if taste for victory. Lokharl was **ordered** to bend the rules.
- **Two tiers of secret, two tiers of damage** (this is the heist's real treasure):
  - **The minor scandal** — Lokharl bent the rules as an *earleatrais*. Embarrassing to the Yerlyaruiwo (a worth ~**MCr500** to bury), but survivable; they can blame a minor branch. **Restarts the Ahroay'if–Tokohfealr war** regardless.
  - **The unforgivable breach** — that the **Tlaiowaha bribed the Yerlyaruiwo to rule against the Tlaiowaha's own vassals.** If the crew **figure this out** and reveal it, it **shatters the Tlaiowaha–Ahroay'if alliance**, forces the Ahroay'if back to their warlike ways, and **cuts the Tlaiowaha off the dust-spice trade route into recession.** Burying *this* is worth "**far, far more**" than MCr500 — more than enough to kill for.
- **The chain reaction the crew are lighting:** Lokharl's recantation reopens the war between the Ahroay'if and Tokohfealr, **escalating it from a War of Assassins to a Strictly Limited War (*tehlaicho*)**. Both clans will want it over fast — Kasiyl wants the crew's guns on hand to help his side win. **This is the stone thrown into the water of the Hierate; its ripples are felt back on Drinax later (in the Finale).**
- **The hidden hand — Princess Rao.** The whole scheme is **partly Rao's doing** (she steered Kasiyl toward it). **Open question (GM-defined, explored in *Blood of the Star Dragon*):** does she truly want Kasiyl restored, or is she **deliberately sowing chaos and strife inside the Hierate to weaken the clans before Drinax declares itself**? Treat her as a manipulator with her own agenda; never confirm which.
- **Conflicting stories (Kasiyl vs. Hwehalokharl):** both agree Lokharl judged the war and that an assassination is normally legal. **Kasiyl** insists *any* killing method is allowable. **Hwehalokharl** says Kasiyl's kill was **overkill** — a *wye'oiheasarl* plasma streamer (PGMP-class) used in a civilian area, which *is* restricted. Hwehalokharl **also recalls senior emissaries from other clans visiting his father during the deliberations** — unusual, since such cases are normally decided only by consulting the *tala'tyaeo* (scrolls of the ancestors) and *tala'si'khiraeohaor* (scrolls of precedent). He thinks nothing of it; it is in fact the **fingerprint of the bribe.**
- **Crews who blindly obey Kasiyl make things worse** for Drinax and themselves. The smart play is to learn the truth, then **sell the confession to the clan that benefits the crew most** (see Rewards) — not simply hand Kasiyl his revenge.

## Scenes / Locations
Three acts: **Phase 1 — The Hunter's Quarry / Capturing the Quarry** (find & grab Hwehalokharl) → **The Captive / Prisoner Complications** (stash him, pressure Lokharl) → **Phase 2 — In the Jaws of the Beast / Finding Lokharl / A Visit from the Shadows / Lokharl's Confession** (deep-Hierate infiltration & extortion) → **The Yerlyaruiwo Response & Approaching the Clans / Restarting the War** (fallout & payday). Travel in Aslan space uses the *Ihatei!* rules (papers of passage from Tyokh, Yerlyaruiwo assassin risk if Kasiyl is along).

### 1. The Hunter's Quarry — finding Hwehalokharl
- **Trigger:** Crew accept Kasiyl's favour and start hunting the boy.
- **Where he is** keys off *Ihatei!*: if the crew redirected the swarm, Hwehalokharl is fighting on **Fantasy / Hilfer**, stuck in the war on **Akoaft**, or still arguing in the **Kteiroa** camp. If *Ihatei!* was skipped, segue into it now or just place him in the Kteiroa camp.
- **Finding him (once the right system is known):** **Very Difficult (12+) Investigate (INT, 1D months)** by brute force — give generous DMs for clever plans, efficient contact use, or Aslan allies. **Failure = he has moved on** before they arrive.
- **Rumours:** **Average (8+) Carouse or Investigate (SOC, 1D days)**, **DM−2 with no Aslan along.** On success roll on the Hwehalokharl Rumours table (rumours **6− are false**, **7+ true**):
  | 2D | Rumour |
  |---|---|
  | 2 | He died in battle (**false** — only wounded, fully recovered). |
  | 3–4 | False *ihatei*-raid rumour sends them wrong → **DM−1** to next find attempt. |
  | 5–6 | He flies a 100t *ihateisho* scout (**false** — he only arrived on one). |
  | 7 | Solid location intel → **DM+1** to find checks. |
  | 8–9 | He's in the retinue of a wealthy **Yerlyaruiwo** *ihatei* on a **1,000t Aositaoh cruiser** under **Teirlas** → **DM+1** (search for Teirlas instead). |
  | 10–11 | His father is a respected *earleatrais* whose influence got the boy his place. |
  | 12 | By luck the crew arrive as Hwehalokharl visits the system. |
- **The truth the rumours hint at:** Hwehalokharl serves aboard the **Aositaoh-class cruiser** of his distant relative **Teirlas of the Yerlyaruiwo** — the **single most powerful clan bloc in the Tlaukhu.** That means crossing this retinue has dangerous repercussions, but Teirlas (a rich heir on a joyride, fascinated by aliens) is cautious and won't gamble everything.
- **Use the hunt** to put the crew in an Aslan frame of mind: the balance of clans, public vs. private honour, the tension between land-hunger and trade dependence.

### 2. Capturing the Quarry
- **Trigger:** Crew locate Teirlas' cruiser.
- **Teirlas' ship** is a 1,000t Aositaoh; ~**100 Aslan aboard**, a **600t Owatarl tender** carrying supplies and the bulk of his ~**500 followers**, plus a swarm of **100t *ihateisho* scouts.** **Hwehalokharl's cabin:** second stateroom forward of the shrine on the quarter deck; on duty he's usually on **deck 2**; Teirlas converted the missile bay into a ground-invasion war room.
- **The menu (crew may invent others; Kasiyl suggests challenge or shadow-and-grab):**
  - **Direct attack** — only if the crew think they can drop a 1,000t cruiser fast; **other Aslan ships will reinforce within a few rounds.** Teirlas fights on until his ship can't continue or **Hull ≤50**, then surrenders / hands the boy over to save his crew if offered the chance.
  - **Infiltrate & kidnap** — Aslan PCs pose as *ihatei*/mercenaries to join the crew: **Average (8+) Persuade (SOC)**; **non-Aslan at DM−4.** Or break in while docked/landed and sneak to the cabin.
  - **Shadow & kidnap** (best, slowest) — follow until the ship lands, then tail Hwehalokharl until he's vulnerable. Teirlas lingers **2D weeks** in the system the crew found him in (likely the world they redirected the swarm to in *Ihatei!*) before returning to **Tlaiowaha (0308)** to celebrate. Shadowing may need a **Pilot → Electronics (sensors) → Astrogation** task chain.
  - **War & kidnap** — Teirlas is hunting a winnable war; shadow the cruiser to its battlefield, sneak onto it, grab the target in the chaos.
  - **Challenge** — **Difficult (10+) Diplomat (TER)** gets Teirlas to accept a **two-on-two duel** (Teirlas + Hwehalokharl vs. two PCs); he hands the boy over but the crew must stake **something equally valuable.**
- **Complication — crossing Teirlas:** if the crew attack or wrong him, he **strikes back with the full fury of the Yerlyaruiwo.** A surviving, aware Teirlas becomes a hunter for the rest of the adventure (DMs on later pursuit/clan-response rolls).

### 3. The Captive & Prisoner Complications
- **Trigger:** Hwehalokharl is in the crew's hands.
- **Stash the prisoner.** Kasiyl suggests an obscure moon or pirate hideaway; **paranoid crews** may pick somewhere safer (the **gravity dungeons of the Floating Palace** — a favour call) or somewhere *away from Kasiyl himself*.
- **Questioning Hwehalokharl:** a **Persuade (INT)** check gets him talking in captivity (run "Conflicting Stories," GM-Only). He considers non-Aslan unpredictable barbarians and just wants to get away from them.
- **The threat with teeth.** To pressure Lokharl the crew need leverage that bites: Kasiyl proposes an **8-week execution deadline.** They must convince the old judge they're serious.
- **Prisoner Complications (keyed roll — run periodically while the boy is held):** roll **2D, on 8+ no complication**; on a fail choose or roll **1D**. With mythic-gm, resolve as `dice.py 2d6` + DMs; on a fail `dice.py 1d6` for the event. DMs:
  | DM | Condition |
  |---|---|
  | +1 | Very secure hiding place |
  | +2 | Boy in suspended animation / similar restraint |
  | +(Deception) | Crew hid their movements |
  | −1 | Crew are an *Irritant* to the Hierate (Standing) |
  | −2 | Crew are *Infamous* foes of the Hierate |
  | −3 | Crew are *Enemies of the State* of the Hierate |
  | −1 | **Kasiyl** left in charge of the prisoner |
  | −1 | Teirlas is in pursuit |
  | −1 | Crew have an Enemy/Rival who might interfere |
  - **1 Escape** — Hwehalokharl breaks out, steals a (jump-capable, if available) ship, races for Hierate space; if not recaptured **before he reaches safety, the leverage on Lokharl is dead.**
  - **2 Rescue attempt** — Teirlas (in his cruiser, if he knows) or **1D *ihateisho* raiders** storm the hideout; success = the boy is carried home.
  - **3 Kasiyl's Fury** — Kasiyl's exile-rage boils over and he attacks the boy; **2D, on 6+ merely injured**, else mortally wounded (needs a **Medic** check to save). *(This is why paranoid crews keep the prisoner away from Kasiyl.)*
  - **4 Pirate raid** — a rival pirate hits the hideout; **2D, on 8+ Hwehalokharl is captured** by them.
  - **5 Sickness** — the boy falls gravely ill; needs an Aslan-biology expert or **TL13+** medical facility, else dies in **6D weeks.**
  - **6 Prince Harrick's Interference** — **Harrick discovers the crew kidnapped a Yerlyaruiwo noble.** Last time Drinax angered the Aslan, the Ahroay'if **bombed the planet** and ended the old Kingdom; he will not let it recur — **he demands the prisoner be released.** *(Foreshadows Harrick's instability and his coup arc in Blood of the Star Dragon.)*

### 4. In the Jaws of the Beast — entering Tlaiowaha
- **Trigger:** Prisoner secured; crew head into the Hierate to find Lokharl (likely on **Tlaiowaha**, billions of population, his stronghold — though Tlaiowaha is the *clan's* world, not its homeworld).
- **The system:** a sun-scorched rock orbited by the **Tlaiowaha Anchorage** — the **main Aslan naval base in the sector**, uncounted thousands of ships, "enough to turn the whole Trojan Reach to ash." Run the dread: a mere fraction of this strength would shatter Drinax again.
- **Customs (intercepted by Tlaiowaha SDBs):** **resist = dead in space.** In an Aslan ship or with valid Tyokh **papers of passage**, pass with an **Average (8+) Admin (SOC)** and no contraband. **DM−1** if Hierate Standing is 0 to −5; **DM−2** if worse than −5. Carrying illegal weapons/items → need **10+** instead (hidden compartments, sensor jamming, distractions grant DMs). Problems → diverted to a secure station for questioning ("unlucky humans vanish for months or years").
- **Piracy here:** **absolute suicide** — the most-trafficked naval base in the sector targets raiders with spinal meson guns and fusion cannon. They are toast.
- **Breaking the Law on Tlaiowaha** (Aslan have **clan agents**, not police — private-eye/freelance-cop/mafia-goon hybrids): roll **2D**, DMs: false identities −1, mild non-lethal violence +2, minor theft/damage +0, major theft/damage −2, heavy weapons −4, attack on a Tlaiowaha-clan member −2, guide informs −2.
  | Result | Outcome |
  |---|---|
  | 6− | Targeted by **Clan Agent(s)** — Tlaiowaha can deploy **1 Battle-Ready Agent per PC**; other clans send **D3 Investigative Agents.** |
  | 7–9 | Tlaiowaha assume human foolishness → assign a **Tlaiowaha guide** (replaces any existing guide). |
  | 10+ | Passes without comment. |

### 5. Access to the Anchorage & Guides
- **Trigger:** Crew dock (or need a cultural fixer).
- **Docking:** an extortionate **Cr10,000** per bay. The Anchorage is built for Aslan — pulsing artificial gravity, holographic savannahs on every wall, the smell of sweat and blood; humans correctly describe it as "**feeling like prey.**"
- **Guides (set the Reaction-table DM):**
  - **AI/robot guide:** **Cr2,000** for the whole stay; **reports anything suspicious to the Tlaiowaha.** Disabling that protocol = **Formidable (14) Electronics (computers, EDU)**; failure alerts them. Carrying one marks the crew as wealthy-but-inferior (treated kindly, like children); **no Reaction roll needed** while accompanied.
  - **Aslan guide:** **Cr25–Cr250/day**; Reaction DM **+0 to +4**; cost = DM + 1D × Cr50. Roll which clan it secretly reports to:
    | 2D | Guide's clan |
    |---|---|
    | 2–3 | Minor / no clan |
    | 4–5 | Ahroay'if |
    | 5–8 | Tlaiowaha |
    | 9 | Yerlyaruiwo |
    | 10 | Tokohfealr |
    | 11 | Syoisuis |
    | 12 | Double agent — roll twice (pretended clan / real clan; re-roll 12 as No-Clan then Syoisuis) |
  - **No guide:** Reaction DM **+0.** A guide gains **DM+1** on the Reaction table with its own clan's representatives.

### 6. Finding Lokharl
- **Trigger:** Crew search the planet of billions for one reclusive judge.
- **Lokharl values privacy** and has no public presence. Routes:
  - **Through clan channels (recommended):** **Very Difficult (12+) Diplomat (SOC)** + a plausible reason, via the Yerlyaruiwo's outside-the-Hierate reps. They run background checks → **DM−1** no Aslan along; **−2** Standing 0 to −5; **−4** Standing −6 or worse; **−4** if Teirlas is back and knows the crew abducted Hwehalokharl. On a **2−**, the Yerlyaruiwo **report the crew as spies** to the Tlaiowaha (= Breaking the Law).
  - **Through the bureaucracy:** if the crew can't justify why "free traders" need an Aslan judge, they're shunted into the surreal Aslan records vault (TL14 servitor robots reading leather scrolls). **Difficult (10+) Admin (EDU)** to start; **1-in-6 per week** to get an answer.
  - **On their own:** **Very Difficult (12+) Streetwise (INT, 1D days)**; failure offends a group of Aslan (roll their number & SOC, consult the Clan table for reprisal).
  - **Socialising:** **Very Difficult (12+) Carouse (SOC, 1D hours)** for a contact who knows Lokharl; after, **2D on 8+** (and a successful check) = introduction; on a fail/7− = take that much damage from potent Aslan liquor or a brawl.
  - **Kasiyl's relatives** (Tlaiowaha system):
    - **Aual (Kasiyl's sister)** — Ahroay'if rep to the **Ya'soisthea**, **New Horizon** (pro-trade) faction; eats/dresses human-style. **Unsympathetic** — thinks Kasiyl a troublemaker corrupted by their aunt and the Syoisuis; urges releasing the boy to avoid destabilising the clans. Good roleplay (her love for her brother) + a **Persuade** check makes her connect them to Lokharl — but she insists they then go straight to the **Ahroay'ifko on Stohyus** and meddle no further.
    - **Fyohierl (Kasiyl's aunt)** — the **Undersecretary for Customs Traffic Operations**, an ex-pirate ("privateer") grounded into a dead-end job; office full of loot, including a **gold-inlaid human skull paperweight.** She **started the War of Assassins** that ruined Kasiyl (recruited him to front it as a landless female who couldn't own territory). **Ascendency** (pro-conquest) faction — wants the Ahroay'if to break free of Tlaiowaha and rise into the Tlaukhu. She hates Lokharl for ending her war on a technicality, doesn't know where he is, and **points the crew to Aual.** If they've already tried Aual, she pulls her **backup**: as Undersecretary she can read dignitaries' travel schedules and knows **Lokharl is due to visit a conference on Suiaoir (Nora'a 0308) aboard a Ktiyhui-class courier** — intercept it and take him. She'd have to turn **Outcast** if blamed for leaking it, but will risk it. She also urges: skip Stohyus — take the confession **straight to Hkakh (Ranib 0807)** so she can reopen her war.

### 7. Intercepting the Courier (optional Lokharl-grab)
- **Trigger:** Crew take Fyohierl's route instead of (or after failing) a face-to-face meeting.
- Lokharl's **Ktiyhui-class 200t courier** runs **Tlaiowaha → Asoieteal → Fiyleakh → Erasaso → Ehrafou → Sei'eal → Suiaoir**, staying in Aslan space throughout. Fyohierl's data includes **security-patrol schedules**, making the ambush "exceptionally easy" **if the Aslan aren't already hunting the crew** — if they are, the crew must dodge their own pursuers while chasing Lokharl. Take Lokharl alive and force the confession in transit.

### 8. A Visit from the Shadows — the Syoisuis
- **Trigger:** At some point while the crew hunt Lokharl, two robed Aslan (**Tye** and **Yekh** — "Star" and "Void") in *soisu'hru* assassin's garb approach them. They are **Syoisuis** — the spymaster/assassin clan, technically allied to the Yerlyaruiwo.
- They are **curious, not hostile.** If the crew use false names, the Syoisuis hint they know exactly who they're dealing with. They ask what the crew want in Aslan space and what it has to do with the outcast Kasiyl (unseen in the Hierate since that shameful War of Assassins).
- **Offer:** the Syoisuis thrive on conflict — they'll **help find Lokharl and help restore Kasiyl** if the crew bring them **Lokharl's confession first**, plus their clan's gratitude and guidance through Aslan politics. *"We are good friends to have — especially when no one else knows we are your friends."* (Their fuller deals are in Rewards / Approaching the Clans.)

### 9. Talking to Lokharl & the Confession
- **Trigger:** Crew reach Lokharl (escorted in via official channels, or sneaking past his palace security).
- **The setting:** a high-security palace, his apartments in an obscure wing (tight but not overwhelming security). No weapons/armour past the scanners except ceremonial *hi'asoi* "monkey-blades"; smuggling a weapon = **Difficult (10+) Deception or Electronics (sensors, INT)**, failure = Breaking the Law.
  - **Official route:** escorted straight in — but Lokharl is **not alone**: a Yerlyaruiwo handler **Yohorlor** and **one heavy bodyguard per two PCs** attend; Yohorlor tries to stop any talk of Kasiyl. The crew can still extract the confession by distracting/threatening (to kill Hwehalokharl).
  - **Unofficial route:** **Difficult (10+) Stealth (DEX) or Deception (SOC)** to bypass security; Lokharl is attended only by a servant and his nursemaid.
- **Lokharl himself:** ancient, almost mummified, retreated into the pre-spaceflight past; no computers in his rooms (just medical gear). He **only speaks *trokh*** and growls at translators (his nurse/handler can interpret). He's contemptuously dismissive of both clans, recalls Kasiyl as "the idiot who used a *wye'oiheasarl* in a War of Assassins," and laughs that ruling against the Ahroay'if was "an easy decision." (He waxes lyrical that when the Aslan bombed Drinax it was "poetry," not a "blind convulsion.")
- **Extracting the confession:** tell him Kasiyl holds Hwehalokharl and the boy dies unless Lokharl admits he was wrong and reverses the judgement. Optional **Routine (6+) Persuade (SOC)** if the crew gave him reason to underestimate them; to force it **without alarming the handlers** (e.g. dropping hints about Teirlas' retinue) it's **Difficult (10+) Deception (SOC).**
- **What he confesses:** he was **ordered** to ruin Kasiyl and the Ahroay'if's war against the Tokohfealr, and **those orders came from his superiors in the Yerlyaruiwo.** He doesn't know *why* — but it "has the stink of **Ya'soisthea politics.**" *(He does not name the Tlaiowaha bribe; deducing that is the crew's leap — GM-Only.)*
- **The artefact:** no recorder? Lokharl writes the confession on a scroll and seals it with his ***eaiasu'ikh*** (soul-shield / ceremonial seal) — to Aslan eyes as weighty as his own voice.
- **mythic-gm keyed reminder:** the confession is the adventure's pivot. There is no must-happen die; resolve the social legs via `task-system.md` and every Aslan reaction via the Reaction table in `factions.md`. The **8-week deadline** is the soft clock.

### 10. The Yerlyaruiwo Response (fallout)
- **Trigger:** Soon after the crew extract the confession, the Yerlyaruiwo dispatch agents to recover it and punish them. *"One does not simply threaten a member of the Yerlyaruiwo."*
- **Response speed:** roll **2D** on the Clan Agent Reaction table, DMs: Teirlas warned them −2; Yerlyaruiwo guide −2; went through their bureaucracy −1; already caused trouble on Tlaiowaha −3; Lokharl alerts them within 24h −1 / 2h −2 / immediately −4; **forced to mislead them +2**; **+ (best Deception among crew)**; **+1 to +4** for prep/misdirection.
  | Result | Reaction |
  |---|---|
  | 1− | Battle-Ready Clan Agents (immediate) |
  | 2–5 | Battle-Ready Agents in 2D minutes, pursue |
  | 6–8 | Investigative Agents in an hour, investigate |
  | 9–11 | Investigative Agents in 8 hours, investigate |
  | 12+ | No reaction (you got away with it) |
- **Investigation (if the crew got a head start):** roll **2D each day**; **8+** = on their trail (+DM2 to find them) and they learn whether the crew left the system; **10+** = found, arriving in force.
- **Pursuit through space:** the clan allocates a **trio of 800t Ekawsiykua escorts** (add Teirlas' ships if he's hunting too); they may summon more — roll **2D**, **+2** if a Tlaukhu naval base is in-system, **+2** if gov code is H/L/N; **6+** adds **D3 *ihateisho* scouts**, **10+** adds a **1,000t cruiser.** Intercepted crews are ordered to surrender and accompany agents to the Yerlyaruiwo stronghold on **Iysewekh (Ranib 0600)** — refusal met with lethal force.
- **Clan Agents** minimise collateral — even a Battle-Ready Agent prefers an accurate hunting rifle or a blade, escalating to laser rifles/gravity spears only if the crew bring heavy weapons. They **try to take the crew alive** (prisoners go before the Yerlyaruiwo elders).

### 11. Approaching the Clans / Restarting the War (the decision)
- **Trigger:** Crew hold the confession and must decide whom to give/sell it to. This is where the payday and the long-term alliances are set. **Note which clans benefit and which become enemies** (drives the Clan Standings, Rewards).
- **Options (full deals in Rewards & Consequences):**
  - **Ahroay'if — go public or private at Stohyus (Ranib 0805).** The golden world of the clan; **Ascendency** faction controls the ceremonial court (warm welcome, treated as heroes) but holds **few ships** — the crew must persuade the **Ahroay'ifko himself** for real military backing. *(Aual urges acting openly.)*
  - **Fyohierl — restart her war at Hkakh (Ranib 0807).** Skip the elders; reopen the conquest directly.
  - **Aual — abandon the scheme.** Return to Kasiyl and talk him down (jump back to the prisoner's hideout).
  - **Syoisuis** — hand them the confession for their brokered solution(s).
  - **Cut a deal with the Yerlyaruiwo or the Tlaiowaha** — sell silence (the big money).
- **At Stohyus, in private:** the **Ahroay'ifko** is ancient, frail, dying of a **degenerative fungal genetic condition** (a **Very Difficult (12) Medic (EDU)** diagnoses it; **the Drinaxians long ago developed a cure** — instant leverage). He **already guessed** the Tlaiowaha bribed the Yerlyaruiwo but has no will to act — the clan has chosen trade over conquest and he wants only the status quo. Asks the crew to **destroy the confession, free Hwehalokharl, and settle Kasiyl outside the Hierate**, offering **MCr100 or a used Ekawsiykua escort** (roll 3× on the Spacecraft Quirks table). A **Very Difficult (12+) Persuade (SOC)** can change his mind — **DM+2** if they won the court publicly first, **DM+4** if they offer the cure — putting the clan's military under Ascendency control (**war back on, Kasiyl returns in triumph**). Or **reveal his weakness/complicity to trigger an Ahroay'if civil war** (Kasiyl still returns, but to a world of burning cities).

### 12. Restarting the War (Fyohierl's route, if taken)
- **Trigger:** Crew bring the confession to **Hkakh (Ranib 0807).** Fyohierl has assembled a small fleet of ex-pirates, mercenaries, and militant clan members ready to reopen the war the moment the paperwork lands. To present a *fait accompli* she must seize the planet fast — which means cutting communications.
- **The set-piece:** two Aslan ships are about to leave Hkakh — a **200t Ileliy-class messenger** and a **600t Owatarl-class tender.** The crew must **intercept and disable both before they jump.**
  - **Both disabled:** the invasion succeeds flawlessly; the **Ahroay'if conquer Hkakh**, reviving the Ascendency and **redeeming Kasiyl.**
  - **Only one:** **2D on 9+** the Tokohfealr respond in time and reinforcements hand them the victory.
  - **Neither:** the **Tokohfealr win on 5+.**
- **Outcomes:** if the **Ahroay'if win**, both Kasiyl and Fyohierl rise to influence and **owe the crew a considerable favour.** If the **Tokohfealr win**, it's still a **partial victory** — Kasiyl is redeemed regardless.

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — no rescues. The big money is in **who buys the confession**; Aslan "do not quibble with outsiders," so prices are take-it-or-leave-it (though conciliatory Diplomacy can sometimes raise them).*

- **Selling / placing the confession (the payday — pick the clan):**
  - **Tlaiowaha** (most to lose): **MCr300** for silence; Kasiyl may live but never return to the Hierate. The crew can instead **blackmail** them (hold it in reserve) — leverage over the most influential clan in the sector, "very useful in the future."
  - **Yerlyaruiwo:** they will pay to bury it — **MCr150 + Hwehalokharl returned safe**; throw in **Kasiyl's head** and it rises to **MCr200.** Downside: the crew are then **forever barred from Aslan space and hunted by Yerlyaruiwo assassins** if they return; the clan becomes a permanent enemy. *(A conciliatory, Diplomat-heavy approach — "we only helped you avoid a misunderstanding" — can claim the payment **without** earning their rancour.)*
  - **Tokohfealr** (the wronged minor clan): little money, but **unencumbered by clan politics** — uniquely free to give **Drinax direct support.** Hand them the testimony and, in future, when the crew visit any Aslan port, roll **2D on the Tokohfealr Agent table** (DMs +1 Pop 6+, −1 Law 6+) for the port's temporary Attitude:
    | 2D | Gov G/H/J/K/L/M/N (representative) |
    |---|---|
    | 2–5 | Unfriendly across the board |
    | 6–8 | mostly Unfriendly; Tolerant/Neutral on some govs |
    | 9–11 | up to Friendly on H/M; Tolerant/Neutral mixed |
    | 12+ | up to **Haven** (G/H/M); Friendly/Neutral/Tolerant elsewhere |
  - **Syoisuis** (the brokers): if the crew only want Kasiyl restored, the Syoisuis stage a **(fixable, one-sided) duel to the death between Kasiyl and Hwehalokharl** — without his son's territory Lokharl becomes worthless and the Yerlyaruiwo discard him quietly, **no war reopened.** If the crew want **help for Drinax**, the Syoisuis approach the Ahroay'if, **restart the war, and send ships to ensure Fyohierl wins Hkakh** — Drinax gains the **Ahroay'if as allies**, the Yerlyaruiwo's reputation stays clean, the Tlaiowaha are weakened — **but Kasiyl must be silenced forever, by the crew's own hand.**
  - **Ahroay'ifko (Stohyus):** **MCr100 or a used Ekawsiykua escort** to **destroy the confession, free the boy, settle Kasiyl outside the Hierate** (status quo); or persuade/cure/coerce him to **reactivate the clan's war faction** (Kasiyl returns in triumph) — or trigger an **Ahroay'if civil war.**
- **The Drinaxi cure** as currency: offering the Ahroay'ifko's fungal-disease cure is **DM+4** to sway him and worth **+4 Clan Standing** with the Ahroay'if (below).
- **Salvage / hulls:** a captured **Aositaoh / Owatarl / Ktiyhui / Ekawsiykua** if the crew take one intact (most are AVOID-tier — disable, don't destroy); the Ahroay'ifko's gift Ekawsiykua comes with **3 Spacecraft Quirks.**
- **Contacts/favours:** **Kasiyl restored** = a powerful, grateful Aslan ally inside the Hierate; **Fyohierl** rising in the Ascendency owes the crew **a considerable favour**; the **Syoisuis** as secret friends; the **Tlaiowaha/Yerlyaruiwo** as bought-off (or blackmailed) powers; the **Tokohfealr** as Drinax's unentangled Aslan backer.
- **What failure costs:** if Hwehalokharl **escapes to Hierate safety, is rescued, or dies**, the leverage on Lokharl collapses and the plan is doomed. **Killing Hwehalokharl** is a major dishonour (**−4 Clan Standing across the board**, below) and likely ends any hope of clan goodwill. Getting caught threatening a Yerlyaruiwo (without buying silence) brings **Battle-Ready Clan Agents**, capture, and the elders' justice. Each **serious crime in Aslan space** is **−2 Standing.**
- **The trade-off to surface:** the lucrative betrayals (selling to Yerlyaruiwo/Tlaiowaha, taking the Ahroay'ifko's hush-money) **leave Kasiyl unredeemed or dead and give Drinax no Aslan ally** — money now versus a friend in the Hierate at the Finale. The poorest-paying choice (Tokohfealr) buys the **most strategically valuable** Aslan support.
