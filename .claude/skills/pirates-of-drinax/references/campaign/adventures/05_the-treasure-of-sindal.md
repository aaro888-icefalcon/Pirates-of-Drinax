# The Treasure of Sindal

> Use this when: an archaeologist (Prof. haut-Belzoni) brings a Sindalian message-globe to Drinax and the crew join (or steal, or replace) his treasure hunt — the dinner & globe-opening at Drinax, the four-world clue chase (Noricum → Thebus → Number One → Ace), the three rival pirate gangs, and the hidden station finale where the "treasure" turns out to be doomsday weapons.

**Logline:** Race three other pirate bands to a fabled hoard lost in the fall of the Star Empire — visiting four worlds to assemble a map keyed to four particle-beam firing solutions — only to find the legendary Treasure of Sindal is a stockpile of atomic and plague warheads, and decide what a pirate kingdom does with weapons that can murder worlds.

## At a glance
- **Locations:** Drinax (Tlaiowaha 0603, the hook) → **Noricum** (Sindal 0408) → **Thebus** (Sindal 0309) → **Number One** (Sindal 0208) → **Ace** (Sindal 0109) → the **Hidden Station** (2 parsecs coreward of Ace, in deep space — uncharted). The four "clue" worlds can be visited in the order above; Number One/Thebus order is flexible (see Scenes).
- **Threat/level:** Mid-campaign (Phase 2). Combat threat **high but manageable** — three rival pirate gangs (Faihlokh, the PRC, Petyr Vallis) plus environmental killers (Noricum's death zones, Number One's crush-depth, Ace's vacuum). The smart play is **playing the rivals off each other** rather than fighting all three. The *moral* hazard (WMDs) is the real climax.
- **Est. length:** Long, multi-jump (5–7 system episodes). 3–6 sessions; compressible (the four clues are the spine; rival encounters scale to taste).
- **Prerequisites:** The crew should be established enough that **King Oleb trusts them with the Drinax Ducal codes** and his "secure it for Drinax without arousing Imperial suspicion" errand. A reputation that lets rival pirates *hear of* the hunt (Dai Kinnear's telepathy, underworld rumour). Optional tie-in: Prof. haut-Belzoni may have been met on the journey home from *The Demon's Eye* (04).
- **Arc position:** Pivotal Phase-2 heist. Whatever the crew do with the warheads can **reshape worlds** (a deployed plague creates **Lost Worlds** on the empire track and is a sector-scale Atrocity). Threads onward to *The Game of Sun and Shadow* (06) and any later Sindalian-relic plots (Scholar Voha / *Shadows of Sindal*).

## Player-Facing
- **The patron/hook (The Archaeologist):** **Prof. Yutal Mirobar haut-Belzoni** of the University of Regina — a cloistered, twitchy, lecture-prone academic specialising in historical naval tech — arrives at Drinax with a letter of introduction to the Imperial Ambassador, **Thao Poloc**. Twenty years ago he acquired a Sindalian **message-globe** bearing the Empire's emblem, from Albe (Sindal 0601). It needs **Ducal-level clearance** to open, and the one place those ancient Ducal codes might survive is the **Floating Palace archives** on Drinax.
- **The legend (the openly-told story):** The **Treasure of Sindal** — a hoard of "unimaginable wealth" (platinum, iridium, lanthanum, precious metals) supposedly carried off by **Admiral Gani Peras**, "the Last Loyal Admiral," in the dying days of the Empire (~-1400). She took the Imperial Treasury from the besieged throne-world **Noricum**, jumped out with five surviving ships, and vanished from history for ~2,000 years. haut-Belzoni believes his globe is the key.
- **The dinner (At Drinax):** Thao Poloc presses King Oleb to host a state dinner for the honoured guest (Oleb happily obliges — an excuse to drink). haut-Belzoni explains the globe and his need for the Ducal codes. He says the treasure "belongs in a museum" (privately he plans to get rich off a book deal). **Use Dinner Encounters (Scene 1)** for colour and to plant rivals.
- **Opening the globe (next morning):** In an anti-bugged chamber in the Scholar's Tower, **Scholar Voha** produces a *second* globe from Drinax's own archives (long ago opened; held only an unbreakable string of numbers). With Oleb's authorisation and the Ducal codes, the Albe globe pops open: a recovered Imperial message + a page of letters/numbers + the word **ACE** + coordinates pointing to a **secret base on Noricum** (not the old palace).
- **The quest:** haut-Belzoni declares he's going to Noricum at once. The crew can accompany him, or let Thao Poloc lay on an Imperial **scout ship** for him. **King Oleb privately tells the crew to secure the treasure for Drinax without arousing Imperial suspicion** — convince haut-Belzoni it belongs to Drinax, steal it, or arrange a "nasty accident." Oleb hands over the **Drinax Ducal codes** on a data module that **self-erases in 12 weeks or if tampered with**.
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** The globes contain identical "code" strings (configuration/targeting data, if anyone breaks it); each clue points to the next world (Noricum → Thebus/Number One → Ace); **three other pirate gangs** are hunting the same prize; the trail leads through the lightly-travelled Sindal subsector "Dustbelt."

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **The treasure is not money.** Gani Peras did **not** save the Imperial gold for posterity. The real "treasure of Sindal" is the **Emperor's personal doomsday arsenal** — planet-killing **atomic** and **artificial-plague** warheads. For centuries the Empire enforced its will by orbital bombardment and bioweapons; Peras convinced the last Emperor she'd build a doomsday retaliatory station to deter the rebels, then **never deployed it** — she hid the weapons on a secret naval base and walked away from the war. The Imperial treasury gold is probably still buried on Noricum, or was looted in the sack 2,000 years ago — and it does not matter.
- **The "code" is a map key.** Each of the four message-globes (and the wall-inscriptions found en route) contains the **core-dump targeting solution for a Sindalian-designed particle beam** — relative firer/target position, beam frequency, particle mix — all for the **same type of weapon**. They are **not** an encrypted cipher. Breaking one (Virtually Impossible 16+ Electronics (computers), 1D hrs, INT; +2 per later code found) reveals this early.
- **Why four solutions?** Ace's argon/CO2 atmosphere lights up under electrical discharge; **a particle beam acts like an electrical discharge.** Fire **four** particle beams (four ships, or jury-rigged satellites) into Ace's sky, programmed with the four firing solutions, and the interfering beams **project a star-map across the sky** — highlighting a point **two parsecs coreward of Ace**, in officially-empty deep space. That is where the Hidden Station (and the arsenal) waits. This is the climax reveal at Ace.
- **The four globes' routes (the in-fiction logic of the chase):** Peras's fleet dispatched key-globes to the Dukes of **Albe, Drinax, Dpres and Yggdrasil** on the eve of Noricum's fall. The Albe globe survived (haut-Belzoni's); the Drinax globe is in Voha's archive. The **Dpres** and **Yggdrasil** globes never arrived — their couriers were intercepted by rebels sweeping in from spinward. One courier died at **Thebus** (Battle of Thebus / Magg's Moon); the other tried to reach the neighbouring **Number One** and was interned there. Recovering those two lost globes is the middle of the chase.
- **The Imperial family's fate (lore the crew may uncover):** The Imperial family died on Noricum; they never reached Peras's "refuge," probably because the rebels broke the line at **Salif** (Sindal 0206) faster than expected.
- **The three rival gangs' specific roles** (full gangs/ships/NPCs below; *play each to win* — they don't stop after their "home" world):
  - **Faihlokh's Raiders** (Aslan exile + telepath Dai Kinnear) — learn of the hunt at Drinax (Dai's telepathy or a planted bomb) and **ambush the crew at Noricum** once the base is found.
  - **The Proactive Recycling Company (PRC)** — scavenger-pirates of Thebus; spring a **Q-ship ambush** (the "distress call") in the Thebus system.
  - **Petyr Vallis's Company** — anti-Aslan raiders out of Theev (already a campaign recurring NPC — see `personalities-and-factions.md`); his agent **Hokmah/Hokmar** spies at Number One; Vallis's ships may appear at Noricum, the Dustbelt, Number One's depths, or Ace.
- **The Confidence (haut-Belzoni's scout, if he travels alone):** a battered Type-S crewed by old scout **Jacro Lekins**. If the crew don't capture/destroy it, **Petyr Vallis's corsairs destroy it** (at Noricum or Number One). If haut-Belzoni travels solo he also blunders into the Thebus ambush.
- **If the crew fail to find a key at Noricum, Thebus or Number One,** a rival gang recovers it instead — then the crew must **steal, capture or negotiate** for the missing codes at Ace.
- **What the hunters do if they get the arsenal first** (decide if the crew lose the race): **haut-Belzoni** reports it to the Imperium (an Imperial *Planet*-class heavy cruiser, the **Sentinel**, arrives from Tobia within six months to seize it); **Faihlokh** tries to sell on Theev and ends up dead in an alley, weapons leaking to Dustbelt warring worlds (Tyr, Acis); the **PRC** sell the location to the Imperium for a nominal fee; **Vallis deploys the plagues against the Aslan Hierate** (e.g. Tliowaha). The crew's choice is open: bury, sell, blackmail, or repeat Sindal's folly.

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. At Drinax — the dinner & Dinner Encounters
**Trigger:** haut-Belzoni reaches Drinax (the crew bring him, or he arrives aboard the Aslan trader **Tai'ao** = Faihlokh's gang, page-139 ship — a plant). Thao Poloc arranges the state dinner; everyone at court attends. → **What's here:** haut-Belzoni's pitch; Oleb's drinking. Roll/choose **Dinner Encounters** to flavour the night and seed rivals:
1. haut-Belzoni traps a Traveller with a lecture on Vilani logistics → **Average (8+) Carouse (END)** to get drunk enough to endure him; fail = the Traveller insults him, **Imperial Standing −1D**.
2. **Lady Hil** (court snob, see `personalities-and-factions.md`) offers sponsorship → a Traveller who accepts may gain **SOC +1** while she favours them, but is drawn into palace intrigue.
3. **Chieftain Galx** (Vespexer envoy) asks the crew to take eager young Vespexer warriors into space (minimal skills, keen).
4. A Torpol merchant rages about piracy and glares at the crew → his anger draws Thao Poloc's eye; unless defused, **Poloc grows suspicious, Imperial Standing −1D**.
5. **King Oleb** drunkenly challenges the highest-STR Traveller to "**hurricane boxing**" on the Palace topdeck in a storm (first to fall off loses) — rude to refuse, ruder to punch the king to death.
6. The crew spot **Faihlokh** talking to **Dai Kinnear**; Dai then flirts with a Traveller. *(GM-Only:* her flirtation is cover — physical contact gives her **DM+2 Telepathy** vs. that individual, letting her read the crew's plans.)
→ **Complications:** Belzoni "talked to the wrong people" — this is *how the rival gangs learn of the treasure* (Scene-3 list). Thao Poloc's suspicions feed Imperial heat.

### 2. Opening the Message Globe (Scholar's Tower)
**Trigger:** The morning after the dinner; select dignitaries (Oleb, Princess Rao, Voha, Thao Poloc, haut-Belzoni, the crew) enter an anti-bugged sealed chamber. → **What's here:** Voha's second (Drinax) globe + the Albe globe; Oleb authorises; the Sindalian computers flicker to life; the Albe globe opens. Contents: a recovered Imperial dispatch (see boxed text below), a page of letters/numbers, the word **ACE**, and coordinates to a **secret base on Noricum**. → **The recovered Imperial message (read aloud):** *"Contents of Imperial Vault transferred to Admiral Peras' flagship. Admiral Peras ordered to set course spinward along Main to Refuge. Imperial family and retainers will follow if defensive lines at Salif fail. Key dispatched to the Dukes of Albe, Drinax, Dpres and Yggdrasil. Signed by His Hand, Felix Star Dragon, Emperor of Sindal."* → **Complications:**
- **Stealing the globe:** Treacherous crews may try; remind them the globe is **useless without the Ducal codes** — a 2,000-year-old paperweight.
- **Why doesn't Oleb just take it?** He believes there's **no treasure left**; the whole show is to curry Imperial favour and keep Drinax looking innocent (so Thao Poloc stays distracted from Drinax's piracy). If treasure *is* real, "ships get attacked by pirates all the time."
- **Analysing the code now:** **Virtually Impossible (16+) Electronics (computers)** (1D hrs, INT); each later code (Noricum/Thebus/Number One) grants **DM+2**. Success reveals it is a **particle-beam firing solution**, not a cipher (GM-Only).

### 3. The Chase Begins — how the rivals find out
**Trigger:** The crew (with or without haut-Belzoni) leave for Noricum. **King Oleb gives them the Drinax Ducal codes** on a self-erasing data module (12 weeks / tamper = wiped). → **What's here:** Establish the three pursuing gangs and *how* each learned of the hunt:
- **Faihlokh's Raiders:** Dai Kinnear (telepath) reads the crew/haut-Belzoni — can't read the code but learns they're bound for **Noricum**; *or* the raiders plant a harmless bomb on the Palace and in the confusion **grab haut-Belzoni and the unlocked globe**.
- **Petyr Vallis:** preys along the Aslani border; may appear in any **Dustbelt** system from Noricum on. If haut-Belzoni took the scout **Confidence**, Vallis **captures it at Noricum or Number One**. Vallis may also **contact the crew directly** with an alliance offer (see Rewards) — and asks what they're doing in the Dustbelt.
- **The PRC:** learn of the quest at **Thebus**; may pursue the crew afterward.
→ **Complications:** The three gangs harass the crew throughout, not just at their "home" worlds; the crew's best strategy is to **play them against each other**. If haut-Belzoni runs independently aboard the **Confidence** (capt. Jacro Lekins), it's destroyed by Vallis's corsairs if the crew don't intervene.

### 4. Noricum — the Secret Base (clue 2)
**Trigger:** The crew jump to **Noricum** (Sindal 0408 — old Imperial throne-world, bombed to pre-spaceflight, **Hostile**, FS 0; see `worlds/00_world-index.md`). Lightly travelled; tourist ships avoid it. Albe-globe coordinates lead into a **Death Zone** in the Black Mountains — mutagenic-weapon fallout, live viral cultures. → **What's here:** A sensor-shielded Imperial arsenal-base, findable **only by landing and searching on foot** (vacc suits required). Surface map (see Map Key below). The entrance is a huge **bonded-superdense hatch** with the Star Dragon crest, **underwater** behind a failing **dam** — the crew must blow the dam or release water to reach it; the base beyond is partly waterlogged.
- **Searching a surface hex:** **Difficult (10+) Recon or Electronics (sensors)** (1D hrs, INT). Keep searching until the entrance is found; **task-chains speed it** (e.g. Drive+DEX → Electronics(sensors)+INT → Recon+INT to sweep by hovercraft).
- **Contamination:** **Routine (6+) Vacc Suit (END)**, one check per day searching; failure → **Noricum Disasters** table below.
- **Inside the base (the crew quickly learn):** it was a **military depot/arsenal** (silos & vaults, few barracks) built to store something valuable; it was **looted long ago, repeatedly** — almost everything of value is gone (wading through waist-deep toxic water for nothing). Searching reveals a **secret door** into an undisturbed vault: empty of treasure but holding, on a pedestal, an **intact naval particle cannon** (identical to the Harrier's, ~3 m long, hard to extract). Burnt into the opposite ferrocrete wall: **(a)** another particle-beam firing solution (digits, like the globe's), and **(b)** a set of jump coordinates in old Star Guard "interlocking hyperspheres" notation → **Average (8+) Astrogation (EDU)** translates it to a **jump solution for the Ace system** (Sindal 0109).
- **The ambush (THE PIRATES):** Faihlokh's raiders waited for the crew to find the base, then swoop to seize whatever's found. Faihlokh's tactic: if crew left ship-guards, one ship **draws them off**; his second ship lands and he **leads a boarding squad into the base**. *If Faihlokh wins:* the "treasure" (one particle cannon) is a crushing anticlimax; furious, he's talked down by Dai Kinnear into **sealing the crew in the base and leaving** to chase the other two globes (Dpres/Yggdrasil). *If the crew kill Faihlokh and he can't escape:* **Dai Kinnear surrenders and offers to switch sides** (she wants the winning team and is an adept telepath).
- **Escaping the base:** easier than entering — a few hours finds a **flooded tunnel** out; **Athletics (endurance) or Vacc Suit check** to swim/walk it.
- **Moving on:** clues point to **Thebus** (couriers attacked there) — via library data / Science (history) 2+ (rebels hit Thebus & Noricum after Salif; couriers would have been at Thebus then), haut-Belzoni's suggestion, Streetwise 2+ or pirate-lore (the **Proactive Recycling Corporation** works the Thebus wrecks), or Dai Kinnear's tip.

**Noricum Disasters table** (failed Vacc Suit check):
| Effect | Result |
|---|---|
| −1 | Fatigued; **DM−2 to all rolls** until rested |
| −2 | Slips down an incline; **1D damage** |
| −3 | Suit seals damaged; **DM−4 to all future Vacc Suit checks** until repaired |
| −4 | Suit penetrated; **1D×50 rad exposure** |
| −5 | Suit penetrated; **END −1 permanently** |
| −6 or worse | Suit penetrated; **END −2 permanently**, **Very Difficult (12+) Medic (EDU)** within 6 hrs or die |

**Noricum surface Map Key:**
- **A. Small Homestead** — questioning locals (**Routine 6+ Persuade, SOC**) → **DM+1** to all checks to find the base.
- **B. Hot Zone** — radiation higher than normal; **1D×100 rad exposure**.
- **C. Old Road** — once found, follow it for **DM+1** to all base-finding checks.
- **D. Broken Ground** — **DM−2 to Vacc Suit checks**.
- **E. Dam** — failing hydroelectric dam; the lake behind is far larger than intended.
- **F. Secret Base Entrance** — underwater; blow the dam / release water to access; base is partly waterlogged.

### 5. Thebus — the Distress Call & the PRC ambush (clue 3a)
**Trigger:** The crew jump to **Thebus** (Sindal 0309 — Po Ni Lo, **Tolerant**, FS 1). On arrival they detect a **Signal GK** (Vilani SOS) distress call from near the gas giant **Olo** (the third planet). → **What's here:** The yacht **Lyonesse II**, ~10,000,000 km off (≈10 hrs at 3G). En route the crew can interrogate "Baron Caruda": he claims to be a noble from Modika (Deneb) who damaged his ship fuel-scooping; he'll say anything to lure them close, dangles "several thousand credits" reward, and admits spotting "just such a ship" (a 2,000-yr-old Sindalian courier) while scooping. → **GM-Only — the Q-ship trap:** "Caruda" is **lying** — he's PRC. Recent piracy cut into PRC profits, so they rigged the **Lyonesse II as a makeshift Q-ship** to bait pirates. The plan:
  - The yacht lures pirates in close to Olo.
  - If the crew **dock**, **four pirates in boarding vacc suits** waiting in the airlock storm the docking ship to take it intact.
  - Meanwhile three more ships — two **Star Ray-class Interceptors** and a **Vulture-class Salvage Hauler** (see Ships / `ships-of-the-reach.md`) — emerge from behind a moonlet and spring the trap.
  - **President Yang** rides the lead Interceptor, the **Invigilator**; her sister ship **Death's Actuary** protects Yang's ship at all costs.
  - If it goes badly, the yacht (jump drive shot, irreparable) becomes a **fireship** — a rack of nuclear missiles in its cargo bay detonates for **6D damage** to any ship at Close range.
→ **The Confidence:** if haut-Belzoni is still independent, he insists on aiding the "stricken" yacht and runs straight into the ambush.
→ **Negotiating with the PRC:** they're not bloodthirsty — they only want no competition in their star-turf. **Very Difficult (12+) Diplomacy (INT)** with DMs:
  - **+1** crew did not attack the yacht; **−2** attacked then surrendered when the trap sprang; **+4** attacked and **forced the PRC to retreat**.
  - **+1** offer to share the Treasure of Sindal; **+1** agree not to attack ships within 6 parsecs of Thebus.
  - **+1** Tolerated by Imperium (Standing 6+); **+2** Allied with Imperium (20+); **−1** Irritant (−6); **−2** Infamous (−21); **−4** Enemy (−40).
→ **The Junkyard:** the PRC base in Olo orbit — old wrecks among "suspiciously recent components" (anyone who scans too closely "ends up as spare parts"). If the crew deal/win, **President Yang** admits they found a Sindalian courier years ago but left it (too damaged to reclaim) far out-system.
→ **The Courier (the clue):** the wreck floats ~900,000,000 km beyond Olo (flight several days). **Electronics (sensors) (EDU)** to find it in the debris, or board and search by hand for the **message-globe** (intact, clutched in a 2,000-yr-dead pilot's glove). Opening it with Oleb's codes = **another particle-beam firing solution + ACE**. The courier's **black box** (computer fried, recorder intact) records its last minutes: it jumped from Noricum to rendezvous, found the rebels had broken through at Salif, took a fuel-tank hit and ran out-system. **Key clue:** the *other* courier (Yggdrasil-bound globe) radioed it was attempting a jump to neighbouring **Number One**.

### 6. Number One — Audience with the Warden & Dragon's Deep (clue 3b)
**Trigger:** The crew jump to **Number One** (Sindal 0208 — Fl, **Suspicious**, FS 2; an ocean world of millions, once a Sindalian prison colony). *(If the crew haven't met Petyr Vallis yet, his ships are in this system.)* → **Warden Rannib:** the hereditary Warden ("Keeper of the Keys… Lord-Regent of the World of Number One") is an **eight-year-old girl**, enchanted by tales of pirates **getting their just deserts** (raiders from Oghma/Acis/Tyr scarred the world within living memory; pirate-punishment stories are her bedtime reading). Her viziers run things, but she wants to meet the crew. → **Dragonsdome:** the oldest settlement (pop. ~500,000), grown around the prison, ruled by the Warden; controls the starport atop a mountain rising from the ocean 5 km north — a **submersible funicular** runs down to the dome. *(GM-Only:* Vallis's agent **Hokmah/Hokmar** is at the Warden's Court and **spies on the crew** — if they reveal the courier's location or damage their pirate-reputation at the audience, he reports it to Vallis.) → **To reach the archives,** impress Rannib: **Art (performer)** (heroic pirate-battle tales), **Persuade** (they need her help to stop pirates), or **Advocate** (befuddle her with legal jargon) — *or* **bribe a vizier Cr7,000** (± Effect of a Streetwise check × Cr1,000). → **The Dome Archives:** date back 2,000 yrs, preserved on imperishable **everglass** (a 300-yr-old cultural movement copied Sindalian-era files). **Investigate or Admin (INT)** digs up the record: a damaged courier landed a week after the Battle of Thebus; its pilot was accused of breaching a secure area and imprisoned **with "all his belongings"** in **Dragon's Deep** (the prison's ultimate solitary — prisoners lowered in bathyspheres into the abyss until they atoned or were crushed). The then-Warden, hedging on who'd win the war, hid the courier in the deepest ocean.

#### 6a. Exploring Dragon's Deep (the dive)
**Trigger:** The crew descend to recover the courier's globe. The Deep is too deep for normal submersibles — they must **hire a deep-sea submersible**. **Streetwise check** finds the **Barnacle** (and captain **Balamira Greenfell**) for **Cr5,000**. → **What's here:** an 8-hr cruise to the Deep through bioluminescent abyssal life and old bathyscape wreckage; Greenfell cheerfully notes Number One still **executes convicted space pirates by pressure-crushing**.
- **The Barnacle under pressure:** it cannot endure the Deep indefinitely. **Locating the courier:** **Difficult (10+) Electronics (sensors)** (1D hrs, INT). Failure inflicts **temporary** damage = hours spent searching (stress of resisting the water-weight) — repairable with a Mechanics check at safe depth, **but if the Barnacle takes *actual* damage while under pressure, the temporary damage becomes real.**
- **The Courier:** still intact in an **everglass shell** (flaws let some water in; it will never fly again). The globe may still be inside. **Three options:**
  1. **Dock** and use the manipulator arm to cut the everglass over the ventral airlock, then cross over and recover the globe — risk: losing the shell makes the ship **crumple** under pressure.
  2. **Send a remote prospecting drone** — but it only works underwater, so the crew must **flood the courier**, possibly hastening its destruction.
  3. **Raise the ship** to the surface and recover the globe conventionally.
- **Breaching the courier:** once breached it survives **2D minutes** before the pressure destroys it; **flooding** (for a drone) costs **1D** of those minutes. Finding the globe = a **Recon check (DEX)**, but the searcher's max DM from DEX+Recon **cannot exceed his Vacc Suit (manual) or Electronics (remote ops) (drone) skill**. Anyone aboard when it collapses is **almost certainly killed (6D/round)**.
- **If the courier is destroyed before recovery:** an **Electronics (sensors) (INT)** check spots the globe tumbling deeper; the **Barnacle can dive after it**, and grabbing it with the manipulator arm = **Electronics (remote ops) (DEX)**, each attempt costing the Barnacle a temporary-damage point. **Lose the globe into the dark and the Treasure of Sindal is forever lost.**

#### 6b. Depth Charges (the attack on the way up)
**Trigger:** As the crew ascend (perhaps towing the wreck), Greenfell detects **depth charges** tumbling down. → **What's here:** each charge inflicts **4D** if it explodes near the submersible; while Greenfell pilots (her Seafarer skill), the crew can set up **task-chains** to help dodge. After near-misses, a **narrowcast sonic message** comes from another submersible — the attacker (pick the most fitting):
- **Petyr Vallis** — learned of the scheme via underworld rumour + Hokmah's report; wants the globe or a treasure-share.
- **Faihlokh** — followed the crew from Noricum; wants the globe.
- **Warden Rannib's Guards** — the dome realised the crew are notorious pirates and intend to send them to the bottom.
→ **Complications / Escaping:** the crew can **cut a deal** or **flee**. Escape = a series of **opposed checks** between the attacker's **Electronics (sensors)** and the pilot's **Seafarer (submersible)**; the submersible must accumulate an **Effect total of 4+** over multiple checks to get away.
→ **Opening the globe:** the Number One globe (originally bound for **Yggdrasil**) again holds only a particle-beam configuration/targeting string + **ACE**.

### 7. Ace — the Sky-Map (the reveal)
**Trigger:** The crew jump to **Ace** (Sindal 0109 — Argon-CO2 atmosphere, **Suspicious**, FS 1; domed/tunnel population, very vulnerable to orbital attack). A religious group, the **Sages of Ace**, read the future in the lightning-blue aurorae. → **Dead ends:** scanning for a courier wreck or asking the authorities finds **nothing** — no surviving institution dates to the Sindalian Empire (only the oldest domes do). Ace is the destination *named* in every globe, not another wreck site. → **The Rivals arrive:** any surviving gangs (Faihlokh, the PRC, Vallis, or others drawn in) **jump into Ace soon after the crew.** If rivals hold one or more globes, the crew must **acquire that data or deal to share the treasure**; if the crew hold all four globes, they must **solve the map before the rivals arrive and try to take it by force.**

#### 7a. The Secret — making the sky-map
- **The principle (GM-Only above):** each globe held a particle-beam targeting/firing solution; Ace's atmosphere flares under electrical discharge; **a particle beam = an electrical discharge.** Fire **four** particle beams into Ace's sky, each programmed from one globe's data, and the interfering beams **paint a star-map five hundred miles wide across the sky**. The map highlights a point **two parsecs coreward of Ace** — **officially empty deep space.** (Faint hearts never won the Treasure of Sindal.)
- **"We don't have four ships!"** Options for four particle-beam sources: (a) **four ships fitted with particle beams**; (b) **jury-rigged satellites** with particle beams, battery packs and computers — **Mechanics check, 1D hrs, Cr50,000 in parts** *plus* the particle-beam weapon each; (c) **bargain with the rival pirates** to use their particle weapons. *(Nearest particle beams for purchase: **Salif**.)*

#### 7b. Making the jump (to the Hidden Station)
**Trigger:** The crew (and possibly rivals) jump to the projected deep-space coordinates. → **What's here:** A blind jump into empty space. **Astrogation check**, taking **1D×10 min** (Core p.148). → **Rival jump procedure** (per rival ship not allied with the crew; **base DM+2 to Astrogation** unless noted): throw **2D** — **6-** they don't attempt; **7-9** they jump, taking 1D×10 min; **10+** they rush it (**DM−3** to finish in **1D** min). If a rival **fails the Astrogation check**, throw 2D again: **10+** they jump anyway and **misjump** (GM may bring them back later, or they're never seen again). **Optional:** make the crew's Astrogation check **secretly** — a failed check is a **misjump**, and they won't know if they jumped to the right place.

### 8. The Hidden Station — the Treasure (finale)
**Trigger:** A successful jump arrives at a lonely deep-space outpost. → **What's here:** An antique space station bearing the **Sindalian Star Guard crest**, plus a large reserve of refined hydrogen (a secret Star Guard refuelling depot). As the crew approach the docking port, an automated message plays: *"This is Admiral Gani Peras of the Star Guard. This station is off limits to unauthorised personnel. If you attempt to dock, this station will automatically self-destruct. Officers of the Star Guard with Ducal-level clearance, transmit your authorisation codes before attempting to dock."* → **The same Ducal codes** (used on the globes) **deactivate the auto-destruct**; transmitting them triggers a second Peras message: *"This station holds the contents of the Imperial Vault of the Star Dragon. I implore you, use this treasure with wisdom and humility. The mistakes of the old dynasty must not be repeated. I have done my duty to Humaniti; I ask you to do likewise and wish you good luck. Admiral Gani Peras, signing off."* → **mythic-gm keyed trigger (the docking gate — must resolve before entry):** the crew need the **Drinax Ducal codes** to dock safely. If they lack them (module self-erased after 12 weeks, was wiped by tampering, or they never had them) or attempt to dock without transmitting, the station **self-destructs** — destroying the arsenal (a possible "the treasure is denied to everyone" ending). Confirm the codes are live before narrating a safe dock.

#### 8a. Exploring the Station
- **Two docked ships:** both **identical to the crew's Harrier** (see `the-harrier.md` / `ships-of-the-reach.md`) but **stripped of spare parts** and needing extensive refit before they can fly — apparently cannibalised to provision a **third Harrier** that left with Gani and her crew. **No clue where the Great Admiral went** after the war.
- **Fuel:** the tanks hold **20,000 + 2D×1,000 tons** of refined hydrogen.
- **The Treasure (main cargo bay):** "the treasure stands in mute ranks; line after line of warheads." The Empire's strength was never gold — it was **atomics and plague bombs**. Gani tricked the last Emperor into surrendering his doomsday retaliatory strike.

#### 8b. The Treasure (the warheads — full mechanics)
- **24 atomic warheads + 24 plague warheads**, each torpedo-launcher compatible.
- **Atomic warheads:** treat as **high-strength nuclear torpedoes, 2DD damage** — devastating, but *secondary* to the plague.
- **Plague warheads (artificial viral/bacteriological, high-TL14, overcome most countermeasures):** if dropped on a populated planet, roll **2D** + DMs:
  - **+1 per TL below 14**; **−1 per TL above 15**.
  - **+2 per extra warhead deployed.**
  - **−DM equal to the planet's Population code.**
  - **−2** if population is dispersed (Ag world or garden world); **+2** if especially concentrated (asteroid or hostile-atmosphere world).
  - **−2** sizeable alien minority; **−4** majority-alien population.
  - **Result 11+:** population **wiped out entirely.** **8–10:** Population score **−1D** (it's an exponent — −2 ≈ 99% mortality). **5–7:** Population **−1** (≈90% mortality). **4-:** no status change, but **1D×10% mortality.**
  - *Worked example (text):* Vallis drops six plague warheads on Aslan **Khusai** (Tliowaha 0403, A576655-C, TL C/12, Pop 6, Ag, majority-Aslan): +10 (5 extra) +2 (TL12) −6 (Pop) −2 (Ag) −4 (majority Aslan) = **DM +0**; on **8+** he kills millions of Aslan.
  - **Aftermath:** a successfully bio-attacked planet may collapse into chaos even if many survive (**Government → 0, Law → 0**) and/or be designated a **Red Zone**. Formerly-allied worlds may turn on the crew if their WMD use is discovered. A buyer for the plagues is unlikely at best — "these goods are literally toxic."

## Rewards & Consequences
- **The arsenal (the "treasure"):** **24 atomic warheads (2DD each) + 24 plague warheads** (mechanics above), plus **20,000 + 2D×1,000 tons** of refined hydrogen at the station, and **two stripped Harriers** (extensive refit needed — a long-term hull/parts windfall; see `the-harrier.md` RU economy). The intact **naval particle cannon** found on Noricum (Harrier-grade) is a salvageable weapon if the crew can extract/transport it. *There is no gold — the legendary hoard is a lie.*
- **A defector:** **Dai Kinnear** (a Telepathy-3 psion) may join the crew if Faihlokh dies and they win — a rare and valuable asset.
- **An alliance with Petyr Vallis:** if the crew are successful pirates who've expanded Drinax's reach/prestige, Vallis may offer mutual access to his **network of safe havens and fences**. Accepting lets the crew **roll 1D+2 and move that many "port-steps" toward Haven status** (split across systems as they like, e.g. roll 5 = five systems +1 step toward Haven, or one system +5 steps, or any combination summing to 5). **Cost: Aslan Hierate Standing −2D.**
- **Pay/relationships:** haut-Belzoni pays **High Passage** if ferried; goodwill with Drinax court (Oleb, Voha) for delivering on the errand; possible Imperial-favour points if the crew let the Imperium take the weapons cleanly.
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest — no rescues):**
  - **Noricum death zones** kill via the **Noricum Disasters** table (permanent END loss; death on −6 without a Medic save) — botched Vacc Suit checks have lasting costs.
  - **Number One's Deep** is lethal: anyone aboard the courier when it collapses takes **6D/round**; lose the globe into the abyss and **the treasure is lost forever**; the dome **executes pirates by pressure-crushing**.
  - **The PRC ambush / fireship** (6D at Close), **depth charges** (4D), and three armed gangs make the chase deadly if the crew try to out-shoot everyone instead of dealing.
  - **A misjump** at Ace (failed/secret Astrogation) strands or scatters the crew (1D×1D parsecs per Core).
  - **Losing the race:** if rivals get the arsenal, see the GM-Only "what the hunters do" list — most likely the Imperium ends up with it, or Vallis murders an Aslan world.
  - **Using the WMDs openly is an abhorrent crime** — an Atrocity with multiplied weight (see Empire/Reputation below).

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(Apply via `empire-reputation.md` "Per-adventure effects." This adventure's signature is its capacity to create **Lost Worlds**.)*
- **WMD deployment = Atrocity (the central consequence).** Using a weapon openly against a civilian population counts as a **double Atrocity** if used against a world **not** aligned with a great power (including the opposing power's worlds), and an **octuple-strength Atrocity** if used against a **great-power** world. *Example (text):* Vallis bombing Aslan **Khusai** would drop his **Imperial Standing by 2D** and his **Aslan Standing by 8D**. Resolve Standing/PRI per `piracy-raiding.md`; surface both empires' reactions.
- **Lost Worlds:** any world the crew (or a rival they enabled) **destroy** with these warheads is **removed from the recruitment table and contributes no Fighting Strength** at the Finale (`empire-reputation.md`: Lost Worlds, Recruitment). A bio-attacked survivor world may collapse to **Gov 0 / Law 0** and/or become a **Red Zone** — and **formerly-allied worlds may turn against the crew** if WMD use is discovered (Attitude drops; betrayal).
- **The clue-worlds (relationship colour, mostly):** Noricum (0408, **Hostile**, FS 0) is a dead world — no recruitment value. **Thebus** (0309, **Tolerant**, FS 1), **Number One** (0208, **Suspicious**, FS 2) and **Ace** (0109, **Suspicious**, FS 1) are all recruitment candidates — how the crew treat the PRC, Warden Rannib's court, and the Ace authorities should nudge those Attitudes (deals, restraint, or raiding) per the standard step rules. Negotiating peace with the PRC (e.g. "no attacks within 6 parsecs of Thebus") is a relationship the GM can convert to a Thebus-area Attitude bump.
- **Vallis alliance:** the **1D+2 port-steps toward Haven** is a direct empire-track reward — apply across chosen systems; record the **Aslan Standing −2D** cost.
- **Doctrine note:** quietly removing/hiding/selling the weapons is a **subtle** outcome; using them as a Norse/Danegeld threat is an **overt/intimidation** outcome — and "repeating the follies of Sindal and Drinax" caps loyalty and invites rebellion (record which doctrine the crew lean on). The Imperium taking the weapons cleanly may earn Imperial goodwill but forfeits the prize.
- **Imperial heat at Drinax:** mishandled Dinner Encounters (insulting haut-Belzoni; the Torpol merchant) cost **Imperial Standing −1D** each via Thao Poloc; success keeps Drinax looking "innocent" per Oleb's plan.
