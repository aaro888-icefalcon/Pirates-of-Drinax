# Blood of the Star Dragon

> Use this when: King Oleb crashes and all three Drinaxian royals vanish at once — running the search for the king in the Pillars of Night, the premature palace coup (board-game minigame), the trail to Asim, the Dragon Fortress and the kidnapped Princess Rao, and the Zhodani "ghost ship" rendezvous at the gas giant Calvin. This is the **succession-crisis** adventure that sets up the Finale.

**Logline:** The grav-belt of fat, fatalistic King Oleb fails and he falls into the radioactive Pillars; the same day Prince Harrick jumps for Asim and Princess Rao simply disappears — and the crew, who likely helped build her power, must rescue the dying king, win a civil war for the Dragon Throne, and then choose who (if anyone) rules Drinax as the campaign turns toward Finale.

## At a glance
- **Locations:** **Drinax** (0603) — the **Pillars of Night** (a flash-storm-wracked ruined arcology field on the surface) and the **Floating Palace** (the coup). Then **Asim** (0503, B867564-6 Ag Ni) — Capital City / Governor's Palace, the hidden **Dragon Fortress** in the northern mountains, and the gas giant **Calvin** (Zhodani rendezvous). Tliowaha subsector throughout.
- **Threat/level:** **Late campaign**, immediately before Finale. Threat is **political more than martial** — the deadly bits are optional (the Fungal Vole, an assassin, storming an entrenched fortress, a fight with battle-dressed Hawk Warriors or teleporting Zhodani jump-marines). The smart path is search → maneuver → persuade. **Outcomes here set the Finale's starting conditions.**
- **Est. length:** Medium-long (2–4 sessions): a search set-piece, a strategic coup minigame, an investigation/infiltration on Asim, a ship boarding.
- **Prerequisites:** The crew should be **deeply entangled in Drinaxian politics** — ideally sworn (even loosely) to Princess Rao or Prince Harrick. Standing with the campaign's major palace NPCs (Lord Wrax, Scholar Voha, Lady Hil, etc.) **directly feeds the coup**. *The Vorito Gambit* outcomes color the GeDeCo/assassin background; *The Treasure of Sindal* WMDs may be stashed in the Fortress vault.
- **Arc position:** **The penultimate chapter of Book 1.** It resolves the Harrick-vs-Rao question (or hands the throne to the crew) and seeds *Finale* directly: who holds the Dragon Throne, whether the palace is united, and whether Drinax leaves with secret Zhodani backing. (`empire-reputation.md` already keys the Zhodani recruitment bonus to this adventure.)

## Player-Facing
- **The title — "Star Dragon":** Not a ship or monster. It is the **Star Dragon Throne of Sindal** — the Dragon Throne, a monstrous chair forged from three thousand years of defeated spaceships. "Blood of the Star Dragon" = the **royal bloodline** and the fight over who inherits it. The whole adventure is a succession crisis.
- **The triple disappearance (hook):** In a single day: King **Oleb's** grav-belt fails on a hunting trip and he spirals down into the **Pillars of Night** (distress call received; Oleb was "not in control"); Prince **Harrick** abruptly jumps for **Asim** on a "mysterious mission"; Princess **Rao** simply vanishes. The Floating Palace teeters toward chaos.
- **The search (Scene 1):** The Hawk Warrior bodyguard squad (commander **Brie Tollar**) lost the king in a flash storm over the Pillars — a field of superdense arcology stubs that jam sensors and throw lightning. Every flyable craft scrambles. If the crew rank high enough in Drinax, command of the search is theirs; otherwise Brie commands and they assist.
- **Finding the king:** Oleb is alive but **impaled and dying** on spiky scrap at a pillar's base. He is lucid in flashes — appoints the crew absurd titles, reaffirms the **Letter of Marque** ("Bring back the Kingdom and all will be forgiven"), warns them off the **Scholar's Tower** ("don't let those vampires get their claws into my carcass"), and gives a dying steer: **Harrick is "not right for it"; Rao "will be Queen, but… there's too much of the Sindalian in her… warn her."** Then he lapses unconscious. He has effectively issued a do-not-resuscitate order.
- **The coup (Scene 3):** Returning to the palace, the crew find **partisans of Prince Harrick have prematurely declared the king dead** and are seizing key sections before Rao can rally — even though **Harrick himself didn't order it** and has already left for Asim. A fighter orders the crew to the Starport Dock "on Lord Wrax's authority"; docking instead at the **Royal Dock** declares them against Harrick. Civil war engulfs the Floating Palace.
- **To Asim (Scene 5):** However the coup ends, the trail leads to **Asim**, where Harrick jumped. The presenting puzzle: Harrick's ship landed in the northern mountains, then took off and **filed a flight plan for Torpol still squawking the Imperial callsign** — i.e., it looks like Harrick left the system. (Veterans/Deception/comms experts smell a false flag.)
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** Oleb is dying and wants the kingdom restored but is ambivalent about both heirs. Harrick's faction launched the coup. Rao is missing. On Asim, Governor **Telwax** (Harrick's old army friend) is evasive about the prince's whereabouts.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **Harrick kidnapped Rao.** Security analysis of Rao's quarters (after the coup) shows she was abducted by her brother, smuggled out in stealth suits, and is held — **in a cryoberth** — in the secret **Dragon Fortress** on Asim. Most of the garrison has no idea she's a prisoner; only Harrick and his yacht crew know.
- **The Torpol flight plan is a decoy.** Harrick's yacht "very obviously and blatantly" jumped for Torpol but is actually doing an **in-system micro-jump** and will **reappear in orbit of Asim in about a week**. (If the crew take the Torpol bait uncritically even after the GM prompts "are you sure?", run the Bad Ending in Scene 5.)
- **Why Harrick did it.** Harrick believes Rao is **secretly allied with an enemy of the Kingdom** and is too persuasive to expose in open court (he fears that would itself trigger civil war). His plan: kidnap Rao, force a confession, and bring proof to Oleb. He is the **"gardener of worlds"** — wants a *small*, peaceful kingdom of a few worlds (Asim, Pourne, Torpol, Clarke, Hilfer, Paal, Blue, Byrni at most), teachers not pirates. He is horrified that his followers launched the coup in his name.
- **Rao's secret allies are the Zhodani.** Rao's "distant friends" are agents of the **Zhodani Consulate's Tozjabr** (intelligence/special-ops). They meet her every ~2 months at Asim's gas giant **Calvin**. The Zhodani are the real-world identity of the **"Ghost of the Reach"** (their stealth hulls + memory-wiping abductions spawned the legend). They back a strong Drinax to distract the Imperium from the Consulate border — **and if Drinax must fail, they want it to fail *catastrophically*** as a lasting distraction. Their price so far has only been **trade/disposition intel**.
- **Rao's escalation plan (the real stakes for Finale).** Rao intends to ask the Zhodani to **telepathically nudge wavering planetary leaders** into committing to the reborn Kingdom — coercion that would taint the restoration. (This is the "too much Sindalian in her" Oleb warned about.) **If the crew let this proceed, it carries into Finale as a recruitment cheat** — see Empire effects.
- **Who downed Oleb? (deliberately left open.)** The GM may pick — or leave it a lingering mystery; **the choice does not change the scenario.** Candidates: random equipment failure ("a very Traveller result"); Oleb's own despair/suicide; a Vespexer pot-shot; **Harrick's** Scholar's-Tower allies; **Rao** (to clear an obstacle); a rival courtier; an **Imperial** assassin (if crew Imperial Standing ≤ −40); an **Aslan** assassin (if crew are Aslan enemies); a **GeDeCo** assassin (psychohistory wants Drinax to *nearly* succeed — *Vorito Gambit*); or an enemy the crew personally made. The **missing Hawk Warrior Hurgon/Horgan** may secretly be the assassin.
- **The Fortress vault.** Built for planet-searing WMDs Drinax never actually had. **If the crew recovered the Sindalian doomsday weapons in *The Treasure of Sindal* and gave them to Oleb, one bomb is stashed here.**

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. The Lost King — search of the Pillars of Night
**Trigger:** Distress call from Oleb's hunt; the palace scrambles every craft toward the Pillars. Crew lead the search (if high Drinax standing) or assist under **Brie Tollar**. → **What's here:** The Pillars are blackened superdense arcology stubs — a giant lightning-rod field of EM interference that **jams sensors and navigation**; flash storms can hit at any moment. Ask the crew how they hunt:
- **Scan:** **Formidable (14+) Electronics (sensors) (EDU)** to find Oleb's life-signs. Build task chains for DM — **Flyer/Pilot** to weave the Pillars, **Science** to calibrate sensors.
- **Trajectory:** From the point the Hawk Warriors lost him, **Very Difficult (12+) Pilot (INT)** to retrace his course, then **Difficult (10+) Recon or Electronics (sensors) (INT)** to spot him.
- **Ground search (if Vespexer contacts):** local guides → just **Difficult (10+) Recon (INT)**.
→ **Complications (Dangers):** roll/choose as the search runs — **Hostile conditions** (a flash storm: flyers make Pilot/Flyer, those afoot make Survival to shelter); **dangerous airspace** (superdense spikes + arc lightning); **Vespexers** (some tribes shoot at flyers with SAMs/laser rifles but welcome people on foot as fresh meat — **1D Vespexers per 2 Travellers, round up**); **assassins** (if one downed Oleb, it lurks to confirm the kill — human + Aslan stat blocks below; **suicide-charge on death/KO**; two-assassin team if 4+ crew); the **Fungal Vole** (Oleb's imported quarry — a cockroach the size of a small elephant, ambush-pouncer, spore-burst). **Failed search check → hit them with a Danger, or raise the difficulty of healing the king.**
→ **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** Reaching Oleb is a **must-happen** beat. If the crew flounder, narrate Brie's squad or a Vespexer guide closing the gap — but make them *pay* (a Danger, or a worse Medic difficulty in Scene 2). The king **must** be found alive to deliver his dying steer.

### 2. Oleb, Alive!? — the dying king
**Trigger:** Crew reach the blasted cave at a pillar's base. → **What's here:** Oleb, impaled on superdense spikes, half-lucid on wine and painkillers. **Roleplay beats:** the "emergency medical pack" is a bottle of red wine; he names the crew Court Poet Extraordinary / Bard of Sindal / "Grand High Tum-ti-tump of the Outrim Void" and offers a dukedom (**any world in the old Empire of Sindal** — current locals may not recognize the title); reaffirms the **Letter of Marque**; warns off the **Scholar's Tower** healers ("vampires"); and gives the load-bearing line: **Harrick "not right for it"; Rao "will be Queen… too much of the Sindalian in her… warn her."** Then he passes out and snores. → **Treating Oleb: Difficult (10+) Medic (EDU)** keeps him from dying immediately (does **not** cure him; harder if the crew were slow to find him). → **Complications:** Oleb has effectively ordered **do-not-resuscitate**; the palace's TL15 facilities *could* save him, but it's the crew's moral/political call whether to honor his wish. **Either way he is "mostly dead" for the rest of the adventure** — out of the decision. If lurking Dangers remain, the crew must fight clear of the Pillars hauling Oleb's considerable bulk.

### 3. The Premature Coup — civil war for the Floating Palace (minigame)
**Trigger:** Crew approach the palace and three flyers order them to the **Starport Dock** "on Lord Wrax's authority." (Comms checks reveal weapons fire aboard and that **Harrick partisans declared the king dead** and are seizing sections before Rao can react; Harrick is absent and didn't sanction it.) Docking at the **Royal Dock** = siding against Harrick; the lead fighter will try to intimidate (won't fire if it thinks the king/corpse is at risk). → **What's here:** A **strategic minigame on the Floating Palace map** (see `the-floating-palace.md`). Each side projects power as **faction dice** (Harrick / Rao / Neutral / Travellers) placed by region.
- **Initial disposition:** Royal Dock 2 Rao · Great Hall 2 Neutral · Ambassador's Wing 4 Neutral · Palace 2 Rao + 4 Neutral · Starport Docks 2 Harrick · Starport Control 2 Rao · Bazaar 1 Rao + 3 Neutral · Life Support 1 Harrick + 1 Neutral · Star Guard Barracks 2 Harrick + 2 Neutral · Scholar's Tower 4 Harrick · Gallery 1 Harrick + 1 Neutral · Royal Apartments 1 Rao + 2 Neutral · Court Chambers 2 Harrick + 2 Neutral · Gardens 1 Rao + 1 Neutral · Throne Room 2 Rao. Add a **King Oleb token** at his location.
- **Adjust by campaign history (the payoff of prior play):** **Lord Wrax** (default Harrick) — if he respects/befriends the crew, flip the Barracks Neutral dice to the crew's side (or all-Neutral if crew back Rao). **Scholar Voha** (Harrick, immovable) — if weakened, reduce Scholar's Tower to **3 Harrick dice**. **Lady Hil** (default Rao) — if impressed → keep; if killed/discredited → her Palace Rao dice become Neutral. **Kasiyl** (Ahroay'if ally) → +1 die to the crew's faction at the docks. **Rachando** (if kept alive post-*Vorito*) → +1 die at the docks. **Imperial Ambassador Thao Poloc** — Imperial Standing **6+** flips 1 Neutral die in the Ambassador's Wing to the crew; **20+** flips 3. **Chieftain Galx** (if helped) → roll 2D, on **8+** add 2 dice in the Gardens. **GM may add 1–4 dice** to any faction for other past actions (other pirate ships, refugees, grudges).
- **Turn structure:** factions act in order, Harrick first. Per turn: pick a region with ≥2 of your dice; for **every 2 dice** take one action (**Attack / Persuade / Secure / Lockdown**); repeat in other valid regions; then any **unrolled** dice may **Move** to an adjacent region (usable next turn — so defenders fire first when attacked). A present Traveller may aid **one** action/turn with a relevant skill.
  - **Attack:** roll your dice on the region — **8+** kill 1 enemy die, **12+** kill 2, **4-** lose 1 of yours. Aid: Gun Combat / Heavy Weapons / Leadership / Melee / Tactics(military).
  - **Persuade:** **8+** convert 1 Neutral (in region or adjacent) to your side; **12+** two Neutrals or 1 enemy die. Aid: Persuade / Leadership. **DM+1 per adjacent region you hold.**
  - **Lockdown** (clear non-combatants): **4+** remove 1 Neutral, **8+** two, **12+** three.
  - **Secure** (only regions you hold): **8+** → enemy Attacks here at DM−1; **12+** → DM−2. Aid: Tactics(military) / Medic. (Lost if you lose hold.)
- **Winning:** hold **3 of 4** key locations — **Throne Room, Palace, Life Support, King Oleb** — at the start of your turn (a region is "held" if only your dice are there), **OR** wipe out all rival dice (Attacks or Persuaded surrender). GM may call it early if hopeless/unassailable.
- **Optional complications:** **Return of the Searchers** (the 6-dice Hawk Warrior search squad returns — whose side?); **Fire** (any die rolling a 1 sets a region alight; spreads to a random adjacent region each turn; Securing puts it out); **Through the Underlinth** (an ally offers a perilous anti-grav route to flank — navigate gravity pockets to surprise the enemy); **Voha's Weapons** (Scholar Voha fields **4 dice of TL15 combat androids** against Rao); **Self-Destruct** (cornered young Harrick zealots holding Life Support threaten to overload the fusion reactors — must be stopped). Out-of-game Traveller moves (jamming comms with Electronics, strafing the palace with the Harrier's turrets) are GM-adjudicated outside the dice.
→ **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** the coup **must resolve** before Asim. If the crew stall, advance the AI factions on their turns honestly (Harrick first) and let the dice fall — **no rescue from a losing position** beyond what the rules allow.

### 4. After the Coup — wherever it lands, the road runs to Asim
**Trigger:** Coup resolves. → **What's here:** Security analysis of Rao's quarters reveals **Harrick kidnapped her** (stealth suits; sabotaged/insider-manned monitors) and smuggled her onto his yacht. **All branches converge on Asim:**
- **Rao's faction won, crew backed Rao** → entrusted to **rescue the princess** from her brother (and either get Harrick to publicly recant + swear loyalty, or eliminate him — but finding Rao comes first).
- **Harrick's faction won, crew backed Harrick** → sent to Asim to tell the prince the coup happened, and to make Rao endorse his regime — **"or else she must meet with a suitable accident."**
- **Crew's faction lost & they must flee** → their path to legitimacy is to **capture Harrick or Rao** and lead a reconquest.
- **Crew seized the palace for themselves & won** → secure rule by **marrying Rao and/or Harrick**, or by ensuring neither can ever challenge them — which means a trip to Asim.
→ **Complications:** "Uneasy squirms the arse that sits on the … Dragon Throne." Whoever the crew back (or become), the unresolved royals on Asim are the loose thread.

### 5. To Asim — the trail and the decoy
**Trigger:** Crew jump to **Asim** (0503). → **What's here:** A dull agri-world; ex-bureaucrat cabal (**the Foundation**) still runs local affairs under Drinaxi tribute. Governor **Lady Telwax** (Star Guard veteran, Harrick's army friend) rules from an overstated Governor's Palace with a dozen battle-dressed Hawk Warriors. Land openly at the starport (Telwax summons the crew; lies that Harrick "already left for Torpol") or land covertly outside Capital City. **Leads to the Fortress:**
- **Veterans of Drinax shortcut:** any crew member who is **from Drinax with a Marine/Army/Navy term** has heard of the secret **Dragon Fortress** and roughly where it is — skip the hunt.
- **Playing Telwax:** she's Harrick's, but pragmatic — she'll switch to Rao/the crew if Harrick's coup looks doomed, and **interrogates the crew** under cover of asking for news (they're the first ship in since the coup). **Deception (INT)** reveals she's pumping them and likely knows where Harrick is; **Deception/Persuade** can turn her; or monitor her comms to locate the **Dragon Fortress north of the city.**
- **Starport logs:** show Harrick's ship jumped in, landed in the northern mountains, took off, and **filed for Torpol still on the Imperial callsign** (Deception/comms experts flag the **false flag**). Access = **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics (computers)** hack (1D×10 min, INT), or **Difficult (10+) Deception** to sneak into the tower (1D×10 min, SOC), or take it by force (4 Hawk Warriors + two 40-soldier platoons), or **Very Difficult (12+) Diplomat** to request them (failure: "only Governor Telwax can release them"). **Full access also reveals Rao's yacht visited Asim ~every 2 months and each time micro-jumped to another body in-system** (the Calvin rendezvous).
- **Street rumours:** **Carouse/Persuade (SOC)**, add Effect, on the Semi-Accurate Rumours of Asim table (1 Foundation plots a coup [true, perpetually]; 2 Oleb dead, heirs fighting [true]; 3 Rao's yacht refuels here [true-ish]; 4 secret psionic institute in the northern mountains [false, but note "psionics"]; 5 GeDeCo experiments in the mountains [false, closer]; 6 a free trader misjumped into the belt and saw a **giant warship** [true — not yet relevant]; 7 secret Drinaxi base north of the city; **8+** exact coords of **"Fort Dragon,"** probably sitting on WMDs). **Cleon Hardy** (if befriended in prior play and brought along) can simply hand over the 8+ rumour — for a favor (better treatment for Asim later).
→ **The Bad Ending (if the crew chase Torpol uncritically):** Rao tells Harrick where she meets her allies → Harrick's yacht collects Harrick, Rao and troops → at the Calvin rendezvous a "misunderstanding and frank exchange of views" leaves the yacht and **the entire next generation of Drinaxian royalty as an expanding debris cloud** → go to **Finale with no royal survivors.**

### 6. The Dragon Fortress — Asim's WMD redoubt
**Trigger:** Crew locate the fortress in the northern mountains (sensor/Recon: **success** if they know the rough area; **DM+4** and they spot the lurking patrol if they know the exact site; on a fail they **miss the flying Hawk Warrior patrol overhead**). → **What's here:** A plasma-mined warren — surface signs are only an unnaturally flat landing pad and narrow **vertical shafts**; inside, twisting shafts with kill-zones and strongpoints designed to hold out **indefinitely** against an Asimen revolt. Garrison: **~24 Hawk Warriors + ~50 local mercenaries**. Commander **Colonel Sedelti Wrax** (Lord Wrax's daughter, pro-Harrick, despises pirates) — **does not know Rao is here** (smuggled in by cryoberth; she won't search royal baggage). **Rao is held secretly; only Harrick and his yacht crew know. Harrick's apartments sit at the fortress heart, above the WMD vault** (which holds a Sindalian doomsday bomb **iff** the crew gave Oleb the *Treasure of Sindal* weapons). → **Ways in:**
- **As allies** (crew back Harrick): land, be escorted in → skip to Scene 7.
- **As guests:** submit to disarm/search → Harrick meets them; **Diplomat (SOC)** to keep basic gear; **Effect 4+** and he doesn't disarm them at all.
- **Sneak in:** patrol outside — **Very Difficult (12+) Stealth (DEX)**, or **Average (8+)** if you counter their thermal/light-intensifier sensors; **Difficult (10+) Athletics (strength)** + climbing tools down the shaft; **Electronics (sensors)** to beat each of two internal sensor lines; plus a manned pillbox kill-zone (2 of Harrick's men). Aids: steal Hawk Warrior armor; find connecting natural caves (parasitic cave-worms, rockfalls, crevasses); have **Telwax** (who outranks Sedelti) smuggle them in or demand to inspect the fortress; convince **Sedelti** to search Harrick's quarters with proof of the coup; stage an **Asimen revolt** (needs a rallying figure like Cleon Hardy) and slip into the understaffed fort; or **wait ~1 week** and intercept the returning yacht (Scene 7).
- **Storming it (the bloody path):** charging entrenched troops up a chokepoint is **suicide**. Survivable only if you first **tell the Hawk Warriors that Harrick kidnapped Rao** (**Persuade (SOC)**, DM+ with Telwax/Wrax backing) to sow confusion, or **raise an actual Asimen rebellion** (Cleon Hardy + armed ships + elite crew).
→ **Complications:** Sedelti is loyal and competent; the Hawk Warriors here are battle-dressed (laser rifles 5D+3) but **not** truly elite (outdated, ceremonial-rooted training). The mercenaries are mesh-and-autorifle filler.

### 7. Splitting Heirs — the confrontation
**Trigger:** Crew reach Harrick (and the captive Rao) — as conspirators, guests, eavesdroppers, or conquerors. → **What's here:** **Both royals petition the crew.** Both are shocked if told Oleb is dead. Harrick wants the crew to come to the Calvin rendezvous and witness Rao's "perfidy" firsthand (and back him on his return); Rao wants the crew to bring Harrick along so she can prove she was justified. **Harrick's case:** Rao is in a destructive co-dependence with Oleb, has made a shameful secret alliance she won't even admit, and the crew are "the lynchpin of her schemes" — if they reject her, her shadow-allies fall away; he wants a small peaceful kingdom. **Rao's case:** Sindal can rise again; Harrick's little-kingdom vision is folly ("greatness or obscurity, nothing between"); she admits overtures to many powers and offers to introduce her **"distant friends"** — **if pressed, that they are Zhodani Tozjabr** (she hides this if she can, since forewarned telepath-hunters are dangerous). → **Decision:** the crew choose whom to support / whether anyone goes to Calvin. → **Harrick's plan if he leads:** board the ghost ship at the rendezvous and capture Rao's allies — taking **4 psionic shield helmets** for himself and 3 lead warriors if he knows they face Zhodani; if the crew run tactics for him he asks only that **one enemy be taken alive.** → **Rao's plan if she leads / is a prisoner:** if captive, she "thinks very hard" to **warn the Zhodani** via their routine telepathic life-scans (they recognize her mind-pattern); if Harrick is *her* prisoner, she'll ask the Zhodani to **mind-edit Harrick** into loyal support.

### 8. Coreward Ghosts — the Zhodani rendezvous at Calvin
**Trigger:** Someone goes to the rendezvous near Asim's lonely gas giant **Calvin** (Rao's coords). → **What's here:** A **stealth-hulled Zhodani Tozjabr ship** lurking — the real "Ghost of the Reach." Detect it on a **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics (sensors)** check. Aboard: **Intendant Chbradlklodgiepr Dlmshar** + Zhodani jump-marines + Tozjabr crew. The Tozjabr think this is a routine support meeting with Rao. → **If the crew don't intervene (default play-out):** the approaching yacht signals to dock; **if Rao is a prisoner, roll 2D — on 10+ Dlmshar catches her distress call** and readies a teleport assault (2 jump-marines + scuttling charges gut a yacht's isolated engineering); the ghost ship maneuvers to dock at the yacht's starboard airlock; **roll 2D — on 8+ the Zhodani life-scan detects the boarding party** and they ready for battle (Hawk Warriors must then thruster-pack across); if the yacht docks unnoticed, Hawk Warriors charge the moment airlocks open. → **Zhodani priorities (in order):** **Best** — quietly help Rao/the crew secure Drinax (the Kingdom owes the Consulate a favor, unseen); **Good** — kill or memory-wipe attackers; **Acceptable** — **self-destruct to avoid exposure.** Their overriding goal is **secrecy** — they will not let their Reach operations become public. → **Quick boarding resolution:** the two sides are ~equal in armor/weapons; **Zhodani edge in skill/tactics, Hawk Warriors edge in numbers; the swing factor is whether the Zhodani had marines on-duty (i.e., were forewarned).** → **Complications:** psionics in play (Telepathy mind-blasts, Teleport, Telekinesis); a captured-but-not-killed Zhodani is needed for "proof"; killing them all destroys Harrick's evidence; pushing them too hard triggers self-destruct.

### 9. The Heir to Drinax — set the board for Finale
**Trigger:** The Calvin business resolves. → **What's here:** The open questions the crew now answer (and carry into *Finale*): **Is Oleb alive? If not, who is heir?** **Who rules Drinax** — Rao, Harrick, a co-rule, or the crew themselves? **Is the palace united or still bleeding?** **Are the Zhodani now friends (favor owed, in secret) or captured/dead?** **Are the crew still pursuing Oleb's restoration plan, or building their own pirate empire?** → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** record the resulting **Finale starting conditions** on the empire tracker now (throne-holder, palace unity, Zhodani status, royal survivors). *Alea iacta est* — the next chapter is Finale.

## Key NPCs

**King Oleb (dying Monarch of the Floating Palace; the prize to be saved, the steer to be heeded)** — *Severely wounded, "mostly dead" all adventure.* Fat, fatalistic, given to black moods and a death-wish; reaffirms the Letter of Marque and effectively orders his own do-not-resuscitate. **Treating: Difficult (10+) Medic (EDU)** keeps him alive but uncured (harder if found slowly). Motive: a tired king who wants the Kingdom restored but trusts neither heir fully — fears the "Sindalian" excess in Rao and the lack of fire in Harrick.

**Prince Harrick — "the Gardener of Worlds" (heir #1; reluctant, idealistic)** — Human, male, 54. STR 5 DEX 6 END 8 INT 8 EDU 10 SOC 14. Skills: Leadership 3, Persuade 1, Gun Combat (energy) 1, Melee (blade) 1, Athletics (dexterity) 1, Vacc Suit 1. *Cell-by-cell rebuilt clone, wracked by phantom pains; speaks simply, halts to find words, stiff military bearing.* Motive: a small, peaceful kingdom of teachers; convinced Rao's secret alliance is shameful treason; horrified his followers launched the coup in his name; **kidnapped Rao** to extract a confession, not to seize the throne.

**Princess Rao — "Architect of Empire" (heir #2; brilliant, ruthless, Zhodani-backed)** — Human, female, 28. STR 8 DEX 6 END 6 INT 11 EDU 12 SOC 14. Skills: Diplomat 4, Carouse 3, Persuade 3, Science (philosophy) 2. *Charming, eloquent, never doubts herself.* Motive: restore Sindal to greatness ("greatness or obscurity, nothing between"); willing to use **Zhodani telepathic coercion** to whip wavering worlds into the Kingdom — the seed of the restoration's moral rot.

**Brie Tollar (commander, Hawk Warrior bodyguard)** — Oleb's chief bodyguard for years; genuinely alarmed (this is no prank). Commands the Pillars search if the crew don't; uses Hawk Warrior stats. Motive: find the king, hold the palace together.

**Lady Telwax (Governor of Asim; Harrick's army friend)** — Star Guard veteran governor; lives at the overstated Governor's Palace with wife, child, servants, and ~12 Hawk Warriors. Pro-Harrick but a pragmatic survivor — switches to Rao/the crew if Harrick's coup looks doomed; **interrogates the crew under cover of asking for news.** Motive: back the winning royal and keep Asim quiet. (Use a high-SOC officer profile; emphasize Deception/Admin/Persuade.)

## Ships

**The Star Dragon Throne (the campaign's MacGuffin — not a vessel).** The **Dragon Throne of Sindal**, a monstrous chair forged from three thousand years of **defeated spaceships**. While Oleb sits it, his reign keeps Drinax from anarchy; with the king down and both heirs gone, its sudden vacancy is what triggers the coup. The entire adventure is the fight over **who sits the Star Dragon** — record the answer for Finale. *(If a player expects a literal "Star Dragon" ship or beast, there is none; the title is the dynasty/throne.)*

**Prince Harrick's Yacht — refitted Type-Y Yacht (Jal Trader hull); Harrick's transport and Rao's prison-mover.** TL ~12. ~400t. **Refit: cargo space traded for a bigger jump drive → jump-2.** Armament: **two pop-up triple turrets, each three pulse lasers** (6 pulse lasers total). **16 staterooms** (room for crew, the two nobles, and Hawk Warriors; 4 staterooms reserved for crew). Used to micro-jump in-system (faking the Torpol departure) and to carry the cryoberthed Rao. Role: the decoy, the prison transport, and the ship that goes to the Calvin rendezvous (and is destroyed in the Bad Ending). *(Tonnage/standard Type-Y assumed; the text specifies the jump-2 + dual-triple-turret + 16-stateroom refit — `(verify)` exact High Guard hull figures.)*

**Zhodani Tozjabr Stealth Ship — the "Ghost of the Reach."** A **stealth-hulled** Consulate special-ops vessel; routine telepathic life-scans of approaching ships, capable of memory-wiping abducted crews. Carries Intendant Dlmshar, jump-marines, and Tozjabr spacers; has a **self-destruct** it will trigger rather than be exposed. The legend of the Ghost of the Reach is **this ship** (see Ships of the Reach). Role: the rendezvous antagonist at Calvin; secret backer of Rao. *(Specific class/tonnage not given in this chapter — `(verify)` via the Ships of the Reach / Appendix.)*

**The "giant warship" rumour (red herring here).** A free trader who misjumped into Asim's belt reports a **giant warship** in-system (Rumours table 6). **True but not relevant to this adventure** — a deliberate dangling thread (likely the Sindalian-treasure/Finale-scale threats elsewhere); do not let it derail the Calvin business.

## Rewards & Consequences
- **The Dragon Throne (the real prize).** This adventure decides **who rules Drinax** going into Finale: Princess Rao, Prince Harrick, a brokered co-rule, **or the crew themselves** (by conquest and/or marrying into the line). Whoever sits the throne shapes the Finale's policy choices and recruitment.
- **Oleb's gifts:** the **Letter of Marque reaffirmed** ("all will be forgiven"); a nominal **dukedom over any world of the old Empire of Sindal** (locals may not honor it); absurd court titles. **The 10% Drinax tithe still applies** (refusing forfeits the marque — see piracy-raiding.md).
- **Zhodani relationship (a double-edged reward).** If the crew end the adventure with the **Zhodani as secret friends** (Rao's alliance intact / a favor owed), Drinax gains a covert sponsor — and a **Finale recruitment cheat** (telepathic coercion of wavering worlds). If they **capture a Zhodani** they hold Harrick's proof of Rao's treason; if they **kill/expose** the Tozjabr, the Consulate connection dies (and the Zhodani prefer **self-destruct/memory-wipe** to exposure).
- **Salvage / hardware:** captured **Hawk Warrior TL15 combat armour** and **Stagger Laser Rifles**; the Fortress's **Sindalian doomsday bomb** *iff* the crew previously stocked the vault (*Treasure of Sindal*); a captured **Zhodani stealth ship** would be an extraordinary (and extraordinarily dangerous) prize.
- **Contacts:** **Cleon Hardy** (Asim representative — favor for the 8+ rumour, wants better treatment for Asim); a pragmatic working relationship with **Governor Telwax**; possibly **Colonel Sedelti Wrax** if turned with proof.
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest):**
  - **Slow search / failed checks in the Pillars** → eat a Danger (flash storm, Vespexers, **assassin suicide-charge**, **Fungal Vole** spore-burst) and/or a **harder Medic check** to keep Oleb alive — he can simply die.
  - **Losing the coup** → the crew (or their faction) are forced to flee; legitimacy now requires a risky reconquest. **Self-Destruct complication** can destroy the palace if cornered Harrick zealots aren't stopped.
  - **Chasing the Torpol decoy uncritically** → **all three royals die at Calvin**; Finale begins with no royal survivors.
  - **Storming the Dragon Fortress head-on** → "dying horribly in a narrow chokepoint"; only survivable after sowing confusion (telling the garrison of the kidnap) or raising a real Asimen revolt.
  - **Fighting Hawk Warriors** (Combat Armour +19, 5D+3 laser rifles) or **Zhodani jump-marines** (Combat Armour +18, PGMP/Gauss, Teleport, mind-blasts) → lethal; the Aslan/human assassins detonate on death.
  - **Pushing the Zhodani** → memory-wipe or **ship self-destruct**; you can lose your prisoner, your proof, and your covert ally at a stroke.
  - **Killing Rao and/or Harrick** carelessly → you may extinguish the bloodline and inherit a fractured, illegitimate kingdom going into Finale.

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(Apply via empire-reputation.md "Per-adventure effects.")*
- **This is a campaign-state adventure, not an Attitude-shift adventure.** Its outputs are **Finale starting conditions**, recorded on the empire tracker:
  1. **Throne-holder** (Rao / Harrick / co-rule / the crew / none). Sets who picks Finale **Royal Policy** and the **starting Policy ratings** (if the crew hold the throne, they set policy freely — empire-reputation.md "Shaping Royal Policy").
  2. **Palace unity** (clean win vs. a blood-soaked or contested palace) — colors how willingly Drinax's own people back the restoration.
  3. **Royal survivors** (both / one / none) — "none" is the Bad-Ending state.
  4. **Zhodani status** (secret ally / captured / dead-or-exposed).
- **Telepathic Coercion bonus (keyed in empire-reputation.md):** if the adventure leaves Drinax with **secret Zhodani support**, at the Finale **roll 1D**; the crew may apply **DM+2 to that many recruitment attempts** (psionic tampering of wavering worlds). This is Rao's plan made mechanical — and the moral cost Oleb warned about.
- **Drinax itself stays Haven** (the crew's home base) regardless — but **who commands it** changes. **Asim** (0503, default Neutral) attitude may shift on how the crew treat it: honoring Cleon Hardy / sparing the Foundation / not brutalizing the Fortress nudges it toward alliance; a bloody conquest or a stashed planet-buster sours it.
- **Standing hooks:** an **Imperial-assassin** backstory implies very low Imperial Standing (≤ −40); an **Aslan-assassin** implies Aslan enmity; a **GeDeCo** assassin ties to *Vorito Gambit*. None are mandated shifts — they're consequences of prior Standing, surfaced here.
- **No Lost Worlds** are created (unless the crew detonate the Fortress WMD — GM's call on the atrocity fallout, per ihatei.md/empire-reputation.md "atrocities").
