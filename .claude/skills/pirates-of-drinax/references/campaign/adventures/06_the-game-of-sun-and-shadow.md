# The Game of Sun & Shadow

> Use this when: an Imperial pirate-hunting fleet (the **198th Punitive Flotilla**, flagship *Eurisko*) sweeps out of Pax Rulin under the ambitious **Vice-Admiral Krond** and parks four parsecs from Drinax, arresting pirates by the dozen. The crew must keep Drinax's role as a pirate sponsor **secret** and drive the fleet out — by deflecting suspicion, freeing captured pirates, kidnapping Krond's betrothed, or making his command untenable. Cross-ref `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Imperial Standing, world Attitude, Lost Worlds), `setting/personalities-and-factions.md` (Krond, Darokyn, the pirate lords), `setting/factions.md` (Imperium scale; Aslan Hierate lever), `setting/ships-of-the-reach.md` (Imperial hulls), `setting/worlds/00_world-index.md` (target worlds).

**Logline:** A vainglorious young noble brings an Imperial battlecruiser to the Reach to hunt down the "pirate world" and burn it for the glory of his court — and Drinax is at the top of his suspect list. There is no monster here, only ambition, a manipulated bride, and a telepath nobody knows about. The crew win not by fighting a 75,000-ton warship but by making sure the hammer never falls on the Floating Palace.

## At a glance
- **Where:** Sector-wide. Imperial base at **Pax Rulin** (Tobia 0604); the flotilla forward-bases at **Byrni** (Borderlands 0107). Pressure falls on **Drinax** (Tlaiowaha 0603) and rival pirate worlds — **Theev** (Sindal 0506), **Oghma** (Sindal 0410), **Tyr** (Dpres 0608), **Wildeman** (Tobia 0409). Sub-sites: pirate meet at **Thebus** (Sindal 0309); prisoner-transfer route to the internment camp on **Iilgan** (Tobia 0309); a waystation in **The World** system; smuggling run to **Paal** (Tlaiowaha 0805).
- **Threat:** A **75,000-ton Planet-class heavy cruiser** plus a dozen+ escorts. The crew are **hopelessly outmatched** in a stand-up fight. This is **stealth / intrigue / sabotage / clock-management**, not fleet battle. Picking ship fights with the flotilla loses the campaign.
- **Length:** Long — plays out over **many months of game time** as a parallel clock. Designed to run **alongside** other adventures and empire-building, not as one block. Subsystem-driven (a toolbox of events, not a linear scene chain), so expect heavy improvisation.
- **Prerequisites:** The crew operate from Drinax as sponsored pirates. **Triggered by Heat:** enough raiding near Drinax / in the coreward Reach summons the sweep (see `00_overview.md`: "enough raiding... summons the *Game of Sun and Shadow*"). The crew's **Imperial Standing** sets the starting difficulty (Drinax's starting Suspicion = a function of Standing).
- **Arc slot:** Mid-campaign **Peril / survival** episode (Adventure 6). It is the Imperium's first serious attempt to find and destroy Drinax. Surviving it cheaply protects the campaign's foundation; bungling it can **end the campaign** (Floating Palace destroyed) or saddle Drinax with crippling Standing loss. Dual-POV in print (players run both pirates and a B-team of navy crew); **solo play runs only the Pirate Travellers** — the navy strand is the antagonist clock the GM/engine drives.

## Player-Facing (the hook)
*Safe to narrate.*

- **The threat arrives.** Word reaches Drinax (via merchant **Rachando**, **Sal Dancet**, or the crew's own informants) that an **Imperial punitive fleet** has crossed the Reach from Pax Rulin and based itself at **Byrni**, only ~4 parsecs from the Floating Palace. Unlike past flotillas that swept through and went home, this one is **staying**. Its commander, a young Tobian noble named **Vice-Admiral Krond**, has vowed to find the "pirate world" that sponsors the corsairs of the Outrim Void and **blast it from orbit**. Pirates are being captured daily and crammed into a prison hulk.
- **King Oleb is afraid.** With a 75,000-ton warship parked next door and his secret patronage of piracy one denunciation away from exposure, Oleb wants the crew to **make Drinax safe** — deflect the fleet's attention, get it to leave, and above all keep Drinax's name off Krond's target list. He does not care how, as long as it does not invite the whole Imperium down on him.
- **The Corsair's offer (Pirate Hunters).** The infamous pirate **Zhuekvi the Corsair** (a garrulous old Vargr) makes contact through a mutual broker. If the crew are riding high (**6+ ports at Friendly/Haven**) he offers to **join them with his four Corsairs**; otherwise he just wants a **non-aggression pact**. He proposes meeting at a hunting lodge on **Thebus**. *(He is also being hunted — Gazelles may catch him mid-meeting.)*
- **The Letter (Sun & Shadow).** Through covert channels the crew meet a scoundrel, **Lang Fossa**, who will sell them — for **Cr50,000** — a private letter from Krond to his betrothed, **Eridani**, niece of the Sector Duke. The letter lays bare Krond's plan to stay until he finds and destroys the pirate world, and hints at the levers the crew can pull against him (rescue the prisoners; kidnap Eridani; make his position untenable).
- **The dinner (Guess Who's Coming to Drinax).** Sooner or later Krond and his courtiers **visit the Floating Palace** (it is famous; there is even an Imperial ambassador at Oleb's court). The crew can attend as "courtiers" — cover to investigate, deflect suspicion, or attempt something reckless against Krond.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned.*

- **No villain, only ambition.** Krond is a wealthy fop with a planet-sized ego — and an iron will beneath it. His rise in the Tobia Planetary Navy rode his name and his **engagement to the Sector Duke's niece**. He needs an **epic victory** (a destroyed "pirate kingdom") to cement his court position and keep the marriage. He wears an **Imperial Navy** uniform he is not entitled to (he commands a *planetary* navy) — a provocation that signals overreach. He is sincere that piracy is "a fraud the navy tolerates," and he means to prove it by burning a world.
- **Ogleby is a secret telepath.** Krond's grotesque servant/bodyguard **Ogleby** is a hidden **psion (PSI 12, Telepathy 5, Teleportation 2, Clairvoyance 2)**. He drives Krond's enemies mad, and **murders Captain Lenat** with a telepathic assault that mimics a stroke (see *A Verdict of Accidental Death*). After Lenat dies he turns his attention on the crew. **Ogleby also "pushed" Eridani** into the engagement and into visiting Byrni, to reinforce his conditioning. **Krond has no idea** how much Ogleby manipulates everyone around him. A vial of **Psi-Drug** hidden in Krond's luxuries cargo is the clue that "someone in Krond's circle is a psion."
- **The Old Woman is the crew's hidden ally — and is doomed.** **Captain Kaja Lenat** ("the Old Woman"), the *Eurisko*'s real commander, opposes Krond: she knows that a sustained Imperial build-up on the Aslan border is dangerous, and that Krond is endangering the Reach. She gathers an **Imperial Intelligence report** warning that an excessive military presence could **unify the warring Aslan clans against a common enemy** — intending to use it to get Krond recalled. **Ogleby kills her before she can.** Her death is the campaign's turning point: morale collapses and Krond rallies the fleet to his "destroy the pirate world" crusade in her name.
- **The Traitor leaks Krond's letter.** Krond's letter reaches Drinax because **someone in the fleet betrayed their oath**. Pick one: **Lenat** (diverts it, then commits suicide to escape the shame); **Fixer Brell** (sold it for cash); **Red** (a disillusioned ex-pirate's child who wants to defect); or **a Navy B-team Traveller** who wants to turn pirate. The traitor's identity is a recurring intrigue thread.
- **The real win condition is the clock, not a battle.** The fleet has a **Hunting DM** that climbs every month and a **Suspicion Rating** per candidate world. When either maxes (**Hunting DM +12**, or a world's **Suspicion Rating hits 25**), the **Planetary Assault** fires on the highest-Suspicion world. **Drinax's starting Suspicion is set by Imperial Standing** — the more notorious the crew, the closer Drinax already is to the crosshairs. The crew's job is to keep Drinax's Suspicion **low** (deflect it onto Theev/Oghma/Tyr/Wildeman, or onto the Aslan) and/or to force Krond out before the clock runs.
- **If Drinax is the target and the assault fires, the campaign is effectively over.** The Floating Palace is a hard target but the *Eurisko* has the firepower; its destruction kills key NPCs (Oleb et al.) and the dream of a renewed Kingdom. Be honest about this stake — it is the whole point of the adventure.
- **Theev's hidden backer.** If the crew deflect the assault onto **Theev** and it is destroyed, **GeDeCo** (Theev's secret backer) is enraged at the loss of a major investment, and the crew's underworld reputation is ruined **if their hand is ever revealed**. Theev's destruction would also remove the Reach's main repair yard/fence — which Drinax could then **inherit** (a perverse upside). *(Cross-ref Darokyn's secret in `personalities-and-factions.md`.)*

## Core subsystems (run these in parallel)

### Suspicion Rating (the target-selection clock)
- Each candidate world has a **Suspicion Rating**, starting at **3** — **except Drinax**, which starts at a value keyed to the crew's **Imperial Standing**:
  | Pirate Travellers' Imperial Standing | Drinax starting Suspicion |
  |---|---|
  | Ally (+21) | −6 |
  | Tolerated (+6) | −3 |
  | Ignored (+5 to −5) | 0 |
  | Irritant (−6) | +3 |
  | Infamous (−21) | +6 |
  | Enemy (−40) | +9 |
- **Other candidates:** Theev, Oghma (Sindal 0410), Tyr (Dpres 0608), Wildeman (Tobia 0409). The Navy strand's intelligence-gathering **raises** ratings; the Pirate strand's deflection **lowers** Drinax's and can shift the surplus onto another world (see Deflecting Suspicion).
- **The world with the highest Suspicion after the campaign's intelligence phase is the Planetary Assault target.** Display the scores openly; their *purpose* stays hidden until **The Letter** or the assault.
- *Solo note:* track Suspicion Ratings as a **Mythic clock per candidate world**. The engine raises Drinax's rating on Imperial-success beats and lowers it on the crew's deflection successes.

### The Hunt clock (Punitive Action)
- Each month the GM rolls **2D + DM** on the Punitive Action table to see how many pirates the flotilla bags. **Apply DM +1 per month after the first** (so +0 month 1, +1 month 2, …). **If this DM reaches +12 → Planetary Assault.**
  | 2D+DM | Result |
  |---|---|
  | ≤5 | No captures. |
  | 6–7 | A handful of free traders / thugs barely count as pirates. |
  | 8–9 | A few minor pirate ships. **One port moves one step away from Haven.** |
  | 10–11 | A notable pirate ship (Corsair+). **Two ports move one step away from Haven.** |
  | 12+ | A pirate base, a **Named Pirate**, or an **ally of the crew**. **Three ports move one step away from Haven.** |
- **Crew actions modify the roll** — e.g. ratting out rival pirates' locations to the navy = **DM +2** (lowers Drinax's relative danger but harms those worlds).
- **Natural 1 or natural 6** on either die → a flotilla vessel is damaged/degraded. **Double 1 or double 6** → a small ship lost with all hands, or the *Gorluun* badly damaged. (This is how the crew's indirect pressure visibly wears the fleet down.)
- **Named Pirates** the navy may capture/kill (pick one, then 1D: 1–2 killed, 3+ jailed on the *Petal*): **Petyr Vallis, Admiral Darokyn, Hroal Irontooth, Failokh, Proactive Recycling, Mira Silverhand, Zhuekvi.** **Allies the crew recruited can also be arrested/killed** — losing them is a real cost of letting the Hunt run.

### Spies & informants (the exposure subsystem)
- Whenever the **Pirate Travellers visit a port**, roll **Risk of Spies** (per `piracy-raiding.md` / `00_overview.md`). With pirate-hunters active, cross-reference the **Effect** with the **Hunter Threat** table:
  | Effect | Result |
  |---|---|
  | 1–2 | An informant reports the crew's movements. **DM +2** to the next Risk-of-Spies roll. |
  | 3–4 | An Imperial detachment arrives **2D weeks** later; finds clues to the crew's next stop on **10+**. |
  | 5–6 | An Imperial detachment arrives **1D weeks** later; finds clues on **8+**. |
  | 7–8 | A detachment is in-system and arrives **1D hours** after the crew land. |
  | 9+ | **Ambush** — roll **three times** on the Imperial Detachment table for the opposition. |
- **Imperial Detachment (2D):** 2 = *Storm of Arrows* + 16 Light Fighters / 100 Marines · 3–4 = 2 Patrol Ships / 50 Marines · 5–6 = 1 Patrol Ship / 20 Marines · 7 = 5 Light Fighters + Modular Cutter / 10 Marines · 8–9 = 1 Gazelle Close Escort / 20 Marines · 10–11 = 2 Gazelle Close Escorts + 4 Light Fighters / 50 Marines · 12 = *Gorluun* (8,000t escort) / 100 Marines. *(Re-roll impossible results, e.g. multiple *Storm of Arrows*.)*
- **DMs:** disguise / low profile = **negative**; law-breaking / property damage / loitering = **positive**; the crew's **Imperial Standing** modifies (Ally −2 … Enemy +4). **Attacking Imperial ships/troops, even in self-defence, lowers Standing by ≥1D.** Alternatively the GM may skip the roll and just **raise Drinax's Suspicion by +2**.

## Scenes / Subsystems (the toolbox)

### A. Sun & Shadow — Deflecting Suspicion (the core pirate task)
- **Trigger:** From the moment the crew learn of the fleet (or read The Letter), they work to keep Drinax off the target list. This is the **inverse of the navy's intelligence-gathering**: instead of finding clues, the crew **hide their tracks and plant false ones.**
- **Tactics (the menu):** find & bribe/mislead Imperial spies; have allied governments lie to the Imperium; plant evidence "proving" the pirates aren't from Drinax; **prey on ships leaving Drinax** (so attacks don't trace home); **gift the pirate-hunters "thank-you" tokens** for "saving" Drinax; sneak aboard the *Eurisko* and **alter its computer records**; mount **false-flag raids** (broadcast a *false* identity, e.g. raiders "from Theev").
- **Resolution:** GM assigns a check or **task chain** per scheme (e.g. **Investigate** to find a spy → **Deception** to feed him lies; **Diplomat** to get Torpol's government to lie; **Electronics (computers)** to plant files). Each task takes ~**1D weeks** to take effect and **reduces Drinax's Suspicion Rating by the Effect** of the check.
- **Moving the Crosshairs:** Each deflection, choose to **lower Drinax's rating** *or* **shift the surplus onto another world** (raising that world's Suspicion by an equal amount — e.g. framing Theev). **Implausible target** (a TL1 wasteland) → only **half** the Effect transfers. **The Aslan Hierate** is a valid deflection target: even Krond won't start a punitive war with a whole star empire — pinning the raids on the Aslan can take Drinax out of the picture entirely (see Political Pressure for the related, larger lever).
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** No single must-happen die. Run each deflection as a `task-system.md` check/chain; apply the Effect to the relevant Suspicion clock. The inexorable mechanics are the **Hunt DM** and **Suspicion clocks** above — advance them on the calendar even while the crew detour into other adventures.

### B. In Chains (rescuing the captured pirates) — *optional*
- **Trigger:** The flotilla has jailed dozens of pirates on the prison hulk **Petal** and stacked their ships in the megafreighter **Prophet Zamon**. ~6 months in, supplies run low and Krond sends the **Petal + Prophet Zamon + 2 Gazelles** back toward the Imperium: route **Tech-World → Exe → Cordan → Wildeman**, then a detour to **Iilgan** (Tobia 0309) where the wrecks are dumped in orbit and prisoners interned in a wilderness camp. The crew can **intercept the convoy en route** or **follow it and break the prisoners out of the camp**.
- **What's here — the *Petal*:** lightly armed (3 double turrets, twin pulse lasers each); crew 9 + **~30 Marines**; prisoners crammed in stripped staterooms (key/dangerous prisoners chained). **The whole prisoner deck is rigged with anaesthetic-gas dispensers** — any room or the whole deck can be knock-out gassed at a button; Marines wear rebreathers on duty.
- **The *Prophet Zamon*:** standard 30,000t megafreighter; crew 40 + ~12 Marines. Captured ships ride in **support webs** inside 2,000t cargo containers. **Freeing a ship = 30 min per 100 tons + an Athletics (dexterity) check**, or blast it free (1D damage to that ship, 2D to any others in the container). **Per freed ship roll 2D:** 10+ flyable now (tanks 1D×10% full); 8–9 hours of repair (tanks 1D×10%); 7− wrecked / months of work.
- **The Internment Camp (Iilgan):** prefab shelters in equatorial savannah; **~40 local troops, TL7 weapons**; a regional spaceport only a few km away launches giant agri-transports to orbit. Freed prisoners can **steal a transport**, reach orbit, and reclaim functional hulls.
- **Pirate's Honour (the payoff):** on a successful rescue, one Traveller makes a **Leadership check (SOC)** — **Effect** = number of pirate bands that **join the crew's fleet**. The rest owe big: **1D Allies, 2D Contacts, 4D Favours.** A **Favour** = **DM +2** to any check interacting with a Reach pirate; **5 Favours** = move a **Tolerant+** port one step toward **Haven**.

### C. The Duke's Niece (kidnapping Eridani) — *optional*
- **Trigger:** Krond's letter (and Ogleby's telepathic nudge) bring **Eridani**, the Sector Duke's niece, to secretly visit **Byrni**. She travels on the **Sunrise** (Herald-class fast messenger, refitted luxury), escorted by the Close Escort **Leopold**; route from Tobia (Fist → Cordan → Tech-World) then branching for Byrni — **leaving Cordan in haste ahead of her convoy** (the window to intercept). The crew can take her *en route*.
- **Defences:** 4 **bodyguards** (elite ex-Marines in battle dress, below) + combat-trained servants; a **TL15 android duplicate** of Eridani; **a single stealth escape pod (DM −4 to detect)**. **If boarded, the real Eridani flees to the pod**, leaving the android to impersonate her. Spotting the android = an **Investigate check** (or any medical/densitometer scan); the android protects "Eridani" then switches to **combat mode** (Self-Destruct 12D, Blast 3). Reprogramming a disabled android = **Formidable (14+) Electronics (computers)**, 1D hrs.
- **Using Eridani (the leverage):** **Ransom** to Krond (for prisoners) or the Duke (~**MCr100**; **−2D Imperial Standing**); **bargaining chip** (Krond's command *and* his marriage both depend on success, so threatening the engagement threatens the fleet); **convince her to stop Krond** (show her he's attacking innocents/committing atrocities → she uses her influence to recall the fleet); **break her telepathic conditioning** (a scan/observation reveals Ogleby's tampering); or, longest shot, **convince her to turn pirate** (she secretly longs for adventure). **Any attack on Eridani = −3D Imperial Standing.**

### D. Planetary Assault (the failure / climax state)
- **Trigger:** **Hunting DM +12**, *or* any world's **Suspicion Rating hits 25**, *or* GM discretion. The flotilla departs Byrni for the **highest-Suspicion** world; *Gorluun* + escorts engage defences while the **Eurisko shells the pirate port**; Marines land to "make arrests."
- **Target outcomes:**
  - **Drinax** — Floating Palace is hard to hit but the *Eurisko* has the firepower. **If hit, the campaign is effectively over** (key NPCs die; the renewed Kingdom is dead). The crew's only saves are: keep Drinax's Suspicion below the rivals', force Krond out first, or have a **Treasure of Sindal** atomic planet-killer to smuggle aboard the *Eurisko* (see Ending the Game).
  - **Theev** — Port Blacksand has many ships but none cruiser-grade; ortillery beats the Widows of Blacksand. **Only save:** if Failohk's company got the **Treasure of Sindal** weapons, an atomic planet-killer detonates on the *Eurisko* at the battle's height. Destroying Theev enrages **GeDeCo** and ruins the crew's underworld rep **if their hand is revealed**.
  - **Oghma** (TL9 ships) — the slave-raiders charge to their deaths in space but the Oghman Clans wage brutal guerrilla war on the surface (minor flotilla losses). Campaign largely unaffected if destroyed.
  - **Tyr** — extensive **automated satellite defences** make it a hard target for an understrength flotilla. Campaign largely unaffected if destroyed.
  - **Wildeman** — TL12 miner ships outclass civilian craft; they lose, but **accelerate a rock into the *Gorluun*'s prow, destroying it.** Campaign largely unaffected if destroyed.
- **Running it:** focus on a small-scale slice for the crew (knock out a defence satellite, hunt a specific pirate amid burning ruins). If the navy B-team clashed with Krond/Ogleby, they get a **suicide mission**.

### E. Ending the Game (proactive ways to break Krond)
- **Attack his lines of supply.** The flotilla needs **~MCr8 of supplies/month**, much shipped from Tobia. The crew can't fight warships but can **blow up freighters/haulers** bound for Byrni and use friendly governments to **cut off local spares/consumables**. This forces Krond to peel ships off pirate-hunting to escort convoys. A **big heist** — e.g. crippling the *Prophet Zamon* on its Tobia return — forces an **ignominious retreat**.
- **Political pressure (the Aslan lever).** As **Lenat** warned, past flotillas swept through and left precisely to **avoid the look of a military build-up on the touchy Aslan border**. If the crew **make contact with an Aslan noble and draw the Hierate's attention** to the Imperial fleet sitting **~4 pc from the key Aslan trade world of Tyokh**, they can spark a confrontation — the crew can't beat the *Eurisko*, but **the Aslan have capital ships of their own.** *(This dovetails with the* Ihatei! *border tension and the Hierate's reaction rules — see `factions.md` and the Threads below.)*
- **Weapons of mass destruction (atrocity option).** With the **Treasure of Sindal** loot, smuggle a **nuclear weapon onto the *Eurisko*** (extremely hard but lethal to the ship) or **drop a virus-bomb on Byrni** (also infects the fleet). **Either is an atrocity: −4D Imperial Standing.**
- **Mutiny / assassination (mostly via the navy B-team).** A crew-side mutiny is implausible. Murdering Krond is possible but hands command to **Commander Steyland**, who follows Krond's last orders until countermanded; navy skullduggery typically ends with those Travellers **fleeing the ship to turn pirate**.

## Key NPCs
*The navy command. Run Lenat as the crew's doomed secret friend; Krond as the engine of the threat; Ogleby as the unseen knife.*

### Vice-Admiral Krond (the antagonist)
Human male, age 34. **STR 8 DEX 8 END 10 INT 6 EDU 10 SOC 13** · skills *Admin 2, Carouse 3, Diplomat 2, Electronics (comms) 1, Leadership 1, Persuade 2, Tactics (naval) 1.*
*Wealthy Tobian fop with a planet-sized ego and a coterie of sycophants; wears an Imperial (not planetary) uniform to overawe. Play the idiot — but there is an **iron fist** behind it: ambition keeps him sharp.* **WANTS:** an **epic, career-making victory** — a destroyed pirate world — to secure his court position and his marriage to Eridani. Will spend the lives of half his crew to get it.

### Captain Kaja Lenat — "the Old Woman" (the doomed ally)
Human female, age 68. **STR 6 DEX 5 END 10 INT 10 EDU 11 SOC 11** · skills *Leadership 5, Tactics (naval) 4, Mechanic 4, Astrogation 3, Athletics (dex) 3, Melee (blade) 3, Admin 3, Vacc Suit 3, Investigate 2, Jack-of-all-Trades 2, Deception 2, Electronics (comms/computers/sensors) 2, Engineer (power) 2, Gunner (turret/capital) 2, Science (planetology) 2, +others.*

Talks in a croak; never backs down.* **Knows** the Aslan-border danger of Krond's build-up; gathers the **Imperial Intelligence report** to get him recalled. **Ogleby murders her** (telepathic "stroke") before she can act. **The crew's best lever inside the fleet — but she will die.**

### Ogleby (the hidden telepath; the real monster)

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — no rescues.*

- **What success buys:** Drinax stays off the target list → the **Floating Palace and the nascent Kingdom survive**; the crew earn Oleb's lasting gratitude. Driving Krond out (supply strangulation, the Aslan confrontation, a kidnapping that forces a recall, or sheer political collapse) ends the Imperial threat for the foreseeable future.
- **In Chains payoff:** a freed-prisoner fleet (**Leadership Effect** = bands that **join**), **1D Allies / 2D Contacts / 4D Favours**; salvageable pirate hulls from the *Prophet Zamon*; gratitude across the Reach's underworld.
- **Zhuekvi:** as an Ally, **four 400t Corsairs** added to the crew's potential fleet (and a lifetime of anecdotes).
- **Eridani:** up to **~MCr100** ransom, or political leverage that can recall the whole fleet — at the price of **−2D to −3D Imperial Standing** if you ransom/harm her.
- **Theev's fall (perverse upside):** if the assault is deflected onto Theev and it burns, **Drinax can inherit** Theev's role as the Reach's repair yard and fence — but **GeDeCo** becomes a dangerous enemy and the crew's rep is wrecked if their hand is revealed.
- **What failure costs (Planetary Assault on Drinax):** the **campaign effectively ends** — the Floating Palace is destroyed, Oleb and key NPCs die, and the dream of a renewed Kingdom dies with them. This is the true stake; do not soften it.
- **Cost of doing it wrong:** **Attacking Imperial ships/troops (even in self-defence) = −1D Standing each time.** **Harming Eridani = −3D**; **ransoming her = −2D**. **WMD atrocities (nuking the *Eurisko*, virus-bombing Byrni) = −4D Standing.** Letting the **Hunt clock** run captures the crew's own recruited allies and named pirate friends. Sloppy port behaviour spikes **Risk of Spies** and invites Imperial Detachments.

## Empire / Reputation effects
Apply per `campaign/empire-reputation.md`. This adventure touches **all three** parallel gauges (don't conflate them):
- **Suspicion Rating** is *local to this adventure* — a target-selection clock, not the campaign Standing track. It opens keyed to the crew's **Imperial Standing** (table above) and is moved by Sun & Shadow deflections / navy intelligence.
- **Imperial Standing** (the great-power heat track in `piracy-raiding.md` / `empire-reputation.md`) is **the lever and the cost**. High Standing makes Drinax start *safer*; firing on Imperial assets, ransoming/harming Eridani, or using WMDs **drives Standing down** (and at **−40 "Enemy"** every non-Friendly port drops one Attitude level — a sector-wide penalty layered on top of the fleet).
- **World Attitude:** the Hunt's monthly captures **push ports one step away from Haven** (8–9 = one port, 10–11 = two, 12+ = three) — a steady erosion of the crew's network the longer the fleet operates. Deflecting suspicion onto **rival pirate worlds** (Theev/Oghma/Tyr/Wildeman) protects Drinax but endangers those worlds (and any alliances there).
- **Lost Worlds:** any world destroyed by the **Planetary Assault** (Drinax, Theev, Oghma, Tyr, Wildeman) is **removed from the recruitment list and contributes no Fighting Strength** at the Finale (`empire-reputation.md`). Drinax's loss ends the campaign outright; a rival's loss removes a future ally/competitor.
- **Aslan Standing (the Political-Pressure lever):** drawing the **Hierate** onto the Imperial fleet uses the crew's Aslan contacts/Standing — it does not by itself shift world Attitudes, but it ties this episode to the broader Aslan-border arc (and a clumsy attempt can sour Hierate Standing — see `factions.md` Reaction rules).

- **Treasure of Sindal** — its weapons are the only thing that can kill the *Eurisko* in a Planetary Assault (atomic planet-killer); having or lacking them shapes the climax. Cross-ref `personalities-and-factions.md` (Failohk) and the *Treasure of Sindal* file.

- **Krond surviving** — if the crew merely *survive* without destroying him, Krond and Ogleby remain a recurring menace; the Imperium's interest in the "pirate world" persists and can escalate toward the campaign endgame (*The Grave of Empires* / Finale — see `factions.md` punitive-fleet scale, 35 FS / 6 Capital Ships).
