# Friends in Dry Places

> Use this when: the haughty Aslan Outcast lord **Utea** hires the crew to source and install life-support and power for his failing *ihatei* camp on **Kteiroa** (Tlaiowaha 0505, ~2 pc from Drinax) — a power plant, a water recycler, piping/conduits, and finally an artesian drilling rig — by piracy, salvage or purchase, then keep the camp from tearing itself apart over the chaos that follows. A recruit-a-world-via-deeds story that turns an *ihatei* threat into a friendly port (or lets it self-destruct). Cross-ref `adventures/03_ihatei.md` (same world, same Utea, the *ihatei* threat clock), `setting/factions.md` (Aslan honour/ihatei/Reaction), `setting/worlds/key-worlds.md` (Kteiroa), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Kteiroa Attitude ladder, MCr10→Haven model).

**Logline:** A proud Aslan exile needs a band of bold pirates to steal a colony's worth of plumbing and power so his desert camp can support a swarm of landless warriors — and the real adventure is the slow-burn political bloodbath that erupts when status-obsessed ihatei start fighting over who gets a hot shower first.

## At a glance
- **Location(s):** **Kteiroa** (Tlaiowaha 0505) — the *ihatei* camp; the hook may begin at **Drinax** (0603) or any port on the Sindalian Main. Equipment-gathering ranges to GM-chosen nearby systems: any **Class A/B starport with a starship-breaker yard**, a **starship graveyard / ship-chop** site (Utea supplies a list), and a **derelict mining installation** on a gas-giant moon or rockball (the *Arenson* site). The artesian-rig ship operates in "a nearby system."
- **Threat/level:** **Mid-campaign (Phase 2).** Combat threat is **low-to-moderate** and mostly avoidable — two **Security Robots** at the derelict (SMG 3D), a one-shot **missile** trap, and whatever the crew provoke stealing ships/equipment. The genuine danger is **social/political**: hundreds of armed, prickly Aslan males who translate plumbing failures into honour duels. Pulling a gun in camp can spiral into a hundreds-to-one massacre — see `factions.md` Aslan Reaction.
- **Est. length:** Medium, episodic — a string of acquisition jobs (power → water → pipes → drilling rig) bracketed by escalating camp-management crises. 2–4 sessions; scales with how the crew source the gear.
- **Prerequisites:** The crew are an established pirate/salvage outfit with a ship and a hold (sourcing the gear assumes raiding/salvage capability). Aslan goodwill helps but isn't required. **Hierate Standing starts −5** (pirate) — relevant if they raid Aslan shipping for parts.
- **Arc position:** A standalone Companion side-adventure that **complements *Ihatei!* (Adventure 3).** Both center on Kteiroa and Utea; either can run first. *Ihatei!* asks the crew to **deflect** the swarm; *Friends in Dry Places* asks them to **provision** it — turning a border threat into an ally (or quietly letting it implode). Uses the canonical **invest-in-a-port → Haven** mechanic (empire-reputation.md). Success can move **Kteiroa toward Haven**; the crew choose whether a friendly *ihatei* staging post two parsecs from Drinax is a good idea.

## Player-Facing
*Safe to narrate.*
- **The lordly summons (hook).** On a visit to **Drinax** (or another Sindalian Main port) the crew notice a stir: an Aslan bigwig has arrived in a **600-ton ship**, swanning about in splendid garments behind well-armed bodyguards, making a "lordly peregrination" before grudgingly settling on barely-suitable lodgings. Word goes out that **the lord Utea** invites "bold and adventurous souls" into his presence — he has a **proposition with great rewards and his patronage.** The crew have seen this Aslan routine before; humblest officials act like emperors before outsiders. Still, Utea has money and at least modest prestige.
- **The audience.** Utea provides fine food and drink, asks the crew their story, and **actually listens.** He is haughty and disdainful of everything non-Aslan, but genuinely gauging: are these humans **smart, armed, and willing to do what it takes?** Do they have a starship? (How the crew pitch themselves matters — see Scene 1.)
- **The job (presenting problem).** Utea's frozen desert world **Kteiroa** has a thriving *ihatei* camp that has outgrown its life support. He needs the crew to **obtain and deliver** the equipment to make Kteiroa support huge numbers of warriors. Three key items, paid for **in hard currency** by his wives (handling money is beneath an Aslan lord):
  1. **A large, long-running power source** — a starship reactor is ideal (needs **Power 60+** plus spares).
  2. **A water recycling plant** — the kind used on rockball colonies or large vessels.
  3. **A very large quantity of piping, cabling and conduits** (~**40 tons**) to build a distribution network.
- **The terms (openly stated).** Deliver in a reasonable time and Utea decrees the crew are **always welcome on Kteiroa** and will permit **Aslan warriors to sign on** aboard their vessel or accompanying *ihatei* ships for missions. (He has no real control over either, but if the crew succeed, **Kteiroa moves toward Haven** — see Empire effects.) He also **pays per item** by suitability/condition, **refuels the crew's ship** on every return (a grand gesture given Kteiroa's water scarcity), and **dispenses gifts** worth **Cr1000 × 2D** each visit (furs, ornamental weapons, jewellery — as much to flaunt his largely illusory wealth as for value). He tolerates **a couple of empty-handed arrivals**, then **withdraws favour** and looks for someone else.
- **Why an outsider.** Utea **cannot** buy this gear (he's broke) and **cannot** ask any Aslan clan for help without fatal loss of face. He needs someone **creative and bold but who can never supplant him** — a band of human pirates out of Drinax is perfect.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned.*
- **There is no villain — only Aslan psychology and bad plumbing.** The "twist" is sociological: the crew will solve every *technical* problem and still nearly cause a war, because the *ihatei* convert scarcity into a **status contest**. The drama escalates from the world, not from a hidden mastermind.
- **Utea's authority is mostly bluff.** His wealth and control are "largely illusory." He must be **seen** as the undisputed lord dispensing necessities by personal favour — which is exactly why he can't let any *ihatei* leader help provide services (it would undermine him) and why he off-loads the grubby work onto disposable outsiders. He is **not opposed to a violent cull** of the camp's numbers, but his own warriors are **outnumbered** by the visiting warbands and would lose such a fight — so he must fix the camp instead of thinning it.
- **The competence inversion.** Despite the stereotype that Aslan males grasp nothing more technical than a grenade, **Utea understands the engineering perfectly** — his economic/technical advisors are **two of his wives**, who present the real (bleak) picture and who **handle all payment.** The *ihatei* males genuinely **do not care** *why* the plant isn't running — every "why isn't it working?" really means "**why don't I have power/water yet?**"
- **The real crisis is self-inflicted (the irrigation folly).** ~**16 hours** after the grand switch-on, things break — not because the kit fails, but because **Utea insists on irrigating barren land** for a **hunting preserve** (and farms), forcing water into soil that can't hold it and pulling it back out. This **overstrains the system**: sewage backs into wash water, power browns out when warriors recharge laser weapons. The fix isn't more parts — it's **more water** (so the **artesian drilling rig**, Scene 6) — because Utea **will not give up the hunting preserve** (he'll drop the farms).
- **The status-war truth (why ihatei start dying).** The *ihatei* aren't really fighting over the bathroom queue. **Being clean = visible proof of higher precedence**; muddy fur (after the artesian geyser, Scene 6) = a public mark of low status. Those who get clean **strut and taunt** those who can't; others scream **bribery/corruption.** After the **first death**, the crew realise it's a **status duel**, not a utilities dispute. Some of the cut-offs are caused by **deliberate sabotage** of the pipes (Scene 7) — by ihatei who think they've been cheated of pressure they were promised.
- **The looming deadline.** A **prodigious, highly important ihatei leader** is en route to make Kteiroa his base (Scene 5). Utea must have everything **in order or lose enormous prestige** — bad for everyone. This is the ticking pressure under the whole back half. (In *Ihatei!* terms, this is the kind of swing-leader influx — e.g. the camp's most influential arrival — whose decision can aim the swarm; here his arrival is the social stakes, not a stat block.)
- **The crew's hidden option — let it burn.** A major *ihatei* staging post **two parsecs from Drinax** is arguably a threat the crew should *want* gone (this is literally the danger *Ihatei!* addresses). They can **take Utea's pay, then quietly sabotage or simply abandon** the camp: deny the gear, botch the install, or stir the status war until Kteiroa **"self-eradicates."** Cost: they make **enemies of the survivors**, forfeit the Haven, and may face later *ihatei* retribution — but they remove a border threat. **Honest GM ruling:** this is a legitimate win condition, not a failure.

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. Hiring On — the audience with Utea
- **Trigger:** Crew answer Utea's open invitation at Drinax (or a Main port). → **What's here:** The audience and the three-item brief (Player-Facing). Utea is sizing them up: **bold, armed, ship-equipped?**
- **The pitch check (this sets the whole reward).** How the crew describe themselves to Utea **determines Kteiroa's Attitude payoff** on success:
  - **Boast openly** of their illegal, violent, daring exploits (the *ihatei* ideal — proud of one's deeds, prepared to answer for them) → Utea is **favourably disposed**; success later moves **Kteiroa TWO steps** toward Haven.
  - **Downplay / weasel** (talk like they're wriggling out of criminal charges) → far less impressed; success moves **Kteiroa only ONE step.** No human is the equal of an *ihatei*, but they can aspire to be *something similar* — weaselling is not the hallmark of a notable warrior.
  - **Adjudicate as roleplay** (a single **Persuade/Diplomat (SOC)** can color it, but the *content* of the boast is what matters; see `factions.md` on Aslan respect for daring over wealth).
- **Complications:** Utea will **not** haggle money himself — **his wives** negotiate price and pay in hard currency. Trying to deal-make with Utea over credits earns disdain.

### 2. The Power System — getting a reactor
- **Trigger:** Crew set out for the power source (Power 60+ plus spares; fuel tankage can be improvised locally; they'll also want **fuel processors** to crack water→liquid hydrogen). → **What's here / the menu** (any approach works; price/heat differ):
  - **Drop a whole pirated starship (≥400t, or several small craft totalling that) onto the Kteiroa pad.** Solves *most* of the brief in one stroke (power + much piping; see Scene 4) and **wins status with some ihatei**. But it means **stealing entire vessels**, which may **breach the Code of the Stars** (`factions.md`) — possibly more trouble than it's worth.
  - **Buy or steal a reactor from a starship-breaker yard** at a **Class A/B starport.** Spares from dismantled ships; the completed plant **needn't fit any specific hull.**
  - **Strip a powerplant from a starship graveyard / ship-chop site** (Utea supplies locations). **Not undefended:** at minimum **remote sensors** summon yard owners or system-defence ships; wrecks may be **booby-trapped**; breakers may have salvaged a **working missile launcher or two** (more likely at illicit ship-chop ops, which respond **lethally**). Dismantling a plant in place and hauling it to the cargo bay is **lengthy**; better to **lift a whole small craft** and pull the plant at leisure.
- **Pricing (Utea pays in hard currency via his wives):** a small powerplant (Free Trader / Scout-Courier grade) is worth **KCr200** to Utea if stolen/pirated, **~KCr300** if provably legitimate.
- **Complications:** Delivering parts to Kteiroa **right after** matching gear went missing nearby can finger the crew — **retribution may catch up later** when they've forgotten the job. Removing a recycler or plant **legally** costs far more than scavenging.
- **Installation, not parts (recurring).** Utea's people have *some* technical skill but little starship-equipment experience (especially **human-made** systems). The crew are expected to hand over a **working** plant, not a heap of components. **Leave without setting it up → Utea displeased; next visit frosty.** While installing, expect **galactic-level impatience** and a parade of ihatei leaders demanding to know why power isn't on — answers of the "*because you keep interrupting*" variety **go down badly.** Each leader also frets he'll be **cheated out of the distribution grid** and uses threats + vague promises to ensure he gets **his share first** (some bribe to cut rivals out).

### 3. The Water Recycling System — the derelict (the one set-piece site)
- **Trigger:** Crew pursue the water recycler. Pulling one from a starship is possible but **buried deep and even slower than a powerplant**; legal salvage is expensive. The clean lead is the **derelict mining installation.** → **The site:** A few years ago a small prospecting-and-mining company went bust and sold its installations cheap. One — bought at rock-bottom by **Arenson Investments** (no mining company) — was **mothballed but never crewed**, and now stands **derelict** on a **gas-giant moon or rockball** in a nearby system (GM picks). Arenson's mothball team **set deterrents** for intruders.
- **Layered defences (in order):**
  1. **Automated warning + missile.** On approach an automated system broadcasts an escalating **stay-away** warning, then fires a **surface missile** from near the installation. **It only ever had three missiles and already fired one earlier — so only ONE shot remains — but the crew don't know that.** The launcher is a **one-shot container** stood in a drilled hole, on a basic sensor system (no proper turret).
  2. **Two Security Robots** inside (stat block below). Small, ~1 m, fat-tyred, turret-mounted **submachinegun**; they **issue no warning** (anyone past the missile is "assumed hostile"), **shoot-and-scoot**, and may **lure the crew into a crossfire** (one drawing them onto the other's ambush).
- **What's here:** Prefab base segments linked by flexible tunnels — a small maze: accommodation, utilities, **power centre** (its reactor/key systems **already removed at mothball — disappointing**), workshops, ore-sampling lab. The **water recycler** is mostly **modular but scattered**; extracting the **main unit is a big, heavy job** (the additional modules are easier but time-consuming). **All work in vacc suits** unless the crew rig a **power feed from their ship** — life support and lights are **dead** without the reactor.
- **The two bodies (a quiet mystery).** Soon after arrival the crew find **two bullet-riddled corpses** in vacc suits; stray rounds smashed gear and holed the outer walls. **Tools, weapons and small salvage have been taken from them; bodies left where they fell.** (They were killed by the Security Robots; someone — looters? a prior crew? — later stripped the dead. The GM may leave this ambiguous or seed a thread.)
- **Pricing & extra loot:** A suitable recycler fills **~20 tons** of hold and is worth **KCr100** to Utea. Once the robots are dealt with, removal is at leisure; some **lab/workshop systems** remain to loot, though **everything small and valuable was taken at mothball.** Still, some extra salvage is possible.
- **Complications:** Removing the **main unit** is heavy, slow work; doing it in vacc suits without a rigged power feed is grueling. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** when the crew first cross the installation threshold (past the missile), the Security Robots are live and hostile — run an ambush: pre-roll initiative and have one robot **shoot-and-scoot to bait** the crew toward the second.

### 4. Pipework Galore — 40 tons of mundane plumbing
- **Trigger:** Crew tackle the piping/cabling/conduits (~**40 tons** total to fully wire the camp for water recycling **and** power distribution; **some arrives bundled with the power and water units**). → **What's here / the menu:**
  - **Strip pipes from ships in a graveyard or a derelict** — possible but a **very lengthy** spannerwork slog (≈**a week** to extract pipes/conduits from one derelict). Good if the crew "have time to spare and like spannerwork."
  - **Dismantle a ship or installation** they already control for its conduits, pumps, filters, connectors.
  - **Just buy it** (the simplest path): **Cr1000 per ton or less** if the crew bargain well (a **Broker/Persuade** haggle). Cuts the margin but **dispatches the most time-consuming part fast.**
- **Pricing:** Utea pays **KCr50** for "enough piping," but only **after his engineering-minded wife pronounces it sufficient.** **Utea himself won't even look at a pile of pipes** — beneath his dignity.
- **Complications:** Let the crew over-engineer this if they want (the GM "can have some fun watching them collect a bunch of pipes"); the **simplest answer is to buy.**

### 5. The Grand Unveiling — switch-on, then collapse
- **Trigger:** All components delivered and installed; only connecting and throwing the switch remains. → **What's here — Act One (false triumph):** Utea makes **grand pronouncements the moment parts arrive**; everyone expects instant power/water. The crew face **another round of demands about "what went wrong"** even though nothing has — Utea **manufactured an unrealistic expectation** and now the crew are pressured to **instantly deliver** something that takes time to set up.
- **Quick-and-dirty vs. do-it-right (a real choice).** Utea pushes for an **immediate hack job**; the crew can **persuade him to do it properly** (approach him right). A botched grand switch-on would **damage his reputation more** than a few extra days of methodical work — he's **receptive to that argument** but **uninterested in any detail.**
  - **Rush** → it works *now* but **some sections must be redone later** (raises the chance of a serious malfunction / major delay when a cobbled section is torn out and rebuilt).
  - **Do it right** → takes longer but **reduces malfunction risk.** Track this as a flag affecting Scenes 6–7 severity.
- **The switch-on works.** Utea announces sufficient energy to recharge **every** fuel cell and water for everyone's needs, throws the switch, and **it works** — congratulations and **another Cr1000 × 2D in gifts** flow; Utea basks in admiration. → **Act Two — sixteen hours later, it breaks (the irrigation folly).** Utea's **hunting-preserve irrigation** overstrains the system: one ihatei leader **rages about sewage in his washing water**; another **can't recharge his lasers without browning out** his section's grid. **Disappointment turns to anger.** → **The deadline lands:** a **prodigious ihatei leader has decided to base here** and arrives soon — Utea **needs everything in order or lose prestige.** → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** when the crew declare the system online, run the **16-hour goodwill timer**, then trip the irrigation-overstrain crisis (sewage + brownouts) as the inciting event for Scenes 6–7.

### 6. The Final Piece — the artesian drilling rig (and the muddy geyser)
- **Trigger:** With the irrigation overstrain raging, the crew look for a real fix. → **The diagnosis:** Utea **won't abandon the hunting preserve** (he'll drop the farms), so the only answer is to **reduce strain by adding water.** Kteiroa **has** water in **artesian basins** reachable by drilling a shaft (a kilometre or two deep) and inserting pumps — but the gear is **specialist.** → **Utea's plan (another acquisition job):** he has intel on a company that **specialises in exactly this artesian-pump drilling**, currently operating in a **nearby system** with a **ship that carries the rig.** The crew just need to **steal the ship and its equipment**, drill the shaft, and plug it into the water system. (Or, absurdly, **convince the ihatei that sewage in the drinking water is fine** — a non-starter.)
- **What's here:** Get the rig to Kteiroa ASAP; **drilling the shaft is then simple** — amid the usual barrage of "why isn't it done yet." → **Success is unmistakable and disastrous:** **muddy water geysers** out of the shaft, **showering everyone nearby.** This creates **crowds of irate, muddy-furred ihatei** who **can't get a proper shower** (low water pressure until the system is fully operational). → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** when the shaft is plugged in, run the **mud geyser** — it *fixes* the water supply but **ignites the status war** (Scene 7) by visibly marking who is and isn't clean.
- **Complications:** Stealing the rig ship is its own piracy/heist beat (run per `piracy-raiding.md`; if the target is an Aslan or Hierate-flagged vessel, mind Standing −5). The rig company may pursue.

### 7. Solutions — the status war (the real climax)
- **Trigger:** The mud geyser (and lingering pressure problems) tip the camp into open status conflict; the prodigious leader is nearly here. → **What's here:** The camp is **"on the verge of ripping itself apart over shower rotas."** The crew are besieged by **emissaries from ihatei bands of all sizes**, each demanding **privileged access** to water/power — and sometimes the **interruption of a rival's supply.** They **cannot afford to offend any leader** unless they have **Utea's wholehearted protection.**
- **The sabotage problem.** The crew discover the system is being **deliberately sabotaged in places**, cutting off some groups. Victims who **just struck a deal** (with Utea or the crew) are **even more aggrieved** if they think they were cheated. **Fixing the tech is only time + manpower; the real task is preventing a bloodbath while they work.**
- **It's a status duel, not a utilities dispute.** After the **first death**, the truth is clear: clean fur = precedence; the clean **strut and taunt**; the dirty are deemed low-status and fling **bribery/corruption** accusations. (This actually has more to do with **how the pipes are laid out** than anyone's rank — but the Aslan won't see it that way.)
- **The branch (the crew choose the ending):**
  - **Let it collapse (the deniable cull).** If the crew judge a major staging post 2 pc from Drinax too dangerous, they can **stir the pot** or just **sneak back to their ship and leave.** Kteiroa's *ihatei* outpost **"more or less self-eradicates."** They make **enemies of the survivors.** *(Legitimate win — see GM-Only.)*
  - **Salvage it (the diplomat's path).** Apply **persuasion and diplomacy** — first just to **stop angry ihatei shooting them** (run **Persuade/Diplomat (SOC)** vs. the Aslan Reaction table, `factions.md`; gifts and demonstrated daring help). They may **persuade warriors to guard the pipes** and **technicians to help fix them**; **anything that calms things even slightly buys time.** If successful, the crew provide enough water and power to **keep everyone happy (or at least non-violent).**
- → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** treat the status war as a **rising-tension clock**, not a single roll. Each unaddressed grievance, sabotage, or social fumble advances it; the **first death** is the marker that the crisis has gone lethal. Run band-emissary demands as a sequence of `factions.md` Reaction checks; a string of failures (or any atrocity) tips Kteiroa toward collapse.

## Key NPCs
*Run every Aslan through Aslan honour & Reaction (`factions.md`): territory, tradition, harmony of action; the offence ladder; duelling. Respect for **daring deeds** over wealth is the through-line.*

**Utea (Aslan Outcast lord; the patron)** — *(no stat line in source; run as a high-SOC non-combatant noble — treat ~SOC 10+, modest combat, dewclaws; he leads warriors but won't brawl with the crew.)* Haughty, disdainful of all things non-Aslan, but **genuinely shrewd** about the engineering and **listens** to a good boast. Settles a family of Outcasts on Kteiroa and has made it their own; **authority is mostly bluff** and **outnumbered** by the visiting warbands. **Wants:** to be **seen** as the undisputed lord who personally provides necessities — and to build a **hunting preserve** he won't give up. Off-loads grubby work onto disposable outsiders; **galactic-level impatience.** Pays generously in gifts and refuelling; **withdraws favour after ~2 empty-handed visits.**

**Utea's Wives (economic & technical advisors; two of them)** — *(no stat line; non-combatants; treat as skilled Aslan female technicians/brokers — Broker/Admin/Engineer/Electronics as needed.)* The brains of the operation: present the **bleak true picture**, **handle all payment** in hard currency, and **judge** whether delivered pipes are "sufficient." The **engineering-minded wife** must sign off the plumbing before KCr50 is paid. *(Aslan tie gender to role — only females are technical specialists; cf. `factions.md`.)*

**Visiting Ihatei Leaders (camp warband chiefs; many)** — *(no individual stats; run as proud Aslan males — see the *Ihatei!* warlord templates in `adventures/03_ihatei.md` / `personalities-and-factions.md` for ready stat lines if a fight or duel erupts.)* Each demands **his share of the grid first**, frets about being **cheated**, threatens and bribes, and **does not care why** the plant isn't running — only that he has **no power/water yet.** Several are behind the **pipe sabotage** and the **status taunting.** Treat as a rotating cast of Reaction-table encounters (`factions.md`); a botched social beat can summon **1D+ angry warriors** (use the *Ihatei!* camp encounter stat lines: drunken bravos claws 1D / autopistols 3D−3 / cloth armour 5; veterans snub pistols 3D−3 / shotguns 4D / combat vacc 9; champions duel barehand or TL≤3).

**The Prodigious Ihatei Leader (the deadline incarnate)** — *(no stat block; an offstage VIP whose imminent arrival sets the social stakes.)* A highly important warlord who has chosen to make Kteiroa his base; Utea must have the camp **in order or lose major prestige.** Arrives at the back end of the adventure; if the camp is well-set, his arrival **repairs Utea's dented prestige** and the formerly-feuding *ihatei* swiftly start **plotting raids and landgrabs together.** *(If running alongside Ihatei!, this is the kind of swing-leader influx whose backing aims the swarm — `adventures/03_ihatei.md`.)*

**Security Robot (Arenson derelict; ×2)** — Hits 14, Speed 12 m, TL12, **Basic (security)** programming, Cost Cr675,000. Skills: Gun Combat (slug) 1, Recon 1, Tactics 0. Attack: **Submachinegun (3D, Auto 3)**. Traits: **Armour (+3)**. Tactics: **no warning**, shoot-and-scoot, bait the crew into a two-robot crossfire. *(The only hard combat threat in the adventure besides the one-shot missile.)*

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — no rescues.*
- **Per-item pay (hard currency, via the wives):** Power plant ~**KCr200** stolen / **~KCr300** legitimate; water recycler **KCr100**; piping **KCr50** (after the wife approves). Plus **salvage** from the derelict (lab/workshop systems; lesser leftovers) and anything looted en route.
- **Standing gifts & perks:** **Cr1000 × 2D** in ostentatious gifts on the hook *and* again at the grand switch-on; **free refuelling** on every Kteiroa return (genuinely valuable on a water-poor world); standing leave to **recruit Aslan warriors** onto the crew's ship / accompanying *ihatei* ships (handy hands and muscle).
- **The big payoff — a friendly Aslan port:** On success, **Kteiroa moves toward Haven** (see Empire effects) — a **rare friendly, Aslan-run foothold** two parsecs from Drinax and a possible **pirate Haven** (the canonical **MCr10-in-facilities → Haven** model applies if the crew also invest, per empire-reputation.md; this adventure can deliver the *deed-based* steps that get it most of the way there).
- **Contacts & opportunities (if the crew made friends among the ihatei):** an invitation to **join a raid** or a **joint venture** with the *ihatei*; a standing relationship with **Utea** and his camp.
- **Costs of letting it collapse:** the camp **self-eradicates** (border threat removed — a real strategic gain), **but** the crew make **enemies of the survivors**, **forfeit the Haven and all goodwill**, and may face later *ihatei* retribution. Honest framing: a valid choice with teeth, not a clean win.
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest):**
  - **Failing the social crisis** (offending leaders, ignoring sabotage, fumbling Reaction checks) → **deaths**, then a **bloodbath**; Kteiroa collapses *against* the crew's intent and they're **blamed.** Pulling guns in camp invites a **hundreds-to-one** response (`factions.md`).
  - **Botched derelict run** → the **one remaining missile** (a real hull-hit if it lands) and **two SMG-armed Security Robots** in crossfire; working in dead vacuum is unforgiving.
  - **Sourcing heat** → delivering parts right after matching gear went missing **fingers the crew** (later retribution); illicit ship-chop sites respond **lethally**; legal salvage is **expensive**.
  - **Stealing Aslan ships / raiding Hierate shipping** for parts or the rig → **Hierate Standing hit** (starts −5) and possible assassin attention (`factions.md`; cf. Yerlyaruiwo in `adventures/03_ihatei.md`).
  - **Lasting enmities even on success:** "unless the camp collapses (and probably even then) the Travellers' actions will have consequences" — some individuals or whole bands will **resent the crew's involvement**, seeding rivalries.
  - **The strategic double-edge:** a *successful* Kteiroa becomes a **well-supplied ihatei staging post 2 pc from Drinax** — good for the crew's reputation, **bad for nearby human worlds** (it's exactly the muster *Ihatei!* warns about). The win has a downside; surface it.

## Empire / Reputation effects
*Apply via empire-reputation.md "Per-adventure effects." Kteiroa starts **Unfriendly** (Appendix 2) / **Suspicious** in the *Ihatei!* port-context — record which baseline your table uses.*
- **Kteiroa (0505) Attitude shift (the core effect):**
  - **Success after boasting boldly to Utea (Scene 1)** → **+2 steps** toward Haven.
  - **Success after downplaying / weaselling** → **+1 step** toward Haven.
  - These are the **deed-based** steps; stack with the canonical **investment ladder** to reach Haven (empire-reputation.md / `adventures/03_ihatei.md`): **Cr10,000 spent → Tolerant · Cr100,000 invested → Friendly · MCr10 invested → Haven.** A fully-provisioned, crew-befriended Kteiroa can become a **pirate Haven** — a rare friendly Aslan port and refuelling back-door into the Hierate. **Record the cause.**
- **Let-it-collapse outcome:** Kteiroa's *ihatei* outpost **self-eradicates** — **no Haven, goodwill forfeit**, Attitude effectively reset/Hostile toward the crew among survivors. Treat the camp as **removed as an alliance candidate** (no FS from a friendly Kteiroa), but **note the border threat is neutralised** (a strategic positive that does *not* show on the Attitude track). This is **not** a Lost World (the *world* isn't destroyed; the *camp* is).
- **Hierate Standing:** Pure provisioning/diplomacy needn't move Standing. **Raiding Aslan shipping, stealing Aslan ships (Code of the Stars), or the artesian-rig heist if Aslan-flagged** earns a **Hierate Standing hit** (start −5) and possible assassin heat (`factions.md`). The **Imperium is irrelevant** here (Aslan space).
- **Fighting Strength:** A recruited friendly **Kteiroa contributes FS 3** (empire-reputation.md Appendix 2) **if** it later joins the Kingdom — but a friendly *ihatei* staging post is a double-edged ally (see Consequences). Cultivating Aslan goodwill broadly supports later access to **Ahroay'if +6 FS** (long-game, not granted here).
- **No Lost Worlds created** (the world survives regardless). **No mandatory great-power Standing change** absent raiding.
- **Cross-effect with *Ihatei!*:** Provisioning the camp **strengthens** the very swarm *Ihatei!* asks the crew to deflect; deflecting/dispersing it (Adventure 3) **undercuts** Utea's project. If both run, reconcile on the empire tracker: a thriving Kteiroa = friendly port **and** larger border threat; a dispersed Kteiroa = no Haven but a safer border. Record the net.
