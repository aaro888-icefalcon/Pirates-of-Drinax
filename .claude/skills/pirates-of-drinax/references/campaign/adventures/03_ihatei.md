# Ihatei!

> Use this when: King Oleb sends the crew into Aslan space to stop an *ihatei* (landless second-son) swarm gathering near Drinax from launching an invasion. A **25-week clock** runs the whole adventure. Cross-ref `setting/factions.md` (Aslan Hierate, *ihatei*), `setting/personalities-and-factions.md` (the warlords), `setting/ships-of-the-reach.md` (Aslan hulls), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Standing/Attitude).

**Logline:** A horde of masterless Aslan warriors massing on a neutral iceworld two parsecs from Drinax could pour over the border if a strong leader unites them — and Drinax is the prize one warlord already wants. The crew must sneak into the Hierate, infiltrate the camp, and turn the Aslan against each other or against a different target. No villains; only biological and political necessity.

## At a glance
- **Where:** Starts at **Drinax** (Tlaiowaha 0603). Runs into Aslan space — **Kteiroa** (the *ihatei* camp, Tlaiowaha 0505, ~2 pc from Drinax) via optional **Tyokh** (0606, papers of passage); deep-Hierate sites **Keaih** (~9 pc rimward, the disputed waterworld) and **Akoaft** (the proxy-war world). Aslan-named bodies in Kteiroa system: Vansus, Meon, Bellopho (GG), Vorca, Sonca.
- **Threat:** Crew are **absurdly outmatched** at every stage (thousands of warriors, capital ships). This is **stealth/bluff/intrigue**, not assault. Pulling a gun is occasionally right; mostly it gets you killed.
- **Length:** Long. **25-week hard clock** — every week in jumpspace burns it. Failure or partial success both possible.
- **Prerequisites:** The crew operate from Drinax under Oleb. Helps to have an **Aslan ally/Contact** (Kasiyl, below; or an Aslan PC) — many DMs key off it. Hierate Standing starts at **−5** (pirate).
- **Arc slot:** Early-mid campaign external threat. Resolving it protects Drinax's foundation; failing it makes all later Reach adventures more dangerous and cuts Drinax's support. Kasiyl's price ties forward to **Adventure 8: The Prodigal Outcast**.

## Player-Facing (the hook)
*Safe to narrate.*

- On jumping into Drinax, **Lord Wrax of the Star Guard** (condescending; wanted the Harrier for himself) warns of an **Aslan raiding party (≥3 jump-capable ships)** sighted near the Floating Palace and that approaching ships may be attacked. He sneers at the crew to signal for help rather than "run like pirates."
- **King Oleb** summons them (in person — too delicate for holo; possibly via trader **Sal Dancet**). In his cluttered audience hall he shows a holomap of **Kteiroa**: thousands of Aslan warriors in a huge camp, hundreds of ships in orbit. "**Ihatei!**"
- Oleb's brief: masterless **second-sons** are gathering. If a strong leader emerges he could lead them — or even draw clan backing — against a nearby world, and **Drinax is only two parsecs away** and **not yet strong enough to resist**. Go to Kteiroa and **deflect the invasion**: stop them uniting, or give them a different target. **Do not implicate Drinax** in any atrocity, war crime, or "thermonuclear explosion." Consult the exiled Aslan **Kasiyl** if you wish (Oleb doesn't fully trust him).
- **Kasiyl's brief** (if asked): Aslan males are biologically driven to own **territory**; only the first son inherits, so *ihatei* must win land by conquest. They gather in camps, boast targets, and rally followers; clans tolerate this but may **either restrain or endorse** a promising invasion. Something has shifted the balance to flood Kteiroa with second-sons. He offers to come as interpreter and provide a low-profile Aslan ship — **for a future favour** (he'll call it in during *The Prodigal Outcast*).

**Kasiyl's tactical advice (player-facing):** Stay undercover — the Aslan will react with terrible anger if they suspect outsiders are **meddling in the ways of the *ihatei***. Best human cover in camp = **tinker** (Aslan tie gender to role; only females are techs, so human techs are tolerated). Look for **clan agents** (older established males among the warbands) and the **most influential *ihatei*** (the ones who could aim the swarm at Drinax). Go **armed** — to Aslan, an unarmed person is one not worth attacking.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned.*

- **No villains.** The closest is the arbiter **Yae**, and exposing him only **temporarily** delays the invasion. Everyone acts from political/biological necessity. Themes: **fear** (play up horror-movie terror; always ask their escape route) and **inevitability** (the *ihatei* are slaves to a cultural/biological need for land).
- **Root cause — the Keaih war.** The flood comes from the **Htyowao** clan. For ~a decade the **Htyowao** and rival **Hkaaiheir** have fought a *tehlaicho* (strictly limited war) over the waterworld **Keaih** (settled jointly centuries ago; they ran out of land as both populations grew). To spare Keaih's terraforming, they fight a proxy war on the airless poison world **Akoaft**, overseen by neutral arbiter **Yae** of the **Khaukheairl**; last clan standing wins, loser cedes Keaih territory.
  - **Nine-year stalemate.** Original rules: if no clear victory inside **ten years**, both sides may send reinforcements.
  - **Six months ago** the **Hkaaiheir** found and destroyed the **Htyowao underground fortress** in a sneak attack. Htyowao defeat is now obvious. **Truth:** the Hkaaiheir simply **got lucky** — a scout stumbled on an entrance. (Rumour falsely blames spy satellites, or that **Yae sold them the location** — a useful lie the crew can weaponise even though it's false.)
  - Therefore the now-landless Htyowao warriors must seek new territory off-world — flooding camps like Kteiroa. **The best way to save Drinax long-term is to PROLONG the Keaih war** so the swarm keeps draining back toward it.
- **Who already wants Drinax:** broker **Fyukh** owns the ships that scouted Drinax and **advised Eihei the Clever** that the **Floating Palace** would make a perfect high-tech staging post for invading trailing Tlaiowaha/the Borderlands. **Eihei + Fyukh advocate invading Drinax but lack followers.** **Iykhi** and **Toiho** have the followers but not (yet) that target. **The crew's job: keep advocates and followers apart.**
- **The ornate Yuhaih (battle-axe)** recovered from the scout (if destroyed) bears a **family crest** that lets the crew identify **which *ihatei* ordered the Drinax raid** once at Kteiroa. Kasiyl reads it as belonging to an **upstart Outcast** outside the established Hierate (it traces to Fyukh's circle / Eihei's backers).
- **If the crew don't intercede:** the second wave (Htyowao under **Iykhi**) arrives from Akoaft; **Iykhi allies with Eihei and attacks Drinax**, while **Teaw** sends **Elyo and Toiho** to conquer Sink and Fantasy (politically powerless worlds). Iykhi needs **16 weeks** to assemble; **>300 ships / >5,000 warriors** jump into Drinax. The Palace survives (campaign can't end here) but the **nascent Empire of Drinax is severely damaged.**
- **Even a full success is temporary.** Stopping this crop saves the Palace for a few years; when Keaih falls, **millions** of displaced Aslan will look across the border. The only durable fix is changing the war itself (Escalation, below).

## Scenes / Locations
Five sections: **The Threat** (Drinax brief) → **Into the Hierate** (travel) → **Camp of the Ihatei** (infiltration) → **Escalation** (Keaih/Akoaft). Subtract **all travel time** from the 25-week clock.

### 1. The Threat — Intruder Alert (Drinax orbit)
- **Trigger:** Crew jump into Drinax and get Wrax's warning.
- **What's here:** A buried **Ihateisho-class scout** lies under glassy dunes ~200 km NE of the Floating Palace (two other Aslan ships already jumped out). Detect it from orbit: **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics (sensors), INT.** On detection the scout launches.
  - **Chase the scout** — Scout **Thrust 2** vs Harrier **Thrust 6**. The dust atmosphere gives **DM−1 to attacks and −1D laser damage**; referee may call **Pilot** checks for thermals/winds. Turns for the scout to escape (vacuum / safe-jump 640,000 km in brackets / jump-limit):
    | Starting position | Turns to vacuum | Turns to jump |
    |---|---|---|
    | Detected from orbit | 10 (22) | 15 |
    | Fast launch from Palace | 8 (20) | 12 |
    | Slow launch from Palace | 6 (16) | 10 |
  - **Launch trade-off:** "Fast launch" (cargo doors open, umbilicals trailing) = **Very Difficult (12+) Mechanics, INT**; fail → **1D damage ignoring Armour**; fail by Effect −4+ → **2D**. The scout crew get **DM+2 to combat checks**.
- **Complications:** If disabled **in atmosphere**, Oleb's **Hawk Warriors** (grav-belt bodyguards) board the death-diving scout and salvage it. If disabled **after** breaking atmosphere, it's destroyed unless the crew make special efforts. The two young-male crew are **dead** regardless; the computer **auto-wipes**, but **Difficult (10+) Electronics (computers), INT** recovers nav data → it came from **Kteiroa / the ihatei camp**, and the wreck holds the crested **Yuhaih**.
- **If the scout escapes:** it reappears at Kteiroa (Scene 3). Oleb is **morose** (vs boisterous if captured), affecting his hospitality.

### 2. Into the Hierate (travel to Kteiroa)
- **Trigger:** Crew finish prep at Drinax and depart.
- **What's here:** Kteiroa is **2 pc** — a direct jump is possible. But most human traffic enters via **Tyokh** first; passing **Tyokh** (Tlaiowaha clan) buys **papers of passage** that skip immigration checks in any clan with a Tlaiowaha trade agreement (≈all of them).
  - **Get papers (Tyokh):** **Admin (SOC)**. Open arrival → apply the crew's negative pirate Standing as DM (campaign starts **−5** with the Hierate). Disguised → **Deception (INT)** + change transponder or use another ship. Fail → delayed **weeks = |Effect|**; fail by **−6+** → barred or arrested.
  - **No papers:** **Admin (SOC)** at **every** starport; fail → delayed **days = |Effect|**, may be detained.
  - **In an Aslan vessel** (e.g. Kasiyl's), **no papers or Admin checks needed** at all.
- **Complications — Assassins of the Yerlyaruiwo (only if Kasiyl is along):** each time the crew land on an Aslan world with Kasiyl, roll **2D − (highest Streetwise among the crew)**; on **8+**, Yerlyaruiwo assassins mark him (precautions like transponder spoofing / Deception-disguising Kasiyl give extra DMs). They **politely announce the death-mark first** (relayed via neutral comms; identities kept secret), then attack on land. They **won't fight in space** but may **board in flight** (grav belts + boarding vacc suits). They treat the crew as Kasiyl's retainers = valid targets; they avoid mass civilian casualties but escalate weapons to match (claws/knives → personal arms → heavy energy weapons if the crew swagger in battle dress). *Stat block below.*
- **Piracy in the Hierate (optional):** standard piracy rules with: Aslan ship **Morale min 3**; **DM+1** on the Response table (more hunter ships); but clans are **less likely to pursue into another clan's space** — jump to a different clan's system to shake them.

### 3. Camp of the Ihatei (Kteiroa)
- **Trigger:** Crew arrive at Kteiroa.
- **The world:** Arid iceworld, no resources, **never officially claimed** (only Kteiroa itself counts as territory; nothing to gain from blast-scarred tundra). Old Sindalian name **Piper**; a **6 km radioactive crater** marks where a Sindalian warship bombed Pipertown during the punitive wars (lethal in a vacc suit). Permanent residents: a family of **Outcast (clanless) Aslan** who supply pirates/explorers/tramps and have grown rich since the *ihatei* adopted Kteiroa as a neutral campsite. **Kteiroa is only Suspicious** (unusually friendly for an Aslan port) — **not Unfriendly.**
- **The starport:** New Aslan port on an icy plateau west of the crater; amenities in **ice caves** to the south; **bright lights** warn ships off the ice above the caves. Paranoid Outcasts built **anti-aircraft guns** and hid **missile launchers** in crevasses. **Utea** (elderly Aslan female; fur coat sewn from her "former husbands'" hides) hails arrivals. Tinkers / Aslan-ship arrivals may land at the camp directly; everyone else lands at port first. **Landing fee Cr600**; good facilities; ice-cracking plant pipes hydrogen slurry to the camp 5 km north. Six freighters already on the pads (booming supply trade).
  - **Upgrade Kteiroa's Attitude** (it can become a **pirate Haven**):
    | Attitude | Action |
    |---|---|
    | Suspicious | none (default) |
    | Neutral | visit the camp and leave without causing trouble |
    | Tolerant | spend **Cr10,000** at the port |
    | Friendly | invest **Cr100,000** in facilities |
    | Haven | invest **MCr10** in facilities |
- **Utea as an ally:** the longer the *ihatei* debate, the more she earns — so she **wants the deliberations prolonged.** She can suggest stalling tactics (shout down warleaders, rally a hopeless candidate), and points the crew to **Fyukh** and **Teaw**.
- **The camp:** >**5,000 warriors**, ~**100 ships** (mostly Ihateisho scouts, up to **Sakhai** 2,000t carriers). ~95% male; ~5% female techs/merchants/medics. Female Travellers are in real danger here. Clans quietly provide infrastructure so hot-blooded males don't die too early. Each night *ihatei* shout their conquest plans seeking followers and backing.
- **Timeline of the camp (the squeeze):**
  - **First Night:** inconclusive rallying; **Eihei** and **Elyo** stand out.
  - **Day 2 — Warriors from Keaih:** **50+ Htyowao ships** (some Hkaaiheir) land; the **Tale of the War** becomes easy to learn. **Toiho** and **Iykhi** only **arrive after this event.**
  - **Second Night:** the influx makes **Iykhi** the swing factor — most of the camp will follow him.
  - **The Decision:** unless the crew intervene, **Iykhi joins Eihei → attack on Drinax.**
- **Gathering Intelligence (find the key players):** If the crew captured/salvaged the scout, an **Investigate or Streetwise (INT, 1D hrs)** brings them to **Fyukh**. Otherwise **Investigate (INT, 1D hrs)**, **DM+2 with an Aslan ally**, by Effect:
  | Effect | Result |
  |---|---|
  | −3 or less | Unwelcome questions anger a warrior — he + **1D+2** friends try to throw them out |
  | −1/−2 | Nothing useful |
  | 0 | Learn the **Tale of the War** |
  | 1–2 | + identities of the leading *ihatei* |
  | 3–4 | + learn of **Teaw** |
  | 5+ | + learn of **Fyukh** |
  - **The escaped scout pilot** (if the Drinax scout fled) is in camp; confronting him triggers a chase through the camp; capturing him **leads to Fyukh.**
- **Encounters (D66; mythic-gm keyed roll):** wander the camp with `dice.py 2d6` read as a **D66** (tens × 6 + units). **+1 to the tens die** if hunting a specific encounter *type* (1 Violent · 2 Opportunity · 3 Colour · 4 Rumours · 5 heart of camp · 6 notable leaders); **+1 to the units die** with Kasiyl/an Aslan ally. Key results:
  - **11–13** 1D+4 drunken bravos attack (claws 1D, autopistols 3D−3, cloth armour 5).
  - **14–15** 1D+1 veterans challenge their presence (snub pistols 3D−3, shotguns 4D, combat vacc 9) unless justified (tinker cover / Aslan escort / **Diplomat**).
  - **16** A champion challenges the most dangerous PC (barehand or TL≤3 weapon, until one can't stand). Win → **DM+2 to next 10s die**.
  - **17** A warrior tries to **kill the Aslan ally** (gauss rifle 4D, AP5, Auto3, Scope; combat vacc 9).
  - **21–22** Gambling hall — Aslan barely grasp money: **Gambler (INT)**, win/lose **Effect × Cr100**.
  - **23** Recruitable pirate-grade Aslan crew: **5+ on 2D** to find some.
  - **24** Sell **Advanced Weapons/Vehicles, Live Animals, Luxuries, Medical Supplies, Pharmaceuticals, Vehicles, Illegal Weapons/Drugs/Luxuries at ×2 price.**
  - **25** Wealthy *ihatei* wants a lift home: **Cr30,000** to **Oiwoiieaw** (Tlaiowaha 0103, 5 pc).
  - **26** Mistaken for tinkers — **Mechanic (EDU)** fixes his power distribution; refusal → roll **1D**, go to encounter **10+roll**.
  - **27** The Aslan ally meets an aged retainer who never won land — let him die for a chance at territory, or stop him? (moral beat)
  - **32** A bard's tales of the hero **Saifiwil** → **DM+1 to Persuade vs camp Aslan** for listeners.
  - **33** Human tinker **Jarel Yean** (Solomani, from Vorito) — **secretly a GeDeCo spy.**
  - **34** Outcast supply stall — buy fuel/supplies; meet **Utea**.
  - **36** A poisoned warrior's body wearing the **victorious Hkaaiheir badge** (foul play afoot).
  - **37** (ally only) Sacred shrine; priest **Stea** — impress with **Difficult (10+) Persuade (EDU)** to be anointed → **may speak at the nightly meeting** (Manipulating Events).
  - **41–43** The **Tale of the War** (all true; teller 1–3 Htyowao / 4–5 neutral / 6 Hkaaiheir).
  - **44** Briefed on notable *ihatei* + retainers.
  - **45** Htyowao desperately need new land or face internal dissent.
  - **46** Hkaaiheir claim they'll expand outside the Hierate after Keaih (**mostly false**).
  - **47** (ally) contacts name **Teaw** as the most influential individual.
  - **51–52** Wall of bodyguards: **Persuade (TER)** or fight **1D+4** veterans (armour 9, gauss rifles 4D AP5 Auto3 Scope).
  - **53** Party — **Carouse (SOC)**, gain **DM+1 ×Effect** to camp Persuade checks.
  - **54** Duel to first blood — **Very Difficult (12+) Melee (STR or DEX)**; win → **DM+1 ×Effect** to camp Persuade.
  - **56** **Teaw** beckons the crew to his ornate tent.
  - **57** **Fyukh** spots them from his scoutship — flees if he reads them as Drinaxi, else meets guardedly.
  - **61** **Eihei** holds court on his armed light trader; he has bought Drinax surveillance/defence intel from Fyukh.
  - **62** **Iykhi**'s court (Htyowao section; dangerous to reach) — **only after Warriors from Keaih.**
  - **63** **Toiho** arrives in a **Halaheike** pocket warship; few sworn warriors, welcomes visitors (even humans) for prestige — **only after Warriors from Keaih.**
  - **64** **Elyo** holds court at camp centre; **despises humans.**
  - **65–66** A council of leading *ihatei* → opportunity to **Manipulate Events.**
  - **67** (ally) pulls favours → **DM 1D to next Persuade.**
- **Gaining an audience with a leading *ihatei*** (high-SOC princelings, guarded): **Beg** — Difficult (10+) **Diplomat (SOC)**, **+1 per Cr5,000 gift**; **Demand** — Very Difficult (12+) **Persuade (SOC)**, **+1 per combat-ready PC/ship**; **Sneak in** — Very Difficult (12+) **Stealth (DEX)**, failure = spotted and attacked. On any social failure, run a **bad reaction** (Aslan Reaction rules — see `factions.md`).

### 4. Manipulating Events — preventing the early Drinax invasion (at the camp)
- **Trigger:** Crew act to keep advocates (Eihei/Fyukh) and followers (Iykhi/Toiho) apart.
- **Levers (the menu):**
  - **Diplomacy:** Redirect an *ihatei* to a different target — base **Very Difficult (12+) Persuade**, then DM by roleplay/offer. "Please don't attack Drinax" fails; "Paal is a better target, and we'll scout landing sites" → **DM+2**. Suggesting an **Aslan-held** world → **DM−2** (they only attack Aslan worlds for clan rivalry or against a very weak holder). Garden/habitable worlds are best targets — apply **Trade Code DMs** (below). Playing leaders against each other → **DM+1**. But *ihatei* want the **winning side**: if **2+** already back a plan, persuading others to it → **DM+2**.
    - **Trade Code conquest DMs:** Ag +1 · Ga +3 · Ht +1 · In +1 · Lo +1 · Ri +2 · Na 0 · Ni 0 · Lt 0 · Wa 0 · De −1 · Fl −1 · Hi −1 · Ic −1 · Va −1 · Po −2 · Ba −2 · As −3.
  - **Sabotage:** **Spike the starport fuel** (chemicals that jam jump plumbing or force misjumps) to delay any *ihatei* migration — get in via **Stealth** or by enlisting the Outcasts (Utea will help if guaranteed she won't be implicated; a long delay = a longer captive market). Or **plant evidence via Fyukh** that Drinax has powerful defences / is under Imperial protection.
  - **Violence:** **Assassinate** a key *ihatei* (e.g. Iykhi or Eihei) — politely announced or rudely sudden; removing Eihei as advocate **reduces invasion risk**; an "accident" for a leader tips the balance. **Frame** one *ihatei* for attacking another (Htyowao–Hkaaiheir tension is already hot). Or, recklessly, **attack the whole camp** (outgunned hundreds-to-one) by smuggling a **WMD** in or triggering an **ice-slide** with mining charges — *and* this violates Oleb's "no atrocities" order (see Consequences).
- **Partial victory:** If the crew engineer events so Drinax is **not attacked this year**, that's a **partial win** — but only a stay of execution. For a permanent fix, go to **Escalation**.

### 5. Escalation — changing the Keaih war for the worse (deep Hierate)
- **Trigger:** Crew decide the lasting solution is to **prolong the Keaih war** so the swarm drains back to it. They must travel into well-patrolled Hierate space (SDBs, customs cutters, naval bases, capital ships) toward **Keaih (~9 pc)** and **Akoaft**.
- **Keaih (Tlaiowaha 0704 region — the contested waterworld):** Orbital construction stations; asteroids being nudged in to become Htyowao **colony ships** (carrying millions away as the clan loses). **A civilised, heavily defended Aslan world** — audiences with Htyowaoko/Hkaaiheirko are **near-impossible** for humans; the crew gain **little leverage here** and risk **both clans' vengeance** if they cause trouble. Use it for **context**, not action. *(Note: the world index lists hex 0704 as Pourne and places Keaih ~9 pc rimward/deep in Hierate; treat Keaih's exact hex as off the recruitment map — `(verify)`.)*
- **Akoaft (the proxy-war world):** Airless, dense tainted atmosphere; **all native life is lethal poison** to Aslan. Both clans hold **tunnel-linked fortresses**; troops in environmental armour fight through "slime channels." **Orbital/aerial assets are banned** by the rules of war (tanks + infantry + tunnel fighting only). The system is **off-limits** — ships are ordered to refuel at gas giant **Khufu** and jump out. Landing means running a blockade of **Ekawsiykua escorts**; **20,000+ warriors** wait in orbit (reinforcements not yet deployed because the war is "about to be won" inside the limit).
- **Ways to prolong the war (the goal — make the Htyowao able to fight on past 10 years, or void the Hkaaiheir victory):**
  - **Capture/kill arbiter Yae:** Yae plans to leave Akoaft to preside over the **victory ceremony** on Keaih, travelling in his own **Ktiyhui-class 200t courier**. Intercept it and: **(a)** plant evidence / force a confession that **Yae leaked the Htyowao fortress** (the useful lie); **(b)** **prevent Yae attending** the ceremony → no formal victory inside ten years → **Htyowao may send reinforcements**; or **(c)** **kill Yae**, disrupting the ceremony and throwing **Keaih's status into doubt.**
  - **Help the Htyowao:** smuggle **weapons** past the blockade; **raid Hkaaiheir supply ships** so they can't land the killing blow; or **find the Hkaaiheir HQ** with ship sensors and relay it to the Htyowao.
  - **Use the *ihatei*:** the poetic option — convince **Iykhi** to **give up his honour for his clan**, or convince **Teaw** that human–Aslan conflict harms the Hierate, to **divert the landless warriors back to Akoaft.**
- **mythic-gm keyed reminder:** This adventure has **no single "must-happen" die** — it is decision-driven. The one inexorable mechanic is the **25-week clock**: track weeks spent (jumps, Tyokh delays, camp days) and, when relevant, run the **Out of Time** consequence below. Resolve infiltration/persuasion via `task-system.md`; resolve Aslan reactions via the Reaction table in `factions.md`.

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — no rescues.*

- **Money / opportunity:** Camp encounters can pay — **Gambler** winnings, **selling cargo at ×2** (advanced/illegal goods, livestock, meds), **Cr30,000** passenger to Oiwoiieaw, recruiting Aslan crew, **bribing/cultivating Utea**. Investing in Kteiroa port walks it up the Attitude ladder (Cr10k → Tolerant; Cr100k → Friendly; **MCr10 → a pirate Haven**).
- **Salvage:** A semi-intact **Ihateisho** scout (Jump-4!) if Oleb's Hawk Warriors recover it; **Kasiyl's Ktiyhui Courier** (luxurious, fully repaired, in a hollow asteroid at the edge of Drinax system) if he joins; potentially **Yae's Ktiyhui** if captured in Escalation. The crested **Yuhaih** ceremonial axe is an identifying clue, not loot.
- **Contacts/favours:** **Kasiyl** as a loyal Aslan ally (at the cost of a future debt + assassin heat); **Fyukh** as a suborn-able broker inside the *ihatei*; **Teaw** as a guest-friendly statesman; **Galx**'s gratitude if her tribesmen are recovered.
- **What success buys:** prevent or divert the invasion → Drinax's foundation holds; the **Floating Palace and nascent kingdom survive intact**; the crew earn standing at Oleb's court.
- **What failure costs (Out of Time / no intercession):** Iykhi (16 weeks to muster) leads **>300 ships / >5,000 warriors** against Drinax. The Palace **survives** (campaign continues) but the **nascent Empire of Drinax is severely damaged.** Thereafter **Drinax and the coreward subsector face repeated Aslan raids** — never strong enough to take the Palace, but enough to make **all future adventures more dangerous and to reduce the assistance Drinax can give the crew.**
- **Cost of doing it wrong:** Violence/atrocity in the camp (WMD, ice-slide, mass killing) risks **implicating Drinax** against Oleb's explicit order (war crimes / "thermonuclear explosions") → court fallout and reputational damage even if it "works." Causing trouble at **Keaih** can bring **two clans'** vengeance; meddling caught anywhere in the *ihatei* draws "terrible anger." Killing Aslan / raiding Aslan shipping **enrages the Hierate** (Standing hit; Yerlyaruiwo/Syoisuis send assassins — see `factions.md`).

## Empire / Reputation effects
Apply per `campaign/empire-reputation.md` ("Per-adventure effects"). Stated outcomes:
- **Full success (invasion permanently deflected via Escalation):** the *ihatei* threat to Drinax is removed for the foreseeable future — **no Aslan-raid penalty** to later adventures; Drinax's support to the crew is preserved/strengthened. This is a foundational **survival/foothold** milestone (empire track step 1), not a per-world Attitude jump by itself.
- **Partial success (Drinax spared "this year" only):** temporary reprieve; no lasting penalty now, but the Keaih endgame still looms — re-raise *ihatei* pressure as a sandbox clock later.
- **Failure (Out of Time):** Drinax damaged → **persistent Aslan-raider pressure** in the coreward subsector; **reduce assistance available from Drinax** and add danger/Heat to subsequent Reach operations. (Treat as a standing negative on the campaign, not a single world's Attitude.)
- **Kteiroa Attitude:** investing in the port shifts **Kteiroa (0505)** from **Suspicious** up the ladder (Tolerant/Friendly/**Haven** at MCr10) — a rare friendly Aslan-run foothold and a possible pirate Haven. Record the cause.
- **Hierate Standing:** Pure infiltration/diplomacy need not move Standing; but **killing Aslan, raiding Aslan shipping, framing/assassinating leaders, or getting caught meddling** earns a **Hierate Standing hit** and possible assassin attention (Yerlyaruiwo). Conversely, embarrassing one clan to another's benefit can earn quiet goodwill from the beneficiary (Syoisuis-style). Pleasing the **Imperium** is irrelevant here (this is Aslan space).
- **Allied Fighting Strength note:** **Kasiyl is of the Ahroay'if**; cultivating Aslan goodwill in this arc supports later access to **Ahroay'if +6 FS** (see `empire-reputation.md`) — but that is a long-game payoff, not granted by this adventure alone.
