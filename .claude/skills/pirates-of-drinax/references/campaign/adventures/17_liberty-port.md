# Liberty Port

> Use this when: the **Free Sperle Society** hires the crew to set up a smuggling spaceport ("Liberty Port") on a remote island of the GeDeCo-dominated waterworld **Sperle (0404)**, to run whale-biochemicals offworld and fund a "free Sperle" campaign — until a heavy-handed government crackdown spirals the operation into an armed insurrection. Cross-ref `setting/factions.md` (GeDeCo), `setting/worlds/00_world-index.md` (Sperle 0404, Exe 0403), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Attitude shift, per-adventure effects), `setting/personalities-and-factions.md` (Black Eagle / pirate rivals if Enshaari recurs), `rules/trade-commerce.md` (invest-in-a-port / smuggling income).

**Logline:** A "harmless smuggling racket" for an idealist pacifist's freedom movement curdles into a doomed island revolt the crew cannot win — only end. The patron, Armandie Kern, has built a fantasy where her cause never spills blood; when the government's brutality shatters that fantasy she flips from peace-preacher to vengeful firebrand, and the crew's real choice is whether to fight a glorious last stand with her, walk away, or betray her to save the very people her uprising will get killed.

## At a glance
- **Where:** **Sperle (0404)** — `BA8A78A-7`, Ri Wa, **Hostile, FS 2**, independent (Appx var. `BA8A76A-7`). A near-total waterworld, pop ~**90 million** on island chains and seabed settlements; **GeDeCo** owns the Class-A starport outright and, through the government's debt, effectively owns the world government. The crew's "Liberty Port" is built on an obscure, lightly-populated island with a good natural anchorage. The pirate-stash leg is one parsec away at **Exe (0403)** `B300101-A` (vacuum outpost; stash sits on the **outer moon of Exe's gas giant**).
- **Threat:** Low–moderate but **escalating and political**, not a fleet fight. The early phases are logistics, social engineering and minor crime; the climax is a small, hopeless insurrection (small arms + searcher-craft gunships vs. government-hired mercenaries and, finally, landing troops). The lethal danger is **getting branded a pirate and executed** if Sperle's authorities link the crew to Liberty Port — *not* spinal-meson overkill. There is no Imperial fleet here; GeDeCo works through the world government and hired mercenaries.
- **Length:** Medium–long, multi-visit. Three movements — **Setup** (gather gear, build the port) → **Smuggling Run** (months of quiet operation) → **Trigger → Confrontation → Choices** (the crackdown and revolt). A built-in **"several months"** of successful smuggling precedes discovery; the crew may be off-world when the trigger fires and only learn of it on return.
- **Prerequisites:** None hard. A drop-in Companion adventure (Phase 1–2). Crew need a starship (to move cargo on/off the makeshift pad) and ideally some smuggling/setup know-how — pirates already know how to stand up and tear down a hidden cargo facility. Reaching Sperle is a **jump-2 detour** off the Hierate route (escorts skip it — see `setting/trojan-reach.md`; Sperle is a prime ambush spot).
- **Arc slot:** A standalone **anti-GeDeCo** Peril, the counterpoint to *The Vorito Gambit* (where the crew may *help* GeDeCo). Outcomes are mostly **personal and local** (a relationship with Sperle, money, a future enemy in the imprisoned pirate Enshaari) rather than a big empire-track jump; Sperle is a route world worth cultivating, but this adventure rarely flips it to Haven and can easily leave it ruined.

## Player-Facing (the hook)
*Safe to narrate.*

- **The world:** Sperle is owned in all but name by the **General Development Corporation**. GeDeCo runs the whale-harvesting industry (through subsidiaries) and the starport; the world government is so deep in debt for the port that GeDeCo effectively owns it too. Most port profit leaves the system, so the government can never pay down the debt. The people are **weary of GeDeCo** but resigned.
- **The trade:** Sperle's signature export is the **Sperle Whale** — not an animal but a colossal amoeba-like cluster of single-celled organisms, harvested by driving filter-submersibles through it and pumping the organisms into tanks. The biomass refines into valuable **biochemicals**. (A dispersed whale is left to regrow; over-harvesting destroys it.) "Searcher" pilots fly fast wing-in-surface-effect craft just above the waves to spot whale activity.
- **The patron — the Free Sperle Society:** a local independence movement and regional **laughing stock** — noisy malcontents who talk tough, occasionally get arrested, and can't even organise a protest march. For most members it's an outlet to grumble over beers; a free Sperle scares them more than GeDeCo does. **But a serious inner cell has a plan**, and the movement's incompetence is perfect cover for operating openly despite a high Law Level.
- **Hiring on:** representatives of the Society approach the crew — earnest, slightly inept, self-conscious, some *giggling* that their proposal is "a little bit illegal." They want people with **starships** to sneak goods on and off Sperle without using the GeDeCo starport, and to help them **set up a small port of their own** on a remote island. The smuggling income will fund the movement and maybe a legal buy-out of harvesting firms.
- **Armandie Kern (the leader):** a veteran searcher pilot, a **legend** among harvesters, intervenes forcefully. She delivers a tirade about *peaceful* liberty and condemns anyone who wants a militia. She is **fine with theft, smuggling and all manner of crime in a good cause** but **vigorously opposed to violence** — so much that she has been known to *punch* people who advocate it. She concedes a "token" armed guard might deter GeDeCo, and seems certain a few guards with small arms will be enough. (Worldly crew may notice she is *blinkered* — she has built a fantasy in which violence simply cannot happen.) Still, her plan is reasonable and the smuggling can make money.
- **The brief, concretely:** (1) obtain heavy **cargo-handling equipment** and **weapons** (small arms; ideally a few support weapons mountable on searchers or for shore defence); (2) help the Society **build and run Liberty Port** on the island; (3) convince **harvest-ship captains** to divert part of their catch to Liberty Port instead of the GeDeCo port. The Society will pay well — even just *buying* the gear and reselling/installing it turns a (modest) profit; **stealing or scamming it** makes the margins fat.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned.*

- **There is no winning move for the Society — only a longer or shorter fuse.** The whole scheme is built on Armandie's denial. A reliable smuggling income *can* be established and run quietly for **several months**, but a sustained drop in harvest yields **will** be noticed, the authorities **will** eventually find the cause, and once they do they **will** shut Liberty Port down with arrests. The adventure's spine is: quiet success → discovery → escalation the crew cannot prevent, only steer.
- **Armandie's break is the real twist.** She holds her pacifism even under violence *up to a point* — urging non-lethal self-defence and courteous treatment of opponents. But **if serious casualties are taken, she flips to the opposite extreme**: demanding violent retribution, willing to cause collateral damage, riding a wave of popular anger that makes her hard to remove. She becomes **visibly unstable**, and ultimately suffers a **breakdown** — *but not before doing immense damage to her own world and people*. The crew who indulge her militancy are feeding a doomed vendetta.
- **The crackdown is engineered by heavy-handedness, not conspiracy.** No grand villain plot: the GeDeCo-owned government simply reacts clumsily — noticing yield drops, launching an **aggressive surprise boarding** of a sympathetic harvest ship, arresting crews on **spurious grounds**, confiscating equipment. *Each* over-reaction radicalises more harvesters. The militant Society cell distributes the weapons the crew supplied; harvester crews declare they'll resist boardings; the government promises armed resistance will be met in kind. A mercenary boarding where **shots are fired** (no one badly hurt) is the point of no return.
- **The insurrection is small, doomed, and short-lived.** There is simply **not enough popular support** on Sperle. The island population is **sharply divided** — some locals will *help the government troops* to end the fighting fast, some resent the Society for the bloodshed, a few genuinely sympathise. Armandie's followers are determined and inspired but **unskilled**. They can repel the first, over-confident assault on their stronghold but **cannot hold out**. Once the authorities commit, Liberty Port is overrun.
- **The Enshaari trap (if the crew loot the stash).** The easiest source of cargobots and weapons is the abandoned stash of the rival pirate band **Enshaari's Spacers** at Exe. The theft is genuinely easy — *no booby-traps*, near-free gear — but it ties the crew's Liberty Port machinery to a known pirate. **Enshaari is alive**, in an Imperial prison vessel **awaiting execution**. If the gear is later seized by Sperle's government, it can be traced; and **if Enshaari ever escapes (or is sprung) he will come looking for whoever stole his cache.** A grim irony lurks here: the *proof* that the crew are pirates (and thus executable on Sperle) may come from items they stole off *other* pirates.
- **The crew's actual leverage is de-escalation.** They can almost certainly **minimise bloodshed — but only by causing the Society to fail.** Conversely any push toward escalation (e.g. a violent rescue of an arrested harvester crew) makes it spiral fast: gunfights, more crews resisting, the government searching every harvester, the Society fracturing into rabble-rousers vs. those who fight to oppose them. The honest framing for the table: *this is a tragedy you arrived too late to prevent; your job is damage control.*

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. Hiring On (at the meeting)
- **Trigger:** Society reps approach the crew (on Sperle, or wherever the crew can be reached) with the smuggling/port proposal; Armandie delivers her anti-violence tirade and concedes a token guard.
- **What's here:** Establish the comedy-then-menace tone of the Society; let the crew read Armandie (a wisdom/INT-based read, or simple roleplay, reveals she is "blinkered"). Negotiate pay. Surface the three deliverables (gear, port, divert captains).
- **Complications:** The crew may smell that this ends badly — *good*. They can still take the job for the smuggling money. If they try to talk Armandie toward realism now, she **won't hear it** (her pacifism is non-negotiable; her optimism is a fantasy).

### 2. Acquiring the Gear (cargo handling + weapons)
- **Trigger:** Crew accept and go shopping for heavy equipment they can deploy and *break down again* on a remote island.
- **What's here:** Two needs — **cargo-loading equipment** and **weapons**:
  - **Cargo handling:** the simplest workable solution is a pack of **cargobots** plus prefab buildings for warehouses/processing, *or* a semi-permanent crane system. Robots are less efficient than a crane **but far easier to conceal** (drive under cover in moments) and quick to set up/tear down — the classic pirate trick for a temporary port on a "remote rockball or distant corner of a sparsely inhabited world." Crew may invent alternatives.
  - **Weapons:** small arms are the priority; the Society also wants a few **heavier/support weapons** — something mountable on a **searcher craft** or able to **defend the port against maritime vessels offshore**.
- **The cheap source (Enshaari's stash — see GM-Only):** a rival pirate cache one parsec away at **Exe (0403)**, on the **outer moon of Exe's gas giant** — abundant **cargo-handling robots and weaponry**, *unguarded, no booby-traps, near-free*. Belongs to **Enshaari's Spacers** (airlock-the-hostages thugs), whose main ship was crippled by an Imperial pirate-hunter and whose other ship has fled to re-identity itself; Enshaari is jailed awaiting execution. **The only way the theft fails is a major crew blunder.** *(Hidden cost: traceability + a future enemy — GM-Only.)*
- **Complications:** Buying the gear legitimately earns only a modest profit; **stealing/scamming** it (Exe, or elsewhere) fattens the margin sharply. Taking Enshaari's gear is the obvious "free" play and the obvious long-term mistake.
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (the Exe theft):** there is **no forced disaster** — the theft succeeds unless the crew err. Resolve any complication by the player's plan; if they want a "did the cache get noticed" check, `dice.py 2d6` vs **8+** clean, with DMs for stealth/speed.

### 3. Setting Up Liberty Port
- **Trigger:** Gear in hand; crew help the Society stand up the port on the chosen island (good natural anchorage, small population mostly pro-free-Sperle).
- **What's here:** Mechanically simple — install cargo handling and teach the operators to move freight between a **maritime ship in the bay / holding facility** and a **starship on the makeshift landing pad**, both ways. The hard part is **people**:
  - **The dissenters:** a segment of locals dislike the project. Danger: someone **reports it to the world government**, or persistent protesters keep getting in the way.
  - **Winning the island (the core social task):** the best fix is to **convince the locals the project serves their interests.** Selling it as pure concept is **very difficult but possible with strong persuasion**; *easier* is a mix of **inducement + intimidation** ("much easier to take silver than lead") — offer waverers financial advantage for supporting (or merely not opposing) the port, while implying consequences for betraying it. Most then accept.
  - **What NOT to do:** **outright violence alienates locals (even supporters) and is counter-productive.** Arming a militia and imposing de-facto **martial law** on the island *might* work mechanically but **violates the Society's ethos** — acceptable to only a small militant minority, and it pre-loads the disaster.
- **Complications:** The crew must walk a fine line between hinting at threats and dangling gain. Heavy-handedness here directly worsens the eventual revolt's fallout.
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (winning the locals):** treat as a `task-system.md` extended social effort. Suggested gate to swing the island: **Difficult (10+) Persuade (SOC)** to sell the concept, **Average (8+)** if combining money + veiled threat. Failure → a local **reports the port** (advances the discovery clock; jump to Trigger sooner).

### 4. Diverting the Harvest (the smuggling run)
- **Trigger:** Port operational; crew must convince **sympathetic harvest-ship captains** to bring part of their catch to Liberty Port instead of the GeDeCo port.
- **What's here:** Some deception is needed but it is **not that hard**. Because **harvesting is a hit-and-miss business** (some ships fill in days, others cruise for weeks), a captain can take a **first catch to Liberty Port**, then harvest again and deliver *that* to the main port — masking the diversion. A consistent yield drop will eventually be noticed, but a *temporary* downturn raises no alarm: **the operation can run for several months before anyone notices.**
- **Payoff:** The arrival of the first biochemical shipment is a cause for **celebration**; once sold offworld, the Society has a **reliable income** to fund its legal challenge. Militant voices calling for armed struggle are (for now) drowned out by optimists.
- **Complications:** Every month of success builds toward the inevitable discovery (Scene 5). The crew may be **off-world** during the smuggling run; if so, they learn of the trigger only on return.
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (when does the heat land?):** run a slow discovery clock. Suggestion: after **3 months** of operation, roll **`dice.py 2d6` each month, on 10+ the yield-drop is noticed** (DM +1 per extra month, +1 if the crew were sloppy/violent in setup) → fire Scene 5.

### 5. Trigger Incident (the crackdown begins)
- **Trigger:** The discovery clock fires (or a local informs early). If the crew are **on-planet**, they hear it as it happens and may intervene; if **off-planet**, they return to find events already turned violent.
- **What's here (the escalation ladder):**
  1. After a few weeks, the **yield drop becomes obvious**; enquiries get nowhere, so authorities launch a **surprise boarding of a sympathetic harvest ship** — done **heavy-handedly and aggressively**; a scuffle, and the officers + some crew are **arrested at gunpoint**.
  2. Unable to learn exactly what's going on, authorities start **arresting harvester crews on spurious grounds** and **confiscating equipment**.
  3. The Society's **militants find this intolerable** and call for action. Moderate ideas (a **harvest-crew strike**) compete with direct ones; **weapons (the crew's) are distributed**; harvester crews declare they'll **resist further boardings**. The government answers that **armed resistance will be met in kind.**
  4. **Point of no return:** a government-hired **mercenary security force** tries to board a harvest ship carrying **armed Society members**; **shots are fired** (no one seriously hurt) — but the ship is **pursued and forced to anchor near an inhabited island**, mercenaries closing in.
- **Complications:** The crew can intervene at any rung; **escalation is easy, de-escalation is hard.** Their first real fork: help the cornered harvester crew, or not.
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** this sequence is a "must-happen" engine once discovery fires. Drive NPC reactions via the Reaction table; resolve any crew intervention via task checks. No single die — the ladder advances unless the crew actively halt it.

### 6. Confrontation (the standoff)
- **Trigger:** The pursued harvester is anchored, threatened with being **fired on and sunk** if it runs for open sea; mercenaries prepare to board and take the crew prisoner. Armandie turns to the crew for ideas.
- **What's here:** Two structural problems for the Society: (1) its **membership is split** — many want it to back the surrounded crew *without* dragging the world into war (impossible: surrender = "abandoning" the crew; armed help = "the Society/offworlders started the bloodshed"); (2) the authorities now **know something is happening** and will find Liberty Port; once they do, **shutdown + arrests** follow.
- **The crew's options at the standoff:**
  - **Talk the harvester crew into surrendering** — defuses *this* flashpoint; arrests follow but few serious charges. An outside chance a **deal** can be brokered.
  - **Extract the harvester crew** beyond the authorities' reach — a **difficult but possible rescue**. Crucially, an **offworlder-aided** rescue ("criminals aided by offworlders") carries **less political fallout** than a straight harvesters-vs-government battle.
  - **Escalate** (e.g. a **violent rescue**) — guarantees a gunfight, more crews resisting, the government searching every harvester; **conflict becomes unavoidable** and the Society fractures (rabble-rousers vs. those who fight them).
- **Complications:** Whatever the crew do **has consequences**; there is **no clean outcome**. This is the hinge that decides whether the climax is a contained shutdown or a full (doomed) revolt.

### 7. The Revolt & Choices (climax)
- **Trigger:** Events have tipped toward armed conflict (crew escalated, or simply failed to defuse). The authorities move on **Liberty Port**.
- **What's here:** A **small, short-lived insurrection.** Hardening point: a shipload of **uninvolved, probably unarmed harvesters** is **fired on by nervous/trigger-happy government mercenaries** — and **Armandie breaks** (if she hasn't already), now demanding an **uprising**. Her plan: vigorously resist the closure of Liberty Port and present **demands for concessions** — she knows the Society can't *win*, but hopes to **salvage** something. When troops land, her followers are ready: **plenty of small arms** and **support weapons mounted on searcher craft** for fast **wavetop strikes**. They are determined but **unskilled**, and the **divided island** undermines them (some locals aid the troops). They **repel the first over-confident assault** but **cannot hold.**
- **The crew's choices (the real climax — there is no victory, only an exit):**
  - **Leave Sperle.** Their involvement may not be common knowledge; they can pose as traders caught in events and **go**. Their actions may have repercussions later, but **they live.**
  - **Stay and fight (the last stand).** A "glorious" doomed defence they might escape from in their starship — perhaps carrying some rebels. Cutting and running at the right moment may face **rebel opposition**; **Armandie may bless their escape or try to kill them** for considering it, per her attitude toward them.
  - **Betray Armandie's hard core (often the best way to save lives).** Can be **mild** — quietly leaving before it turns graphic, then **selling the authorities information** on the rebels' numbers/leaders (paid well; better still if the crew **capture key rebel figures**), and **persuading rebels to surrender / not fight to the last** despite Armandie. The authorities will **negotiate**, **pardon** the crew's part, and **reward** them for ending it relatively peacefully. *Cost: the Society fails, and some will hate the crew forever; others will call them peacemaking heroes.*
- **The honest stakes:** if the crew **stay and lose**, best case is a **long prison term** when the island is overrun; if linked to Liberty Port's machinery, they may be **executed as pirates** (ironically, on evidence stolen from *other* pirates). The only ways out are to **leave** or to **end the conflict so the government lets them go** — even if that means betraying their patron. *They are pirates; honourable conduct is not guaranteed.*
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (the first assault):** the rebels **always** repel the initial over-confident attack, then erode. Resolve the firefights via combat rules; for "how long can the stronghold hold after the first repulse," `dice.py 1d6` days, **−1 per: divided locals aiding troops / no crew leadership / heavy prior casualties**. There is **no die that lets the revolt win** — honour the doom.

## Key NPCs

### Armandie Kern (patron — Free Sperle Society leader)
Human female, veteran searcher pilot; a **legend** among Sperle's harvesting community. **(stats not given in source — reconstruct; `(verify)`):** treat as a skilled civilian pilot/leader — *Flyer (wing-in-surface) 3, Leadership 2, Persuade 2, Carouse 1, Gun Combat (slug) 1, Vacc Suit 1, Seafarer 1*; STR/DEX/END average, **INT/SOC above average** (charisma). Searcher craft + small arms; no combat training of note.

### Free Sperle Society (the movement)
Two faces: (1) the **public membership** — earnest, inept, self-conscious malcontents who mostly want to grumble (a free Sperle frightens them); (2) the **serious inner cell** — competent, militant, using the movement's incompetence as cover to operate openly despite a high Law Level. Members range from **strike-and-protest moderates** to **arm-for-revolution militants**; under pressure the two camps **split into rivals.**
*Run them as well-meaning amateurs out of their depth — sympathetic, exasperating, and ultimately unable to win.* **WANT:** freedom from GeDeCo; income to fund a legal buy-out — or, for the militants, an armed uprising.

### Enshaari (rival pirate — the buried trap)
Human male, leader of **Enshaari's Spacers** ("airlock the hostages if the ransom's late"). **(stats not given — reconstruct as a hardened pirate captain if he ever appears; `(verify)`.)** **Currently incarcerated in an Imperial prison vessel awaiting execution** — *not present* during the adventure.
*A vicious operator you do not want to cross.* **GM-Only:** if the crew loot his Exe stash and he later **escapes or is rescued**, he hunts whoever took his gear — and seized stash items can prove the crew are pirates. **WANTS (if freed):** his cargobots and weapons back, and revenge. A strong recurring-antagonist seed.

### GeDeCo / the world government (the antagonist apparatus)
**GeDeCo** — Imperial megacorp, most widespread in the Reach, quiet instrument of anti-Aslan buffer-building; owns Sperle's starport and, via debt, the government (`factions.md`). Acts here **through the government and hired mercenaries**, not directly. The **world government** is heavy-handed and clumsy: aggressive boardings, spurious arrests, confiscations, and finally **mercenary security forces** that land to take Liberty Port. *No mastermind — just brutality that radicalises.* **WANTS:** the smuggling stopped, the status quo (and the debt) preserved; willing to **negotiate, pardon, and reward** crew who end the revolt cheaply.

### Government-Hired Mercenaries (the muscle)
Human (or mixed) professional security/boarding troops, decently armed and armoured; competent but **trigger-happy and over-confident** in the early assaults. **(Stat from `setting/ships-of-the-reach.md` / generic mercenary blocks as needed; `(verify)`.)** Use for boardings, the standoff, and the landing on Liberty Port. *More numerous and far more skilled than Armandie's amateurs.*

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — there is **no outcome without cost**, and **no way to make the Society succeed**.*

- **Smuggling pay (the clean money):** the Society **pays a good price** for gear delivered; even merely **buying-then-installing** the equipment turns a **modest profit**, while **stealing/scamming** it (esp. Enshaari's free Exe stash) makes the margins **fat**. Running the diverted biochemicals offworld is a **reliable income stream** for as long as Liberty Port operates (several months).
- **The government reward (the endgame money):** if the crew **end the conflict relatively peacefully** (talk-down, betrayal, capturing rebel leaders, selling intel), the authorities **pay well for information** on the rebels, **pardon** the crew's involvement, and **offer a reward.** This is the surest payday — at the price of the Society's defeat.
- **Salvage:** Enshaari's stash itself (cargobots, prefabs, weapons) is near-free loot; searcher craft and small arms may be scavenged from the wreckage of the revolt.
- **Contacts & standing (mixed):** some Sperlists hail the crew as **peacemaking heroes**; others **hate them forever** (especially if they betrayed Armandie or sided with the government). A **peacefully-resolved** Sperle is a world the crew helped, however ambiguously — a foothold for later recruitment.
- **What failure / violence costs:**
  - **Staying and losing:** **prison** when the island is overrun; **execution as pirates** if linked to Liberty Port's machinery (or stash-traceable gear).
  - **Escalation:** more deaths, a fractured Society, a ruined island — and Armandie possibly **trying to kill the crew** if they cut and run.
  - **The Enshaari debt:** looting Exe plants a **future enemy** who, if freed, hunts the crew; the seized gear is a **liability that can convict them.**
  - **Armandie's fate:** peace-track resolution → she is arrested but largely spared (few serious charges); revolt → she **breaks down** after immense damage, or dies in the last stand.

## Empire / Reputation effects
Apply per `campaign/empire-reputation.md` ("Per-adventure effects"). This is a **local** adventure: it moves **Sperle's world Attitude** (and the crew's reputation on Sperle) far more than any great-power Standing or the Drinax track.
- **Sperle starts Hostile** (`empire-reputation.md`; UWP `BA8A78A-7`, FS 2). Realistic shifts:
  - **Peaceful resolution / saved lives** (talk-down or low-violence betrayal; government pardons + rewards the crew): nudge Sperle **up one step toward Tolerant/Neutral** among the pragmatic majority — a foothold, *not* an instant Haven. The grateful faction is the **harvesters/common people**, not GeDeCo.
  - **Escalation / bloody revolt the crew fuelled:** Sperle stays **Hostile** (and the surviving population's resentment of "offworlders who got people killed" makes future recruitment **harder**).
  - **Heavy-handed crew tactics** (martial law, militia coercion in setup) cost goodwill even when "successful" — track the **doctrine** used (Overt/intimidation vs. Subtle/saviour, `empire-reputation.md`).
- **GeDeCo:** this is an **anti-GeDeCo** action (the counterpoint to *The Vorito Gambit*). Even a "peaceful" ending **annoys** the corporation (its income was bled and its puppet government embarrassed). If GeDeCo (or its Drinax agent **Rachando**) learns the crew were behind Liberty Port, treat it as a **leak risk / strained relationship** — relevant if the crew later want GeDeCo's *Vorito* deal.
- **No automatic great-power Standing change:** the Imperium isn't directly involved (GeDeCo works through Sperle's government + mercenaries). **Reckless violence**, however, can earn a regional reputation and — if the crew are tagged as pirates here — local **kill/arrest attention** (handle via `piracy-raiding.md` heat if it spills outward).
- **Record why** Sperle ends where it does (peacemaker vs. instigator), and **whether the crew incurred the Enshaari thread** — both feed later play.

## Threads & hooks onward
Seed onto mythic-gm Lists:
- **→ Enshaari's revenge:** if the crew looted the Exe stash, **Enshaari** (awaiting execution) is a primed recurring antagonist — **escape/rescue** him and he hunts the crew for his gear; seized stash items are evidence that can convict them as pirates. A clean future-foe hook.
- **→ GeDeCo friction:** an anti-GeDeCo black mark that complicates any later GeDeCo dealings (*The Vorito Gambit*, Theev's GeDeCo patronage — `setting/worlds/key-worlds.md`). Rachando (GeDeCo's Drinax mole) may sniff it out.
- **→ Sperle as a route world:** Sperle is a **jump-2 detour / prime ambush spot** on the Hierate route (`setting/trojan-reach.md`). A peacefully-resolved Sperle is a **future recruitment target**; a ruined one is a cautionary tale and a possible **Lost World** style setback.
- **→ Armandie's legend:** if she survives, she is either a **broken ex-firebrand** or (if the crew kept her on the peace track) a chastened idealist — either way a memorable returning NPC and a barometer of how the crew treat the powerless.
- **→ The harvesters / surviving Society:** grateful or vengeful factions on Sperle the crew can revisit; militant survivors might resurface as desperate allies or enemies elsewhere in the Reach.
