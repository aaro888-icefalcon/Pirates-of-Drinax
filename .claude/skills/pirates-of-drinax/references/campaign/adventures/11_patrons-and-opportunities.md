# Patrons & Opportunities (Adventure Seeds — the sandbox's bread-and-butter)

> Use this when: you need a job, hook, rumour, or Random-Event payload during a sandbox pirate turn — a port contact offers work, the crew want a reason to visit/cultivate a specific world, or the oracle says "introduce a thread/NPC." This is the catalogue of ~40 mission-sized **patron hooks** (one or more per world, by subsector), each with the player-facing offer and the GM-only truth. Most rewards grant a **specific Attitude shift** toward Haven/Friendly — the *main engine* for moving worlds onto the Drinax track. Pair with **empire-reputation.md** (applies the shifts) and **00_overview.md** ("How adventures get triggered").

## How to use these (solo / mythic-gm)
These are **between-adventure content** and **Random-Event fuel** — the punctuation of the sandbox loop, not a script. (00_overview.md: Sandbox + set adventures.)

1. **As a Random Event / new thread.** When Mythic throws a *Remote Event*, *NPC Action*, *Move Toward a Thread*, or *Ambiguous Event* — or when the crew first make port at a listed world — reach for that world's patron here and present its **Player-Facing** offer as the in-fiction hook (a contact in the bar, a holo from court, a noble's summons). Don't reveal the GM-Only twist; that surfaces only as earned.
2. **As a deliberate destination.** When the crew decide to *cultivate* a world (push its Attitude toward Haven for the recruitment endgame), its patron is the ready-made reason to go there and the deed that earns the step.
3. **Seed a Thread, then run it on the engine.** Accepting a hook adds a **Mythic Thread** ("Recover Ferish for Scout Ballis," "Build Number One's second starport"). Resolve the actual scenes with Fate Questions + the rules files (piracy-raiding.md, trade-commerce.md, personal-combat.md, space-combat.md, starship-operations.md). The patron text gives you the *frame and the twist*; the engine gives you the *outcome* — never pre-decide success.
4. **Cash out on the empire track.** On completion apply the stated reward as the **reward line** of what the crew actually did (empire-reputation.md: Shifting Attitude / Per-adventure effects). Most rewards = "World moves N steps toward Haven" or "becomes Friendly/Haven." Partial/failed jobs give less; pick up the trade-off if the win came via raiding or atrocity.
5. **Foreshadow the big episodes.** Several patrons are *doorways* into the scripted adventures or the mini-campaigns — flagged "→ seeds" in each entry. Use them to plant set-pieces in systems the crew already frequent.
6. **Many are full Companion adventures.** Cordan (Baroness Lux), Acrid, Sperle, Kteiroa, and others are expanded into standalone Drinaxian Companion adventures — if the crew bite hard, switch to that adventure's file; otherwise run the seed as a one-scene job.

**Pacing:** a healthy cadence is a few sandbox turns, then a patron job, then court time/consequences — not raid after raid. Vary hunting grounds; a repetitive pirate is a dead one. (00_overview.md: Rhythm guidance.)

## The patron-encounter mechanic
There is **no special dice subsystem** here — these use Traveller's standard patron/encounter framing, surfaced through Mythic.
- **Frequency:** treat a patron as available whenever the crew make port at the named world, or whenever a Random Event calls for a hook. The Reach has **no Xboat net**, so jobs, bounties, and rumours spread only as fast as tramp ships — a job heard at one port may be stale or already taken elsewhere; rule via Fate Question.
- **Vetting the patron (optional):** if the crew investigate a patron before committing, run an Average (8+) **Streetwise/Investigate/Carouse** check; success reveals partial truth — often enough to glimpse the hidden complication but rarely the full GM-Only twist. (Several entries explicitly require Streetwise to even find the patron's real want — e.g. the Alley Cat, Exe.)
- **Reward type:** rewards are usually **Attitude shifts** ("moves N steps toward Haven," "becomes Friendly/Haven"), sometimes flat credits, a contract, a contact/black-market access, or a favour. Apply shifts via empire-reputation.md (one step at a time, fiction-anchored).
- **The "donation" pattern:** a recurring opportunity type where simply *delivering goods/credits* to a needy world buys Attitude (see the Donation section). These need no patron at all — they are standing offers.

## Reading an entry
Each entry is compact:
`World (UWP · hex · subsector start-Attitude)`
- **Patron** — who offers it (player-facing).
- **Job** — what they openly ask. *Safe to narrate.*
- **GM truth** — what's really going on / the twist. *Reveal only as earned.*
- **Complications** — the obvious obstacles + the hidden sting.
- **Reward** — payoff (usually an Attitude shift).
- **Leads** — what it seeds onto the Lists / which adventure or world it connects to.

(UWPs/Attitudes from Appendix 2 — see empire-reputation.md for the full table and the recruitment maths.)

## Borderland subsector patrons

### Byrni (B955699-6 · 0107 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** Regent **Stoylis**, Lord Commander of the Antispin Star Guard.
- **Job:** Stoylis has heard (secret channels) of Oleb's scheme to rebuild Sindal and will back it — *but only if Oleb proves worthy to rule*. Devise a way to demonstrate Oleb's **wisdom and benevolence**.
- **GM truth:** The catch is genuine: the king possesses neither quality in any measure. This is a test of the crew's *spin*, not Oleb's character — they must stage-manage a display of royal virtue (a public act of mercy, relief, justice) convincing enough to satisfy a soldier who knows better.
- **Complications:** Oleb is venal and erratic; any honest demonstration risks exposing him. Failure or an obvious sham loses Stoylis (and word travels).
- **Reward:** **Byrni becomes part of the Kingdom of Drinax.**
- **Leads:** A direct recruitment win for a FS-3 world; ties to the court-politics thread (managing Oleb's image runs into *Blood of the Star Dragon*).

### Cordan (A895347-9 · 0401 · Suspicious) — patron 1 of 2
- **Patron:** **Baroness Lux** of Cordan.
- **Job:** Destroy her rivals, **Baron Fero** and **Baron Halley**. The Imperium would intervene if she hired offworld mercenaries (destabilising Cordan threatens the shipping lane), but a few high-skill professionals with high-tech gear could make her problems *quietly disappear* without alerting the Imperium.
- **GM truth:** **Baron Halley is a Zhodani puppet** — he feeds reports on Imperial shipping to his masters and keeps **Zhodani-trained guards** protecting him and his castle. Killing Halley is both wet-work and a counter-intelligence coup; the crew may not realise whom they're really hitting.
- **Complications:** Imperial scrutiny if it gets loud; Zhodani professional security around Halley; picking the Baroness as a winner shapes who rules a key trade-route world.
- **Reward:** Territory and **estates on Cordan**.
- **Leads:** Expanded as the Companion adventure **The Cordan Conflict** (a small, nasty civil war). The Zhodani angle ties to the Reach-wide Zhodani-agent rumour and *Blood of the Star Dragon*.

### Cordan (0401) — patron 2 of 2
- **Patron:** **Uht Vamos.**
- **Job:** Vamos needs **weapons. Lots of weapons.**
- **GM truth:** There is a **secret civilisation on Cordan** — descendants of the original population who fled into the deep cave network to hide from raiders centuries ago and stayed. Now a barbaric low-tech people, they want their world back. **General Development Corporation (GeDeCo)** troops guard the waystation and shoot the cave-barbarians on sight. Vamos speaks for these hidden people.
- **Complications:** Arming a barbarian uprising against an entrenched megacorp garrison; the rising collapses Cordan's tech/society.
- **Reward:** **Cordan drops to TL2 and Low Tech, but becomes a Haven.** (A Pyrrhic upgrade — trades the world's development for loyalty.)
- **Leads:** Anti-GeDeCo thread (counterpoint to *Vorito Gambit*); can collide with Baroness Lux's plan above.

### Acrid (AAC1388-D · 0302 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Gera Hollis.**
- **Job:** Both Acrid and Argona chafe under **PRQ** rule — hazardous, unpleasant conditions; workers treated as slaves. After winning concessions in the revolt of '38, PRQ now plans to **cut pay and increase the workforce on both planets by 40%**, turning cramped quarters into hellholes. The miners intend to **revolt and declare both planets independent** but have no ships. They want **military support from Drinax** to fend off PRQ's strike-breakers.
- **GM truth:** Straightforward but bloody. PRQ has **two System Defence Boats** at each planet and will dispatch **mercenary cruisers** to crush resistance. The war for Acrid and Argona may be brief but it will be violent.
- **Complications:** Outgunned miners; PRQ SDBs + merc cruisers; an exposed pro-Drinax intervention angers the megacorp.
- **Reward:** **Acrid and Argona become Havens.**
- **Leads:** Expanded as the Companion adventure **Revolution on Acrid**. Anti-megacorp arc; both worlds are route-relevant.

### Argona (B612586-9 · 0503 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** *(donation opportunity — no named patron).*
- **Job/Reward:** Every **MCr1 worth of Luxuries or Illegal Drugs** donated to Argona moves it **one step toward Haven**.
- **GM truth/Leads:** Standing offer; complements the Acrid/Argona revolt above. (See Donations section.)

### Arunisiir (B776530-6 · 0201 · Tolerant)
- **Patron:** **Ekaft**, an Aslan landowner dreaming of reclaiming lost glories.
- **Job:** Carry a message to his distant cousins in the **Hierate**, telling them to come to Arunisiir and **conquer it once again**.
- **GM truth:** Upsetting Arunisiir's status quo will almost certainly trigger **civil war**, so Ekaft specifically wants the crew to fetch an **overwhelming Aslan force** — not a token one. Inviting an Aslan conquest fleet into the Reach has obvious strategic blowback for Drinax.
- **Complications:** Diplomatic trek into Aslan space; arming an Aslan incursion runs counter to most Drinax aims; civil war on the world.
- **Reward:** **Arunisiir becomes a Haven.**
- **Leads:** Ties to the *Ihatei!* / Aslan-expansion threads; a crew opposing Aslan expansion should think twice.

### Exe (B300101-A · 0403 · Tolerant)
- **Patron:** *(none — the whole settlement.)*
- **Job/GM truth:** It is remarkably easy to turn Exe into a friendly port — **just bribe them.** A few thousand credits each and the crew (the tiny population) can be bought.
- **Reward:** Exe becomes a **friendly/Tolerant-flip port** for the cost of a handful of bribes.
- **Leads:** The canonical example of "money flips a small port outright" (empire-reputation.md). Cheap forward base.

### Falcon (A158448-D · 0305 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **John Macule.**
- **Job:** A pirate crew attached to the retinue of **Zuekhvi the Corsair** claims to have planted a **bomb** somewhere on Falcon's outer surface and is demanding ransom from the asteroid. Macule wants the crew to hunt down Zuekhvi's pirates and **force them to reveal the bomb's location**.
- **GM truth:** A pirate-vs-pirate hunt; whether the bomb is real, a bluff, or already armed is the GM's call (Fate Question). Zuekhvi is a fellow corsair — possibly a future ally or rival on Theev.
- **Complications:** Finding/squeezing the pirates before a deadline; the bomb may be genuine; making enemies among the corsair community.
- **Reward:** **MCr0.5**, and Falcon moves **one step toward Haven**.
- **Leads:** New pirate-rival NPC (Zuekhvi) for the Lists; connects to the Theev gang scene.

### Inurin (E668776-5 · 0304 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** Free Trader **Laro Smith.**
- **Job:** Smith believes **Sarna** (a fungal product) can be made into a hugely profitable recreational pharmaceutical, but his prior attempt to steal the secret of the **fungal caves** from the priests ended with him fleeing a torch-wielding mob. The **Inurini now consider him the Prince of Lies** and he cannot return. He wants the crew to **sneak into the fungal caves and learn the origin of Sarna.**
- **GM truth:** A heist against a religious community that already hates Smith; the crew inherit his bad reputation if linked to him.
- **Complications:** Infiltrating sacred caves; a hostile, superstitious populace; Smith's motives are pure profit.
- **Reward:** **A share in profits, if any** (speculative — the venture may flop).
- **Leads:** New recurring NPC (Smith) and a black-market pharma thread.

### Tanith (A589342-B · 0301 · Tolerant)
- **Patron:** **Councillor Abros.**
- **Job:** Four ships — a scout, two traders, and an Aslan vagabond — recently left Tanith, each having **accidentally picked up stowaway primates called volays** (warm-dark-cave dwellers that infest air ducts and lockers). Abros wants the crew to **hunt down all four ships and eliminate the volays and their parasites.**
- **GM truth:** Researchers just discovered the volays carry a **parasitic mite** in their fur. On Tanith a local fly keeps the mite in check; **offworld, with no flies, the parasite multiplies exponentially and becomes deadly poisonous to humans**. All four departed ships may be carrying lethal contamination. If the crew fail, **Tanith may be declared a Red Zone by the Imperium.**
- **Complications:** Tracking four scattered ships across the Reach (no Xboat net); biohazard handling; a ticking reputational clock for the whole world.
- **Reward:** **Cr100,000.**
- **Leads:** A chase/biohazard thread; failure scars Tanith (Red Zone) and the route.

### Umemii (C521877-6 · 0501 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Thona Zorn.**
- **Job:** **Disable the starport at Tanith, Acrid, or Cordan**, forcing Imperial trade ships to divert to Umemii and bring prosperity to this barren world.
- **GM truth:** Naked economic sabotage of a rival port — exactly the kind of deniable disruption Drinax's strategy thrives on, but it cuts against any world the crew are simultaneously courting (note the conflict with the Acrid/Cordan patrons above).
- **Complications:** Hitting a port the crew may also be cultivating; identification = Attitude loss on the victim world; Imperial attention to a disabled lane.
- **Reward:** **Umemii becomes Friendly.**
- **Leads:** Classic "raid one world to win another" trade-off (empire-reputation.md: Piracy ↔ empire tension).

## Sindal subsector patrons

### Pandora (B878313-B · 0410 · Neutral)
- **Patron:** Administrator **Thollett.**
- **Job:** "Rebrand the apocalypse." Everyone believes Pandora's old colony was wiped out by a **solar flare**; if new evidence blamed the **old Empire of Sindal** instead, more colonists might settle. Thollett has heard the crew have **Drinax connections** and wants them to **plant fabricated evidence of a Sindalian genocide** here.
- **GM truth:** Pure historical forgery / propaganda. Smearing Sindal undercuts Drinax's own "heir of Sindal" prestige (empire-reputation.md: reminding worlds of Sindal grandeur *boosts* recruitment) — a subtle long-game cost.
- **Complications:** Forging convincing archaeology; the lie can rebound on Drinax's legitimacy if traced.
- **Reward:** **Pandora moves two steps toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** Sindal-legacy thread; potential conflict with the crew's own Sindalian-revival narrative.

### Sperle (BA8A76A-7 · 0404 · Hostile)
- **Patron:** The **Free Sperle Society.**
- **Job:** Establish a **smuggling route off Sperle** so the population can bypass the **GeDeCo-owned shipping companies**.
- **GM truth:** Anti-GeDeCo economic resistance; the megacorp will not ignore a route that breaks its monopoly.
- **Complications:** GeDeCo countermeasures; building/maintaining a clandestine route; Sperle starts **Hostile** (hard to even approach).
- **Reward:** **Sperle moves three steps toward Haven** (a big swing — reflects its Hostile start).
- **Leads:** Expanded as the Companion adventure **Liberty Port**. Anti-GeDeCo arc (vs. *Vorito Gambit*).

### Borite (E655796-4 · 0609 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** BCA Administrator **Ura.**
- **Job:** One of the mine's **atmosphere pumps** has failed and no surviving local engineer can fix it. Any competent technician can repair it — but the pump is **watched by spies for Oghman slavers**.
- **GM truth:** The moment the crew's technician takes the job, **the slavers target them** for capture. The "simple repair" is bait for the slavers' attention.
- **Complications:** Protecting the technician from Oghman slaver abduction; the repair is trivial, the security threat is not.
- **Reward:** **Cr5,000** and Borite moves **one step toward Haven**.
- **Leads:** Oghman-slaver thread (recurs across Sindal); Borite is the *Honour Among Thieves* refuel waypoint, so easy to seat.

### Chalchiutlicu (B76A787-7 · 0704 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** City Councillor **Jilacua.**
- **Job:** Relieve population pressure in his city by **shipping people offworld**. For every **1,000 people** the crew take, his city moves **one step toward Haven**. But Jilacua **cannot be seen defying the other councillors**, so the crew must **smuggle the passengers out** to claim the reward.
- **GM truth:** Politically deniable emigration; the other councillors oppose it. A clean, repeatable Attitude engine if the crew run low-passage runs.
- **Complications:** Smuggling large numbers of people past the authorities; where the emigrants go (colony seed elsewhere).
- **Reward:** **One step toward Haven per 1,000 people** smuggled out.
- **Leads:** Repeatable; pairs with worlds needing settlers (Pandora, Hilfer).

### Number One (C9C6778-7 · 0208 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Loank Vetis**, ruler of the undersea settlement **Felaka** (far side of the planet from the starport).
- **Job:** Felaka sits on rich minerals (**lanthanum, radioactives**) but rival settlements block its exports with extortionate customs. Vetis wants the crew to help build a **second starport on — or above — Number One.** A port on an **anti-gravity platform** (like Drinax's Floating Palace) could float above the atmospheric toxins. Vetis pays most costs but needs **MCr100 investment from the crew**, plus **TL13+ technical expertise** (from Drinax or similar). Someone must also **protect the new port from jealous rivals.**
- **GM truth:** A major capital project, not a quick job — a long-term investment with armed opposition from the established settlements.
- **Complications:** MCr100 buy-in; sourcing TL13+ engineers; defending the build site from rivals; undersea politics.
- **Reward:** **Number One becomes a Haven**, plus a **cut of the new starport's profits.**
- **Leads:** Big-ticket infrastructure thread; recurring income once built; showcases the Floating-Palace anti-grav tech.

### Salif (B100477-C · Sindal · per Appendix, unlisted-hex)
- **Patron:** **Tiznee Zazzle.**
- **Job:** Zazzle (a cult/celebrity figure) has become **immune to her regular anagathics** and needs advanced gene-tweaking treatment at **Albe (A540A98-E · 0601 · Imperium)** to keep taking the drugs. To preserve her mystique her followers **cannot know she's leaving on medical grounds**. So she wants the crew to **"kidnap" her**, take her to Albe, and arrange a **"heroic escape"** after treatment.
- **GM truth:** A staged abduction; the real job is discreet medical transport into Imperial space and a convincing fake rescue. Zazzle's authority rests on the illusion of immortality.
- **Complications:** Selling the fake kidnap to her followers; operating at Albe (Imperial port); the escape theatre must land.
- **Reward:** **Salif becomes a Friendly port.**
- **Leads:** Anagathics/immortality motif (echoes the Sindal-emperors rumour); new celebrity-patron NPC.

### Vume (B100477-C · 0505 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Priel Marsden**, representing an unnamed Imperial corporation (*"PRQ, perhaps, or GeDeCo"*).
- **Job:** The **Vume station** is a lucrative source of technology and scrap — **once the thousands of current residents are removed.** *How* the crew clear them out is up to them; Marsden "has no interest in their methods." She just wants an **empty station** ripe for redevelopment as a luxury waystation on the **Floriani tourist route.**
- **GM truth:** A corporate-ordered population clearance — potentially an atrocity. The euphemism ("removed") is doing heavy lifting; the moral weight (and Standing/Attitude cost) lands on the crew.
- **Complications:** Thousands of residents; "removal" by force = atrocity (heavy Standing hit, empire-reputation.md); the corporate patron is deniable.
- **Reward:** **Vume becomes a Haven**, plus **MCr0.05.**
- **Leads:** A dark moral-choice job; ties to the megacorp threads (GeDeCo/PRQ).

## Tlaiowaha subsector patrons

### Akoaft (Aslan world)
- **Patron:** **Taofel the Scholarly**, a scientist of the **Hkaairheir clan.**
- **Job:** Taofel spent six years researching a bio-modification to let the **Aslan liver process the toxins in Akoaft's native slimes**; his clan cut funding when the war turned. He needs a patch of **rare lichen** (reported by warriors to soothe slime-exposure pain) — but the lichen's territory has fallen into **Htyowao** hands.
- **GM truth:** A research-fetch into contested Aslan territory; the lichen sits behind enemy (Htyowao) lines.
- **Complications:** Operating in an Aslan war zone; rival-clan territory; toxic native slimes.
- **Reward:** **Cr20,000.**
- **Leads:** Aslan-clan-politics thread; goodwill with the Hkaairheir.

### Blue (B443487-C · 0801 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Metholde Vong.**
- **Job:** Vong's daughter **Kasah** stowed away aboard a ship (the trader **Ginnea**) that called at Blue; he wants the crew to **find her.**
- **GM truth:** The trail darkens: the crew discover the **Ginnea was attacked by pirates** somewhere in Sindal subsector, and **Kasah was found aboard and sold into slavery in the Glorious Empire** (Aslan). A simple search becomes a slave-rescue deep in Aslan space.
- **Complications:** Tracing a pirate attack; a rescue inside the Aslan Glorious Empire; slaver networks.
- **Reward:** **Blue moves one step toward Haven**, plus **Cr20,000.**
- **Leads:** Slavery/Aslan-rescue thread (echoes Blue's daughter ↔ the Oghman/slaver motif); new NPC Kasah.

### Clarke (B899753-8 · 0702 · Neutral)
- **Patron:** **High Psychopomp Boone.**
- **Job:** **Prince Harrick of Drinax** was mortally wounded years ago and preserved with technology like the Psychopomps' own, awaiting revival by the Floating Palace's scientists. Boone argues Harrick **embodies the Psychopomps' promise — resurrection from "the black slab of death."** If Harrick **publicly joins the faith and makes it the state religion** of a revived Kingdom, Boone will **step down and name Harrick his successor as High Psychopomp.**
- **GM truth:** A power play disguised as theology. Boone knows it'll be **centuries** before Clarke can actually resurrect its millions of faithful dead (and even then would need anagathics for everyone) — but installing Harrick as figurehead leader **cements the church's power for generations.** This is about institutional control, not miracles.
- **Complications:** Requires Drinax to adopt a state religion (huge policy implication — see empire-reputation.md Government/Religious-Dictatorship note); Harrick's own willingness; church politics.
- **Reward:** **Clarke moves two categories toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** Deeply tied to **Prince Harrick** (a possible ruler of Drinax post-*Blood of the Star Dragon*) and Royal Policy. *Honour Among Thieves* already introduces the Psychopomps of Clarke.

### Fantasy (E788400-2 · 0808 · Tolerant)
- **Patron:** **Tarya.**
- **Job:** Tarya found the **wreck of the Aslan slave ship** that brought her ancestors to this world, with **notes on genetic modification** beyond her understanding. She suspects the **limits placed on her people's intellect can be reversed** and wants an offworlder to use the genetic data to **undo the Aslan modifications**, letting Fantasy's people develop their own technology and culture.
- **GM truth:** Reversing deliberate Aslan dumbing-down of a slave population — uplift via reverse-engineered gene-work; the science is beyond local capability and may be beyond the crew's too.
- **Complications:** Decoding alien genetic notes; the ethics/risks of population-scale gene editing; possible Aslan objection to freeing their old stock.
- **Reward:** **Fantasy becomes a Haven.**
- **Leads:** Uplift/Aslan-legacy thread; echoes Cordan's hidden barbarians and the slavery motif.

### Hilfer (BA5077A-6 · 0804 · Hostile)
- **Patron:** *(donation opportunity — no named patron.)*
- **Job/Reward:** Allocating a ship to **drag water-ice asteroids to Hilfer** moves it **one step toward Haven**; every **MCr0.5 spent on water reclamation** moves it **one step toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** Standing offer (see Donations); pairs with Chalchiutlicu's emigrants needing a dry world to settle.

### Iroioah (B6301G3-E · 0607 · Neutral)
- **Patron:** The **Nameless Keeper** — an ancient **psionic construct** (Ancients-era defence mechanism).
- **Job:** The Keeper **telepathically contacts** crew **touched by the Ancients** (it cannot reach Aslan; **human/Vargr** crew are vulnerable). It demands they **re-open a temple buried beneath Iroioah's sands and activate a device** there, claiming this will **drive the meddling Aslan from the tombs.** Refuse, and it **torments them with maddening dreams until they go insane.**
- **GM truth:** The "device" is the **generator for the psionic construct itself.** Iroioah's original inhabitants died of plague but built a psionic weapon to **copy their minds into the host bodies of other sophonts** and resurrect their empire once the world was rediscovered. The construct is **decayed** — it can no longer seize victims, only torment them. If the crew fight through the tomb's automated guardians as instructed, the construct will then **try to trick them into repairing its generator and seize control of their bodies.**
- **Complications:** Coercive dream-assault (the "reward" is *relief* from it); a deadly automated tomb; the patron is the threat — succeeding on its terms is the trap.
- **Reward:** **Relief from the psionic dream assault** (i.e., make it stop) — not a normal payoff.
- **Leads:** Ancients/psionics horror thread; resolving it cleanly (destroy vs. repair the generator) is a real GM-Only fork.

### Keaih (Aslan world)
- **Patron:** **Takhweirl**, an ambitious Aslan female trader running the **Company of Profits That Rise Like Mist** (access to the court of **Hkaairheirko**).
- **Job:** Takhweirl wants to bypass the **Tlaiowaha clan's chokehold** on trade (almost all trade routes through their port on Tlaiowaha). She has a cargo of **heavily restricted Aslan terraforming biochemicals** to sell to the government of **Albe** (Imperial protectorate, Sindal); the buyers wait on the backwater **Homestead** (also Sindal). She wants the crew to **smuggle her cargo across the border and deliver it to Homestead**, sending a **detachment of Aslan warriors** along to guard it.
- **GM truth:** Cross-border smuggling of restricted Aslan biochem with armed Aslan supercargo aboard the crew's ship — trust and control issues on top of the legal risk.
- **Complications:** Border-running restricted goods; Aslan warriors aboard the ship; the Tlaiowaha clan's interest in stopping her.
- **Reward:** A **shipping contract** with the Company: ~**1,000 tons Keaih↔Vorito monthly at Cr2,200/ton**, plus **documents of passage** to operate openly in the **Hierate**.
- **Leads:** Opens legitimate Aslan-space access (the documents of passage are valuable for raiding/operating in the Hierate); recurring trade income; Aslan-clan politics.

### Khusai (A5766M5-C · 0403 · Unfriendly)
- **Patron:** **Hunter Syas**, Guardian of Trade (her duty: hunt and destroy pirates).
- **Job:** Syas pays handsomely for **information leading to the capture and death of enemies of the Hierate.** She is especially keen to **locate the legendary pirate world Theev — and blast it from orbit.**
- **GM truth:** A pirate-hunter offering the crew (who *are* pirates, and depend on Theev) money to betray their own kind and their primary Friendly port. Selling out Theev would gut the crew's own infrastructure — a genuine moral/strategic trap.
- **Complications:** The crew are the very people she hunts; betraying Theev costs them their pirate haven (Friendly start); feeding a clan the coordinates to destroy a human world.
- **Reward:** **Cr5,000 per pirate, Cr50,000 per ship** (delivered/destroyed).
- **Leads:** A tempting-but-toxic offer; ties directly to **Theev** (Honour Among Thieves) and the pirate-gang Lists.

### Kteiroa (C4601K4-8 · 0505 · Unfriendly)
- **Patron:** **Utea** (the ihatei leader; see also *Ihatei!* / camp scenes).
- **Job:** The **ihatei need weapons** and Utea knows where: an Aslan world called **Oiwoiiea**, ruled by the **Ahroay'if** trading clan, has a **weapons factory**, and Utea stole the route of a planned shipment. The weapons are aboard a **600-ton Khtukhao-class clan transport** (jump-2, so a slow circuitous border route) bound for the markets at **Tlaiowaha**, passing through the **Kteiroa system**. The transport rides in an **escorted convoy**, but if the crew steal the weapons Utea will **fence them at a very favourable rate.**
- **GM truth:** **Utea's intel is only partly accurate.** The weapons are **not** bound for Tlaiowaha — the **Ahroay'if intend to sell them to human worlds like Hilfer and Paal** along the border, **profiting from the threat posed by their own Aslan cousins.** The crew's "simple raid" intercepts a cynical arms deal aimed at humans.
- **Complications:** Boarding an escorted convoy; the real destination/buyers differ from the brief; arming the ihatei has strategic consequences.
- **Reward:** **Kteiroa moves one step toward Haven** (and a favourable fence rate on the loot).
- **Leads:** Expanded as the Companion adventure **Friends in Dry Places**; deeply tied to *Ihatei!* and the Ahroay'if clan (a potential +6 FS ally — empire-reputation.md).

### Paal (B564679-6 · 0805 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** *(donation opportunity — no named patron.)*
- **Job/Reward:** Every **MCr1 worth** of **Advanced Electronics, Advanced Manufactured Goods, Advanced Vehicles, Cybernetics, Luxury Consumables, Luxury Goods, Medical Supplies, Polymers, Robots, Pharmaceuticals, or Vehicles** donated to Paal moves it **one step toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** Standing offer (see Donations); a sink for high-value plunder.

### Pourne (A9B2887-A · 0704 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Nevin Yrral**, Bureau of Investigation.
- **Job:** A **jump-capable scout ship of possibly non-human design** arrived months ago. Its **sole crewman was dead**, the remains too radiation-degraded to identify the species. The bureaucracy fears it was an **advance scout for an alien invasion.** Locate the **scout's origin world** and determine if it's a threat.
- **GM truth:** An open-ended mystery hook — the origin and danger are the GM's to seed (Fate Questions / oracle). Could be a misjump derelict, a genuine unknown polity, or a red herring; ties naturally to the "Solomani sublight flotilla" or "Space Kraken" rumours if you want a payoff.
- **Complications:** Cold trail (degraded remains, no Xboat data); the origin world could be anything from harmless to hostile.
- **Reward:** **Cr10,000**, and Pourne moves **one step toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** A blank-canvas exploration thread; hang any deep-space mystery on it.

### The World (E100551-6 · 0707 · Tolerant)
- **Patron:** Governor **Olc.**
- **Job:** Find a replacement for The World's **malfunctioning fusion control module.** Replacing the **ancient Second-Imperium-era reactor** outright is *not an option* — it would **expose the population to the truth of The World.** Olc needs a **thousand-year-old reactor part (or a suitable substitute)** brought in **before the lights go out.**
- **GM truth:** There is a hidden "truth of The World" the reactor secrecy protects (the station's population live under some maintained illusion; cf. the rumour that The World's inhabitants have "strange psychic powers"). A modern reactor would reveal it — hence the demand for an antique part.
- **Complications:** Sourcing a millennium-old fusion component (salvage/antiquities hunt); a hard deadline ("before the lights go out"); not disturbing the secret.
- **Reward:** **The World becomes a Haven.**
- **Leads:** Salvage/mystery thread; the withheld "truth" is a GM-Only seed to develop.

### Torpol (B55A77A-8 · 0601 · Unfriendly)
- **Patron:** Provost **Shala.**
- **Job:** There is another waterworld in the Reach — **Thalassa (B88A889-8 · 0803)** — whose people build huge cities on **rafts of woven organic matter** (plants gene-engineered from native flora; the Thalassans guard the secret closely). Shala wants the crew to travel to Thalassa, **break into a Thalassan bio-research facility, and recover the plant seeds plus cultivation data** — and, ideally, **kidnap the famous geneticist Dr. Thorkan.**
- **GM truth:** Industrial espionage + a kidnapping against a rich independent waterworld; Thalassa protects its bio-secrets.
- **Complications:** Infiltrating a guarded research facility; abducting a named scientist; Thalassan security.
- **Reward:** **Torpol moves one category toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** Connects Torpol (already prominent in *Honour Among Thieves*) to Thalassa; bio-tech espionage thread.

### Tyokh (B466AH7-E · 0606 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** The **Alley Cat** — leader (or self-claimed leader) of the **Rea'a Hrillkhir** (the crew need allies to raid in Aslan space).
- **Job:** Investigation (**Streetwise**) reveals the Alley Cat wants **dustspice** — a spice prized in Aslan cuisine; connoisseurs prefer **natural spice from the Imperium** over the widely-available synthetic. **Capture a dustspice freighter** for her.
- **GM truth:** The Alley Cat intends to **poison a shipment of natural spice with an undetectable slow-acting poison, then blackmail the wealthy Aslan who eat it.** Natural dustspice is imported by the Aslan megacorp **Tyeyo Fteahrao Yolr** in **fast, well-armed freighters** that race across the Reach toward the jump-5 Great Rift route to the core Hierate markets.
- **Complications:** Intercepting a fast, well-armed corporate freighter; abetting a mass-poisoning/blackmail scheme; the Alley Cat may be lying about her own status.
- **Reward:** **Access to the Rea'a Hrillkhir black market** — fence stolen goods at **20%** at **any Aslan port.**
- **Leads:** Unlocks Aslan-space fencing (major for a crew raiding the Hierate); ties to the dustspice/Ahroay'if-addiction rumour.

### Vorito (E595ABB-A · 0709 · Hostile)
- **Patron:** **Lando Nierr.**
- **Job:** *(Only if the crew took the Jaskarl Hologram's offer — see The Vorito Gambit / PoD1 p.192.)* Later, Nierr offers the crew the chance to **become Pirate Lords of Theev.** Theev is **secretly operated and maintained by GeDeCo** to sow fear across the Reach and drive worlds under GeDeCo's protection. **Salif** recently fought off a Theev pirate attack — if the crew **make an example of Salif**, Nierr will let them **depose Theev's current rulers** with help from the fabled **Widows of Blacksand.**
- **GM truth:** This exposes the campaign's deep secret — **GeDeCo runs Theev** as an instrument of fear. Becoming "Pirate Lords" makes the crew GeDeCo's deniable enforcers; punishing Salif is the loyalty test.
- **Complications:** Prerequisite (the Jaskarl Hologram path); attacking Salif (which a crew may be cultivating) ; becoming entangled with GeDeCo's grand design.
- **Reward:** Become one of the **Pirate Lords of Theev**, and **Vorito becomes a Haven** for selling cargo and avoiding spies.
- **Leads:** Tightly bound to **The Vorito Gambit** (GeDeCo's manipulation of the Reach, +10 FS ally at a price) and to **Theev / Honour Among Thieves**. A pivotal allegiance choice.

## Tobia subsector patrons

### Boulder (D100758-9 · 0107 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** **Jopple Ort**, Administrator.
- **Job:** Imperial miners in the **Wildeman** system developed a new processing technique that could make some **Boulder belt asteroids economically viable** again — but it's a **trade secret of the Wildeman Mining Consortium.** Ort wants the crew to **capture a Wildeman ore processor and steal the technology.**
- **GM truth:** Straightforward industrial theft (board/capture a working ore processor and extract its proprietary tech). Angers the Wildeman Consortium.
- **Complications:** Capturing a crewed industrial ship; reverse-engineering/extracting the secret; Wildeman retaliation.
- **Reward:** **MCr2**, or **access to the Class B starport** on future visits.
- **Leads:** Ties Boulder ↔ Wildeman (also a patron world below); industrial-espionage thread.

### Exocet (A574126-8 · 0110 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** Terraforming Engineer **McGrath.**
- **Job:** McGrath believes the colony can be made far more valuable by **"capping" the more active volcanoes** to cut off their sulphurous fumes. He needs **two things: nuclear weapons, and a pilot crazy enough** to fly into an **active volcano** and drop a nuke at a **precisely calculated point** to collapse the mountain on top of it. He wants **high-yield atomics** (Illegal Weapons on the trade table).
- **GM truth:** A wildly dangerous geo-engineering stunt requiring illegal WMDs and a suicidal precision bombing run.
- **Complications:** Sourcing high-yield atomics (Illegal Weapons — heat); flying a nuke into an erupting volcano; getting the drop point exactly right.
- **Reward:** **Exocet moves two steps toward Haven.**
- **Leads:** WMD-procurement thread (overlaps with *The Treasure of Sindal*'s WMD cache); daredevil set-piece.

### Iilgan (C467787-8 · 0309 · Suspicious)
- **Patron:** Lord **Selwyn haut-Bizlant.**
- **Job:** A neighbouring landowner, **Poetachus**, has ordered a new shipment of **artificial bacteria from Tobia.** Bizlant wants Poetachus ruined: **intercept the shipment and secretly swap the bacteria cultures** in the transport's hold for different ones, which will **render Poetachus's estates worthless and barren.**
- **GM truth:** Sabotage-by-substitution in a landowner feud; if discovered, the crew are tied to a deliberate agro-ecological attack.
- **Complications:** Intercepting and discreetly tampering with cargo (not a smash-and-grab — it must stay secret); choosing sides in a noble vendetta.
- **Reward:** **MCr1**, plus the use of **haut-Bizlant's land as a hideout.**
- **Leads:** Provides a Tobia-area safe house; landowner-feud thread.

### Scaladon (AAD98DK-A · 0206 · Hostile)
- **Patron:** **Themis Bioplastics** (a.k.a. Themis Industries).
- **Job:** Scaladon's natives believe **only those who dwell on their world will be saved** at the apocalypse; the caustic seas force the overcrowded population onto a few islands. Themis has developed a **bioplastic for building cheap artificial islands** to expand living space — but **the Chosen cult shoots down any ship entering their airspace**, making field-testing lethal.
- **GM truth:** Corporate humanitarian-product trial blocked by a violent doomsday cult; testing means braving cult anti-air.
- **Complications:** Cult anti-aircraft fire; Scaladon starts **Hostile** (FS 3); fanatical, xenophobic population.
- **Reward:** **MCr0.5.**
- **Leads:** Cult/colony thread; a hard-to-approach world.

### Wildeman (B201674-C · 0409 · Neutral)
- **Patron:** **Klim Gobbo**, a belter.
- **Job:** A century ago the pirate **Tamby Dour** raided shipping within four parsecs of Wildeman; the Imperial Navy killed him but never found his base. Klim found it by accident — **hidden inside an asteroid** — but the treasure vault has a **DNA lock**: only **Dour or a descendant** can open it. If the crew find a **direct descendant of Dour**, Klim will show them the base and **split the treasure 50/50.**
- **GM truth:** Dour's only living relative is **Glinnet Dour, a thief on Blacksand (Theev).** Bring her to open the vault and the crew share Dour's "fabled fortune" — which turns out to be **two empty gin bottles. Nothing else remains.** **The real treasure is the base itself: a fully functional, well-equipped, extremely well-hidden Class B starport right on the main shipping lane.**
- **Complications:** Finding and recruiting Glinnet Dour on lawless Theev; the literal "treasure" is a joke — the value is the *base*; rivals would kill for a hidden lane-side port.
- **Reward:** **One empty gin bottle** (the punchline) — **but** effectively a **secret Class B pirate base on the main shipping lane** (a superb forward operating base / fence point).
- **Leads:** A prize hideout — possibly the crew's best raiding base; ties to **Theev/Blacksand** (Glinnet) and the Scurrilous-Dave/buried-treasure rumours.
