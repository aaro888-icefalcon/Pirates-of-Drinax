# The Cordan Conflict

> Use this when: while passing through Cordan (or the Borderland trade route) the crew are hired by **Baroness Lux** to "prevent" a plot against her — actually to ferment a small, nasty civil war between her two rival barons (**Fero** and **Halley**) using deniable assets. Covers the medical-cargo hook, the dinner/true brief, the **Agraaki assassination**, Halley's security response, the **Ellaton** border siege, escalation tools (assassination/infiltration/sabotage/rabble-rousing/starport intrigue), Lux's over-reach, and ending the war (peace conference vs. continued chaos). Cross-ref `setting/worlds/key-worlds.md` (Cordan), `setting/worlds/00_world-index.md` (Borderland subsector), `setting/factions.md` (Aslan *ihatei*, Zhodani, GeDeCo), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Attitude/recruitment).

**Logline:** A wildly popular baroness who bribed her people with unsustainable high-tech gifts hires the crew to quietly burn down her two rivals' domains — a deniable proxy war the Imperium must not officially notice — and she would rather create an unwinnable quagmire than a clean victory; but her own plan has consequences she has not foreseen, and only the crew can end the war they started.

## At a glance
- **Where:** **Cordan (0401 Borderland) A895347-9** — a pristine garden world, Starport A, TL9, official pop ~2,000 (baronial households only; the far larger TL5 mass are "subjects"). Action is **planetside**: the disputed border region between **Baron Halley** (current holder) and **Baron Fero**, the fortified town of **Ellaton**, and the neutral **Starport** + startown.
- **Threat/level:** Low-to-mid, **personal/ground scale**, not space combat. Enemies are TL5 **militia** (Rifle 3D), TL9 **paramilitary guards** (Laser Carbine 4D, Flak +5), and lightly-armoured ground cars. A crew with a **grav vehicle** dominates — the barons' commanders only know how to skirmish with militia, not modern battlespace mobility. Lethal only if the crew are caught red-handed by a security crackdown.
- **Est. length:** Medium and **open-ended/sandbox** — a slow-burn intrigue measured in weeks of escalating unrest, not a fixed clock. As long as the crew keep stirring, it keeps escalating. 2–4 sessions.
- **Prerequisites:** Crew passing through Cordan or the Borderland route with a ship for short hops; willingness to do **deniable dirty work** (assassination, sabotage, agitation). No prior adventure required; pairs naturally with other Borderland-route content (Acrid revolt, Tech-World).
- **Arc slot:** Standalone **patron job / sandbox war** offered by a planetary noble. A foothold-building opportunity (the reward is a **minor noble estate on Cordan**) and a chance to put a Borderland world in the crew's debt — but it is morally grey and the Imperium is watching the route.

## Player-Facing (the hook)
*Safe to narrate.*

- **The cargo run (the in):** Passing through Cordan (or any nearby world), the crew are approached by **a representative of Baroness Lux**. She offers a trivial first job: carry a small cargo of **high-value medical equipment** to a landing site near the baronial capital, for a **slightly-better-than-reasonable fee.** Security and discretion are stressed.
- **Dinner with the Baroness:** If the crew perform adequately, they are invited to **dine at Lux's fortified residence** (a walled compound inside a restricted "citizen" zone; ordinary subjects live contentedly enough beyond the walls). Lux is gracious, formidable, and **extremely popular** — she personally led a campaign to drive Aslan *ihatei* off her holdings, and has gifted her subjects **TL9 medical gear and machinery** (their normal access is only TL5).
- **The presenting problem (her pitch):** Cordan once had more barons; *ihatei*, bad luck and feuding have whittled them to **three**. Lux claims her two rivals — **Baron Fero** and **Baron Halley** — are **colluding to destroy her.** She wants the crew to **prevent this** by quietly causing "internal problems" for the rivals: foment dissent, sabotage their military assets, and ideally **get the two barons fighting each other.** She will consider the job a success if either or both is forced to deal with an uprising or rebel activity; a **resounding** success if they are induced to fight one another.
- **The carrot:** Help her and the crew may use her **private landing field** at will, and — more importantly — she will help them **reclaim an old baronial hall** once held by a defunct minor rival and set up a small estate there. **They become minor nobles on Cordan with their own holdings.**
- **Worth noticing (player-facing):** Cordan is officially a "representative democracy," which is a creative fiction — the barons control all high-tech manufacturing and the world parliament is hamstrung by their rivalries. The **Imperium** runs ships through the Starport regularly and **does not care how the world is run so long as the port stays open**, but is widely assumed to act against anyone upsetting the status quo. Direct mercenary action or overt war would invite Imperial intervention — hence "deniable assets."

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned.*

- **Lux is the aggressor, not the victim.** There is **no plan by Fero and Halley to gang up on Lux** (it could happen someday, but it is not the imminent threat she describes). Lux is **lying** to recruit the crew. Her real goal: spend the **bought popularity** she cannot sustain before it evaporates, by **destabilising her rivals** and seizing the advantage. She would be satisfied reducing their power; ideally she overthrows one or both.
- **The bribery is a ticking clock for HER.** Lux's TL9 largesse is **not affordable long-term** — when the gifted equipment breaks down or the people expect another round, her popularity will collapse. She has bought "a few years" and must act **now.** This is the engine of her urgency, not any rival aggression.
- **She wants a quagmire, not a victory.** Simply attacking is a losing proposition (the balance is too close). Lux would be delighted to make the two rivals **fight one another**, but is **equally happy to create an unwinnable mire neither can afford to withdraw from** — the longer it grinds, the more it costs them and the safer she becomes. **Total victory by either side is NOT desirable to her** — she wants a *shifted* balance with herself dominant, not a polarised two-baron world.
- **Halley's secret (the real volatility):** Baron **Halley has been receiving support from the Zhodani Consulate** for some time. The **Imperium cannot officially notice** this (it would create a diplomatic incident they refuse to confront), so instead they have quietly **imposed economic sanctions on Halley** under invented pretexts — explaining his **down trade income despite steady exports** (the players may deduce "a tariff or a lower price," and that he upset the Imperium). The situation is genuinely volatile, **but not the way Lux presents it.** Halley's paramilitary guards were **trained by Zhodani advisors** and are notably better than other barons' troops.
- **Two-tier truth on the ground:** Cordan's society is **citizens** (a few thousand baronial-household members, holding all high tech) over a far larger mass of low-tech **subjects.** The barons have genuine popular support *and* genuine dissent — none can alienate too many subjects, which is exactly the lever the crew will pull.
- **The conflict will likely happen anyway.** Even without the crew, *some* incident is probable. The referee should let the crew **be the instigators** if they try; if they don't, events unfold on their own and the crew must **react.** (Default spark if the crew sit idle: an **unauthorised raid by Fero's militia** to protect cross-border sympathisers, which starts gunfire and pulls Halley's household troops in.)
- **Lux's hidden end-game twist (Free-for-All):** Once the war tips toward one baron *winning*, Lux **abandons her "peacemaker" pose**, claims she is "restoring order and protecting her borders," and **sends her own militia into the war zone.** This **over-reaches** — both rivals resist her incursion, and the **strongest, most unified baron is suddenly drawn into bitter fighting and a perilous position.** She then **turns to the crew again** to engineer a **quick end** that lets her keep at least some gains — which **cannot become public knowledge** (that she started it). Honouring her bargain (the estate) is contingent on the crew **ending the war they began.**
- **"Other consequences not yet apparent":** the source flags that Lux's workable-but-dishonest plan may have **further fallout** — left open for the GM (Imperial scrutiny if the Zhodani angle surfaces; a destabilised garden world on the Imperium–Hierate route; a baron toppled by an outsider crew making enemies). Seed as Threads.

## Scenes / Locations (the conflict's phases)
*This adventure is a **phased sandbox war**, not a linear dungeon. The phases below escalate in order, but the crew choose tools and pace. There are no required dice tables in the source; resolve with task checks and run unscripted beats with mythic-gm oracle/Adventure-Crafter as noted.*

### 1. Hiring On (the cargo run + the dinner)
**Trigger:** Crew pass through Cordan or a nearby world; Lux's representative approaches with the medical-cargo job. → **What's here:** Deliver TL9 medical equipment to a landing site near the baronial capital (security/discretion stressed); perform adequately → invitation to **dine at Lux's fortified residence.** Over dinner she delivers the **player-facing pitch** (rivals colluding; foment trouble; reward = estate + landing field). → **Complications:** The crew may notice the "representative democracy" is a sham and that Lux's claim is thin; she **waves away any queries** ("the situation is very complex"). Accepting binds them to deniable work. → **mythic-gm:** If the crew probe Lux's story, run a **Fate Question** (likelihood **Unlikely** that her "collusion" claim is literally true) — a *Yes* should still come out garbled/exaggerated.

### 2. The Assassination — Josephus Agraaki
**Trigger:** Crew choose to spark the war (Lux's preferred opening). The designated incident is the **assassination of a highly unpopular official in the disputed region** (currently under **Baron Halley**). → **The target: Josephus Agraaki**, a corrupt taxation-assessment supervisor — notorious extortionist, tolerated by Halley because he is efficient and holds **blackmail on the baron's household (maybe the baron himself).** He **need not actually die** for the plan to work; the *attempt* triggers the chain. → **The setup:** Agraaki tours his region "assessing" taxes, **publishes an itinerary he almost never follows** (he turns up unexpectedly), and has grown **sloppy and complacent** from easy past incidents. Tours last 2–3 weeks, then he "lurks in his lair counting his money" for a few weeks. → **His detail:** a **driver per vehicle, four bodyguards, two attendants** (attendants dress identically to Agraaki and the guards — in the chaos it is **hard to tell who is who** in the seconds it takes to bundle him into a car). Travels in **two identical lightly-armoured ground cars**; each car carries a **shotgun + a submachinegun**; standard drill leaves **two guards with the drivers and the heavy weapons** while Agraaki + two guards + attendants do business. → **Tactics:** Try to hit him **in a small town** on his tour, or **ambush on the road** (his driver bolts while the other car covers; Agraaki **sacrifices his guards** to escape and will not fight it out). Guards are loyal but **not fanatical** — wounded/abandoned ones flee or surrender once Agraaki is away or they're clearly overmatched. → **Complications:** Even an **unsuccessful** attempt triggers Scene 3. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** the attempt **always escalates** — whether Agraaki lives or dies, proceed to The Response. (If resolving "did the shot land," roll the relevant Gun Combat task; success or failure both advance the plot.)

### 3. The Response (Halley's crackdown)
**Trigger:** Any attempt on Agraaki (success or failure). → **What's here:** Halley **cannot tolerate** an attack on his officials even though he dislikes Agraaki, and deploys his **TL9 paramilitary guards** — mostly in **lightly-armoured wheeled patrol vehicles** (Parthian Wheeled Scout / Socrates Field Car) with **a handful of air/rafts mounting machineguns** for fast-reaction roadblocks and escape-route cutoffs. → **Tone — robust but NOT trigger-happy:** Halley's force was **Zhodani-trained**, higher-quality, and understands the need not to alienate the populace. Response sits **between a police and a military operation**: cordon the area, investigators question inhabitants, detachments search for anyone armed. → **The crew's exposure:** They may **talk their way out** if they have a plausible reason to be present — but if **caught with weapons** (especially matching the attack), bluff fails. Local **small-town sheriffs** dislike Agraaki and won't all hunt diligently. The crackdown **further alienates locals** and **raises tensions with Fero** (who has many supporters there and wants an excuse to "rescue" them). → **Complications:** Public sympathy for the attackers flares (loud "better off without him" talk); some loud-talkers are **arrested**, sparking **violence as locals resist the arrests** — an **ideal moment for rabble-rousing.** → **mythic-gm:** run searches/checkpoints as opposed **Deception/Stealth** vs. the guards; a single failed roll while armed-and-matching = detained → improvise an escape or a frame.

### 4. Border Clashes — the Ellaton flashpoint
**Trigger:** Tensions from Scene 3 boil over (or, if the crew sit idle, the **default spark**: Fero's militia raids across the border to protect sympathisers, gunfire is exchanged, Halley calls up his militia). → **The first stage is low-intensity:** skirmishes between **poorly-led militia** (TL5 small arms), household troops dashing between fights as a reserve, minimal casualties, no territory changing hands — "some even find it rather good fun." → **The key terrain — Ellaton:** a fortified town (earth embankments, concrete bunkers, **larger-than-average militia + a couple of artillery pieces**) lying **just inside Halley's territory** but **geographically logical to Fero** (in the crook of a river; far easier to enter from Fero's side than across the **long narrow bridge** from Halley's land). Disputed for years. → **Why it matters:** Halley's only resupply is **over the bridge or by scarce grav vehicles**; he **cannot afford to lose Ellaton** (its loss would trigger **uprisings in other pro-Fero areas**, and the militia might not resist a rising) **— but winning at high cost is also disastrous.** Fero has moved **artillery** up, committed a big slice of his force, and thinks **siege** (surround the landward side, interdict the bridge with shellfire); he is **not expecting a counterattack** and can be **surprised**. → **The crew's openings:** A small **grav-mobile force** can destroy Fero's poorly-guarded **artillery/ammunition**, tipping the local balance to Halley and forcing him to over-commit (a battle he could lose) — **or** engineer a setback for whichever side is winning, per Lux's wish to keep it balanced. → **mythic-gm:** track Ellaton's tilt with a simple swing meter; resolve crew raids as task chains (Recon → Stealth → Explosives/Sabotage). Use an **Adventure-Crafter "turning point"** if the front needs a surprise.

### 5. Escalation — the toolkit (turn skirmish into all-out war)
**Trigger:** Lux is keen to escalate and has **sent other agents** to create incidents; the crew can push it to **all-out war between Halley and Fero** by striking the right spot. Pick any mix: → **What's here (escalation tools):**
- **Assassination of key figures.** Barons/immediate households are too well-guarded; **regional governors and militia commanders** are vulnerable and killing them throws local forces into disarray or disheartens militia.
- **Infiltration / mobility.** A team with a **grav vehicle** can ambush in unexpected spots or strike lightly-defended areas from directions the barons' skirmish-trained commanders never consider.
- **Surprise attacks behind the lines.** Artillery positions, **headquarters**, and **supply lines/convoys/dumps** are vulnerable to a sudden grav strike — forcing the barons to pull troops back (necessary but ineffective; the militia bungle it, causing extra casualties or defensive gaps).
- **Rabble-rousing (the subtlest & most effective).** Engineer situations that make a baron **look incompetent or uncaring**, or simply spread rumours; convince a baron's followers he doesn't care and they **resist or defect.** Lux hopes the crew stir **several small rebellions**, diluting both rivals.
- **At the Starport.** During the conflict the port is **closed to ground traffic** but ships come and go normally. The port workforce/startown are **independent of the barons** (supporters of each present). Opportunities: **smuggle weapons** out through the closed frontiers to a faction; **propaganda war**; intervene in **small rival-supporter clashes** (quickly suppressed by **port security**). A starship may **freely "bounce"** between a baron's landing field and the port (no interference), though the **port authority pressures crews not to get involved.**
- **The Imperial trade delegation** at the port has **no real power** to stop the war — a handful of guards, only enough to protect its own assets. It makes threats of intervention/sanctions, **all but meaningless** against the immediate threat each baron faces. (Each baron blames the other and claims willingness to stop "if the other does.") → **mythic-gm:** each escalation deed = a task check whose Effect feeds an abstract "war intensity" gauge; on a strong success, advance the war one phase toward Free-for-All (Scene 6).

### 6. Free-for-All — the balance tips & Lux over-reaches
**Trigger:** Enough setbacks, destroyed munitions and rebel areas **spread one baron's forces too thin**; a front collapses and the militia grabs border towns; a counterattack fails and morale breaks. Areas start switching sides — **it looks like one baron has won.** → **The twist:** This polarised outcome is **NOT what Lux wants.** So she **drops the peacemaker mask**: claiming she is "restoring order, protecting her borders, and relieving those harmed by the conflict," she **orders her own militia into the war zone.** At first it goes well (grateful people accept protection) — **but both barons resist her incursion**, and **Lux is rapidly drawn into bitter fighting and over-extends.** Though she is the **strongest and most unified** faction, she is now in a **perilous position.** → **What's here:** Lux **turns to the crew once more** — she needs a **quick end** that lets her **keep at least some gains**, with her instigation **never made public.** Honouring the estate deal is contingent on the crew delivering this. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** when "war intensity" maxes (one baron clearly ascendant), **force the Lux-incursion beat** — she commits and over-reaches regardless of crew preference; then re-open negotiations with the crew for an ending.

### 7. Ending the War (the resolution — Lux's terms)
**Trigger:** Crew accept Lux's second commission (end it favourably and quietly). **How is up to them.** Outcomes:
- **Peace conference (best crew option).** Persuading the barons to attend is **hard**; the crew, as **outsiders**, have a better shot than any local faction. Best lever: talk the **Imperial trade delegation** into **hosting** the conference and **guaranteeing attendees' safety.** **Outcome not assured.**
- **Alliance with one baron** to defeat the other — but **total victory is undesirable** to Lux (she wants a shifted balance, not a polarised world; spoils would be hard to divide).
- **Individual surrender / junior-partner alliance** suits Lux best — more palatable if **couched as ceding territory to "rationalise the border"** (e.g. the river line) **or per the populace's wishes.** Putting a reasonable deal to **Fero and Halley** and convincing them to accept is **left to the crew.**
- **Continued conflict = failure.** It is **in no one's interest**; **Lux will NOT reward the crew** if the war simply grinds on. It eventually winds down with **no clear victor**, all parties exhausted, **Cordan grimmer**, simmering border violence — and **the crew achieve nothing** (no estate). → **mythic-gm:** resolve the conference/surrender as a high-stakes social task chain (Diplomat/Persuade/Advocate, with Effect setting how much Lux gains and whether both barons sign). A botched ending collapses to the "continued conflict" no-reward state.

## Key NPCs
*Cordan stat blocks are sparse in the source; the three barons are **described, not statted** — run as capable noble leaders (high SOC/EDU, Admin/Leadership/Tactics; not front-line combatants). Only Agraaki and his troops are statted.*

**Baroness Lux (the patron; aggressor-in-disguise)** — *(stats not given; run as high-SOC, shrewd noble leader, not a combatant.)* Wildly popular (drove off *ihatei*; bribed subjects with unsustainable TL9 gifts); strongest and most unified of the three barons. **Lies** about the rivals colluding; truly wants to **destabilise** them and dominate Cordan before her bought goodwill collapses. Prefers a quagmire; over-reaches in the Free-for-All. Motive: secure lasting dominance **cheaply and deniably** — and **never be exposed** as the instigator.

**Baron Fero (rival; the opportunist)** — *(stats not given.)* **Largest population base** (manpower advantage), **moderately popular**, his barony **politically and economically stable.** Has many sympathisers in the disputed region and **wants an excuse to "rescue" them** from Halley. In a conflict he thinks **siege**, commits artillery, can **retreat any time** but senses a real chance to harm his rival and **will stake a lot.** Motive: expand at Halley's expense; absorb cross-border sympathisers.

**Baron Halley (rival; the sanctioned man)** — *(stats not given.)* **Best-fortified hall** ("his castle"), **most TL9 manufacturing**, **slightly larger personal guard than Lux**, **Zhodani-trained** (higher-quality) paramilitaries. Secretly **backed by the Zhodani Consulate**; secretly **sanctioned by the Imperium** for it (down trade income despite steady exports). **Cannot afford to lose Ellaton** but also cannot afford a costly win. Motive: hold his territory and his secret patron; survive Imperial pressure. **GM-only: the Zhodani link is the adventure's buried geopolitical bomb.**

**Josephus Agraaki (corrupt tax supervisor; the assassination target)** — Human male. STR 6 DEX 8 END 7 INT 6 EDU 7 SOC 8. Skills: Admin 2, Advocate 1, Carouse 1, Deception 1, Diplomat 1, Electronics (computers) 1, Gun Combat 0, Persuade 1. Weapon: **Autopistol (3D−3).** Notorious extortionist; holds blackmail over Halley's household; complacent and sloppy; **will sacrifice his guards to escape** and won't fight it out. Need not die for the plan to work.

**Bodyguard (×4 + 2 attendants dressed identically; Agraaki's detail)** — Human. STR 8 DEX 8 END 9 INT 7 EDU 6 SOC 6. Skills: Athletics 0, Drive (wheel) 1, Electronics 0, Gun Combat (slug) 2, Melee 0, Recon 0. Weapons: **Autopistol (3D−3)**, plus **Shotgun (4D, Bulky)** or **Submachinegun (3D, Auto 3)** (heavy weapons kept in the cars). Loyal but **not fanatical** — flee/surrender once Agraaki is clear or they're overmatched.

**Paramilitary Guard (Halley's TL9 response force; Zhodani-trained)** — Human. STR 8 DEX 9 END 8 INT 7 EDU 7 SOC 7. Skills: Athletics 0, Drive (wheel) 1, Electronics 0, Flyer 0, Gun Combat (energy) 2, Heavy Weapons (man portable) 1, Melee (blade or bludgeon) 1, Recon 1, Survival 1. Armour: **Flak Jacket (+5).** Weapons: **Laser Carbine (4D)** or **RAM Grenade Launcher (frag, 5D, Blast 9).** Robust but disciplined — willing to shoot, but avoids alienating the populace.

**Militia (both barons' border levies; the cannon fodder)** — Human (subjects). STR 7 DEX 7 END 7 INT 7 EDU 5 SOC 5. Skills: Carouse 0, Drive 0, Gun Combat 0, Melee 0, Profession (various) 1, Streetwise 0, Survival 0. Weapon: **Rifle (3D)** (TL5 small arms). **Inexperienced, poorly led, nervous** — "a disaster waiting to happen." Easily panicked, easily roused, easily turned by rabble-rousing.

## Vehicles & Forces
**Agraaki's Armoured Car (×2 identical)** — TL9 wheeled ground car. **Armour: Front/Rear/Sides 15; Hull 15.** Skill Drive (wheel); Agility +0; Speed Cruise High (Medium); Range 500 (750) km; Crew 1, Passengers 3; Shipping 3.5 tons; Cost Cr25,300. Traits/equipment: Autopilot (basic), Communications System (improved, 500 km), Computer/1, Entertainment System, Fire Extinguishers, Life Support (short term), Navigation System (basic, Nav DM +1), Wet Bar, Sensors. **Onboard weapons: a Shotgun + a Submachinegun per car.** A comfortable VIP transport — **proof against small-arms fire, little protection vs. heavy weapons**; meant to shield VIPs from unruly crowds or low-tech foes, not modern militaries; life support lets it run in varied atmospheres.

**Halley's patrol vehicles** — **lightly-armoured wheeled patrol cars** (Parthian Wheeled Scout, *Vehicle Handbook* p.89; or Socrates Field Car, p.111) + **a handful of air/rafts mounting light support weapons (machineguns)** for fast-reaction roadblocks and escape-route cutoffs.

**Crew grav vehicle (the decisive asset, if they have one)** — Any air/raft or grav vehicle gives the crew **operational mobility the barons' commanders cannot counter** (they only know militia skirmishing). Enables behind-the-lines raids on artillery, HQs, supply dumps, and unexpected ambushes — the single biggest force-multiplier in the adventure.

*(No starship combat. Ships are used only to "bounce" between a baron's landing field and the Starport — unhindered, though the port authority applies pressure to stay out of the war.)*

## Rewards & Consequences
- **The promised reward (success):** A **minor noble estate on Cordan** — the **old baronial hall of a defunct rival**, with **Lux's help setting it up** — making the crew **landed minor nobles** with their own holdings, plus **free use of Lux's private landing field** at any time. *Contingent on **ending** the war favourably and quietly per Scene 7.* A standing **foothold on a Starport-A garden world** on the Imperium–Hierate route.
- **Pay & access along the way:** the initial **better-than-reasonable** medical-cargo fee; the landing field becomes available once Lux trusts the crew.
- **Possible spoils of agitation:** looted munitions, captured gear, and **favours owed** by factions/towns the crew aid — and the gratitude (or fear) of whichever side they backed.
- **Costs of failure / violence (be honest):**
  - **Continued conflict = no reward at all.** If the war merely grinds to an exhausted stalemate, **Lux will not pay or grant the estate**; the crew "achieve nothing," and Cordan is left grimmer with simmering border violence.
  - **Caught red-handed:** being taken **with weapons matching an attack** during Halley's crackdown defeats any bluff → arrest, trial, and likely the loss of the job and the crew's cover. Local sheriffs are lax, but the Zhodani-trained guards are competent.
  - **Backing total victory** for one baron **displeases Lux** (she wants balance, not a polarised world) — she may **withhold or shrink the reward.**
  - **Exposure of Lux's hand** (that the crew started it for her) would be **catastrophic for her** and turn her against the crew — she needs it secret. Loose ends (Agraaki survivors, captured agents, Imperial inquiry) threaten this.
  - **The Zhodani/Imperial powder keg:** if the crew **surface Halley's Zhodani backing**, they may draw **Imperial intelligence attention** to themselves — a dangerous thread on a route the Imperium watches.

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(Apply via `empire-reputation.md` "Per-adventure effects." Cordan = **0401 Borderland, A895347-9, Ni Lo, Starport A, TL9; starting Attitude Suspicious, FS 1** per Appendix 2.)*
- **Cordan's Attitude toward the crew can rise — slowly and personally.** Successfully ending the war and **becoming landed nobles** plants the crew **inside** Cordan's power structure. Treat a clean success as grounds to move Cordan **+1 step toward Haven** (Suspicious → Neutral) for the crew, via the **Diplomacy/aid + becoming a resident power** driver — record the cause (Lux's patronage). A botched job or exposed instigation **holds Cordan at Suspicious** (or **−1 step** if the crew are blamed for the chaos).
- **A baronial-faction foothold (Factions, Companion ch.3).** The estate + Lux's favour is effectively a **friendly Asset/Faction on Cordan** (a sympathetic noble household), giving **DM+** to later Attitude-shift / recruitment attempts on Cordan — *supporting* a future recruitment, not replacing Traveller diplomacy. Toppling a baron and installing/strengthening a friendly one is the strongest long-term lever.
- **Recruitment outlook:** Cordan is an **independent garden world** with **FS 1** (a *world's* committed strength, small). It is **not** flagged Profile-XXX (no "no-negotiation" marker in the index) — so it **can** eventually be courted into the reborn Kingdom, but starts **Suspicious (−2 DM)** and is **Imperially watched.** This adventure is the natural **first step** toward that.
- **The Imperium watches the route.** Cordan sits on the **Tobia→Cordan/Acrid→Tech-World→Byrni** Imperial–Hierate corridor; the Imperium "would act against anyone upsetting the status quo." **Overt** crew action (open mercenary war, a distinctive Harrier loitering, an atrocity that draws an inquiry) risks **Imperial Standing/PRI** hits and route patrols — resolve per `piracy-raiding.md`. Deniability is the whole point; keep it covert.
- **No Lost World is created here** (no world is destroyed). A toppled barony is an **internal regime change**, not a Kingdom loss — but record which baron fell and who now holds the disputed region (it colours future Cordan politics).
- **Great-power threads (record, don't auto-apply):** **Halley's Zhodani backing** and the **Imperium's covert sanctions** are live geopolitical facts the crew may exploit or expose — potential Standing swings with **Zhodani** (if they aid/betray Halley) or **Imperial** intelligence (if they hand over the Zhodani angle). Seed onto the empire tracker as **pending** rather than fixed shifts.
