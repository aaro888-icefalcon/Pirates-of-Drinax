# Lions of Thebus

> Use this when: a minor Aslan lord (Ftahkaiw) hires the crew to quietly rescue his downed *ihatei* son (Ftahteas) from the lawless surface of **Thebus**. A wilderness-rescue + social-honour scenario, not a fight. Cross-ref `setting/worlds/key-worlds.md` (THEBUS 0309 Sindal), `setting/factions.md` (Aslan honour, *ihatei*, Reaction), `setting/ships-of-the-reach.md` (Aoa'iw light trader), `campaign/empire-reputation.md` (Hierate Standing, Attitude). *Listed in key-worlds.md under the working title "Hunting Party" — same adventure.*

**Logline:** An Aslan lord's second son crash-landed on a bombed-out wilderness world famed for hunting big cats that look like Aslan — and now the proud, oxygen-starved survivor would rather die with honour than be rescued by humans, while a racist hunter who has already murdered one of his people is angling to make it two.

## At a glance
- **Where:** **Thebus (0309 Sindal) B534320-7** — Po Ni Lo. Three layers: **Thebus Highport** (busy, ignores the planet); the **one settlement** (~8,000, lion-safari trade); the **wilderness** NE of the town — old bombed Sindalian cities, forest, the **crash site**, and Ftahteas's ruin-court ("Kings of the Ruins"). Hook may be received near Thebus or anywhere along the Aslan/human border.
- **Threat:** **Low-to-moderate combat**, **high social/environmental.** The real dangers are the **thin atmosphere** (altitude sickness — confusion, slowed reactions, delirium, sometimes lethal), getting the Aslan to *accept* rescue without triggering an honour-bloodbath, and a sniper-armed hunting party. Thebun Lions are mostly harmless. No big-ship combat unless the crew start the war over again.
- **Length:** Short–medium. One patron, one world, one overland trek and confrontation. Easily run in a few sessions.
- **Prerequisites:** None hard. The crew need a ship and a willingness to ferry Aslan. An **Aslan PC/ally or decent Diplomat/Persuade** makes the honour endgame far survivable. Hierate Standing is irrelevant to the patron (he's a freelancer), but matters for how the crew read Aslan etiquette.
- **Arc slot:** Standalone Sindal-subsector patron job — good filler/income while operating the Sindalian Main. Sits naturally alongside other Sindal jobs (Liberty Port at Sperle, Sea Gods of Marduk). Seeds the "who shot the ship down?" mystery forward.

## Player-Facing (the hook / patron)
*Safe to narrate.*

- While near Thebus, the crew receive something between a **summons and an invitation** to attend **Lord Ftahkaiw** and hear a "potentially lucrative assignment." The tone is condescending and off-putting, but: Ftahkaiw entertains people of influence (useful contacts), and offers **"trade credit"** just to attend and listen.
- **Ftahkaiw** is a minor male Aslan noble who behaves as if he owns the universe. He works the human/Aslan border from a **yacht** escorted by one or two small warships; ashore he takes over the best hotel and holds court (well-dressed humans drinking wine, mostly there to make him look busy). Armed guards, large exclusion zone around the conference suite.
- **The brief he gives:** his second son **Ftahteas** and a band of *ihatei* set out to win a fortune and lands of their own, but met "treachery or disaster." Their ship was **downed on Thebus** — the world "famous for its lion-hunts." Ftahkaiw is openly contemptuous of lion-hunting (he knows the Thebun Lion is far less dangerous than the safari pitch, and resents the racist overtones of hunting something that looks Aslan). **Mission:** go to Thebus, **locate survivors — especially Ftahteas — and rescue them.** Keep it **low-key**; he does not want it known his son needed non-Aslan help to escape his own folly. If the **ship can be salvaged**, bonus.
- **Payment (player-facing):** **Cr50,000** for a credible rescue attempt; **doubled** if Ftahteas is brought back **alive and well**; **doubled again** if the **ihatei ship can be saved** (i.e. up to **Cr200,000**). Plus Ftahkaiw's "extremely valuable" goodwill/influence with other Aslan, and **trade credit**: a **5% discount/premium on trade with Aslan clients up to Cr10,000 total benefit** (i.e. a 5% break that expires once it has saved/earned the crew Cr10,000).
- **Thebus, openly perceivable:** **Thebus Highport** is large and busy but cares only about Sindalian-Main trade; **few ships go to the surface.** On arrival the crew are contacted and told the rules: **dock and refuel at the port, pass a token spaceworthiness check, enjoy "party season."** They learn an **Aslan vessel recently went straight to the planet surface** and was never tracked leaving — its **rough landing location** can be had by **bribery/hacking/charming port staff.**

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned in play.*

- **Ftahkaiw's mixed motives (subtext, not stated):** he is at once an anxious father, a haughty lord who fears his clan/the Hierate may suffer, and a **minor leader who knows he'll be the scapegoat** if the incident embarrasses Aslan generally. His priority order is literally: **save the son → avoid embarrassment for himself/clan → spare the Hierate** (in that order). His offered goodwill/"pull" is **largely self-flattering empty promise** — he believes his own propaganda and has **far less influence than he claims.** The trade credit is real but worth less than he thinks.
- **The biggest obstacle is Ftahteas himself.** He is honour-bound to **die rather than bring shame on his father or clan**, and the idea of being rescued *by humans* — and of thanking the father who sent them — is galling (he may even read the *choice* of rescuers as an insult). He is also suffering **weeks of untreated altitude sickness** (headache, irritability, confusion) on top of grief and pride, so he is volatile and may **misread the crew as enemies.** He genuinely **does not want to die** and knows he must get his people offworld — he is **looking for a reason he can accept** to take rescue without dishonour. The crew must *give him that reason* (respect, a face-saving framing) or he will dig in and die with stubborn dignity — possibly attacking the crew first.
- **The crash was no accident.** Inspection of the wreck by someone with **naval/shipboard engineering experience** can reveal the **drive section was hit by a missile** — not conclusive (the damage is catastrophic), but the most plausible read. If true, the strike happened **while the ship flew low over the surface**, which means **whoever fired knew it was an Aslan ship and chose to.** **There is no in-adventure way to learn who or why** — this is a deliberately open thread (see Threads). Candidates: rival *ihatei*, pirates, someone else operating on Thebus, or "another ship."
- **The murdered hunter / the racist killer.** A second human party has been **shadowing the *ihatei*** (their boot-tracks parallel the Aslan trail). **One of Ftahteas's hunters was shot dead yesterday "by persons unknown."** Truth: it was **this hunting party — and specifically one member who came to Thebus to kill something that looks like an Aslan and got the real thing.** He is **both delighted and slightly horrified**, and is **passing it off as a Thebun Lion kill** (the body crawled off and was later recovered by the Aslan, so he thinks there's no proof). The rest of his party are **ordinary rich thrill-seekers who genuinely don't believe an Aslan was murdered** and will resist any Aslan attempt at vengeance/justice as "Aslan troublemaking." **The murderer will try to engineer a "self-defence against Aslan aggression" incident** — e.g. by "mistaking" another Aslan for a lion — to get his party to fight the Aslan for him.
- **The safari swindle (atmosphere is the real killer).** Guides massively **overplay** radiation, mutagens, poison, plagues and lion ferocity to justify their fees, and **quietly omit** the one genuine lethal hazard: the **very thin atmosphere.** If the crew hire a guide and *don't* ask about breathing gear, the guide may "forget" to mention compressor masks/oxygen — a cynical ploy that **has killed clients before.** Detectors confirm hazard levels are **mostly extremely low**, with a few real low/moderate spots. The crash region is genuinely **slightly hotter** (background radiation up, occasional toxin alerts) from concentrated old chemical weapons — nothing serious, but atmospheric flavor that the crew can mistake for danger.

## Scenes / Locations
Sequence: **Hiring On** (Ftahkaiw's court) → **Arrival / Highport** (the planetside-access trap + finding the landing site) → **Planetside** (settlement or direct landing) → **Overland** (the trek north) → **Kings of the Ruins** (Ftahteas's court) → **Confrontations** (the honour endgame + the hunters) → **Extraction**.

### 1. Hiring On — Ftahkaiw's Court
- **Trigger:** Crew accept the summons while near Thebus.
- **What's here:** Pretentious court, armed guards, exclusion zone, filler-guests. Once announced, the crew approach and hear the brief and the **Cr50k / ×2 / ×2** terms + trade credit. **Useful-contact potential** among the genuine business guests.
- **Complications:** Ftahkaiw is insulting by default; **botching his etiquette** sours the deal or the future goodwill. He **will not** volunteer his scapegoat-fears or that the offered "pull" is hollow.

### 2. Arrival & Thebus Highport — the Planetside Trap
- **Trigger:** Crew jump into Thebus.
- **What's here:** Port hails them, states the rules, runs a **5-minute token spaceworthiness check** (look at the maintenance log + a walkaround). "Party season" is on (tone varies by what big ship is in dock). The port is **well-defended** (missile + beam weapons) but has **no real flotilla** — only shuttles and a couple of repair/rescue tugs.
- **The leverage rule (key constraint):** the **Thebus Highport Corporation** lets ships go straight to the surface, **but any ship that does so is barred from re-docking at the highport.** This is commercial pressure dressed as "customs/legality checks." So the crew choose: **shuttle down** (keep highport access) or **take their own ship down** (lose re-docking — they'll have to leave the system or work around it later when extracting the Aslan).
- **Finding the landing site:** it's known an Aslan ship **went straight to the surface and was never tracked leaving** (presumed gone; actually still down). Get its **rough landing location** via **bribery, hacking, or socially working port staff.** (Adjudicate as a single info-gather: Streetwise/Persuade/Electronics/an appropriate bribe — `task-system.md`.)
- **Complications:** Carrying **weapons is banned on the highport**, but moving arms down via shuttle is routine — even absurd loads (a 30mm autocannon in the shuttle hold) go through **if the bribe is big enough.** The locals will indulge any offworld idiocy for credits.

### 3. Planetside on Thebus — Settlement or Direct Landing
- **Trigger:** Crew descend (shuttle to the town, or their own ship to the surface).
- **What's here:** One **town of ~8,000** + outlying farms. At both ends of any shuttle trip the crew are **hammered with safari pitches** (photograph / shoot / "hunt something that looks just like an Aslan" — the pitch flexes to the crew's apparent attitude). **No enforceable law outside the settlement** → no limits on what weaponry the crew bring into the wild. Guides are "highly recommended" (for the overplayed hazards) and **omit the atmosphere warning** unless asked.
- **Local intel:** townsfolk confirm **no Aslan ship has landed at the settlement in over a year**, but **a ship passed low overhead a few weeks ago, heading roughly north-east** into a once-densely-populated, well-watered (now forested) region — a natural city-site, therefore a bombardment target, and "the sort of place *ihatei* might try to claim." Cue another round of guide-fearmongering.
- **Complications — landing your own ship:** good landing sites are **hard to find**; the scrubby dryland hides **crumbly soil and gulleys.** Landing anywhere unscouted risks the ship **tipping as a foot sinks** — **minor breakages and the occasional struggle to free a landing foot** (major components unlikely to be disabled). Adjudicate as a Pilot/Engineer check on an unscouted set-down.

### 4. Overland on Thebus — the Trek North
- **Trigger:** Crew head NE/N from the settlement (or from their landing) toward the crash region.
- **What's here:** Dry, harsh-glare environment; brown spiny vegetation, **clinging tendrils that snag boots (constant tripping hazard)**, mostly knee-high scrub, taller and greener near water — the target region is effectively **forest.** **Atmosphere is the real threat:** unacclimatised characters get short of breath, then **tire/confuse/suffer delirium** (use compressor masks/oxygen, or acclimatise over weeks). Detectors read **mostly very low** threat with a few genuine low/moderate spots; guides theatrically "manage" fake threat-zones.
- **Thebun Lions:** the crew may sight them — they **do resemble Aslan**, travel on all fours, rear up to look around/reach prey, and **slink away from humans.** They **only attack if threatened** (e.g. a group with un-movable young). The "sneaky ambush predator" story is **safari hype.** *(See Key NPCs for the stat block. **Beware mistaken identity:** an Aslan at a distance, not responding to a hail, can get shot by a trigger-happy hunter — and so can a real lion that the crew assume is hostile.)*
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (must-happen clue — the parallel trail):** As the crew track the *ihatei* north, **someone has been shadowing the same trail** (human-sized boot-prints crossing/paralleling it, with periodic checks; the makers are never seen). To time the reveal, on each travel-leg with a tracker (**Survival or Recon**) roll `dice.py keyed 1d10 7+` to notice the second trail this leg; on a miss the clue surfaces by the next ruin-marker regardless (it **must** land before Scene 6 so the hunters aren't a cold surprise). Background radiation/toxin alerts tick up here (atmosphere, not plot). Old wall-mounds and ridges appear → the crew realise they're **walking through a dead city** (open blast-craters = nuclear ground-zero, low residual radiation now, but sobering).

### 5. Kings of the Ruins — the Crash Site & Ftahteas's Court
- **Trigger:** Crew reach the wreck and/or push on to the survivors' camp.
- **The wreck (Aoa'iw-class light trader):** easily found from orbit, or by **residual powerplant emissions** on the ground; it lies at the end of a **long furrow ploughed through the forest**, having **crash-landed and shed pieces** along the path. The **tumbling drive section** went furthest and lies **upside-down** in broken trees; it smashed most of the ship and **killed most who'd survived that far.** Survivor signs: **internal wreckage cut away** (bodies retrieved), **no corpses aboard**, and a **line of graves** nearby — someone buried their dead, stripped salvage, and **moved off roughly north.** Track them: a handful of survivors.
  - **The missile clue (gated):** **naval/shipboard engineering** inspection may reveal the **drive area was missile-struck** — not conclusive (overwhelming damage), but the best explanation, implying a **low-altitude attack by someone who knew it was Aslan.** No way to learn who, here.
- **The court ("on his throne"):** Further north, intact-ish creeper-covered walls, then a **clearing with intact steps and a stub of tall building.** The crew feel **watched**, then emerge to find **Ftahteas on a salvaged throne on a flat dais.** His "court" = a handful of male/female Aslan **in poor condition.** They have **only a couple of worn-out compressor masks** between them, **plenty of hunted food** but **few tools, little weaponry, only the clothes they wear** — proud and dignified in extremity.
- **The challenge:** Ftahteas, **breathless and struggling to concentrate** but regally composed, **demands to know why the crew murdered one of his people then walked into his court.** There is a **fresh grave** off the clearing and **one or two Aslan hidden in the tree-line.** He is weighing **spending his last ammunition and his followers' lives** to seize the crew's gear — risky — but would prefer another way out.
- **Complications (must-handle):** The crew can **trip a bloodbath instantly** by mishandling this. They **must** convince Ftahteas they are not enemies. *(See Confrontations for the honour endgame.)* Astute crew **spot the hidden Aslan** and the fresh grave and connect it to the parallel trail (the murdered hunter, Scene 6).

### 6. Confrontations — the Honour Endgame & the Hunters
- **Trigger:** Once at the court (and once the murdered Aslan is in play).
- **The honour problem (core of the adventure):** Ftahteas **knows he must get his people offworld** and **wants to be given a reason to accept rescue**, but **duty obliges him to die rather than shame his father/clan.** If the crew are **respectful and persuasive**, they can get through to him and offer a **face-saving framing** for retreat. If they **insult him** (or, confused by altitude sickness, he *thinks* they have), he is **honour-bound to dig in, declare he'll die on the ground he won — and may turn on the crew.** Adjudicate with **Diplomat/Persuade** filtered through **Aslan honour/Reaction** (`factions.md`): respect territory/tradition/harmony-of-action; never make him "thank" the crew or admit failure; give him a path where leaving is *strength*, not surrender.
- **The murdered hunter (revealed):** One of Ftahteas's hunters was **shot dead yesterday** — almost certainly the **shadowing party.** Could read as accident; **more likely a racist killing** (anti-Aslan hatred runs high on the border). Even if Ftahteas agrees to leave, **the hunters remain a live problem.**
- **The hunting party:** a typical safari group — **rich offworld thrill-seekers**, most of whom **would never murder a sentient** and **genuinely don't believe** an Aslan was killed (they'll resist any Aslan "justice" as troublemaking). **One of them is the murderer** — came to kill something Aslan-shaped, killed a real Aslan warrior, is hiding it as a "Thebun Lion" kill (no body = no proof, he thinks). **He will try to manufacture a "defending ourselves against Aslan aggression" fight** — e.g. by **"mistaking" another Aslan for a lion** — to make his party do his killing.
- **The Aslan want vengeance** for their friend; **most hunters are innocent.** This is a **moral/tactical pinch:** justice for the murderer vs. not slaughtering bystanders who don't believe a crime occurred, while the murderer actively works to start a firefight.
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (the murderer's gambit):** If the crew let the two parties come into contact without defusing it, the murderer pushes for an incident. Each scene the parties are co-present and tense, roll `dice.py keyed 1d10 6+` — on a hit he **engineers the "self-defence" provocation** (a staged lion-mistake shooting) this scene; track it as a `mythic-gm` Thread and surface his manipulation if the crew investigate.

### 7. Extraction
- **Trigger:** Aslan convinced to leave (and hunters dealt with or evaded).
- **What's here:** Ftahteas's people must be **escorted back to the settlement or the crew's ship**, then perhaps to the starport. **The catch:** the *ihatei* **did not arrive through the port** and will be **refused entry** unless the crew supply a reason. Options: **drop Ftahkaiw's name** (his border reputation may swing it), **bribery, deception**, or **bring the crew's own ship down to collect them** — but a ship that lands on the surface is **refused re-docking at the highport** (Scene 2 rule), so that route burns highport access.
- **Salvage option:** if the crew can recover/repair enough of the **Aoa'iw** to fly it off (a tall order given the wreck), that triggers the **second bonus double** (see Rewards).
- **Lingering thread:** whether Ftahteas lives or dies, **who shot the ship down — and why — is still unanswered.**

## Rewards & Consequences
*Be honest — no rescues.*

- **Pay:** **Cr50,000** for a credible attempt; **×2 (Cr100,000)** if Ftahteas returns **alive and well**; **×2 again (up to Cr200,000)** if the **Aoa'iw is salvaged/flown off.** Paid on delivery to Ftahkaiw.
- **Trade credit:** a **5% discount on buys / premium on sells with Aslan clients**, expiring once it has netted the crew **Cr10,000** of benefit. Real but minor.
- **Goodwill/contacts:** Ftahkaiw's offered Aslan "influence" is **mostly hollow** (he overrates his pull) — treat as **flavour + a possible minor Contact**, not a mechanical Standing boost. Genuine **business contacts** are available at his court. A **respectfully rescued Ftahteas** (and his diplomat) can become a **personal Aslan Contact** worth more than the father's bluster.
- **What violence/failure costs:**
  - **Triggering the court bloodbath** (mishandling Ftahteas, or insulting him while he's altitude-confused): the crew face statted Aslan warriors with autorifles in close terrain, the mission collapses, and **no bonuses** — Ftahkaiw pays nothing (or the base only for a "credible attempt" if the crew can show they tried before it went wrong).
  - **Letting the murderer win his provocation:** the crew may end up in a **firefight that kills innocent hunters and/or Aslan**, with no clean justice and reputational mess on the border.
  - **Ftahteas dies "with dignity":** the crew can still claim the **base Cr50,000** for a credible attempt, but **forfeit both doubles** and gain a grieving, possibly resentful patron.
- **Atmosphere/terrain attrition:** unmasked characters degrade (confusion → bad decisions → possible death); unscouted ship-landings risk minor damage and a stuck landing foot.

## Empire / Reputation effects
Apply per `campaign/empire-reputation.md` ("Per-adventure effects"). This is a **patron job, not a recruitment beat** — it does **not** by itself move Thebus's Attitude onto the Drinax track (Thebus is low-value, lawless surface / corporate highport; **good as a bolthole/rendezvous**, not a kingdom member).
- **Hierate Standing:** the patron is a **freelance border noble**, so success need not move Hierate Standing — but **how the crew treat Aslan** matters. A **respectful, honourable rescue** earns quiet **personal Aslan goodwill** (a Contact; a small positive note with the patron's circle). **Killing the *ihatei*, desecrating the dead, or being seen to side with the racist hunter** earns **Hierate ill-will** and possible vendetta heat (`factions.md` Reaction/assassins) — Aslan honour does not forget murder of their kin.
- **Imperium:** essentially irrelevant here (private corporate world, no Imperial stake). Pleasing/angering the Imperium is not in play.
- **Local (Thebus):** raiding/shooting up the settlement or highport drops Thebus's Attitude as usual; otherwise it stays a low-value, neutral-to-suspicious wilderness stop. **Keeping the job low-key** (per Ftahkaiw's wish) avoids drawing attention to the crew on the Sindalian Main.
- **Record the cause** of any shift (good Aslan deed / atrocity / murderer-collusion) so later scenes can reference it.

## Threads & hooks onward
Seed onto mythic-gm Lists:
- **"Who shot the ship down?"** — the **missile-strike clue** is the adventure's deliberate open hook. *"Perhaps it was rival ihatei, pirates or someone else operating on Thebus… or maybe another ship."* Whoever fired **knew it was Aslan and chose to.** Plant as a recurring **investigation Thread** that can lead to new adventures once Ftahteas is home — tie to anti-Aslan border tension, a Sindal-subsector pirate, or a hidden third party operating covertly on Thebus.
- **The racist hunter / anti-Aslan border sentiment** — even if defused, the murderer (or his survivors) and the broader **hunt-the-Aslan culture** can recur as a border tension thread.
