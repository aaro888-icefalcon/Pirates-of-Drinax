# Shadows of Sindal

> Use this when: running the standalone three-part *Shadows of Sindal* mini-campaign as Pirates of Drinax side content — the hunt for King Oleb's "treasure vault" on Paal, the trail of an Aslan-killing bioweapon up the Dustbelt, and "Prince" Richter Grehai's powerplay at Acis. OPTIONAL / related content (a separate book, not a PoD-1/2 chapter); load only if the crew engage Paal, the Grehai Movement, or the bioweapon thread.

**Logline:** A rod of office from the Floating Palace sends Oleb's pirates to a forgotten Sindalian vault on Paal — where they find Aslan *ihatei* turned into feral cannibal monsters by a biochemical weapon. Tracking the weapon's maker up the Sindalian Main, the crew uncover that the affable "Prince" Richter Grehai — Oleb's would-be ally and rival "son of Sindal" — is mass-producing *Agent Sindal* and is about to use a manufactured crisis at Acis to seize leadership of the whole Dustbelt; the crew become his intended scapegoats and the only thing standing between him and an empire.

## At a glance
- **Three parts, one campaign:** **Part 1 — In Search of Sindal** (the vault on Paal); **Part 2 — The Plague of Sindal** (chase the bioweapon ship *Dawnseeker* up the Dustbelt); **Part 3 — Prince of Sindal** (Richter's coup at Acis). Each part can stand alone but they are designed to run in order. ~121-page book.
- **Where:** **Part 1** = Drinax Chain (Drinax 0603 → Pourne/Hilfer → **Paal 0805 B564679-6**, Ag Ni Ri, TL6, Suspicious, FS2). **Parts 2–3** = the **Dustbelt / Sindalian Main** (Dpres + Sindal subsectors): **Thebus** (0309 B534320-7), **Tktk** (0808 D542640-6), **Sagan** (0607 C796453-8), **Number One** (0208 C9D6778-9), **Ace** (0109 E7A0889-9), **Janus** (0406 C575321-7), **Caldos** (0507 B530879-6), **Tyr** (0608 A551557-B), **Hecarda** (0609 B560332-9), and the climax at **Acis** (0809 B885796-A, Ag Ri).
- **Threat/level:** Mid-campaign side arc. Ground combat is the spine; the headline danger is **feral Aslan** (huge STR/END, immune to pain/fear, cannot be reasoned with — see stat blocks). Part 1 is a **defensive last-stand** scenario; Part 2 is a **sci-fi haunted-house** plus a piracy intercept; Part 3 is **investigation → civil-crisis → infiltration of a downed ship**, with a heavy *political/moral* climax rather than a slugfest.
- **Prerequisites:** A jump-capable ship (the **Harrier** or any **jump-2** hull; jump-1 needs deep-space refuelling/extra tanks). The hook needs the crew to hold **the rod-of-office key** and know it opens a vault on Paal — normally a gift from King Oleb; can be a lucky find or an academic Contact. An independent (non-Oleb) crew works fine; reassign the patron.
- **Arc position:** Free-standing side campaign that **ties Drinax to the Dustbelt** and sets up a recurring antagonist (**Richter Grehai / the Grehai Movement**). It assumes (and reinforces) Oleb's empire-building. Thematically a sibling to *The Treasure of Sindal* (05) — another "Sindalian cache" that turns out to be a horror, not a hoard. Optional throughout; can be skipped without breaking the main campaign.

**Through-line (GM):** "Prince" Richter Grehai — affable founder of the anti-*ihatei* **Grehai Movement**, claimed heir to the Sindalian Empire, and Oleb's would-be ally — is secretly developing **Agent Sindal** (a biotoxin that reverts Aslan to feral cannibals but spares humans). Part 1 is its first field test (Paal). Part 2 is the crew foiling further tests (Sagan). Part 3 is Richter using a fabricated *ihatei* crisis at Acis to make himself the hero-architect of a Dustbelt alliance — pinning the bioweapon and the crisis on **the crew (and Drinax)** as scapegoats. The crew end the book holding the proof that can destroy him.

# PART 1 — IN SEARCH OF SINDAL (Paal / the vault) — pp.4–38

## P1 · Player-Facing
- **The hook (At Drinax):** King Oleb summons the crew to the Floating Palace (cluttered throne-hall, scholars pricing treasures he's being forced to sell). Furious "but not at them," he thrusts a 40 cm gold/jewelled **rod of office** at them: *"It's a key!"* — the rod of office of "the Grand Jerhebop of something or other." His scholars repaired its decayed data unit; it holds **access codes for a set of repositories on Paal**, sealed because the only key fled the planet when it was bombed. *"The goodies are STILL THERE!"* **Mission:** go to Paal, find the vault, plunder it, bring the contents to Drinax. Oleb expects vast riches but admits he has no idea what's inside; reward is loud vague promises. He tolerates honest failure but **not** lack of effort (Drinax becomes "unhealthy" for slackers within days).
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** Sindalian "survival bunkers / rebuilding caches" are common knowledge (and mostly looted long ago — Oleb won't hear it). The rod ties to the **Counts of Ghormount**, a ruined city ~500 km N of the Paal downport. Paal is heavily Balkanised (kings/dukes/princes of small states), TL6, **no overflight allowed** (trigger-happy surface missiles), **strict weapon control** (good scanners at the port), and historically resentful of Drinax.
- **Presenting problem on arrival:** the vault is occupied — a low-tech human remnant community lives on top of it — **and** it is about to be overrun by Aslan *ihatei* "behaving very strangely."

## P1 · GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **The vault is NOT a treasure house.** It's a Sindalian **emergency-rebuilding store** (the Counts of Ghormount fled with the only key, abandoning the food/medical supplies their people needed — a betrayal). Contents: ~200 t metal **ingots** (steel/copper) + small **crystaliron**, **1 t gold + 1 t silver bullion**, drums of powdered chemicals, **a fabrication machine that can make metal components to spec**, ration packs (inedible after 200 yrs), and first-aid kits. Modest market value; genuinely useful for kick-starting industry or repairing the Floating Palace. **The machinery is exactly what the Grehai Movement needs for gun-making** (deal hook).
- **The feral Aslan = first field test of Agent Sindal.** A subsidised merchant (later identified as **Dawnseeker**) landed at the *ihatei* **fort** near Ghormount a few weeks ago, infected the colony with the bioweapon, then **looted the fort** (against near-zero resistance after the Aslan turned), making multiple trips. The canisters the crew may find near a hunting ground are the spent delivery containers. This is the seed for the whole book; the crew should leave Part 1 asking *where was this weapon made, has it been used elsewhere, and who flew a spacecraft over Paal with permission?*
- **The weapon (Agent Sindal, early form):** yellowish-white liquid, toxic to most animals for minutes after air-exposure then degrades to near-harmless. **Aslan only.** Ingestion (contaminated water/food/meat) → 1D days asymptomatic → ~1 day mild-viral fever → rapid cognitive collapse to **feral**. STR & END increase by **+1D+1**; constant hunger; sees all sophonts (human + Aslan) as prey/rivals; retains weapon *skill* but used instinctively; cannot speak/reason. **Not** a contagious plague — only spread by ingestion; **cannot** be passed by being bitten (only by biting an infected Aslan). An area is safe again within weeks (≈100% infection in first days, falling off). The only partly-affected non-Aslan is a **Paal bird** used in development (sick birds are an early clue).
- **The remnants' value:** treat them generously and Ghormount becomes a **tiny friendly Haven on Paal** (repairs, hiding place) — and a Grehai-Movement supply partnership if the machinery stays.

## P1 · Scenes / Locations
1. **Drinax — the throne-hall hook.** *Trigger:* Oleb's "boisterous invitation/arrogant demand." *What's here:* the rant, the rod, the mission (see Player-Facing). *Complication:* refuse/dawdle and Oleb turns hostile fast.
2. **Drinax → Paal (the transit).** *Trigger:* leaving Drinax. *What's here:* jump-2 via **Pourne** or **Hilfer** (~16–18 days quick). **Imperial-ship encounter most likely at Pourne/Paal** — dangerous if the crew have been pirating (a detached *Gazelle*/patrol corvette from the *Game of Sun and Shadow* flotilla, or a lone destroyer/fleet escort, or a flag-showing heavy cruiser). Pourne = nit-picking customs + jump-3 Imperial freighters; Hilfer = anti-Drinax, raider-prone. Optional generic ship encounters: *Starhawk* (luxury Scout/Courier "personal liner"), *Cashcow* (incompetent Type-A free trader), *Mayfly II* (battered Type-A2 far trader) — colour/recurring NPCs, no fixed plot.
3. **Paal Starport.** *Trigger:* arrival; orbital holding pattern (outer pattern = pirate-strike-vulnerable). *What's here:* drab highport vs. vibrant downport; a barrage of shills and **trade-fair/Embassy-Town pitches** — *King Earheart of Orarle Valley* (exclusivity on ag-machinery), *Duke Maix* (showy high-status goods), *Railway Institute* (train components), *Merchants' Guild* (real; wants exclusivity), plus scams (*"Paal Merchants' Guild"* front, *"local business association"*). **The Grehai Movement** approaches *politely, professionally* (holographics) — vague about its backer; wants "machinery" that's obviously **gun-and-ammo production gear**. *Complication:* Drinaxians are unwelcome; concealing origin speeds info-gathering.
4. **Searching for the vault location.** *Trigger:* the crew investigate. *What's here:* best source = **Railway Institute survey data** (combine destroyed-city + disused-rail records → Ghormount, on a disused spur ~500 km N, off a still-used line). Alternatives: orbital maps via personal computer; own high-orbit survey (looks like a pirate — may draw the defence flotilla); just asking around. Old admin records tie the rod to the **Counts of Ghormount**.
5. **Encountering the Grehai Movement (Paal Chapter).** *Trigger:* poking around draws them. *What's here:* ~30 earnest, amateurish ordinary members (NOT racists — *political*, anti-*ihatei*) led by a small core of competent **offworlder Inner Circle** organisers. They pitch friendship/membership ("friends in every port"); want news + minor favours + that gun-making machinery. *Secret:* the **leaders know the "Aslan Rampage" is coming** and want uninvolved offworld witnesses to spread the story — so they'll **arm the crew** for the trip (see below).
6. **Making preparations / weapons.** *Trigger:* the crew commit to the overland trip. *What's here:* ship is **not** an option (overflight = shot down). Route: **graintrain to Parflex Junction** (~6–7 hrs, cheap, safe) then **rent an offroad vehicle** (Cr500 deposit + Cr50/day; ~500 km range/charge; no ATVs/grav for rent). Weapons must clear port scanners — **outgoing crews are scanned too**. If allied with the Movement, they provide a *pathetic* arsenal "for pirates/bandits/ihatei": sawn-off shotgun, shotgun, **rifle w/ Scope**, revolver; ammo **28 rifle / 19 revolver / 15 shotgun** rounds, no documentation (must be concealed).
7. **The Wilderness of Paal (overland to Ghormount).** *Trigger:* leaving Parflex Junction. *What's here:* semi-desert; vehicle speeds — following rail line ~40 km/h (+10%/Drive(wheel) level); open semi-desert 20–30 km/day. Locals at Parflex Junction warn of *ihatei* landings in the north. **Encounters:** *Ruined Farmstead* (wild **Horx**, signs of camping — see GM note that scavengers were *non-lethally* run off by Aslan, contrasting current behaviour); *A Flock of Birds* (some sick/dying — early bioweapon clue). *Complication:* don't let the crew die pointlessly in the desert (it's a side trip, not a deathtrap), but stack difficulties.
8. **The Ruins of Ghormount.** *Trigger:* arrival. *What's here:* 3 zones + cratered hill — N depression (mildly radioactive bowl, safe weeks/months, don't eat the food), central rubble-sea, S shanty (intact rail buildings). **Encounter — An Aslan Behaving Strangely:** a feral ex-warrior **stalks** the crew (see *Degenerate Male Aslan*); tragic + terrifying (GM note). Discoveries: **the Ihatei Fort** (2 m rubble wall + ditch; ~200-capacity prefab Aslan settlement, comprehensively **looted**, only 3 unburied + ~2 dozen buried bodies, "raided by spacecraft against no resistance" — feels wrong); **the Canisters** (4 empty industrial canisters near a hunting ground; degraded, now harmless; *p.67 notes = bioweapon delivery containers*).
9. **Deus Vault — the Ghormount Remnants.** *Trigger:* approaching the vault (someone lives right on top of it). *What's here:* ~60 humans, alert, scared, holding a few rifles (little ammo); led by **Eteane** ("deals with the traders — and everything else"). They've built a pseudo-religion around the **statues** outside the blast doors and tap power off the vault door (so it still has power). They tell the **Remnants' Tale** (Aslan arrived ~3 yrs ago, were *honourable conquerors* until recently; the lone dying Aslan female who left her baby — GM note: she went away to die so as not to endanger it). **A Possible Deal:** help repel the imminent feral attack → they grant vault access (and want a fair share). *Complication:* the vault **cannot be opened before the battle** (decrepit lock; see Ch.8).
10. **The Last Battle of Ghormount.** *Trigger:* lookouts spot Aslan approaching. *What's here:* a defensive last-stand in **three waves** — **First Skirmish** (5–6 cunning ferals probing; parlay *fails bloodily*; they won't withdraw until ≥2 down; play for tension/half-seen figures); **Second Attack** (8 fully-degenerate **berserkers** charge mindlessly — easy to lure into ambushes, never learn); **Final Assault** (~25 ferals using cover; run as dramatic narrative, not dice-by-dice). The crew can flee (thinning the herd) or stand (then they **must win or die**). ~30 able-bodied but scared remnants; Leadership/military rallying matters. *mythic-gm keyed trigger (must-happen pacing):* the attack arrives regardless once the crew reach the vault — if they stall, run it **off-camera** and let them return to the aftermath. To pace wave onset, `dice.py keyed 1d10 7` per scene-beat (on/above = next wave presses in).
11. **The Legacy of Ghormount (open the vault).** *Trigger:* battle won (or the crew force entry). *What's here:* **the lock** — insert rod beside door → warbling signal → holographic Sindalian countdown that *jumps around* (decrepit lock generating bad codes); opens during/just-after the battle, never in time to help. **One Last Problem: a Sindalian combat robot** behind the blast doors (treats everyone as a threat; **terrible aim, half its shots non-lethal** — roll 1D per shot, odd=stun). Disable/topple/hack it, or slot the rod into its **rear override slot** to take control (reprogrammable later — pity-the-thing tone). If the door opens *during* the siege the robot may lurch out and help/randomly join — optional deus-ex-machina. Then explore the vault (see contents in GM-Only). **Decisions:** divide spoils among Oleb (expects riches; gets ingots — placate by selling it as furthering his cause), the remnants (fair share), and the Movement (covets the fabricator).

# PART 2 — THE PLAGUE OF SINDAL (chase *Dawnseeker* up the Dustbelt) — pp.39–80

## P2 · Player-Facing
- **Hook:** a short while after Ghormount, the crew investigate **the mysterious starship** that infected the fort and the **bioweapon** itself (Oleb's standing concern; if the crew didn't tell him, he found out and is *deeply* worried — a weapon like this could upset the regional balance against his interests). Start at Paal; modifiable to start elsewhere.
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** at Paal, port techs recall a Subsidised Merchant whose clearances were a confused mess (waved through off-the-books) that **took on far more fuel than a Type-R can normally carry** — i.e. **extra fuel tanks** (cross-main capable, still only **jump-1**). Name surfaces: **Dawnseeker**, registered out of Tanith, captain **Almex Vindersson**, armed; passed Paal a few times at multi-month intervals. The Grehai Movement on Paal is *what it seems* — sincere, mostly opposed to the weapon's use; **Prince Richter Grehai** comes up (Movement founder, claims Sindalian descent, never visited Paal, location unknown). The trail of *Dawnseeker* runs up the Dustbelt: Thebus → Number One → Ace → **Tktk** → Sagan.

## P2 · GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- ***Dawnseeker* is the bioweapon delivery ship**, crewed by fanatical anti-*ihatei* personnel (racists / bereaved / political) under cover as a stuck-up merchant crew. She delivers **Agent Sindal** to a **research installation on Tktk** and is carrying out a **multi-site field test**, culminating at **Sagan**.
- **The Tktk installation = the bioweapon lab.** Buried, near-invisible research base. By the time the crew arrive it has **suffered a containment breach**: thawed Aslan test-subjects went feral and slaughtered the 7-person staff + their pet Chirpers. *Dawnseeker* had already left (a few days earlier) carrying **12 cylinders of Agent Sindal (final weaponised, air-dispersible form)** bound for Sagan. The base holds the only other copy of the **research data** and the *Dawnseeker* mission file — which **echoes Richter's exact phrasing** ("cast a long shadow over the Aslan Frontier") = the smoking-gun link to the Grehai Movement.
- ***Dawnseeker*'s Sagan mission (Agent Sindal final test):** three delivery methods at different targets to evaluate efficacy — **Wide Dispersion** (into ecosystem; patchy, ≤30%, burns out), **Water Dispersion** (water table; slow), **Direct/Air Dispersion** (canisters → cloud; ~**100% infection within hours**, new respiratory/ingestion vector, hoped-for final form). Crew to withdraw and observe; if air-dispersal works, mass production begins. Targets: two ~200-Aslan enclaves + one **2,000+ ihatei** settlement (air target).
- **The detour fork (GM logic):** *Dawnseeker* is jump-1, so from Thebus she could only go to **Noricum** (no trade reason) or **Number One** — i.e. up the Main. At **Number One** she dropped pre-paid containers (electronics/life-support spares for an outpost) and crews sell **Chirper trinkets** → points at **Tktk** (the only nearby Chirper world). At **Ace's refuelling station**, chatty staff reveal *Dawnseeker* "was carrying stuff for the research station on Tktk."
- **Separate, parallel piracy job (Oleb + Richter):** intercept the far trader **Principled Profiteer** at **Thebus** — she's gun-running industrial/colony hardware to an *ihatei* foothold. This is Richter's "win-win" favour to Oleb (denies the *ihatei* their cargo; nets Oleb loot). Genuine offer; tip via Richter at Drinax (see the Richter-at-Drinax scene below).

## P2 · Key scene cluster A — Richter at Drinax & "No New Order" (Ch.3) — pp.47–49
*(Runs when the crew return to Drinax between/around the chase, e.g. after Paal.)*
- **Player-Facing:** approaching Drinax, **4 foreign ships** in orbit + **1 on the Palace pad**, all squawking an unofficial **"Royal Bodyguard Squadron"** code (whose? none stated — high arrogance). Ships: patrol corvettes *Stalwart Bastion* & *Heroic Fortress*; far trader *Fort Kreinda*; scout/courier *Royal Messenger*; and the Broadsword-class mercenary cruiser **Sindalsword** on the pad (smart, battle-tested, well-run). Oleb spams **three summonses**; the crew (in court dress) must report **no treasure** in front of the court — and in front of his guest **Prince Richter Grehai** (richly dressed, ornate sword, polished, charming). Oleb rages then recovers.
- **Meeting Richter (Player-Facing):** subtle, gracious, *quietly* powerful (the anti-Oleb). Seeks an **alliance with Oleb** to "harden the Aslan Frontier"; genuinely believes the *ihatei* must be blocked; offers a mutually beneficial arrangement (he knows where *ihatei* ships/cargoes are → targets for Oleb's pirates). Hands over **Principled Profiteer's schedule** (Thebus run). Offer stands as long as he's in contact.
- **GM-Only:** Richter's *stated* anti-*ihatei* belief is **true**; his *hidden* goal is to **rule the Dustbelt as Prince of Sindal**. He's doing exactly what Oleb does, using *ihatei* fear as the unifier.
- **Oleb's true orders ("No New Order"):** Oleb privately tells the crew *"I don't trust that man"* — he's seen Richter wants the same throne; there's no room for two sons of Sindal. **Mission:** (1) build cooperation with Richter & learn about him; (2) **intercept Principled Profiteer at Thebus**, steal her cargo (pleasing Richter by denying the *ihatei*); (3) **find out about the bioweapon and possibly secure it**; (4) bring home riches. Gift: a pair of **Sindalian Heavy Laser Pistols** (ornate, iridium-inlaid; w/ belt, 3 power packs, stock+foregrip accessories) — *not* an apology.

## P2 · Scenes / Locations
1. **At Paal Starport (Ch.2)** — *Trigger:* investigating the ship/weapon. *What's here:* the off-the-books fuel/clearance detective work → name **Dawnseeker** (above); Movement context + first mention of **Prince Richter**. *Complication:* if the crew miss the obvious inference, Oleb spells it out.
2. **Thebus (Ch.4) — intercept *Principled Profiteer*.** *Trigger:* Richter's schedule + Oleb's order. *What's here:* **Thebus 0309** (B534320-7, thin air, ~8,000 in Thebus City + modern highport). ## P3 · GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **The Hustwik was a set-up.** It carried the **Ailairlil** ("relatives of successful *ihatei*") diplomatic party; Richter had them **assassinated with the bioweapon** (slipped into a galley meal) and **engineered a pirate attack** to (a) destroy witnesses, (b) deflect suspicion, and ideally **(c) pin it on Drinax/the crew**. By the time the crew board, most Ailairlil are dead/feral and the human crew murdered; a few Aslan sealed themselves on the bridge (too late). **Jenville is innocent** (genuinely thought the tip came from Oleb) — and is killed in an "accident" days later to cover the trail.
- **The crew are Richter's chosen scapegoats.** They interfered with the bioweapon (Part 2); Richter believes them "out of the picture" but wants them dead/blamed. The assassins act on relayed orders from a minor Movement official at the relevant starport (couriers/electronic only — untraceable).
- **Richter's Plan (the Acis powerplay):** Acis is the key jump-2 entry point for *ihatei* into the Main. Richter wants to **force Acis into an anti-*ihatei* alliance with himself as architect-hero**. Steps: (1) assassinate the visiting **Ailairlil** and blame Drinax/the crew → fear of Hierate reprisal; (2) convince **Tyr** (Acis's old enemy) that Acis is signing a secret "*ihatei* treaty" → Tyr mobilises (win-win for Tyr); (3) **mercenaries** (raised on Caldos, shipped via **Porthang**) provoke/massacre an outback *ihatei* settlement and stage a **false-flag missile strike**; (4) fabricate riots/incidents in the cities (recycled news footage); (5) a **"decapitation"** (downport/HQ explosions + comms blackout) leaves only Richter's **rescued** officials (a defence-force **commodore** + the **Senior Secretary for Foreign Affairs**) able to speak — who then **formally request aid**, which Richter & Tyr instantly "offer." Result: Richter heads a new **"Guardians of Sindal"** alliance (Acis + Tyr + Movement). **Backup plan:** the bioweapon as threat/last resort (earlier, still-toxic version aboard *Porthang*).
- **The unraveling (Richter's Undoing):** the false-flag missile salvo (aimed at the Imperial cruiser **Sun Tzu**) is fired from a battery near the massacred settlement; the pickup ship **Porthang** is **shot down by surviving Aslan warriors** and lies crippled in **Seioairah canyon**. *Porthang* holds **everything**: the missile launchers, mercenaries on Movement funds, **comms logs with Richter's explicit orders** (attack the settlement, fire on Sun Tzu, etc.), and **a canister of the (earlier) bioweapon** + aerosol drones. **Securing Porthang = the proof that destroys Richter.**

## P3 · Rewards & Consequences
- **Material:** Hustwik haul (MCr60 currency [launder at 5–50%] + ~97×Cr50,000 metals + ceremonial goods); **salvage rights to Porthang** (post-decon) + its weapons/missiles; if the crew surrender the bioweapon, **MCr1** from Sun Tzu's fund (or take the bargain payoff from Richter).
- **Consequences (honest):** broadcasting the logs **destroys Richter** but earns the crew Imperial *suspicion* (they're genuinely guilty of piracy/some acts) — polite thanks, not medals. **Aslan clan bounty on Richter** = he's a recurring, vengeful enemy either way (especially if bargained with). Massacring the innocent Grehai chapter, or getting lynched, is a dead end — no rescues.

## P3 · Empire / Reputation effects
- **Drinax vs. Richter:** discrediting Oleb's rival **"son of Sindal"** is a major win for Drinax's empire ambitions (and pleases Oleb, briefly). If the crew let Richter win/bargain, **Drinax is damaged** and a powerful Dustbelt rival rises.
- **Hierate Standing:** exposing Richter's anti-Aslan bioweapon conspiracy and aiding Eoao's band is a strong **Heroic Deed** with the Hierate/Ailairlil (+ Standing); being framed (if the crew can't prove it) does the opposite. (`empire-reputation.md`; `setting/factions.md`.)
- **Acis/Tyr Attitudes (0809 / 0608):** saving Acis from the coup earns Acis goodwill (toward a future **+1** shift / recruitment) but lingering suspicion; Tyr is left thwarted. **Lost World risk:** if the crew fail to stop the deployments (here or at Sagan), a devastated Aslan settlement is effectively a Lost World and a sector-scale atrocity on the empire track.
