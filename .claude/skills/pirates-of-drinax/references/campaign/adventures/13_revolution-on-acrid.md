# Revolution on Acrid

> Use this when: the crew are recruited by the Acrid worker-revolt leader **Gera Hollis** to help her seize the PRQ hell-world; covers the patron pitch, smuggling guns into a sealed city, neutralising the two PRQ system defence boats and the port's missile turrets, running the actual rising, and surviving PRQ's counter-revolution (assault → blockade → face-saving deal). The reward is **Acrid as a Haven** (and a favour-owing friendly world).

**Logline:** A demoted citizen-turned-revolutionary asks the crew to arm her downtrodden workers, knock out the orbital defences that would let PRQ glass any uprising, and midwife a revolution that turns a corporate slave-world into the crew's own bolthole — if they can win it fast enough that the megacorp decides retaking it costs more than letting it go.

## At a glance
- **Location:** **Acrid (0302 Borderland) AAC1388-D** — Fl Lo Ni Ht; **Starport A**, TL D(13); ~1,000 PRQ citizens + ~25,000 debt-bonded workers. Insidious corrosive atmosphere (constant seal/suit decon). Sister world **Argona (0503)** is also PRQ. On the Imperium–Hierate route (Tobia→Cordan or **Acrid**→Tech-World). GeDeCo also has facilities here.
- **Threat/level:** Mid-campaign. **Space threat:** two PRQ **system defence boats** + later a PRQ **mercenary cruiser** counter-assault — real but beatable with planning. **Ground threat:** a chaotic, bloody urban rising (hand weapons, shotguns/SMGs, improvised grenades) where the **sealed environment is itself a weapon** (a hull/section breach = the atmosphere kills hundreds). Not a dungeon crawl — a logistics-and-leverage problem.
- **Est. length:** Long. 2–4 sessions (recruit + prep smuggling/defences → the rising → counter-revolution/negotiation).
- **Prerequisites:** A crew with an **armed ship** (the swing factor against the SDBs and the counter-assault), access to **weapons/missiles to smuggle**, and ideally backing from **Drinax or a local power** (external support is what lets Acrid survive PRQ). Best run once the crew want a forward base on the Borderland trade route.
- **Arc position:** A **patron/alliance adventure** (Companion ch.21). Outcome feeds the **empire track**: success makes Acrid a **Haven** — a rare independent base astride the trade route, useful for raiding the Imperium–Hierate lane and as a recruitment candidate at the Finale. Pairs naturally with **The Cordan Conflict** (next-door Borderland intrigue) and **Friends in Dry Places** (Kteiroa, the following Companion chapter).

## Player-Facing
- **The discreet approach (hook):** While docked at Acrid (or on a nearby world where worker sympathisers can vouch for them), the crew are quietly approached by a **representative of the workers' revolution** — affiliation not advertised at first. The pitch: if they will **meet Gera Hollis on Acrid**, they'll be offered a deal they'll find "most suitable." Discretion is everything.
- **The meeting:** A back room of a grim workers' bar at the starport (bad food, worse beer). **Gera Hollis** lays it out plainly — she knows the crew aren't paragons of law-abiding virtue, and that pirates need places to rest, repair and lie low without looking over their shoulders. She can provide exactly that… eventually.
- **The presenting problem (what she openly asks):** Acrid's workers are about to revolt against **PRQ** (Pax Rulin Quartermasters), the megacorp that owns the world. They want to seize the planet and become an **independent state**. To survive PRQ's inevitable retaliation they need to **neutralise the two PRQ system defence boats** already in-system and **fend off any attempt to retake the planet**. The port has two missile turrets that would help, but everyone who can crew them is part of the privileged "**around a thousand**" — Gera doesn't trust them to defend a world just taken from them.
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** Acrid is a corporate hell — nominal wages gutted by "clean air/water" surcharges and company-store debt, so **few workers can ever afford to leave** (~25× as many debt-slaves as citizens; an overseer/security buffer class keeps the rest down). Living conditions are declining; disaffection is boiling over. The rising **is going to happen sooner or later no matter what** — with the crew's help it can be organised and survivable; without it, a series of uncoordinated risings get bloodily crushed.
- **The offer (safe to narrate):** Help make Gera the leader of Acrid and she will grant the crew **no-questions-asked shelter and repairs** — making Acrid a **Haven**, and possibly more: a friendly world that **owes them a favour**. Acrid has a Class A port with repair facilities, a modest industrial base, and a population that will adore the crew as heroes… if they help.
- **What she wants, concretely:** (1) a way to take the two SDBs out of the picture; (2) help fending off PRQ's retaking attempt — their armed ship, and/or working orbital defences; (3) ideally, **weapons for the revolutionaries** and **navy-grade missiles** to stock the port turrets; (4) once she's in power, **trade** — quietly spread word that Acrid is "open for business."

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **Gera is not a hero.** She has a real gift for **leadership and manipulation**, but she's motivated far more by **regaining her lost comfortable life** than by justice. She was a well-off citizen industrial chemist, abruptly demoted to non-citizen worker "for reasons she is unclear about," her savings gone, former friends unwilling to help. She is "committed to doing right, but not for altruistic reasons." She runs much of the revolution **personally** (a big risk) because she doesn't fully trust others and wants the credit — and dislikes anyone else getting credit she could claim.
- **The revolution will happen regardless.** The crew are an accelerant and a force-multiplier, not the cause. This matters for adjudication: if they stall or refuse, an **ill-coordinated rising** kicks off anyway (and gets crushed, or descends into a grinding multi-faction street war). Use a rising clock if the crew dawdle (below).
- **The SDBs are a long-term complication, not an instant threat.** At any time one is on patrol near the mainworld; the other may be down for maintenance, crew training, or off investigating something in-system (rarely, ordered to sweep for smugglers/pirates). They **cannot easily crush the rising directly** — to do real harm they'd have to **fire on rebellious city sections**, which breaches the seals and the corrosive atmosphere kills hundreds and wrecks infrastructure. The boat captains **won't do this except under extreme circumstances.** But the boats *can* **blockade** the port (no ships in or out) — and their own supplies are limited, so they eventually must surrender, negotiate, or act drastically.
- **The turrets are over-valued by Gera.** She fixates on the two port turrets, but they're "largely irrelevant in the short term" **unless there is a spaceborne component** to the fight. Their true function is to **impose a siege** (keep relief ships out) — so the rebels need at least one turret working *and in their hands* to control the port, and both **out of the equation** before any ships can land. Without a working port, Acrid's independence is simply **strangled**. Gera would benefit from someone correcting her appraisal.
- **The sealed city cuts both ways.** Both sides *can* breach access tunnels or city sections to deny ground to the enemy — but both have **strong reasons not to** (it kills everyone in that section). A desperate faction might do it anyway. Improvised explosives and massed home-made grenades are the workers' main firepower if they don't get real guns — random, collateral-heavy, and **prone to causing fatal breaches in a sealed environment**.
- **PRQ will come back — but only if it's cost-effective.** PRQ won't simply eat the loss (a major asset + dead personnel + reputational damage + the risk of copycat revolts elsewhere). But PRQ "is in business": its response must be **cost-effective**, and that ceiling is the crew's real leverage. The likely sequence:
  1. **First attempt:** a straightforward assault — a **mercenary cruiser** (or similar) full of PRQ security/mercenaries hits the port. Stoppable by an armed offworld presence (the SDBs if seized + the crew's ship) or by a **functional orbital defence system**.
  2. **If the assault is repelled → blockade + negotiation.** Attackers stand off, blockade the world, and open talks. PRQ's opening offer (amnesty for laying down arms + addressing grievances "in good faith") is weak; accepted only if Acrid is being strangled or has bad internal problems. More PRQ ships (chartered resupply merchants) arrive over **weeks** until the blockade is **unbreakable**; Acrid can only last so long before it needs spares it can't make.
  3. **Sympathiser contact:** the PRQ response team will try to contact **planetside sympathisers** (former privileged employees, disaffected workers unhappy with Gera, defeatists) to coordinate a **counter-revolution**. **Hunting down covert transmissions** denies them coordination.
  4. **The face-saving deal (most likely real ending):** if PRQ can't retake Acrid in a few weeks, a negotiator offers: **Acrid becomes an "autonomous business unit" of PRQ** — self-governing, run by the revolutionary government, with PRQ "experts" assisting; PRQ funds development/repairs and pretends it wasn't kicked out. In effect **Acrid governs itself** while PRQ saves face. How much money PRQ commits scales with how strongly the rebels hold the world (a **bold front** — convincing PRQ the situation is better than it is — improves terms).
- **The biggest obstacle to a good ending is Gera herself.** She dreams of a **fully independent** Acrid and will resist any compromise — unrealistic, because PRQ won't accept that unless retaking is far too expensive, *and* Acrid needs external support and trade to survive alone. **If the crew secure external support (Drinax / a local power),** Acrid can survive independent **provided** the retake attempt is defeated or deterred. **If not,** they must broker the PRQ deal — and the hard part is **persuading Gera to accept it.** If she won't, "there are others who might," and Acrid may see **another round of revolt and negotiation** before it settles.

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. Hiring On — the discreet pitch
**Trigger:** Crew are at Acrid's port (or a nearby sympathiser world). A workers'-revolution **representative** approaches, affiliation hidden, and invites them to meet Gera. → **What's here:** The bar-back-room meeting; Gera's frank pitch (Player-Facing). She asks for SDB neutralisation, help fending off the retake, weapons/missiles, and post-rising trade; she offers Acrid as a **Haven** (and a favour). → **Complications:** Discretion — PRQ security watches incoming crews closely (see Scene 2). If the crew are obvious pirates trying to lure *merchant captains* to a backwater (the trade-attraction job), note the irony: smugglers/shady operators are the most likely takers, which isn't quite what Gera wants — but she's a realist ("any trade is better than no trade").

### 2. Smuggling In the Guns
**Trigger:** Crew agree to arm the workers (the rebels have chemists/engineers and improvised explosives + crude hand weapons, **but few guns**). → **What's here:** Two routes: **steal from Acrid's security armouries** (shotguns/SMGs — ideal for fighting inside a sealed city) **without anyone realising** (theft that's noticed triggers a clampdown and may force an early rising), **or smuggle weapons in from outside.** PRQ security is alert to smuggling (keeping workers down means denying outside goods); every incoming ship's crew is watched. → **Mechanic — the cover deception:** Any attempt to bring in weapons/armour needs a **clever deception** to throw security off. **Best trick:** let security **intercept a decoy** package of luxury/necessary goods — if they think they caught the contraband and it was innocuous, they relax (they won't tear the place apart). If they believe **guns** are coming in, they start ripping the port apart. Run as opposed **Deception / Stealth** vs. PRQ security alertness; success = guns delivered quietly; failure = clampdown (advance the rising clock). → **No-guns fallback:** if the rising goes ahead without firearms, the workers lean on **improvised explosives and massed home-made grenades** (drawing response teams into explosive ambushes) — creative and nasty but random, collateral-heavy, and **risks fatal section breaches**. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** if the crew delay arming/prepping, roll the **rising clock**: `dice.py keyed 1d10 8+` once per in-game week of delay — on a hit, an uncoordinated rising erupts before they're ready (jump to Scene 6 mid-prep).

### 3. Laying the Groundwork (training, defences, economy)
**Trigger:** Crew help prepare beyond just guns. → **Combat coaching:** the workers are determined but have **no military experience** — anyone who's been in a gunfight has wisdom to offer. Proper training is hard, but the crew can improvise tactical exercises ("any attempt is better than nothing"); apply situational **DM+** to rebel rolls during the rising for whatever training they manage. → **Setting up orbital defences (the wish-list item):** Gera hopes the crew can obtain **navy-grade missiles** and **maybe a launcher or two**, set up temporarily (e.g. outside the city) and tied into an **improvised fire-control/tracking system**. The rebels have no idea how hard this is and just toss it onto the wish list — but a competent crew may build an effective defence net cheaply (Engineer / Electronics(sensors) / Gunner). The rebels may not want to know where the gear came from. **Not a prerequisite** to the rising, but it greatly increases the odds Acrid lasts beyond a couple of months. → **Building a post-rising economy:** Gera knows Acrid can't stay independent without **trade**. Acrid can export minerals/metals/chemicals + workshop goods, and offer technical schooling (hostile-environment mining, chemicals). She wants the crew to **quietly spread word** that Acrid will soon be "open for business" and ideally convince a couple of trader captains a profit can be made (it couldn't before — the economy was a closed PRQ shop). → **Complications:** every preparation risks tipping off PRQ; balance thoroughness against triggering an early rising.

### 4. Neutralising the System Defence Boats
**Trigger:** Crew tackle the two PRQ SDBs (a plan to neutralise them is **necessary** to a successful revolution). → **What's here / the two grades:**
- **'Soft' neutralisation (ideal):** leave the boats **available to the rebels** afterward; carried out **ahead of time without alerting authorities.** Options: **draw one boat off** to a distant station it can't quickly return from; **persuade a crew it needs repairs** and get it into dock with key components removed at zero hour; or **subtle sabotage**. Best outcome = rebels **capture/control** the boats (and don't kill the crews — the rebels much prefer this; they know little of naval matters).
- **'Hard' neutralisation:** **sabotage or violence** — probably **triggers an alert** ("a boat disabled by a missile from a suspect vessel it was inspecting" might not be blamed on an internal uprising — divert suspicion if you can). Gera's **fallback** is **home-made missiles** in container/launchers fired in a **massed salvo** at an unsuspecting boat at zero hour — a sucker-punch that *might* land enough hits to disable it (rudimentary targeting; not much of a plan).
- **Shooting it out:** straightforwardly engaging one or both boats with the crew's ship remains an option (see stat blocks; the crew's armed ship is the swing factor).
→ **Smart additions the rebels haven't thought of:** **disabling Acrid's comms** so the boats can't learn what's happening on the surface; using a friendly in-system ship/SDB to provide **mid-course guidance** for indirect missile launches. Gera is hugely impressed by plans above her own strategic level. → **If a boat is *not* neutralised:** its crew opposes the rising but can do little directly **unless** it fires on rebel city sections (mass casualties via breach — only under extreme circumstances). Mainly it can **blockade** the port; with limited supplies it must eventually surrender/negotiate/act drastically. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** when the rising launches, for **each un-neutralised boat** check whether it acts: `dice.py keyed 1d10 9+` — on a hit the captain takes a drastic step (fires on a section / runs the blockade aggressively); otherwise it holds station and besieges.

### 5. Seizing the Turrets
**Trigger:** Crew/rebels move on the port's two **dual missile turrets** (atop armoured bunkers near the landing area, reachable via maintenance tunnels). → **What's here:** The bunkers' **underground tunnels** let work crews move without hostile-environment suits — and let attackers reach the turrets, magazines, or fire-control centre. Taking any of these out nullifies the port's launch capability. **Ammo is scarce:** **6 ready rounds per turret + 10 more in an adjoining magazine** (reloaded manually on trolleys); Acrid has never fired more than a single warning shot. → **Geometry that matters:** turrets **cannot hit a ship on/near the landing pad** (a missile would loop back and endanger the port). They **can** hit a ship in **orbit** *if targeting data exists* — but the port's sensors only cover the sky **directly above**; a ship **over the horizon** can't be tracked without **mid-course guidance** from an SDB or other in-system vessel. → **Methods (Gera wants control + working condition, minimal damage):**
- **Seize by ruse:** approach under **routine-maintenance pretext**, then take the turrets at gunpoint. Always guarded (more technicians than guards; a handful, some resistance expected). With **no alert**, surprise is feasible; an alert brings reinforcements **unless drawn off by a crisis elsewhere**.
- **Cut the power:** Acrid is **not a military base** — defences run off the **starport power grid** with no complex backups; interrupt/switch off if the **control room** is captured, or blow **power conduits**.
- **Blast them:** position a ship so the turrets **can't return fire** and destroy them (not a comprehensive military installation). **Avoid magazine explosions** — they'd wreck the port and ruin the turrets for future defence.
→ **The truth to surface (Gera's blind spot):** with **no spaceborne component** the turrets are largely a short-term irrelevance — a noose to remove eventually. Their real value is **siege control**: hold **≥1 working turret** to control the port; get **both out of the equation** before relief ships can land. **No working port = strangled independence.** → **Complications:** an alert during the turret grab pulls security in fast; coordinate with a diversion (tie to Scene 6 timing).

### 6. Revolution! — the rising itself
**Trigger:** Zero hour — either the crew launch a **coordinated** rising, or the rising clock fires and it goes off **ill-coordinated** (Scene 2/3 trigger), or someone jumps the gun. → **What's here:** Ideally a **coordinated series of seizures** — security armouries, control rooms, power & life-support installations, the turrets, and the SDBs. **Realistically it's messy:** phases launch in the wrong order, spreading from a trigger incident as teams scramble to their start positions; Gera has compartmentalised missions and assigned backups, but there are no guarantees — expect "confused but escalating violence all over the port and main settlement." → **The fighting:** the ruling elite + security **fight hard** (they expect no mercy) — close-quarters and nasty: hand weapons, improvised grenades, handguns, shotguns, SMGs. **Few heavy weapons / few places to use them.** Either side **might breach a section** as a weapon (both have strong reasons not to). As it stabilises: **armed-band deathmatches** in some sections; **secured areas under repeated assault** in others. → **The endgame opening:** most likely **neither side fully wins** but an endgame becomes possible. If either thinks it can win outright, it regroups to finish the other; otherwise **negotiations** may begin. Control of **amenities** is decisive (if one side denies **power** but the other holds the **water supply**, the outcome is genuinely in doubt). → **Trickery & the crew's role:** negotiation can pick off **isolated pockets** (convince the air-processing-plant defenders they're the last remnant of a beaten faction and should surrender — when they're actually the **other side's main bargaining chip**). As **outsiders** with **no history of resentment**, the crew can act as **honourable brokers** of a surrender or new order — though **neither faction is guaranteed to honour** a deal the crew strike in good faith. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** resolve the rising as a multi-stage conflict — for each major objective (armouries / control rooms / power / life-support / water / turrets / SDBs) run an appropriate check or skirmish; apply the crew's training DM (Scene 3) and whether guns were delivered (Scene 2). Track a rough **rebel-control level**; a **decisive amenity capture** (power *and* water split between sides) sets up negotiation rather than total victory.

### 7. Counter-Revolution — PRQ strikes back
**Trigger:** The rising succeeds (or partly succeeds); PRQ moves to retake or recoup. → **What's here / the sequence (GM-Only details above):**
1. **First assault:** a **mercenary cruiser** of PRQ security/mercenaries hits the port. **Stop it** with seized SDB(s) + the crew's ship, or a **functional orbital defence**. If repelled, attackers **stand off and blockade**, opening negotiations.
2. **Blockade tightens:** chartered resupply merchants reinforce over **weeks** until it's **unbreakable**; Acrid runs short of spares (worse if the rising did heavy damage).
3. **Counter-revolution coordination:** PRQ contacts **planetside sympathisers** to seize key installations / force surrender. **Hunt covert transmissions** (Electronics(comms) / Investigate) to deny them coordination — without it, their attempts are **not well orchestrated**.
4. **The negotiator:** if PRQ can't retake Acrid in a few weeks, it offers the **"autonomous business unit" deal** (face-saving; Acrid self-governs, PRQ funds development and pretends it wasn't ousted). Money scales with how strongly the rebels hold the world; **a bold front** (overstating rebel strength — aided by denying PRQ reliable info) extracts more.
→ **The crew's leverage = cost.** Make retaking Acrid look too expensive and PRQ folds to the deal (or, with external support, withdraws). → **Complications:** **Gera resists compromise** (wants full independence). Resolve via the Negotiation/Persuasion scene (below). → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** model the blockade as a **clock** — each week without relief or a broken blockade, advance it; if it maxes before PRQ negotiates or the crew break it, Acrid is **strangled** (forced into the weak amnesty or collapse). Breaking the blockade / repelling the assault / external support each **reset or slow** the clock.

### 8. Negotiation & Persuading Gera (the real climax)
**Trigger:** PRQ opens talks (or the blockade forces the issue). → **What's here:** Two outcomes diverge on **external support**:
- **External support secured (Drinax / local power):** Acrid can survive **independent** if the retake is **defeated or deterred** → full **Haven**, owes the crew a favour.
- **No external support:** broker the **PRQ "autonomous business unit" deal** → Acrid effectively self-governs (a reasonable outcome where "everyone gets out with their skin intact").
→ **The hard part — Gera:** she hates PRQ and dreams of full independence; talk her into the compromise with **Persuade / Diplomat / Advocate** (apply DMs for how strongly the rebels hold the world, the crew's relationship with her, and a realistic appraisal of the strangulation threat). **If she refuses:** "there are others who might," and Acrid faces **another round of revolt and negotiation** before it settles (run an abbreviated repeat; possible leadership change among the rebels). → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** if persuasion fails and no external support exists, roll for the rebel council overriding Gera: `dice.py keyed 1d10 7+` — on a hit a rival rebel leader accepts the deal (Gera sidelined); otherwise the second round of fighting begins.

## Key NPCs

**Gera Hollis (revolution leader; ex-citizen industrial chemist; the patron)** — *(stats not given in source; run as a high-INT/EDU/SOC civilian leader, not a frontline combatant.)* Skills as appropriate: **Leadership**, **Persuade**, **Deception** (her "manipulation"), **Science (chemistry)/Engineer (chemical)**, Admin. **Motive:** regain her lost comfortable life and become Acrid's leader — committed to "doing right, but not for altruistic reasons." Runs the revolution personally (distrust + craves credit); a big personal risk. **Blind spots:** over-values the turrets; under-rates the need for external support and trade; resists any compromise short of full independence. *Play her: shrewd, driven, charismatic, prickly about credit — impressed by strategic thinking above her own level.*

**Worker Revolutionary (typical rebel; many chemists & mining engineers)** — STR 7 DEX 7 END 8 INT 7 EDU 6 SOC 5. Skills: **Vacc Suit 1–2** (hostile-environment natives), **Explosives 1** (improvised), Mechanic 1, Gun Combat (slug) 0, Athletics (end) 1. Armour: **Hostile-Environment Suit (+6, sealed)** — needed to survive Acrid's atmosphere; doubles as protection. Weapons: improvised hand weapons (workshop-made), **improvised grenades/explosives** (treat as TL-improvised demolition), scavenged shotgun/SMG **if** guns were smuggled in. **Motive:** freedom from debt-slavery; determined but **no military experience** (apply crew-training DMs from Scene 3). *Creative and nasty with explosives; dangerous to themselves in a sealed city.*

**PRQ Security Trooper (citizen/buffer class; defends the regime)** — STR 8 DEX 8 END 8 INT 7 EDU 7 SOC 6. Skills: **Gun Combat (slug) 1–2**, Melee 1, Vacc Suit 1, Tactics (military) 0, Recon 1. Armour: Hostile-Environment Suit (+6) or Flak Jacket (+5) indoors. Weapons: **Shotgun (4D)**, **Submachinegun/Autopistol (3D)**, the occasional **autorifle (3D)**. **Motive:** keep order and their privileged status; expect no mercy from the workers, so **fight hard** at close quarters. *Few heavy weapons; technicians outnumber guards at the turrets.*

**PRQ Mercenary / Security Trooper (counter-revolution assault force)** — STR 9 DEX 9 END 9 INT 7 EDU 7 SOC 6. Skills: **Gun Combat (slug or energy) 2**, **Vacc Suit 2**, Tactics (military) 1, Heavy Weapons 1, Melee 1, Recon 1. Armour: **Combat Armour (+12)** or Vacc-rated battle gear (sealed for boarding). Weapons: **Gauss Rifle (4D)** / **Laser Rifle (5D+3)** / **Autorifle (3D)**, grenades. **Motive:** retake the port / recoup PRQ's asset cost-effectively; arrives by mercenary cruiser. *The professional muscle of the counter-assault — far more dangerous than the home guard.*

**PRQ Negotiator (corporate fixer; counter-revolution endgame)** — *(non-combatant.)* Skills: **Advocate**, **Admin**, **Persuade**, Broker. **Motive:** end the affair acceptably for PRQ — offers amnesty first (weak), then the **face-saving "autonomous business unit"** deal; commits more money the stronger the rebels look. *Reads the real situation; a bold front by the crew shifts terms.*

**PRQ Sympathiser (planetside fifth column)** — *(varies.)* Former privileged employees, disaffected workers unhappy with Gera, or defeatists who think the rising is doomed. **Motive:** be rewarded/spared by PRQ; feed the response team intel and seize key installations. *Countered by hunting their covert transmissions.*

## Ships

**PRQ System Defence Boat ×2 (Acrid system pickets)** — Use the **High Guard standard SDB** as the type (the lighter boat, High Guard p.124; for a **greater challenge** use the heavier SDB, High Guard p.142). Generic profile: **no jump drive**, fast and heavily armed for its tonnage (typically ~400–800t, high Thrust, mixed beam-laser + missile turrets, military armour, fire control). **Crew may be less skilled than a hardened pirate band.** One patrols near the mainworld; the other is often down for maintenance/training or off in-system. **Role/tactics:** the campaign-long complication — best **soft-neutralised and captured** for the rebels; if not, they **blockade** the port (limited supplies force eventual surrender/negotiation) and will only fire on the surface under extreme circumstances. **Verify exact line in High Guard pp.124/142** if a number is load-bearing in a stand-up fight.

**PRQ Mercenary Cruiser (counter-revolution flagship)** — A **mercenary cruiser or similar** packed with PRQ security/mercenaries (see the **Type-C Mercenary Cruiser** profile in ships-of-the-reach.md as a model: ~HP 320, multiple dual pulse-laser + missile + sandcaster turrets, a **marine/security platoon**). **Role:** the first retake assault; if repelled it **stands off and blockades** and opens negotiations. Followed over weeks by **chartered resupply merchants** that make the blockade unbreakable. *Stoppable by seized SDBs + the crew's armed ship or a working orbital defence net.*

**Crew's ship (the swing factor)** — Not a PRQ asset, but the decisive variable: an armed crew ship can **disable/capture an SDB**, **break or impose** the blockade, and **repel the merc cruiser**. The Harrier is hugely capable here — but note **exposure**: a distinctive hull (the Harrier auto-flags watch lists) seen fighting PRQ near an Imperial trade route is a **Standing/PRI risk** (piracy-raiding.md).

## Rewards & Consequences
- **The prize — Acrid as a base.** Full success makes **Acrid a Haven**: no-questions-asked **shelter and repairs** at a **Class A starport with repair facilities** astride the Imperium–Hierate trade route, plus a population that **adores the crew as heroes** and a government that **owes them a favour**. A rare independent bolthole for raiding the Borderland lane and a Finale recruitment candidate.
- **Trade & economy:** post-rising, Acrid can export minerals/metals/chemicals + workshop goods and sell technical schooling — a legitimate income stream the crew helped seed (good for the "saviour" doctrine).
- **Captured hardware:** **soft-neutralised SDBs** can become rebel (and, by extension, crew-aligned) assets — a major military windfall if taken intact; a working **turret + missile stock** secures the port. Navy-grade missiles the crew supplied stay in Acrid's defences.
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest — no rescues):**
  - **A sealed city is lethal:** any breach (SDB fire on a section, a desperate tunnel-collapse, an errant improvised grenade) lets the corrosive atmosphere in and **kills hundreds**, wrecks infrastructure, and can hand victory to the other side. A no-guns rising leaning on improvised explosives is especially prone to this.
  - **The counter-assault is real:** the PRQ merc cruiser + security/mercenaries (Combat Armour, Gauss/Laser Rifles) can retake the port if the crew can't stop them; lose the space fight and Acrid is **blockaded into strangulation** — the rebels forced into the weak amnesty or outright collapse.
  - **Botched smuggling/theft** → PRQ clampdown → an **early, ill-coordinated rising** that's bloodily crushed (and conditions get *worse*).
  - **Gera's intransigence** can sink a winnable position: if she refuses a sensible PRQ deal with no external support, Acrid burns through **another round of revolt and negotiation** (and may need her sidelined).
  - **Without external support and without breaking the blockade,** the *best* realistic outcome is the **PRQ "autonomous business unit"** compromise — self-rule under a corporate fig-leaf, **not** a clean Haven.
