# Adventures — Index & Router

> Use this when: you need to find the right set-piece adventure or patron seed during play — pick one by region/phase/trigger, then open its file. This routes; the per-adventure files hold the scenes and secrets.

These are the campaign's **set adventures** (the ten Book-1 episodes) plus the Companion side-adventures, the **patron-seed catalogue** (11), and the optional rival-claimant mini-campaign (18). They are **SPOILER-GATED**: each file splits **Player-Facing** (safe to narrate) from **GM-Only — Truth & Secrets** (reveal only as earned). Run them as **mythic-gm keyed/available content, not rails** — surface a hook when a trigger fires, let the crew choose to engage, run Player-Facing first, honour any `dice.py keyed` must-happen beats, then apply each file's `## Empire / Reputation effects` and `## Threads & hooks onward`. Order is mostly free (see sequencing). Only the start (1) and the *Finale* (10) are effectively fixed; everything else is a menu. For the whole-campaign picture see **../00_overview.md**; for the victory track and how outcomes cash out see **../empire-reputation.md**.

## Suggested flow / sequencing
- **The canonical order is the PoD Book 1 sequence 1–10** (the Companion's episode numbering), but it is a **suggested sequence and a place to foreshadow, not a railroad** (../00_overview.md). With the exception of the start and the *Finale*, you do **not** have to run any episode, nor in order — insert your own mythic-gm-generated adventures freely, drop episodes, retime them.
- **Fixed bookends.** *Honour Among Thieves* (1) is the de-facto opener (the crew receive the marque + Harrier). *Finale* (10) is the **endgame** and the only other mandatory adventure — fire it when the late empire milestones are reached.
- **Phase mapping.** 1–2 sit in **Phase 1 (raiding & establishing)**; 3–9 fill **Phase 2 (building alliances)**; 9 is the **hinge into Phase 3**; 10 *is* Phase 3.
- **Companion adventures (12–17) and the patron seeds (11) slot into the sandbox** wherever the crew already are — mostly Phase 1–2, drop-in, reorderable. Patrons (11) are the connective tissue you run *between* and *to seed* the big episodes; many patron seeds are doorways into the scripted adventures.
- **Watch the timed ones.** Don't trigger a timed adventure until the crew can realistically engage it: *Ihatei!* (3) = a hard **25-week clock**; *Treasure Ship* (2) = a hard **12-week clock**; *The Game of Sun and Shadow* (6) plays out over **months** as a parallel clock alongside other adventures and empire-building.
- **Threaded dependencies (run these in relative order where they apply).** *Ihatei!* (3) recruits Kasiyl, who is the patron of *The Prodigal Outcast* (8); *The Prodigal Outcast* (8) seeds *Blood of the Star Dragon* (9) (Rao's scheming, Harrick's interference); *Blood of the Star Dragon* (9) sets who rules Drinax and whether the Zhodani back it — both of which **directly set the Finale's starting conditions**. *Friends in Dry Places* (14) shares Kteiroa/Utea with *Ihatei!* (3) (either may run first). *Liberty Port* (17) is the anti-GeDeCo mirror of *The Vorito Gambit* (7).
- **Shadows of Sindal** (18) is **optional** — a parallel rival-claimant arc that can run alongside Phases 2–3; it is not on the critical path.
- **Rhythm.** A few sandbox turns, then a patron job or scripted episode, then court time / consequences — not raid after raid. Vary hunting grounds.

## How each adventure is reached (triggers)
Adventures and patron seeds enter play **through the sandbox** — surface a hook, then let the crew choose (../00_overview.md "How adventures get triggered"). Four trigger types, cross-referenced to the gauges in **../empire-reputation.md** (world Attitudes / Standing) and **../00_overview.md**:
- **Patron** — a contact at a port, the court at Drinax, or a Random Event offers a job. The most frequent trigger; covers all of **11** and the patron-driven episodes (4, 5, 8, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16, 17). Many patron seeds *are* the seeds of the bigger episodes.
- **Travel to a world** — the crew jump into the system where an episode is seated: Clarke/Torpol cluster → **1**; a treasure-ship rumour near Arunisiir → **2**; near Tech-World → **4**; Noricum/Sindal → **5**; Marduk → **12**; the Borderland route → **13**/**15**; Thebus → **16**; Sperle → **17**. Place scripted content in systems the crew are heading to.
- **Reputation / standing milestone** — as the crew climb the empire track (../empire-reputation.md) or anger a power: enough raiding near Drinax **summons** *The Game of Sun and Shadow* (**6**); Imperial Standing sets its difficulty; growing *ihatei* pressure foreshadows **3**; court intrigue ripens into **9**; reaching the **late empire milestones** fires the *Finale* (**10**). Starting in **1** banks a permanent recruitment DM that pays off in **10**.
- **Random Event (Mythic)** — roll on each new scene; a Remote Event / new NPC / Move Toward a Thread is the natural moment to introduce a rumour, patron, or the hook for the next episode. The **Rumours of the Reach (D66)** table in **11** feeds these interpretations.
- **GM clocks** — for timed episodes (3, 6; and the hard window on 2), once triggered, track the clock as a Mythic thread and let it run regardless of detours.

When an adventure resolves, apply its `## Empire / Reputation effects` to **../empire-reputation.md** and push its `## Threads & hooks onward` onto the mythic-gm Lists, then return to the sandbox loop.
