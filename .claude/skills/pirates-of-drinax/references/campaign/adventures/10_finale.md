# Finale

> Use this when: running the campaign's endgame — the Declaration of the reborn Kingdom of Drinax, recruiting worlds, the trade-route **blockade**, **negotiations** with the Imperium and Aslan Hierate, and the **final Aslan assault** on Drinax. This is the fixed climax; it cashes out the entire empire track. The crunch (recruitment rolls, Fighting Strength, blockade procedure, policy DMs, reward tiers, negotiation difficulty) lives in **empire-reputation.md** — this file holds the scenes, secrets, NPCs, fleets and endings; cross-ref it constantly.

**Logline:** *Alea iacta est.* The ruler of Drinax gathers the rulers of the Trojan Reach, proclaims the Kingdom reborn, and dares the great powers to acknowledge it — the crew must recruit allies, throttle the Imperium–Aslan trade route with a pirate blockade, negotiate recognition from two empires, then hold the Floating Palace against an Aslan invasion fleet. Reborn kingdom or annihilation; no middle ground.

## At a glance
- **Locations:** **Begins and ends on Drinax** (0603 Tlaiowaha, the Floating Palace). Spans **Sindal, Tobia, Tlaiowaha and Borderland subsectors** — the Imperium–Aslan trade route across the Reach. Travel during recruitment/stragglers and (optionally) relocating the final battle.
- **Threat/level:** Campaign climax. **Lethal and final** — if the crew lose the last battle, **everyone dies** (no escape after the final roll). Mechanically a **framework of dice-rolling + chart-checking** (recruitment, blockade, negotiation, abstract fleet battle) married to roleplay; not a dungeon crawl.
- **Est. length:** Long (multi-session endgame). Five sequential parts.
- **Prerequisites:** **Depends entirely on the accumulated empire/reputation state.** Before starting, you must know: the crew's **fleet Fighting Strength**; **world Attitudes** near Drinax; **who rules Drinax** (King Oleb / Prince Harrick / Princess Rao / the crew — set by *Blood of the Star Dragon*); the crew's **Standing** with the Imperium and Hierate; which campaign events landed (Lost Worlds, Kasiyl's fate, Zhodani support, GeDeCo deal, Sindalian WMDs). Crews who began in *Honour Among Thieves* get **DM+1** to recruitment.
- **Arc position:** **The fixed Phase-3 endgame** (PoD1 ch.13). The only mandatory adventure besides the start. Run when the late empire milestones are reached (a power base, allies in place — empire-reputation.md "Milestones"). Hinges directly off *Blood of the Star Dragon* (who rules, Zhodani support) and pulls callbacks from every prior episode.

## Player-Facing
- **The summons (the trigger event).** The ruler of Drinax declares it is time for the final phase of the great plan. A major event is staged to draw dignitaries and rulers from across the Reach to the Floating Palace. Pick the in-fiction trigger (the crew may shape it):
  - The **state funeral of King Oleb** (if he died in *Blood of the Star Dragon*).
  - The **coronation** of a new King or Queen of Drinax.
  - The **wedding of Princess Rao**.
  - A **conference on the future of the Trojan Reach**.
  - A **celebration / feast** called by the ruler.
  - **News of an impending Aslan attack.**
- **The presenting task.** The crew can openly perceive that Drinax intends to announce the **rebirth of the Kingdom of Drinax**, the last outpost of the old Sindalian Empire that once ruled the whole Reach. They are asked to help: gather more allies, build relationships with allied worlds, prepare and stage the trigger event, and shape Royal Policy. After the Declaration, the plan is a **blockade** of the trade route to force the Imperium and Hierate to the negotiating table — then survive whatever fleet comes for them.
- **The open stakes.** The crew know this likely ends the campaign. They know Drinax's advantages are its **history** (the Sindalian legacy) and its **science** (the Scholar's Tower, a TL15 knowledge repository, brimming with blueprints even the Imperium has not mastered). They know the great powers may not take a "petty Outrim Void polity" seriously without a real show of force.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **This adventure is a framework, not a plot.** Marry every die roll to a decision, scene, or beat of colour. Do not just call for rolls — dramatise (e.g., a snarling Oghman warlord insulting the "pathetic empire" before the Recruitment roll). Optionally grant DMs/rerolls for excellent roleplay.
- **Who rules matters.** Starting Royal Policy and several beats depend on the ruler set by *Blood of the Star Dragon*: **Oleb** (Gov 3/Sec 1/Tech 0), **Prince Harrick** (0/2/2), **Princess Rao** (2/2/1). **If the crew control the throne** (took over, or ruler is incapacitated/in their thrall) they set policy freely. (Policy DMs: empire-reputation.md.)
- **"Our Own Empire" branch.** If the crew abandoned Drinax to build their **own** pirate empire, four options: (1) ignore this adventure and improvise an ending; (2) have them make a **bid for legitimacy** — alter Royal Policy rules to fit what *they* can offer, but run the rest as written (Imperium/Hierate react to a wholly new power as they would to old Drinax); (3) run as written, and during Securing the Empire have the ruler of Drinax try to **re-recruit** the crew; (4) run as written, then continue afterward as the crew **pick apart the corpse** of the revived Kingdom.
- **Marked decisions (!).** Some choices have non-obvious mechanical fallout later. **Warn the crew in general terms.** Chief example: leaving gaps in the blockade makes the Imperium/Hierate **less wary**, weakening later negotiations ("you've got to scare them to get them to the table"). Also: breaking a Royal-Policy promise in a treaty costs Fighting Strength.
- **The blockade is the lever.** Oleb's true plan: flood the trade route with so many pirates/warships that the Imperium concludes it is **cheaper to make peace and pay Drinax's "reasonable" taxes** than to crush the new Kingdom. The Imperium ignores petty polities (Senlis Foederate, Strend Cluster get a fancy Christmas card, nothing more); only a **trade-strangling blockade** earns real negotiation. The Aslan are usually even less bothered — until a clan takes offence.
- **The final battle is rigged to the choices.** *Who* attacks at the end is determined entirely by which treaties the crew signed (see Ships/Fleets). The attacker always brings **capital ships** — virtually invulnerable to average pirate weapons — so a higher Fighting Strength alone may not save them.
- **The Eikhoifiruah.** If the attackers are Aslan (even ihatei), the capital ship among them is the **Eikhoifiruah**, the antique heavy bomber that **burned Drinax two centuries ago**, leaving only the floating palace. It returns either as a deliberate Ahroay'if provocation, or sold-on and crewed by a minor clan, or arranged by Hierate lords to humble the upstart humans. Thematic gut-punch; it tries to bombard the surface again.
- **Hidden Aslan agendas.** Each of the five Aslan clan representatives has a **secret agenda or exploitable opportunity** the crew can only discover through roleplay or Aslan-culture insight (not listed for them — see Part 4).
- **Hidden betrayals available.** If the crew cut the **GeDeCo Bargain** in *The Vorito Gambit*, GeDeCo will "take care of them" if they let Drinax fall — the crew may deliberately jump out of the final battle at the key moment so the Floating Palace is destroyed (the Jaskarl Plan predicts Drinax's *myth* will then inspire the Reach to defy the Aslan). The crew may also seize the Kingdom for themselves if they let the ruler perish (Death to Drinax event).

## The five-part structure (overview)
1. **The Long-Awaited Hour** — preparing: stage the event, gather/build allies, set Royal Policy.
2. **The Rebirth of the Kingdom** — recruitment rolls; collect stragglers; proclaim the Kingdom; ennoble the crew (rewards scale with size).
3. **Shouting into the Void** — the blockade: assign Fighting Strength, block the jump-2 or jump-3 route.
4. **The Knife Edge** — negotiations with the Imperium and the five Aslan clans (difficulty set by Standing).
5. **The Battle of Drinax** — the final Aslan/Imperial assault; abstract fleet battle; victory or total annihilation.

## Scenes / Locations

### PART 1 — The Long-Awaited Hour (Preparing the Event)
**Trigger:** The ruler declares the great plan's final phase; the crew help prepare. → **What's here:** Four preparation activities, all optional, all boosting later odds:
- **Stage the trigger event.** Once the crew decide on the event's nature and setting (grandest state funeral in history, a royal wedding fit for the gods, etc.), the GM picks the most apt **characteristic + skill** and calls for a **Formidable (14+)** test. **Success → DM+1 to ALL Recruitment rolls.** Warn them: even an awe-inspiring event rarely sways delegates on this scale — they must work *wonders*. Optionally they hire the galaxy's greatest party-planner/architect/speechwriter/comic (an adventure in itself). Emphasising Drinax's **history** and **science** (the Scholar's Tower) at the event improves the impression on wavering worlds.
- **Gather more allies.** Visit any star systems not yet contacted; seek Patrons who might push those governments toward tolerating piracy / joining Drinax.
- **Build relationships.** Visit allied worlds (Suspicious+) and complete Patron missions / donate money or facilities to move them **toward Haven** (one step at a time — empire-reputation.md "Shifting Attitude").
- **Shape Royal Policy.** The crew help set **Government / Security / Technology** (each 0–3). One attempt to nudge the ruler's ratings: **Persuade or Diplomat** (or **Carouse** with Oleb) — apply the **Effect** to move ratings up/down. Starting ratings by ruler (above); the crew set freely if they hold the throne. Policy decides how easily each world recruits (Policy DMs vs. each world's Profile — empire-reputation.md).
→ **Complications:** Policy is a **marked (!) decision** — choices reshape the whole recruitment board. Pre-compute recruitment Final DMs (Attitude + Policy + Other) **at end of session** once policy is locked; roll dramatically next session.

### PART 2 — The Rebirth of the Kingdom (Recruitment & the Declaration)
**Trigger:** The Reach's rulers gather on Drinax. → **What's here:**
1. **Initial Recruitment rolls.** For each prospective world (Appendix 2 list — empire-reputation.md), roll **2D, target 8+**, summing **Attitude DM** (Haven +6 … Unfriendly −4; **Hostile never joins**) + **Policy DM** + **Other DM**. *Honour Among Thieves* crews: **DM+1**. **Telepathic Coercion:** if *Blood of the Star Dragon* left Drinax with secret **Zhodani** support, roll **1D** — apply **DM+2** to that many attempts. **Lost Worlds** (destroyed in play) are removed and give no Fighting Strength. Aslan worlds almost never recruit (a clan *might* switch sides).
2. **Collecting the stragglers.** A **2D-week** window: a crew member **visits a declining world in person** for a second roll (must have an existing relationship with its government *or* pass a GM-set challenge — "defeat the King of the Southern Continent of Scaladon in a poetry slam, make an Art check"). Second roll changes: may **justify the policy** with Diplomat/Advocate (Difficult 10+ if Policy DM −1; Very Difficult 12+ if −2; Formidable 14+ if −3 or worse) to offset the penalty; **proximity** DM−1 if no Kingdom worlds within 3 parsecs, DM+2 if all within 3 parsecs are loyal. They must be **back on Drinax before the window closes**.
3. **Proclaim the Kingdom.** Once all willing worlds are known, the ruler proclaims the rebirth. The crew are asked to produce their **Letter of Marque**; the ruler reads it and declares their crimes **(any committed within Kingdom borders)** retroactively forgiven. He then **ennobles** the crew or grants another reward of their choice — tier set by Kingdom size (Rewards, below).
→ **Complications at the Event** (use as scenes):
  - **Old Grudges** — two delegations nearly brawl (Dustbelt Worlds vs. Oghman Clans; Byrni Star-Guard loyalists vs. the crew's pirate allies; Aslan allies like Kasiyl of the Ahroay'if vs. worlds threatened by Aslan invasion). Intervene or the gathering is marred.
  - **Unexpected Offers** — the Reach is about to change; outside powers may court well-known crew with post-Kingdom positions: a seat on **Theev's pirate council**; **command of a border-world planetary navy**; **command of a derelict starbase** off the trade route (newly busy if the blockade reroutes traffic); **military advisor to the Floriani League**.
  - **Skyquake** — the Floating Palace's grav-drives fail briefly and the whole palace lurches (coup damage, or just centuries-old neglect) → panic the crew must manage.
  - **Lost Friends/Enemies** — bring back career-creation Contacts/Rivals/Enemies as ghosts of the past to show how far the crew have come (a former Rival now an Imperial fleet commander; an old enemy who sits down for a quiet drink instead of a duel). Do **not** start new plots this late.

### PART 3 — Shouting into the Void (The Blockade)
**Trigger:** Kingdom declared; the great powers must be *forced* to recognise it. → **What's here:** Mount a blockade of the **Imperium–Aslan trade route** (Appendix 2/4). Three sets of worlds: the **jump-3 route**, the **jump-2 route**, and the **Backwaters**. **Block either the jump-3 OR jump-2 route to succeed.** Blocking both, or Backwaters, buys **negotiation leverage** (and spare blockaded systems can be dropped as concessions later). Full procedure and Fighting-Strength math: **empire-reputation.md** "Fighting Strength" / "The blockade." Summary:
1. Assign Fighting Strength dice to systems (unassigned dice held in reserve). Crew / allied captains with **Tactics (naval) 3+** may command systems.
2. Per system: optional **Tactics (naval)** check (task chain) → **roll the assigned FS dice** → total **beats the system's target number** = **Blockaded**. **Discard dice that rolled odd (1/3/5)** (attrition/return home); a **Leadership** check halves casualties by half the Effect.
3. Repeat for all systems; when an entire route is blocked, declare the blockade complete.
→ **DMs:** recruited Kingdom system **+1**; sabotaging enemy planetary defences/escorts **+2** (with requisite checks); starport agents (social/stealth piracy) **+2**; **gaps** in the blockade **−1 (one gap) / −3 (two gaps)** to *all other* rolls — Wildemann is the obvious "leave it off" choice (10 parsecs coreward of Tyokh). **Recruited Planets** get +1.
→ **Complications (run as scenes):** a civilian freighter tries to run the blockade — fire on it? a vessel gets in trouble fuel-skimming — risk the crew's ship to rescue? a **Florian League** ship asks to be let through (smuggling? undermining the blockade?); **Imperial diplomats threaten** a blockaded world's government — how do the crew reassure a wavering ally?
→ **"Screw your blockade, we're pirates!"** The crew may substitute any other show of force (seize a key world, run war games, wipe out Theev). Whatever it is must prove the Kingdom is a force to be reckoned with; the GM sets a suitable risk and **saps 20–50% of their Fighting Strength** (clever tactics reduce losses).
→ **Failed blockade:** if they completely fail to block either main route, the Drinaxian plan is in dire jeopardy — throw all remaining FS dice, discard odds, and proceed (negotiations will be far harder).
→ **mythic-gm keyed trigger** (a *must-happen* dramatic beat during the blockade): force at least one civilian-ship dilemma as a moral pressure point. Frame it, then resolve the crew's choice (no roll needed unless they shoot/rescue).

### PART 4 — The Knife Edge (Negotiations)
**Trigger:** Blockade established; the crew demand the great powers parley. → **What's here:** The crew's **Standings** now decide everything (piracy-raiding.md owns the Standing rolls; this is where they cash out).
- **Pre-checks:** Standing **−40+ with both** powers → they're hunted by a rare Imperium/Hierate bipartisanship (use "Pirates? You Mean The Other Guys" — *if both want you dead, you are dead*). Standing **−40 with only one** → that one won't negotiate. Otherwise negotiate in order of **highest Standing first**.
- **"Pirates? You Mean the Other Guys!"** If the crew hid their connection to Drinax, one of them makes a **Deception (INT)** check: success → **halve** their Standings with both sides (the resurgent Kingdom's "suspiciously experienced naval commanders" aren't openly the raiders); failure → both Standings **−1D**.
- **The Imperial negotiation** (two phases): both sides propose terms = temporary **Standing adjustments** (Imperial Treaty Handout, p.275); then the lead negotiator makes a **Diplomat (SOC)** check, difficulty by final Standing (table below). Fail → may retry only **after a new concession** (a clause that boosts Standing). **Dealbreakers:** the Imperium **will not accept** any treaty that doesn't **lift the blockade**; Drinax **must** retain the right to **levy some tolls**. **Broken Promises:** a treaty clause that contradicts Royal Policy (e.g. agreeing "all piracy punished by death" with Security 0) → roll all remaining FS dice, discard **1s** (minor breach) or **1s and 2s** (major breach). Treaties can't be broken once signed. *"It's Your Kingdom":* if no crew member has good Diplomat, the ruler can negotiate (assume **DM+6** across SOC+Diplomat).

| Standing (Imperium) | Diplomat difficulty |
|---|---|
| 20+ (Ally) | Average (8+) |
| 6 to 20 (Tolerated) | Difficult (10+) |
| 5 to −5 (Ignored) | Very Difficult (12+) |
| −6 to −20 (Irritant) | Formidable (14+) |
| −21 to −40 (Hated) | Virtually Impossible (16+) |
| −41 or worse (Enemy of the State) | No deal possible |

  - **Imperial representative:** **Standing positive → Viscountess Palaphontane of Gazulin** (Darrian-descended; wants Drinax as an anti-Aslan bulwark, accepts the trade hit). **Standing negative → Vice-Admiral Krond** (from *Game of Sun and Shadow*; vain/cruel, but politically backed — possibly married to the Duke of Tobia's daughter; here to threaten Drinax into lifting the blockade). If Krond was killed/discredited, use **Vice-Admiral Steyland**.
- **The Aslan negotiation.** The **five clans** most interested in Drinax each send a representative; **each has a secret agenda the crew must uncover by roleplay/Aslan-culture insight** (not handed to them). Apply each clan's adjustment to **overall Hierate Standing** unless dealing with clans **separately** (below). The Dealbreaker/Broken-Promise rules apply as for the Imperium.
  - **Ahroay'if:** if the crew reinstated **Kasiyl** (and parted well), *he* negotiates → **+10 Standing**, and he offers **+2 Standing per Faction-Strength die** the crew hand to Aslan control (the Ahroay'if are likely at war with the Tokolfeahr again). If Kasiyl is still renegade, his sister **Aual** comes; if the crew convinced Kasiyl to stay in exile → **+5** (lost if they *handed Kasiyl over*). Aual wants only to keep her brother exiled/dead and Ahroay'if–Imperial trade flowing; play her pro-human, less alien.
  - **Tlaiowaha:** the great trader clan sends **Staisarleal**, an immensely old, overweight Aslan who nods off and gambles. **Carouse** him into a card game → add a **Gambler (INT)** Effect to Standing. If the crew reinstated Kasiyl, Staisarleal instead wants to **undermine the rebellious Ahroay'if** — engineer that (betray Kasiyl, or cut a separate deal excluding the Ahroay'if) → **+5**. Harsher levies on Aslan than Imperial merchants → he's furious, **−10** on top of other modifiers.
  - **Yerlyaruiwo** (guardians of Aslan honour): their champion duellist **Ehalra** attends. **Defeat Ehalra in single combat → +10 Standing.** If the crew never crossed the Yerlyaruiwo, Ehalra stays aloof. If they *did* cross them, honour demands revenge: he repeatedly, publicly **demands an honour duel** with a crew member — refusing to fight him **−20 Standing**.
  - **Syiosuis** (spies/assassins): the mysterious **Yekh** (from *The Prodigal Outcast*). The Syiosuis care little for the Reach and mostly want to make trouble for other clans — she suggests dealing with clans **separately** and offers **+20 Standing** if the crew **exclude the Tokolfeahr and Yerlyaruiwo** from the final deal.
  - **Tokolfeahr** (militants): a male, **Hrilya**, brings ships and immediately proposes a **Limited War for control of Asim**, gambling the other clans won't tolerate a human power on their border. Accept the limited war → **+10**; refuse to fight → **−10**.
- **Separate Deals (the cats vs. the single will).** The Imperium acts with one will; the Hierate is "a squabbling bag of cats." The crew may approach clans individually: start from current Standing, apply only that clan's adjustments + that clan's special offers. **Trade Levies and Imperial Relations must be identical for all clans**; everything else is per-clan. Side benefit table (cut clans out → adjust remaining clans):

| Clans cut out | Adjustment to remaining clans |
|---|---|
| 1 (4 remain) | +5 |
| 2 (3 remain) | +10 |
| 3 (2 remain) | +5 |
| 4 (1 remains) | −5 |

  (The Aslan like having weaker clans to prey on, but dislike being left out in front — a target.) Decide cuts **before** rolling; re-opening a cut clan needs a fresh concession.
→ **Optional split-party:** run the crew as Drinaxian negotiators vs. the Imperial/Aslan reps (assign each rep a goal), or run both negotiations simultaneously.

### PART 5 — The Battle of Drinax (The Final Assault)
**Trigger:** Negotiations concluded (or failed); an invasion fleet approaches. **Whose fleet depends on the treaties** (see Ships/Fleets). The crew may move the battle to another system, meeting the force en route — **−1 Fighting Strength per parsec** from Drinax (logistics). → **What's here:** an abstract fleet battle. The invaders are mostly smaller ships **plus one or more capital ships** (true warships, near-invulnerable to pirate weapons) — so more ships ≠ guaranteed win.
- **Engagements (each round):** both sides roll **all their Fighting Strength dice** for an Engagement total. **Odd dice (1/3/5) count but are destroyed end of round; 2/4 always count; a 6 counts only for a side that still has a Capital Ship.** Higher total wins the round and picks **one** benefit:
  - **Tactical Advantage** — next round, enemy dice rolling **1** don't count (double advantage cancels enemy 3s; triple cancels 5s).
  - **Press the Attack** — remove one enemy FS die per **5-point** margin (round up).
  - **Disable Capital Ship** — remove one enemy capital ship.
  - **Force Conclusion** — if your total was **>2×** the enemy's, you may try to end the battle next round (succeed if again >2×; else you overextend and the enemy picks a benefit).
- **Abstract ship damage:** each round roll **D3** hits on the crew's own ship(s) (DM+2 if their side lost the round); **each hit = 4D damage**.
- **Traveller actions (one per crew member per round; +2 difficulty per extra crew doing the same; task chains allowed):**
  - **Seize Tactical Advantage** — Tactics (naval) (INT/EDU); Difficult (10+) if enemy FS higher. Win = +1 Tactical Advantage; fail = lose one (can blunder and hand it to the enemy).
  - **Lead the Attack** — Pilot/Gunner (DEX); Effect 1–5 removes one enemy FS, 6+ removes three; fail = own ship takes 4D.
  - **Inspire the Fleet** — Leadership (INT/SOC); add Effect to this round's Engagement total.
  - **Defensive Tactics** — Tactics (naval) (INT/EDU); on success save FS dice that rolled odd (Effect-many, not adding to total; or half-Effect-many that *do* add).
  - **Boarding Action** — Melee (STR/END) at chosen difficulty; success scales benefit (Average: −1 enemy FS; Difficult: −1 enemy FS + gain 1 FS; Hard: −1 FS + −1 enemy capital ship; Formidable: that + gain a capital ship); fail = 4D damage.
  - **Heroic Sacrifice** (the crew member dies; needs their own ship): **Suicide Run** (destroy an enemy capital ship); **Into the Teeth of the Enemy** (Pilot/Gunner DEX, reduce enemy FS by Effect); **Draw Their Fire** (pick 1/3/5 — pirate dice rolling that number aren't destroyed this round).
- **Events in the battle (use as needed):**
  - **The Challenge** — enemy commander taunts ("no pirate has ever won in true battle against a professional navy!"); a witty rejoinder = **+2 Engagement** round one.
  - **Head-Hunters** — enemy boards the crew's command ship (task chain Electronics (sensors)→Pilot to avoid, else a boarding action).
  - **Lord Wrax's Assault** — Wrax leads the old Star Guard's few antiquated SDBs into battle: **+4 Fighting Strength**.
  - **Distracted by Loot** — some of the crew's pirates break off to loot a disabled enemy ship — how do the crew rein them in?
  - **Planetary Bombardment** — the **Eikhoifiruah** lines up to bombard the Floating Palace's surface — how do the crew stop it?
  - **Flight of the Hawk Warriors** — Drinax's winged Hawk Warriors fly up in vacc suits/grav-belts; some carry **nuclear mines** (last of the Sindalian arsenal) and **destroy one enemy Capital Ship**.
  - **The Palace Rises** — if the day seems lost, the Floating Palace itself rises off Drinax, grav-engines redlined, venting atmosphere, shedding gargoyles into a ring of relics — it won't survive, but its **massive defence cannons add +10 Fighting Strength**.
  - **Death to Drinax** — the enemy commander rams their flagship at the Floating Palace; the crew stop it **or let the ruler perish and seize the Kingdom for themselves**.
- **Decision points (unresolved business):**
  - **The GeDeCo Bargain** (if struck in *Vorito*) — deliberately jump out at the key moment, let the Aslan destroy the palace (GeDeCo's Jaskarl Plan wants Drinax to become an inspiring *myth*).
  - **The Treasure of Sindal** — if the crew still hold the Sindalian WMD cache, turn it on the Aslan fleet: **−2D enemy Fighting Strength**.
  - **Escape!** — if the pirates are losing, warn the crew they will die if their side loses (no escape after the final roll). Do they bug out before the last die roll?
→ **mythic-gm keyed trigger** (the climactic *must-happen* beat): when one side nears zero FS or a Force Conclusion looks live, narrate the decisive turn and the crew's last action **before** the final Engagement roll — this is the campaign's last moment of agency.
→ **End:** battle ends when one side **Forces a Conclusion** or is reduced to **zero Fighting Strength**.

## Key NPCs
*(Reconstructed; the source PDF interleaved Aslan-rep text — Ehalra's printed block carries a duellist's Melee (blade) 6 + Crescent Blade, consistent with the Yerlyaruiwo champion. Other reps are described in Part 4 rather than stat-blocked in the source.)*

**Ehalra (Yerlyaruiwo clan Envoy & duellist champion; honour-duel opponent)** — Aslan male, age 52. STR 10 DEX 13 END 12 INT 7 EDU 8 SOC 10. Skills: Melee (blade) 6, Melee (natural) 4, Gun Combat (slug) 3, Diplomat 2, Vacc Suit 2, Advocate 1. Armour: Ceremonial Armour (+4) + Subdermal Armour (+3). Weapons: **Crescent Blade (2D+3)**; Yeheal Autorifle (3D, Auto 2). Motive: keeper of Aslan honour; if the crew ever crossed the Yerlyaruiwo, honour demands he kill one of them in a duel — defeating him in single combat is worth **+10 Hierate Standing**.

**Kasiyl (Ahroay'if; from *The Prodigal Outcast*)** — the exiled-then-restored Aslan prince. If reinstated and on good terms, he negotiates for the Ahroay'if (**+10 Standing**; offers +2 Standing per Faction-Strength die handed to Aslan control). Play him with swagger, "a nine-foot housecat" pouncing on his friends. (Full stat block: *The Prodigal Outcast* file.)

**Aual (Ahroay'if; Kasiyl's sister)** — pro-human, comfortable with human culture; play her less alien. Wants only her brother safely exiled/dead and Ahroay'if–Imperial trade preserved. **+5 Standing** if the crew kept Kasiyl in exile (lost if they handed him over).

**Staisarleal (Tlaiowaha trader clan)** — immensely old, overweight, drowsy, inveterate gambler. **Carouse → card game → add Gambler (INT) Effect to Standing.** Wants the Ahroay'if kept in line; harsher Aslan-than-Imperial levies enrage him (**−10**).

**Yekh (Syiosuis; spies/assassins; from *The Prodigal Outcast*)** — mysterious, here to sow discord among clans. Offers **+20 Standing** if the crew deal with clans separately and **exclude the Tokolfeahr and Yerlyaruiwo**.

**Hrilya (Tokolfeahr; militants)** — male, brings a ship detachment; proposes a **Limited War for Asim**. Accept the war **+10**; refuse to fight **−10**.

**Viscountess Palaphontane of Gazulin (Imperial negotiator — positive Standing)** — Darrian-descended, golden-skinned, graceful. Wants Drinax as an anti-Aslan bulwark and accepts the trade-revenue hit. The "friendly" Imperial face.

**Vice-Admiral Krond (Imperial negotiator — negative Standing; from *Game of Sun and Shadow*)** — vain, cruel, arrogant, but politically backed (possibly married to the Duke of Tobia's daughter). Here to threaten Drinax into lifting the blockade. **If killed/discredited earlier, replace with Vice-Admiral Steyland.**

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(This adventure **is** the resolution of the empire track — apply via empire-reputation.md throughout, not just at the end.)*
- **This is where the whole track cashes out.** The crew's accumulated **world Attitudes** become recruitment-roll DMs (Haven +6 … Unfriendly −4; Hostile never joins); their **fleet + recruited-system Fighting Strength** decides the blockade and the final battle; their **Imperium/Hierate Standing** sets every negotiation difficulty and (with the treaties signed) **chooses who attacks at the end**. **Lost Worlds** are removed from recruitment and contribute no FS. **Royal Policy** (set here) gates which worlds join via Policy DMs.
- **Doctrine pays off or backfires.** A **subtle/saviour** campaign tends to leave higher Attitudes and Standing → easier recruitment and negotiation. An **overt/intimidation** campaign may have raised some FS fast but capped Attitudes and worsened Standing — surfacing as harder rolls and a nastier attacker. (empire-reputation.md "Two doctrines.")
- **Standing crossings re-rate ports** (apply before computing recruitment DMs): 20+ (Ally) with a power → its ports jump to Friendly (+SOC 2 in its space); −40+ → non-Friendly ports drop a level (and that power won't negotiate). Recompute affected recruitment DMs.
- **Outcomes feeding the track / aftermath:**
  - **Victory:** the Kingdom (size N) is the permanent state of the Reach; every recruited world is now a Kingdom member; the crew are nobles. No further per-world shifts needed unless play continues.
  - **Defeat:** Drinax is destroyed (a catastrophic Lost World) and the crew are dead/executed — the track ends.
  - **GeDeCo betrayal ending:** Drinax falls *deliberately*; treat as the crew choosing power/myth over the Kingdom (record the GeDeCo alliance honoured).
  - **Death to Drinax / seizing the throne:** the crew may end as rulers of their **own** polity even on the Floating Palace's ashes (the "pirate empire of your own" ending).
- **No new Attitude shifts are *mandated* mid-adventure** beyond recruitment results and the deeds done in Parts 1–2 (each "build relationships" success = a step toward Haven as usual).

## Threads & hooks onward / aftermath
- **This is the campaign's terminus.** It resolves, rather than seeds, the long game. After it, play continues only if the crew want it (campaign/00_overview.md "Possible endings"):
  - **Restored Kingdom** → the crew rule as nobles/admirals of a recognised Reach power (Emperor of Sindal at 16+ worlds); a Rao/Harrick marriage may stand; new campaigns can spin off governing it, defending it, or expanding it.

  - **Annihilation** → the worst case; the Reach falls to the Aslan.
