# The Vorito Gambit

> Use this when: the crew capture a courier with a GeDeCo data drum, unmask the merchant Rachando as a spy, send a Drinax delegate to the opening of Vorito's new highport, and run a timed heist into GeDeCo's secret Vault — culminating in the Jaskarl hologram's offer. Covers the *Exalted Spirit* capture, the data drum, choosing the Delegate, investigating/interrogating Rachando, the GeDeCo assassins, the ceremony at Vorito Highport, and the Countdown break-in.

**Logline:** A captured data drum exposes GeDeCo as a doomsday conspiracy that plans to feed Drinax to the Aslan as a "lightning rod" — and when the crew heist GeDeCo's secret Vault, a dead psychohistorian's hologram offers them power, resources, and protection in exchange for letting their own kingdom burn on schedule.

## At a glance
- **Locations:** Open anywhere in Tlaiowaha or an adjacent subsector (the *Exalted Spirit* capture). → **Drinax** (Floating Palace 0603, choosing the Delegate + investigating Rachando). → **Vorito Highport** (Vorito 0709, the ceremony and the Vault heist). Six parsecs Drinax→Vorito.
- **Threat/level:** Mid-to-late campaign. The conspiracy reveal is a **major arc pivot**, not a fight. Combat threat is **high but optional** — GeDeCo Assassins / Elite Guards / Combat Drones are lethal (Battledress +22, Gauss Rifle 4D, Laser Rifle 5D+3); the heist rewards stealth, deception and tradecraft over firefights. The Vault is **not winnable by force** — Jaskarl's offer is the real climax.
- **Est. length:** Long (capture → home intrigue → off-world heist). 3–5 sessions.
- **Prerequisites:** The crew should be an established pirate power with influence at Oleb's court (so their opinion picks the Delegate) and ideally a reputation that precedes them (Rachando/GeDeCo react to it). Designed to follow several earlier adventures — cameos pull in NPCs from *Honour Among Thieves*, *Treasure Ship*, *The Demon's Eye*, *Ihatei!*, *The Treasure of Sindal*, *The Game of Sun and Shadow*.
- **Arc position:** **The conspiracy keystone.** This is where the campaign's hidden engine — the Jaskarl Plan and GeDeCo's intent to sacrifice Drinax — is exposed. The choice in the Vault (take the deal / refuse / destroy the Vault) and whether the crew later flee Drinax is **resolved in the *Finale*.** GeDeCo is a **+10 FS** allied power if courted (empire-reputation.md).

## Player-Facing
- **The sour jump (hook):** A Far Trader jumps in right beside the crew — well outside the 100-diameter limit, riding a legendary streak of bad luck (a "sour jump"), with a full cargo bay. It is the *Exalted Spirit*, a centuries-old rustbucket familiar across the Dustbelt, captained by **Fria Belsen**. As she turns to flee, a hull panel cracks and leaks air **from what should be a fuel tank** — a tell that there is a hidden compartment. A juicy, suspicious prize.
- **What the capture reveals:** Aboard is MCr8 of cargo (40t Advanced Machine Parts, 10t Advanced Electronics, 10t Robotics), a hidden smuggling compartment holding MCr2 in gemstones, and a **TL14 data drum**. The drum has quietly collected encoded status reports from GeDeCo agents in dozens of systems over the years. Belsen, if questioned, admits she runs supply contracts for GeDeCo and that they installed the drum; she never knew who the agents were.
- **The low-security trail (presenting problem):** The drum's communications log (easy to read; the encrypted payload is not) lists which systems it picked up messages in, with timestamps. One of those systems is **Drinax** — visited nine times in seven years, with seven timestamped messages. Cross-referencing those dates against who comes and goes from the small, gossipy Floating Palace points to a single suspect: the beloved merchant **Rachando**, who arrived seven years ago and grew impossibly rich.
- **The invitation (the public event):** Two weeks before the crew get home, a courier invites King Oleb (or a representative) to a grand ball celebrating the **re-opening of GeDeCo's highport at Vorito** — a Class A port destroyed in a terror attack seven years ago, now rebuilt. The whole sector is sending dignitaries; even the insular Floriani will attend. Oleb refuses to go and dumps the decision on the crew: **who represents Drinax?** Vorito is a mere six parsecs away.
- **Safe to narrate:** GeDeCo is the sector's great development megacorp — it backs 70%+ of worlds near the Aslan trade route, offering Faustian "development contracts" that make warlords into kings and kings into clients. Rachando runs a charitable-seeming Bazaar that trades basic goods for the palace's treasures. The Vorito ceremony is a glittering political marketplace. None of this *looks* sinister.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **GeDeCo is a doomsday conspiracy.** A small secret society inside GeDeCo (the Vorito/Tlaiowaha branch) worships the mathematics of the **Jaskarl Report** like an apocalyptic cult. **Head Office in Deneb has no idea** the Vorito branch has gone rogue; their accountants only grumble about strange investments and unexplained losses. Most of GeDeCo (and nearly everyone the crew meet — Ammun Ko, Borshune, Brule) knows **nothing** of the conspiracy.
- **The Jaskarl Report.** Psychohistorian **Dr. Gaemal Jaskarl** (disgraced for plague-experiments on Red Zone worlds; fled into the Reach) modelled the sector and concluded: **the Aslan will inevitably devour the Trojan Reach and cut Deneb off from the Imperium**, likely timed to an Imperial crisis/civil war. Trillions die. His second report outlined a branching plan to turn the Reach into a **"tiger trap"** — fortify some worlds, turn others into xenophobes/conquerors, manipulate both Imperium and Hierate into militarising the border — costing millions of lives but stalling the Aslan for a millennium. The Vorito branch became the tool of that plan.
- **GeDeCo's secret interventions (the plan in action):** sponsored **Theev** to foster piracy (forcing the powers to spend on patrols); founded the transhumanists of **Tech-World** (toward superweapons); engineered amphibians on **Thalassa**; secretly arms the crazed priests of **Tlazotleotl**. The crew's own resurgent Drinax is the latest predicted "new power" the math says must be co-opted.
- **The highport sabotage was an inside job.** In 1098 "Aslan saboteurs" fusion-bombed Vorito's Class A Highport and assassinated key GeDeCo executives. **GeDeCo did it to itself** — the Highport was a "necessary sacrifice" per the Report, and the dead executives were replaced by loyal conspirators. The new highport (re-opening now) was **built around the Vault.**
- **Jaskarl is (barely) alive — and dispersed.** Kept going as little more than a brain in a jar full of anagathics; he left thirty years ago to study manipulation with the **Hivers**. What waits in the Vault is an **interactive simulation hologram** of Jaskarl, running the most up-to-date psychohistorical model (augmented by live data from the Hiver sculpture at the party).
- **Rachando is GeDeCo's spy on Drinax.** His "charity" Bazaar is cover; he observes shipping, traffic and palace visitors and reports via the drum's handshake protocol. His handler is the dull conspiracy-member **Lando Nierr** on Vorito. Rachando **believes in GeDeCo's cause** but fears the company far more than Drinax; under pressure he will reveal just enough to save his skin while protecting GeDeCo — and will always steer the crew toward **breaking into the Vault** (his bid to make himself useful again).
- **The Vault's real contents and the offer.** At the highport's heart is a black-metal cube far older than the station (scarred by old laser fire). Inside is holographic darkness hiding archives, treasures and weapons — and the Jaskarl hologram. **The hologram's pitch:** the crew match a psychohistorical profile (89.4% certainty) of a resurgent Reach polity using piracy/economic pressure to force concessions from a great power. GeDeCo will **fortify Drinax and lend aid** to make it a lightning rod — but **Drinax must then be destroyed by the Aslan** (a strong, admired Drinax falling reignites the Sindalian "golden age myth," traumatising the whole Reach into anti-Aslan belligerence). GeDeCo will "protect and provide for" the crew afterward (a cushy retirement — or a bullet; the hologram won't say which). **Refuse and they walk free** — GeDeCo reckons Drinax is doomed anyway (88% chance of Aslan attack within a few years, equally high chance it's wiped out).
- **The Null Choice (the bleak truth).** If you take psychohistory seriously, the Vault decision **changes nothing**: deal or no deal, Drinax-or-some-other-world will fall, and history plays out regardless. King Oleb gambled a few brave souls can change an Empire's destiny; GeDeCo bets destinies are dictated by population, economics and inevitable force. **The Finale settles who is right.**

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. The Sour Jump — capturing the *Exalted Spirit*
**Trigger:** Anywhere in Tlaiowaha or adjacent. A Far Trader jumps in beside the crew, far outside the limit, full cargo bay, having a legendary "sour jump." → **What's here:** As she turns to flee, a hull panel microfractures and leaks atmosphere from a spot that *should* be fuel tank (the tell of a smuggling compartment). Belsen has dealt with pirates: as soon as the crew shoot she **offers to dump part of her cargo as a bribe**; if that fails she fights to make the bribe look attractive; she **surrenders at 20 points of damage or a critical hit**. → **What the crew gain:** MCr8 cargo (40t Advanced Machine Parts, 10t Advanced Electronics, 10t Robotics — fence takes most); a hidden compartment (Investigate to search / Engineer to match schematics to the leak / Electronics(comms) to trace the drum's wiring / Electronics(sensors) to probe structure / Persuade to make the crew talk) containing **MCr2 gemstones** (Fria's emergency fund) + the **data drum**. → **Complications:** *Alternate hooks if the crew don't bite* — one of their other fleet ships captures her and forwards the drum; or (good reputation) Belsen quits GeDeCo, signs on, and **gives** them the drum as proof of faith; or an alien salvage trader sells them a drum recovered from the *Exalted Spirit*'s wreckage and points them at the Scholar's Tower.

### 2. The Data Drum — reading the trail
**Trigger:** Crew examine the drum. → **What's here:** **Easy (4+) Electronics (comms or computers)** (1D min, EDU) reveals it taps the ship's comms after each jump, listens for a code, records what follows, and pings the bridge — and that it is **rigged to erase everything if it detects a hack**. Cracking the encrypted payload is **Formidable (14+) Electronics (computers)** (1D years, INT) — effectively impossible aboard ship; **Scholar Voha** of the Scholar's Tower could do it in months for a huge favour. → **The usable lead:** the **low-security comms log** lists systems visited + timestamps of messages picked up. **Drinax appears: 9 visits in 7 years, 7 timestamped messages.** Cross-referencing (optionally an Investigate check) those dates against the handful of regulars who come and go from the small Floating Palace yields **one suspect: Rachando**, who arrived 7 years ago. → **Complications:** Without the small-community angle the log alone wouldn't ID a spy; the palace's gossip-scale is what makes it work.

### 3. A Time to Roll Carouse — choosing the Delegate
**Trigger:** Crew return to Drinax to find the court in a fresh intrigue over the Vorito invitation. → **What's here:** Oleb won't go (he's busy with his new Torpolian water-polo obsession on the Underlinth — not leaving for a month) and accepts **whatever the crew suggest** for Drinax's representative (the **Delegate**, referenced in later scenes). Candidates (all detailed in *The Third Imperium* p.24): **Princess Rao** (obvious heir/diplomat, but Harrick's faction objects and her scheming risks annoying GeDeCo); **Prince Harrick** (Oleb's rebuilt eldest son, wants to present himself to civilised dignitaries); **Lord Wrax** (military man, "fly the flag"); **Lady Hil** (self-styled mistress of decorum, keeps Drinax's low/eccentric profile). The crew may also **put themselves forward.** → **Lobbying & favours:** If two+ crew disagree, opposed **Persuade (SOC)** before Oleb. Three candidates court the crew's backing (their opinion carries weight): **Prince Harrick** offers a salvaged, repaired suit of **TL15 battle dress** to the "Admiral of his Royal Fleet" (whoever backs him most); **Lord Wrax** offers **three Excellent-quality crewmen**; **Lady Hil** offers a permanent **+1 SOC** (she'll be seen having sherry with them). → **Complications:** Note who is chosen — the Delegate determines who GeDeCo personnel fixate on at the ceremony (see Scene 7) and is the **hostage** GeDeCo uses if the heist fails.

### 4. Investigating the Merchant Rachando
**Trigger:** Crew want proof before accusing the influential, universally-indebted Rachando. → **What's here:** Several investigation methods; **track total Effect** across all checks (any plausible approach works — set the appropriate check). Each method takes time and risks angering someone who owes Rachando a favour.

| Method | Check | Yields |
|---|---|---|
| Gathering Rumours | Average (8+) Investigate (1D wks, INT) | He lied about his trader past — was only a free trader a few months before arriving; his prior history is a mystery (where did the ship/cargo come from?) |
| Observation | Difficult (10+) Deception (1D days, INT) | He meets fellow pirates/traders — people who might know the crew's secret royal operations |
| Financial Analysis | Average (8+) Broker (1D wks, INT) | His "basic goods" trace back to **GeDeCo suppliers** within 1–2 jumps |
| Hitting the Starport Bar | Difficult (10+) Carouse (1D days, END) | He runs his own agents in the bar/Underlinth — he is **spying on the palace** |
| False Signals | Very Difficult (12+) Electronics(comms) (1D hrs, EDU) | Spoofing the drum's handshake gets an answer **from somewhere in the palace** — there *is* a GeDeCo agent on Drinax |
| Sneaking onto the *Invisible Hand* | Very Difficult (12+) Stealth (1D min, DEX) | A transmitter like the drum + encrypted instructions on a **Vorito-made data crystal** |

→ **Complications (failed checks anger Rachando's debtors):** Fail **1** check → social friction (complaints about pirates harassing "nice Rachando"). Fail **2** → Drinax drops from Haven to merely **Tolerant** for the crew (good trigger to launch The Assassins, Scene 5). Fail **3+** → Drinax becomes **Suspicious**; Oleb summons the crew to a private hearing; if they can't convince him, the adventure diverts (see *Turn In Your Pirate Hat*).

### 5. The Assassins (run any time the action lags)
**Trigger:** GeDeCo dispatches assassins if the crew's investigation nears the truth, or to silence Rachando if he's about to be exposed / is imprisoned. → **What's here:** **One assassin per two Travellers**, arriving as passengers on a Marduk merchant ship, posing as a Marduk trade delegation in elaborate Sea-God headdresses with tongue-puppeted tentacles. Beneath the masks they are **identical clones** (a different genetic template than the Widows of Blacksand from *Honour Among Thieves*, if recognised). Rachando can identify them as GeDeCo clones. → **Tactics menu:** stalk the crew; pose as informants and lure them to an isolated ballroom or down onto Drinax's seared surface to ambush; stow away / seek passage on the crew's ship; fake a distress call for "rescue." **If targeting Rachando:** break into the prison mid-interrogation; cross the Underlinth and blow into his cell; or hijack a ship and crash it into the prison. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** Send them after crew or Rachando "whenever the action lags." Treat as a floating complication during Scenes 4 and 6.

### 6. Confronting & Interrogating Rachando
**Trigger:** Crew confront Rachando (or have Oleb imprison him). → **Confrontation:** **Difficult (10+) Persuade (SOC)**; add this Effect to the investigation total (failing it doesn't count toward the fail-penalties). Total Effect sets his reaction:

| Total Effect | Rachando's reaction |
|---|---|
| < 0 | Denies everything, runs to Oleb, convinces the King the crew are attempting a **coup** → *Turn In Your Pirate Hat* |
| 1–2 | Denies, but evidence makes Oleb suspicious; King jails Rachando + ransacks the *Invisible Hand* for proof → run Interrogation; public investigation **alerts GeDeCo** (run Assassins) |
| 3–5 | Admits GeDeCo involvement → run Interrogation (+ Assassins if not yet run) |
| 6–8 | Admits and is **eager to talk** → DM+2 to all Interrogation checks |
| 9–11 | Confesses fully — no Interrogation rolls needed |
| 12+ | Confesses and, devastated, tries to atone by **giving the crew the *Invisible Hand*** (converted Far Trader, pop-up beam laser turrets) |

→ **Interrogation topics** (he gives the first bullet in each automatically; more with earned Effect / good play). **He always steers them to break into the Vault.**
- **Rachando & GeDeCo:** he's the spy; believes he's the only agent on Drinax but isn't sure; only contact was handler **Nierr**, a GeDeCo spymaster on Vorito.
- **Drinax & GeDeCo:** his mission was to watch shipping/traffic + palace visitors; Vorito sees Drinax as a future source of stability/tech once it sheds its Sindalian warmongering past.
- **GeDeCo (the company):** it's more than a trader — a secret conspiracy manipulating governments and systems across the Reach to prepare it against the Aslan; "ultimately beneficial," but **there is nothing they will not do**. (A **Very Difficult (12+) Science (EDU)** check recalls Professor Jaskarl, psychohistorian.)
- **The new highport:** exactly what it looks like — re-opens Vorito for trade, brings more traffic past Drinax (more piracy targets, but draws Imperial attention to Tlaiowaha). Heavily defended; the **only raid window is the opening ceremony**.
- **The Vault:** at the highport's heart; the station was built around it; precious to GeDeCo. Rachando saw it once (sworn in). Threatening its contents could be **leverage to negotiate** with GeDeCo. Nierr hinted there are **weapons** in the Vault. **He knows only two security measures: a DNA sample (Nierr is on the registry) and a passcard (one of his escort guards carried it; Nierr had none).**
→ **Branch — *Execute the Traitor*:** Unconvinced crews can have Oleb dungeon or airlock Rachando (he dies pleading he spied but never truly betrayed Drinax); they may still pursue the Vault or their own counter-GeDeCo scheme. → **Branch — *Turn In Your Pirate Hat* (if censured/disowned):** recover by (a) gathering an **additional Effect 6+** of investigation to convince Oleb; (b) gaining a new palace patron (Rao or Harrick); (c) suffering a rival pirate promoted over them for an adventure or two; or (d) abandoning the political plot to go loot something.

### 7. The Unveiling — arriving at Vorito Highport
**Trigger:** Crew travel to Vorito (six parsecs) for the ceremony. Plan the heist first (Scene 8). → **What's here:** Vorito Highport is a rotating dodecahedron with reconfigurable docking arms (a metallic spider/squid). The system is **crowded** — GeDeCo SDBs guard the approaches; megafreighters bigger than anything seen in the Reach drift past; a cloud of traders/couriers/transports broadcasts a who's-who of IFF (Imperial, Aslan, Floriani, GeDeCo clients). The station is **staggeringly huge and nearly empty**, built to welcome tens of thousands. → **Gatekeeping:** **No Delegate aboard** → challenged on arrival, told Vorito is "temporarily closed to unauthorised visitors," directed to refuel at the gas giant **Voree** and leave; non-compliance = force. **Delegate aboard** with credentials → permitted to dock; greeted by liaison **Ammun Ko** and escorted to the Grand Banqueting Hall. → **Complications (note for the heist):** A single ship draws less attention than several. There is **no time for reconnaissance** before the ceremony — this is a one-shot infiltration.

### 8. Planning the Heist
**Trigger:** Before departing Drinax. → **What's here:** Settle: **who the Delegate is**; **which ship** (more than one draws attention); optionally what criminal kit they bring (you may let them declare tools retroactively — "of course I packed a codebreaker"). From Rachando they know the Vault has **≥2 security systems**: a **passcard of sufficient clearance** (unknown level) and a **DNA sample** from Nierr or another registered person. Some checks can be bypassed but slowly and at risk of countermeasures. → **Complications:** No extensive recon possible; the crew improvise on site.

### 9. Security on the Way In
**Trigger:** Crew's ship approaches and docks. → **What's here / the layers:**
1. **Pre-dock close scan** by a System Defence Boat **densitometer** (detects unusual objects/weapons aboard).
2. **At dock:** met by **Ammun Ko** + a GeDeCo honour guard (one per Traveller); one guard has a **handheld densitometer + bioscanner** for weapons/contraband. Concealing illegal weapons = **Difficult (10+) Stealth or Deception (DEX)**; failure → ordered to return the items to ship + watched closely (**DM−1** to all later sneaking).
3. **Passcards:** Ammun Ko issues each Traveller a **Level 1 clearance** passcard, preloaded with Credits (drinks/souvenirs free, within reason); opens the corporate lounges.
4. **Escort through several checkpoints**, Ammun Ko swiping at each — the ceremony is in an extremely secure core section; the crew may never get this close again.
→ **Complications:** Everything later keys off clearance level and DNA registry; banking a higher-level passcard or registered DNA now (via the NPCs in Scene 10) is the heist.

### 10. Mingling at the Party
**Trigger:** Crew are escorted to the Grand Banqueting Hall. → **What's here:** Free movement. Exit a conversation gracefully = **Easy (4+) Carouse (SOC)**; enter a *specific* person's conversation = **Carouse (SOC)**. The party is the venue for plot hooks, side missions, and gathering what the Vault needs (clearances/DNA). **Spotting GeDeCo agents secretly reporting to Nierr** = **Investigate (INT)**. → **Key NPC clearances (the heist's shopping list):**

| NPC | Role | Passcard | DNA on Vault registry? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fae Borshune** | Highport administrator | Level 4 | **Yes** |
| **Security Chief Brule** | Acting security chief (Vargr) | Level 3 | No |
| **Lando Nierr** | Conspirator; Rachando's handler | Level 2 | **Yes** |
| **Ammun Ko** | Liaison | Level 2 | No |
| **Hiver Jim** | "Consulting artist" | None | **Yes** (and a **hatchling's DNA** also works) |
| Crew (default) | Guests | Level 1 | No |

→ **The Sculpture (a clue + a tool):** A grotesque alien tree of glowing tubes/marble nodes at the buffet centre, wired to the station network by a high-density data cable. Staring too long = **Difficult (10+) END** vs. a mild headache; psionic Travellers sense **psionic tech**. It is **Hiver Jim's** work: it scans everyone's **thought-structure** (instincts/personality, *not* telepathy) and feeds **psychographic profiles** into the Jaskarl Plan. **Electronics (computers)** tracing the cable confirms it connects to the **secret Vault**. **Cutting the cable** is harmless to the crew but a perfect distraction — a swarm of techs rushes to fix it (Borshune dismisses it as "environmental adjustment"), opening a window to slip away (DM+2 to Sneaking Out). → **Ceremony beat:** Borshune addresses the crowd from a floating podium (GeDeCo investments will "end piracy," unite worlds, uplift the fallen); a squadron forms outside a transparent wall and burns the GeDeCo logo into a gas cloud with lasers/particle beams. Afterward the party resumes; the real deals happen in the small hours — **the crew slip out to hit the Vault.** → **Optional cameos (bring back established NPCs):** Falx of Torpol, Olais of Marduk (*Honour Among Thieves*); "Vargas"/Purser Vaughn (conspiracy member, reports to Nierr), Regent Stoylis of Bynri (*Treasure Ship*); Gerret Hodal of Tech-World (ally or loyal employee, per *The Demon's Eye*); Takhweirl, an Aslan trader (*Ihatei!*); Warden Rannib, Prophet Zarn, Tiznee Zazzle (*The Treasure of Sindal*); Eridani of Tobia (awkward if kidnapped in *Game of Sun and Shadow*); Ht!/Ujj! of the Florian League. Most have only Level 1 clearance unless noted (Vaughn: Level 2 + DNA).

### 11. Breaking Into the Vault — the Countdown
**Trigger:** Crew slip out to the unfinished, industrial core sections (a warren of metal corridors). → **The clock:** Roll **1D+6** for the safety buffer (the **Countdown**). Modify:
- **−1** for each roll that takes <60 seconds.
- **−Effect** of a *failed final* roll in a security task chain.
- **−1D** for each check that takes 1D minutes.
- **+1** if a check succeeds with Effect 4–5; **+2** if Effect 6.

If the Countdown drops below 0 **and** the crew aren't yet inside the inner Vault, the alarm trips: **one more check** to make it in; failing that, a **GeDeCo security team** hits them (defeating it **+1D Countdown**). Run out again → **next team is twice as big, in battle dress, holding the Delegate hostage.** → **Use task chains, not per-Traveller rolls** (e.g. Sneaking Out: one Deception(INT) spots an opening, one Carouse(SOC) distracts, one Stealth(DEX) slips out). → **Sub-steps:**
  - **Sneaking Out:** Carouse or Deception (1D min, SOC); accept **DM−2** to go fast (1D×10 sec). Matching the right Delegate to the right GeDeCo rep = DM+2; Lady Hil bores anyone for a flat DM+1; sabotaging the Sculpture = DM+2.
  - **Avoiding Detection** (corridor task chain): Stealth (DEX); supported by Electronics(sensors) (1D min, INT) to kill scanners, Melee(unarmed) (1D rds, STR) to drop guards quietly, Engineer (1D×10 sec, INT) to navigate access tubes.
  - **Bypassing Security (two systems):** **Passcard** — Level 3 opens the Vault door; **Electronics (computers)** (1D×10 sec, INT) with **DM−2 (Level 2 card) / −4 (Level 1) / −6 (no card)**. **DNA** — registered sample = automatic; otherwise hack the sensor via **Electronics (computers) or Medic** (1D min, INT).
  - **Internal Defences:** If the crew lacked **both** a Level 3+ passcard **and** valid DNA, their tampering pops ceiling hatches and deploys **Combat Drones** (Laser Rifle 5D+3, Armour +9).
→ **The Vault entrance** is suspiciously unguarded and deserted; the cube is dark, humming, scarred by old laser fire, far older than the station; robot pods attach power cables outside. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** Run the Countdown literally — pre-roll `1d6+6`, apply modifiers per check, and resolve the security-team escalation when it hits 0.

### 12. In the Vault — the Jaskarl Hologram (the climax)
**Trigger:** The Vault doors open. → **What's here:** Endless holographic black — no floor/walls/ceiling, objects dimly cloaked in the distance. A hologram of an old, thin-bearded man appears: **the interactive simulation of Professor Jaskarl.** He delivers **the offer** (full text under GM-Only): the crew fit a psychohistorical profile (89.4%); GeDeCo will fortify Drinax as a "lightning rod" to draw and slow the Aslan; **Drinax must then fall** to reignite the Sindalian golden-age myth and traumatise the Reach into anti-Aslan belligerence; GeDeCo will "protect and provide for" the crew after (cushy retirement — or a bullet). **Refuse and they go free** (Drinax is ~88% doomed regardless). → **Interaction rules:** He answers **only** questions bearing on the proposition ("your query is outside the predicted parameters of this interaction" otherwise — including any scheme to take the help but not betray Drinax). **Each question asked subtracts 1 from the Countdown.** → **If they agree:** GeDeCo covertly builds Drinax up *until* the Aslan attack; the crew must then **ensure Drinax's destruction** (possibly sabotaging their own defences) or break the bargain. → **Searching the rest of the Vault:** one roll on the **Vault Treasures** table per point of Countdown remaining (no easy search — the dark occludes everything; a densitometer + **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics(sensors) (INT)** adds Effect to the next roll). **No guarantee they keep any of it** (see Escape).

| 2D | Vault Treasure |
|---|---|
| 2–4 | Nothing obvious (maybe something buried in financial/personnel archives) |
| 5 | MCr1D in portable Credits/gold/jewels/bearer bonds |
| 6 | Highport schematics + a high-clearance access terminal (prep an escape route — open hatches, redirect bots, overload reactors) |
| 7 | MCr2D in portable valuables |
| 8 | Intel on a GeDeCo scheme on another world (exposing it moves that world **1 step toward Haven**) |
| 9 | Intel on an exceedingly valuable cargo in transit (a piracy target) |
| 10 | Identity of a GeDeCo agent in power on a neutral planet (exposing moves that world **2 steps toward Haven**) |
| 11 | Computer archives of the Jaskarl calculations (destroying them severely disrupts GeDeCo in the Reach) |
| 12+ | Something extraordinary — an Ancient relic, cryo-frozen clones of key Imperial officials, lost Sindalian treasure, a **Black Globe generator**, or a planning document that (if exploited) moves a world **3 steps toward Haven** |

→ **Destroying the Vault:** If they smuggled in explosives/heavy weapons, they can wreck the Vault and its archives. It's not the only copy of the Plan, but it's the most current model (with the Hiver sculpture's data) — **destruction sets GeDeCo back several years.**

### 13. Escaping the Vault / Time to Leave the Party
**Trigger:** Crew finish in the Vault. → **If Countdown remains — clean exit:** Close the door + leave no trace = **Difficult (10+) Electronics(computers)** (1D min, INT) **and Difficult (10+) Investigate** (1D min, INT) to scrub forensics; then drift back to the party, drink GeDeCo's free cocktails, and stroll to their ship — **the perfect crime.** Alternatively, race to the ship and try to clear the 100-diameter limit before GeDeCo catches them (GeDeCo may simply *let them go* — they want a "lightning rod"). → **If Countdown is gone — captured:** A heavily armed GeDeCo marine squad (battle-dress brutes) led by **Nierr** arrives, **with the Delegate as hostage if needed**. They strip any stolen money/gear/weapons but **otherwise don't interrogate** ("it's better not to talk about it"). Fighting or arguing → "violent repercussions" (the Plan says let them go, but not unscathed). Trying to hold the Vault's contents hostage → Nierr sneers: **"We only make deals on our own terms. You have already had our offer."** → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** Resolve by remaining Countdown — clean escape, hot pursuit to the jump limit, or capture-by-Nierr.

## Rewards & Consequences
- **The conspiracy unmasked (the real "loot").** The crew learn the campaign's central secret — GeDeCo's plan to sacrifice Drinax — which reframes every later choice and sets up the *Finale*. Hard intel: the Jaskarl Report's existence and intent; GeDeCo's hand behind Theev, Tech-World, Thalassa, Tlazotleotl; the self-inflicted 1098 Highport sabotage.
- **Capture haul:** MCr8 cargo + MCr2 gemstones from the *Exalted Spirit* (fence takes most of the cargo). Possible recruit: **Fria Belsen** + the *Exalted Spirit* if reputation is good.
- **Delegate favours:** TL15 **battle dress** (back Harrick), **three Excellent-quality crewmen** (back Wrax), or permanent **+1 SOC** (back Lady Hil).
- **Rachando outcomes:** at confrontation Effect 12+, the **Invisible Hand** (converted Far Trader, pop-up beam lasers).
- **Vault treasures (per Countdown roll):** MCr1D–2D portable wealth; highport schematics + access terminal; a **Black Globe generator**, **Ancient relic**, cryo-clones, or lost Sindalian treasure (12+); and intel that **moves named worlds 1–3 steps toward Haven** (see below). **GeDeCo confiscates all of it if the crew are captured** (Countdown 0).
- **The Jaskarl offer:** taking it = GeDeCo covertly builds Drinax up now, in exchange for the crew engineering/allowing Drinax's destruction later (resolved in the *Finale*). Refusing costs nothing immediately.
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest):**
  - **GeDeCo combatants are lethal** — Elite Guards (Battledress +22, Gauss Rifle 4D), Combat Drones (Laser Rifle 5D+3, Armour +9), Assassins (Polycarapace +12). Triggering security mid-heist escalates to a battle-dress squad **holding the Delegate hostage**.
  - **Failing the investigation** drops Drinax's own port Attitude for the crew (2 fails → **Tolerant**; 3+ → **Suspicious**) and can force a King's hearing → *Turn In Your Pirate Hat* (rival pirate promoted over them, or loss of standing) if they can't convince Oleb.
  - **Botched confrontation (Effect <0)** → Rachando frames the crew for a coup; political fallout.
  - **Captured at the Vault** → all stolen wealth/gear stripped; fighting Nierr's squad invites "violent repercussions"; **the Delegate may be taken/used as a hostage** (a court NPC's life on the line).
  - **Destroying the Vault** delays GeDeCo "several years" but openly marks the crew as GeDeCo's enemy.
  - **The Null Choice:** per the Plan, none of it may matter — Drinax's fate is settled in the *Finale*, not here.
