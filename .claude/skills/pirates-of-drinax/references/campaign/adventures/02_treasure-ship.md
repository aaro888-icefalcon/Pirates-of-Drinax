# Adventure 2 — Treasure Ship

> Use this when: the crew chase the grounded Imperial **Treasure Ship Martin II** at Arunisiir, infiltrate/board/intercept her, or get caught in the GeDeCo treaty plot or Hroal Irontooth's ambush. Spoiler-gated — withhold the GM-Only section until earned.

**Logline:** A crippled Imperial Treasure Ship — a floating bank-vault on the worst day of its life — sits grounded at a neutral port, and *every* pirate in the Reach wants it; the crew can rob it, steal it whole, or get tangled in two rival conspiracies already aboard.

## At a glance
- **Location:** Borderland subsector, Trojan Reach. Starts at **Arunisiir** (0201, B776530-6, Tolerant); route runs Arunisiir → Acrid → **Exe** → **Tech-World** → Ergo → **Byrni**, with a final Aslan rendezvous at **Sink** (0806). Year 1105.
- **Threat:** High. The prize itself is a freighter, but it is guarded (6 Star Marines aboard, 2 Gazelle escorts, system patrols) and contested by a peer pirate band (Hroal Irontooth). A stand-up fight is **lethal**.
- **Length:** Long / multi-session. Non-linear sandbox — the crew pick their own approach.
- **Prerequisites:** None hard. Connects to *Honour Among Thieves* (Hroal Irontooth, Theev, the Dancing Spider). Best run mid-early once the crew can crew/fly the Harrier and have some Standing to spend.
- **Arc position:** A signature "big score" patron-less heist. Feeds the GeDeCo war-stirring metaplot and the Drinax fleet (the Martin II can be *taken whole*). Standing with both empires is on the line.
- **Hard time limit:** Pirates must reach Arunisiir within ~**12 weeks** of the landing (rumour spreads at ~3pc/week; flotilla arrives wk 8; Martin II relaunches wk 12). Miss it and the prize sails.

## Player-Facing
*(Safe to narrate / what the crew can openly learn.)*

- **The rumour:** One of the fabled Imperial **Treasure Ships** suffered a jump-drive explosion and made a hard landing on the lawless world of **Arunisiir** for repairs. Her convoy left her behind with a single **Gazelle-class escort** in orbit. Treasure Ships are armoured vaults of Imperial wealth — the prize of a lifetime.
- **Picking up the rumour:** any starport bar/pirate den, **Streetwise** check (1D days, INT); roll once per Effect on the *Rumours and Lies* table. Once in-system, dock rumours come at **1D hours per Streetwise**. Not all rumours are true (see table below).
- **Getting there:** weeks of travel; the window is closing (see time limit).
- **What's visible at Arunisiir:** Class B port built around a wrecked Aslan **Sakhai** carrier ringed by missile turrets (TL6 world, still makes surface-to-space missiles). The Martin II sits on a cracked pad on the port edge, stern on grav platforms, wrapped in scaffolding, **strictly guarded** by armoured marines (shoot-to-kill signage). Six ships share the port (below). The **Gazelle Arshad** waits in orbit.
- **The presenting problem:** how to get *at* the prize — get hired onto the repair crew, sneak aboard, raid it, or shadow it when it relaunches.
- **Open NPC threads:** an Aslan extortionist (Ekaft) shaking down landed ships; a strange offworld broker (**Aparo**) who'll pay Cr1000 for a report on the Martin II's engines; hard-drinking crewmen **O'Leary & Ilin** who'll smuggle the crew aboard for a bribe; superstitious acting-chief **Mechanic Polo** who hires "lucky" people.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*(Reveal only as earned. Three subplots run in parallel aboard.)*

1. **The Treasure Ship is bait twice over.** The Martin II carries the usual mail and goods — **and** a secret diplomatic channel between the Imperial Moot and the Aslan Hierate. That makes her a tool for forgers.

2. **The GeDeCo plot — the fake Treaty of Fist.** The megacorp **GeDeCo** (psychohistorians who believe only war with the Aslan can save the Reach) plants a **forged treaty** in the vault. The fake "Treaty of Fist" appears to sanction Aslan annexation of a dozen worlds across the Reach. If it is "discovered" aboard a Treasure Ship with an open vault, panic drives the neutral worlds into GeDeCo's arms and inflames the Imperial war-faction. (The *real* treaty is the Treaty of **Ftaharl/Fhaharl**, which keeps Aslan clans 30pc from Imperial borders.) Two GeDeCo agents are aboard, **unaware of each other's identity**:
   - **Astrogator Osha** — planted a **black box** inside the navigation console that harvests jump data + vault-lock data. At **Tech-World** she carries it to a shell company ("CP Computing") whose **TL15 supercomputer** cracks the vault code.
   - **Purser Vaughan** — keeps the fake treaty hidden in his quarters; receives a small **vault-opening device** at Tech-World; opens the vault and plants the treaty during the **Byrni** customs inspection.
   - Outside contact: broker **Aparo** at Arunisiir (verifies the plot is viable; works the crew so *he* doesn't have to approach the ship).

3. **The Hroal Irontooth plot — the saboteurs.** Aslan pirate **Hroal Irontooth** (from *Honour Among Thieves*) wanted the Martin II to misjump into his ambush at **Exe**. Three crew were his pawns: **Mechanic Felmar** (now dead), **Marine Salvesdes**, **Launch Pilot Hanshaw/Henshaw**.
   - **What actually crippled the ship:** Felmar tried to alter the plotted jump solution; Chief Engineer **Eco** caught him; Felmar panicked and opened the jump-drive safety interlocks → the hydrogen explosion that killed most of engineering and forced the landing. (This is why the ship is grounded at all.)
   - **Salvesdes** is under **Aslan telepathic conditioning** (Hroal had a telepath wipe/program him) — he *must* obey Hroal but doesn't consciously remember why. He is the main combat threat aboard: battle dress + laser rifle/PGMP. He will resume the plot: kill assistant astrogator **Ven Laffit** for his nav codes, then storm the bridge and force the jump into Hroal's trap.
   - **Hanshaw** was coerced (Hroal's men threatened her daughter on Cyan). She is **sick with guilt** and wants out — a natural ally/turncoat for the crew. If she tries to warn anyone, Salvesdes' programming makes him kill her.
   - **Plan B:** if the Martin II reaches Exe intact, Hroal abandons subtlety and **assaults Exe directly** with his flotilla.

4. **The vault is genuinely (near) unbreakable by force.** Its lock code derives from **jump-space coordinates + genetic material** — it opens only **in the right system (Tyokh) by the right person with the ceremonial key** (kept in the captain's safe). Punching through the hull won't help: the vault resists starship fire, and anything that could crack it would incinerate the contents. Some vaults have **psionic shielding vs teleportation**. The only realistic ways in: the **GeDeCo TL15 crack** (ride it / steal its fruits) or the **Drinaxian Scholar's Tower** doing the same TL15 trick back home (only if the crew steal the *whole ship*).

5. **Other competitors at the port:** husband-and-wife traders **Hral & Gila Zafnen** (*Fafnir*, ex-pirates, given up); scout **Yan** + robot **Bucket** (*Whistlestop*, the courier who fetched the rescue flotilla, basking as "hero"); **Bel** (*Hopeful Wanderer*) who saw Irontooth's flagship in the **Exe** belt; Irontooth's own scout **Dancing Spider** (Thedik Vance) buzzing the port to check on the prize.

#### S2. Arrival & the Arshad
**Trigger:** crew reach Arunisiir. → **What's here:** the **Gazelle Arshad** in orbit. Check the crew's **Imperium Standing**: if **−6 or worse** and the Arshad IDs them, it **attacks**. Hide identity with an opposed **Electronics (sensors)** check (EDU). Arunisiir is lawless — once past the patrol, land anywhere (landing near the Martin II draws heat). → **Complications:** a hostile Gazelle is a fight to **avoid** (ships below; doctrine → space-combat.md).

#### S3. The starport (encounters, loose order)
**Trigger:** crew work the port/bar. → **What's here / Complications** (run in this order):
1. **Aslan Extortion** — **Ekaft** (faded Aslan aristocrat) demands a fee by "inspecting" the crew's ship. Refuse → empty threats + unwanted attention. *Hook:* flatter his ego and he'll demand to **inspect the Martin II** — a clean way aboard (the pad is technically his territory).
2. **Wild Times at the Bar** — Routine (6+) **Carouse** befriends crewmen **O'Leary & Ilin**: the ship needs hands; they may **smuggle the crew aboard for a bribe**. Best place to seed NPCs/rumours. If the crew stall, **Polo** offers them a job.
3. **The GeDeCo Man** — **Aparo** offers Cr1000 for a status report on the Martin II's engines (cover: he's a parts broker pricing his margin). Digging (break into his cabin / question Bel / hack his hand computer) reveals he's renting a warehouse as **"CP Computing,"** joined the *Hopeful Wanderer* at **Tech-World** → GeDeCo lead.
4. **The Buzz of the Scout** — Irontooth's scout buzzes the port. Difficult (10+) **Electronics (comms)** (INT) catches a **burst transmission** from inside the Martin II (Henshaw → Hroal, "be patient"). If the crew saw Theev, Electronics (sensors) IDs the scout as the **Dancing Spider** (Hroal's band). Intercept + parlay with captain **Thedik Vance** → learn Hroal's plan.
5. **The Imperial Cavalry** — three ships jump in: **Gazelle Komino**, repair ship **Henry Ford**, patrol cruiser **Sincerity**. Within **48 hrs** the Ford finishes repairs; Martin II joins the two Gazelles in orbit. The **Sincerity** scans every docked ship before refuelling — Standing **−6 or worse** → disguise/hide (its sensors are better: **DM−4** to the sensors check if berthed). (Sincerity then patrols the Borderlands/Sindalian main — recurring NPC ship.)

#### S4. Getting aboard (three doors)
**Trigger:** crew commit to a method. → **What's here:**
- **Getting Hired:** while grounded, the ship needs deckhands/techs. **Engineer 2+, Mechanic 2+, or relevant Profession 2+** → repair job (earn Effect × Cr200; or × Cr100 for general dock work). Repair crew are confined to engineering, watched by **2 armoured marines**. Impressing officers (roleplay + useful skills + clean/fake IDs) → temporary **contract for the whole voyage** to the Hierate — the cleanest infiltration.
- **Stealth:** all approaches watched; shoot-to-kill signs. **Very Difficult (12+) Stealth** (DEX) to slip the guards. Once aboard, hide easily in the cargo bays (rarely checked in flight). A repair-crew member slipping away needs only **Routine (6+) Stealth**.
- **Raiding (a direct assault):** 6 Star Marines + armed crew + local ground-car troops (slug rifles, minutes away) + the **Arshad's ortillery & assault launch** overhead. **A direct assault on the Martin II is likely suicidal.** A *small* strike team with a distraction might storm the bridge or hit the vault.

### ACT 2 — Voyage to the Hierate (intercept on the road)

#### S5. Following / shadowing the flotilla
**Trigger:** crew trail the Martin II once she relaunches. → **What's here:** restricted to **two-parsec jumps** (Torsa won't push the repaired drive), so the route lengthens to **Arunisiir → Acrid → Exe → Tech-World → Ergo → Byrni** (~70 days; ~3 days/port, ~7 in jump). The three ships **jump in unison** (synchronised jump solutions → starship-operations.md). → **Complications:** following the *same* course for 1–2 jumps is normal courtesy (expect a friendly hail); **3+ jumps in convoy is suspect** — without a plausible excuse the crew may be intercepted/boarded by the **Arshad**. Safer: jump to a *different* part of each system and pick the Treasure Ship up on long-range sensors.

#### S6. System set-pieces (per stop)
**Trigger:** crew choose where to strike. → **What's here / Complications:**
- **Acrid** (0302) — landing only via a calm **weather-control corridor** through acid storms. Attack inside the corridor → exposure to **4 automated missile turrets**; stepping outside → **DM−4 Pilot** in the winds. The narrow corridor lets the crew **isolate one Gazelle at a time**. Caustic atmosphere eats armour over hours.
- **Exe** (0403) — **Hroal Irontooth's band lurks here** (the original ambush point and his Plan-B battlefield). See S8. Last good belt to stage from.
- **Tech-World** (0204) — repairs + 3 days' shore leave. **The GeDeCo handoff happens here** (S9). Osha leaves with the black box; Vaughan receives the vault device.
- **Ergo** (0205) — Red Zone; the ships use only the automated refuelling station. A last intercept chance before Byrni.
- **Byrni** (0107) — Sindal-loyal naval world rebuilt by GeDeCo. **The treaty-plant climax** (S10). Patrol = **3 System Defence Boats** + a second patrol of **3 more** ~20,000km off.
- **Sink** (0806) — at the far end an **Aslan escort flotilla** meets the Martin II (Aositaoh cruiser + 2 Ekawsiykua escorts — far too much firepower). **Last window to rob her before she joins the Aslan**; fail and the prize is lost for good.

#### S7. The damaged jump drive bites again (After Exe)
**Trigger:** after Exe (referee timing). → **What's here:** the repaired drive **fails again** — safety systems hold this time (no second explosion), but **both Gazelles jump away**, leaving the Martin II alone. → **Complications:** an Engineer (jump) (INT) success by a Traveller aboard can save it. The Gazelles are **Jump-4** and only jumped 2pc, so they **return in ~2 weeks**; protocol says the Martin II waits. **This solo window is the single best moment to attack** if shadowing.

### ACT 3 — Aboard the Treasure Ship (the ship's own intrigues)

#### S8. Irontooth's Ambush / The Battle of Exe
**Trigger:** if Salvesdes succeeds (S-timeline) the Martin II misjumps into Hroal's trap; OR if she reaches Exe intact, Hroal switches to a direct assault. → **What's here:**
- **The trap sprung:** the Meatgrinder's twin particle bays outgun everything; without intervention the Martin II is pummelled dead-but-vault-intact (~45M km from her escorts; ~a day for the Gazelles to respond — Komino jumps for help, Arshad tows the hulk to Exe). Crew aboard will be **killed unless they outwit Hroal or switch sides and ally with him**. Hroal can't crack the vault and *needs* the crew to do it.
- **The Battle of Exe (Plan B):** a bloody brawl. Gazelles (nuclear missiles) gut the Meatgrinder; the Arshad charges, is wrecked to scrap but stays faster/more agile and forces Hroal to **jump out**; the Komino's nukes kill *Sindal's Shame*; Monkey Business + Dancing Spider flee. Imperials win but battered — **Komino at Hull ~82** (both beam turrets gone), **Martin II at Hull ~402**. Either way the vault survives intact for whoever can open it.
- **Vaughan** (GeDeCo) will try to slip the fake treaty *into* the vault or where the crew find it during the chaos.
→ **Complications / alliance:** the crew can deal with **Hroal** to split the loot or ally — he honours it or eliminates them on a whim once they've opened the vault. (Hroal also appears in *Honour Among Thieves*; this can seed/repay that relationship.)

#### S9. Tech-World — the GeDeCo crack
**Trigger:** crew shadow Osha ashore (Streetwise or Stealth). → **What's here:** Osha (guided by her robot) carries the black box to **"CP Computing"** — Admin/Electronics (computers) (INT) shows it's a **GeDeCo shell**; the warehouse holds a **TL15 dedicated supercomputer** built to crack the vault lock, overseen by android **Virgil**. *(Option:* the computer needs Captain Torsa's **genetic data** — Virgil/Osha may hire the crew to **steal a sample of Torsa's blood** before departure.) Before the ship leaves, Virgil sends Vaughan the small **vault-opening device**. → **Complications:** intercept the black box, the device, or the crack itself = the crew can open the vault on GeDeCo's coattails (or expose the plot). Tech-World is a spy nest — strange creatures, watchers.

#### S10. Byrni — the treaty plant
**Trigger:** the Martin II reaches Byrni; GeDeCo has leaned on the **Regent of Byrni (Stoylis)** to order all passing ships **searched for contraband**. → **What's here:** a Byrni patrol docks; **marines in antique Star Guard of Sindal armour** board — all wearing **headsets that transmit everything they see to base**. They inspect the holds, then demand the vault. Vaughan covertly triggers the device; the **vault swings open** over the officers' objections; in the confusion Vaughan plants the treaty on a shelf. One marine (GeDeCo's) is briefed to look for documents — if she **picks up the treaty, her headset transmits the image and it cannot be hidden.** → **Complications:** if the crew are aboard they may **catch Vaughan planting it**, or **stop the Byrni marines**. Tensions run hot — a shooting battle between Byrni SDBs and the Imperial convoy can erupt if the crew push it. If no one intervenes, **Torsa presses on** to deliver the (now-compromised) vault to the Aslan and sends a Gazelle racing home to warn the Imperium.

#### S11. Stealing the whole ship (the big prize)
**Trigger:** the crew decide to take the Martin II entire (not just loot her). → **What's here:** they must (1) **eliminate or suborn the crew**, (2) **seize the bridge**, (3) **override ship security** — **DM−6 to all relevant checks** while the security systems are live. → **Complications:** selling a Treasure Ship is impossible, but the crew can use her as a pirate ship or **strip her for parts**, and the **Drinaxian Scholar's Tower** can open the vault back home using the same TL15 technique GeDeCo used. Mutiny is a viable route in (exploit the grumbling crew — S-timeline).

- **Lower Cargo Deck** — unoccupied in flight; stowaways hide here if they beat security. Each deck has a **manual double-beam-laser turret**.
- **Engineering Deck** — explosion damage everywhere. **Engineer(jump)** (INT) → diagnose the disaster; **Difficult (10+) Electronics(computers)/Engineer(jump)/Astrogation** (INT) → find someone tampered with the **jump/navigation data** (the misjump attempt). Two survivors (in a survival locker on the power plant); **Hanshaw's launch** is here. Repair specifics above.
- **Upper Cargo Deck** — crew social hub; the black **secure Vault** dominates it. **Vault Entrance** = titanic bonded-superdense hatch w/ golden Imperial seal (see Vault). **Difficult (10+) Electronics(sensors)** (INT) at the lock reveals Osha's **microscopic data tap + black box** on the navigation computer. **Ship's locker:** 8 stunners + 20 snub pistols + ammo, vacc suits, flak jackets.
- **Crew Deck** — most populated; double-occupancy staterooms (locked when empty); **Marine Barracks + Armoury** (battle dress — Salvesdes draws his here); a manned-during-drills laser turret; **airlock** for boarding/exercises (Salvesdes exits here to walk the hull to the bridge).
- **Officer's Deck** — off-limits to most crew. Two empty diplomat staterooms; **Computer Room** (second officer + Vaughan work here); **Conference Room** holds the **ship's safe** (MCr5 in credits **+** the ceremonial vault key); locked Doctor's office (autodoc only — doctor dead).
- **Flight Deck** — well-appointed **bridge**, manned even in jump; second escape launch + a laser turret.

## The Vault (how to open it; what's inside)
**Opening it — the only realistic routes:**
1. **The legitimate way:** ceremonial **key from the captain's safe**, inserted by the **right person** (genetic code), **in the Tyokh system** (the destination). The lock derives from **jump-space coords + genetics** — wrong system or wrong person = no.
2. **The GeDeCo crack:** ride/steal the **black box → TL15 supercomputer at Tech-World** → the small **vault-opening device** (Vaughan's). Intercept any link in that chain and the crew can open it (S9–S10).
3. **Take the whole ship home:** the **Drinaxian Scholar's Tower** opens it with the same TL15 technique (S11).
- **Brute force fails:** the vault resists starship weapons; anything strong enough to crack it incinerates the contents; some vaults have **psionic shielding vs teleportation**. (Rumour 52: built-in self-destruct — referee's option.)

**Vault contents (60t):**
- 4× 5-ton **secure mail drums** (Imperium↔Hierate comms; crack encryption = **Impossible (16+) Electronics(computers)**).
- **Legal/diplomatic documents** — sellable/ransomable to the Imperium for **MCr10**; seed future heists. **If the GeDeCo plot runs, the fake treaty is planted among them.**
- **40t Luxury Goods** (Cr200,000 each) · **20t Pharmaceuticals** (Cr100,000 each) · **20t Radioactives** (MCr1 each) · **gemstones & rare items** of negligible mass worth **MCr30**.

## Encounters & Events timeline (keyed; assume crew inaction)
Run in order; any crew interference (stopping Salvesdes, intercepting the ship) changes everything. The "must-happen" beats below are written so you can let mythic-gm decide *timing* if undecided — when in doubt whether an event has triggered this leg, roll `dice.py keyed 1d10 7+` (7+ = it happens this leg).

1. **Jump Restrictions (before leaving Arunisiir):** Torsa sets 2-pc jumps → the long route (Acrid→Exe→Tech-World→Ergo→Byrni). More intercept chances for the crew.
2. **Grumbling on the lower decks:** Niven & others want to turn back; Torsa refuses → **dissent the crew can exploit** for allies/mutiny.
3. **The New Plan (before Exe):** Salvesdes & Henshaw meet secretly in her launch to re-rig the misjump. Crew nearby can eavesdrop or whip up a mutiny.
4. **Killing Laffit (before Exe):** Salvesdes silently murders **Ven Laffit** for his nav login codes (tries to frame the crew / create a distraction; hunts him if he's not in quarters — so it may happen near the crew). `dice.py keyed 1d10 7+` if timing undecided.
5. **Seizing the Bridge:** Salvesdes dons battle dress, exits via the crew-deck airlock, **walks the hull** to the top, forces the launch-bay airlock, storms the bridge (kills Halbinar & Smith), inputs the trap coordinates, jumps. Cutting the airlock takes **several minutes** — the crew's window to stop him. Shadowers in space may spot him on the hull.
6. **Trapped in Jump Space (only if Salvesdes succeeds):** a week to Hroal's ambush. The Martin II **cannot win** a stand-up fight and Torsa has no plan — the crew can save the ship *or* foment mutiny. → **S8 Irontooth's Ambush.**
7. **The Second Debate (after Exe / after a foiled takeover):** crew elect **Jen Laral** to petition Torsa; he refuses and threatens the brig → more dissent / mutiny spark.
8. **Jump Drive Blowout (after Exe):** drive fails (no explosion); **both Gazelles jump away ~2 weeks** → **best solo-attack window** (S7).

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(Apply via empire-reputation.md "Per-adventure effects." Never give a free positive — surface trade-offs.)*
- **The fake treaty spreads (crew let the Byrni plant succeed / they expose a treaty publicly):** for **every independent world the crew visit**, roll **2D — on 8+ that world moves one step toward Haven** (panic drives independents toward protectors, which helps Drinax's pitch). **BUT** revealing the treaty raises tensions everywhere: **Standing −1D with BOTH the Imperium and the Hierate.** Big swing — record it.
- **Hitting Imperial assets** (attacking the Martin II / her escorts) **pleases no one openly** and risks **Imperium Standing** (this is an Imperial ship; a hostile-ID Gazelle attacks at Standing −6). Keep the Harrier's identity hidden (it auto-flags watch lists — the-harrier.md).
- **Hitting Hroal Irontooth / saving the Treasure Ship from him** can please the Imperium (a Heroic Deed against pirates → +1D Imperium Standing if credited) and shape the Theev pirate-politics from *Honour Among Thieves*.
- **GeDeCo as a faction:** GeDeCo is a campaign-spanning ally worth **+10 Fighting Strength** if cultivated (empire-reputation.md). Helping or thwarting their plot here sets that relationship. Their psychohistorical war-stirring is the throughline behind several Reach adventures.
- **Arunisiir** (start **Tolerant**) and **Byrni** (start **Suspicious**) can both be nudged by how the crew behave in-system (Byrni's regent Stoylis recurs; Byrni ultimately joins only if Drinax proves worthy).
- **Taking the Martin II whole** adds a large armed hull to the fleet → **Fighting Strength** for the Finale blockade (count per empire-reputation.md; any single hull caps at +2 FS).
