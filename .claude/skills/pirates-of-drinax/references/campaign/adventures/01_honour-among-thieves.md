# Honour Among Thieves (Adventure 1 — Campaign Opener)

> Use this when: running the first adventure / starting the campaign — King Oleb's letter of marque, the Harrier loan, Princess Rao's mission to bring the Torpol/Clarke raiders to justice, the investigation trail (Clarke → Torpol → Borite → Theev), Blacksand City and the pirate gangs, and the capture of Ferrik Redthane. Also the canonical introduction to **Theev** and the **Aslan/pirate gangs** the crew will deal with all campaign.

A "test" mission from Drinax: hunt the upstart pirate **Ferrik Redthane**, who raided two would-be-allied worlds, by following his trail to the pirate world of Theev and capturing him dead or alive. Doing so wins Torpol and Clarke toward the reborn Kingdom — and quietly proves the crew worthy of the marque.

## At a glance
- **Where:** Tliowaha & Sindal subsectors, Trojan Reach. Start: **Drinax** (0603). Trail: **Clarke** (0702) → **Torpol** (0601) → **Borite** (0609) → an empty-space refuelling dump → **Theev** (0506, Blacksand City) → **Palindrome** (0606, 4th moon of the gas giant, `540PD-5/4`). Year 1105.
- **Threat:** low–moderate. Investigation-and-pursuit, not a slugfest; one survival-horror beat (the Chamax). Final fight is the Harrier vs. a single 200t armed Far Trader (the crew outgun it). Real danger is **social**: getting killed on Theev for breaking the gangs' law.
- **Length:** long — the de facto campaign tutorial. Several jumps, three port investigations, a dungeon-crawl, a sandbox city, faction negotiation, and a climax.
- **Prerequisites:** none — this is the opener. Assumes the crew have just accepted (or are being offered) Oleb's marque + the **Harrier**. Can begin in any system rumours can reach; default is Drinax.
- **Arc slot:** **Episode 1.** Introduces the marque, the Harrier, Theev/Blacksand, and four gang leaders (Darokyn, Petyr Vallis, Hroal Irontooth, plus Ferrik). Seeds *Treasure Ship* (Hroal Irontooth), the Sindal mini-campaign (Sindalian station + the lost crown jewels), and Darokyn's Imperial-Intelligence secret. Crews who start here get a permanent **DM+1 to all Finale recruitment rolls** (empire-reputation.md).

## Player-Facing
*Safe to narrate.*

### The summons (the campaign opens here)
The crew are brought before **King Oleb XVI** in the throne room of the **Floating Palace** and offered a **letter of marque** and the loan of the **Harrier** — full read-aloud framing, terms, and the "marry my daughter" dangle are in **drinax.md → "The Travellers' starting situation"** and **the-harrier.md** (do not re-state the ship stats here). The short version the players hear: prey on undocumented shipping inside the old borders of Sindal, win over the independent ex-Drinaxi worlds, keep the marque secret, pay Oleb 10%, *be thieves, not murderers*.

### The mission (the "test" — delivered by Princess Rao)
The first concrete job is pitched not by Oleb but by **Princess Rao**, who *invented the privateer scheme* and treats this as a chance to prove the concept:
- If the crew are on Drinax she briefs them **in person** (conspiratorial; frames it as *clever subsector politics*, not a royal order). Otherwise she sends a **holographic message** carried by the smuggler **Sal Dancet**, who can find the crew anywhere (brief and to the point by holo).
- **The presenting problem:** two nearby worlds, **Torpol** and **Clarke**, were both hit by the **same** raiding party. The raiders landed on Clarke first, then struck Torpol, then jumped out and vanished. Both governments are furious and have **put a price on the raiders' heads**.
- **The political angle (what Rao openly wants):** Torpol's provost merchant-princes are *moderately friendly* to Drinax; Clarke's insular Psychopomps are *less trusting* but have lately made overtures to Oleb's court. If the crew **catch the raiders and turn them over in the name of King Oleb**, it tips both worlds toward an alliance with Drinax. She urges all speed to Clarke and Torpol.
- **The bounties:** **Clarke = MCr0.5; Torpol = MCr2** for the pirates, **dead or alive** — *proof required* that these are the actual raiders. (News travels only as fast as tramp ships — no Xboat net out here — so the bounties are still spreading.)

### Open rumours the crew can pick up (Streetwise/Carouse/contacts)
- Nearly every pirate in this region is based at or visits the **"pirate world" of Theev**.
- Getting to Theev is lethal: land without permission from the **Lords of Blacksand City** and you die. You need **a pirate's code** to visit safely — the crew have none (yet).
- Raiding a *planet* with so small a force is unusual for pirates — they were either after something specific or trying to **make a name for themselves**.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned in play.*

### What actually happened
- The raids were the work of **Ferrik Redthane**, a young, daring upstart pirate, *formerly* one of **Admiral Darokyn's** followers on Theev. Ferrik and Darokyn quarrelled; Darokyn now wants Ferrik **dead** and Ferrik has a price on his head on Theev.
- **Ferrik cannot set foot on Theev** without a powerful gang's protection or he'll be killed. He launched the Torpol/Clarke raids **to advertise how dangerous and competent he is**, hoping to buy his way into one of the other gangs. The raids were a *résumé*, not normal piracy. (Clarke target: a relic-quarry, stealing TL14+ recoverable computer scrap the **Florian Empire** buys for recycling. Torpol target: an automated orbital chemical refinery — they sheared off and stole its cargo module.)
- Ferrik used **three ships:** his Far Trader (refitted), a refitted Type-S **Scout** (the *Misery's Company*, captained by the Vargr **Krrsh**), and a 400-ton armed salvage hauler — the **Mercifuge**, actually a **stolen Imperial Navy ship** (starburst-and-starship logo painted over). The Mercifuge is run by Ferrik's lieutenant **Miria Silverhand**.
- After the raids the gang fled toward **Borite**. En route Krrsh impetuously attacked the wrong freighter (the *Sarcomond*, which had hidden pop-up turrets); one of his crew died, the *Misery's Company* was mauled. For that failure Krrsh was **marooned by his own crewmates** on the ancient Sindalian station orbiting the Borite gas giant — a known pirate execution method.

### Where Ferrik really is, and how the trail works
- Ferrik himself is hiding **one jump out from Theev**, on the **4th moon of the gas giant in the Palindrome system** (`540PD-5/4`), aboard his Far Trader the **Janal Torsk**. The crew will only find this location by getting it from **Miria** (capture her, search her quarters/ship, or trick/turn her), because Miria is on Theev negotiating Ferrik's protection.
- Miria is on Theev pitching Ferrik to two gangs: **Petyr Vallis** (anti-Aslan zealot) and **Hroal Irontooth** (Aslan loot-pirate). **If the crew do nothing**, Miria succeeds: she places Ferrik and his two ships in **Petyr Vallis's** gang, Vallis leads them off to raid Aslan worlds **beyond the crew's reach**, and the trail goes cold. The clock matters.

### Darokyn's secret (the sting in the tail)
- If Ferrik faces capture/death, he makes a **last-ditch offer**: he'll sell **Admiral Darokyn's secret** for his life. The secret: **Darokyn is still in contact with, and secretly funded by, Imperial Intelligence** — the Imperium uses the "renegade admiral" as their **agent/spy in the wild Reach**. Ferrik discovered this (it's *why* Darokyn purged him) and has **proof** — intercepted comms recordings — but they're stored on a **secure computer aboard Darokyn's flagship**. Acquiring them lets the crew **blackmail Darokyn or bring his whole organisation down**, and may reveal Imperial anti-piracy operations across the sector. (A major sandbox thread; not resolved here.)

### Sindalian treasure seed (easy to miss)
- The Borite station holds a preserved **painting of a Sindalian Emperor wearing the lost crown jewels** — the lanthanum-alloy crown, the **Thalassan Pearl**, the diamond rings of Kether, and the **Eagle of Sol**. This is deliberate foreshadowing for the *Shadows of Sindal* / treasure threads. Note it; don't dwell.

## Scenes / Locations
Run as a branching investigation. Each port answers "where did they go next?" The hard gate is **getting a code for Theev** — which is what rescuing Krrsh provides.

### 1. The Psychopomps of Clarke (0702)
- **Trigger:** crew follow Rao's lead to Clarke (the raiders hit it first). Grey death-world; starport is neutral ground run by the death-cult priests, atop a million-strong cemetery and a black-pyramid **necrotemple** of carbon grave-markers (each holds a corpse).
- **What's here:** **Keeper Malos** (starport admin + priest) directs them to the **relic-quarry** attack site at the ruins of Hiewad City. An **Average (8+) Investigate** check (1D hrs, INT; may chain Electronics(sensors)/Recon) reconstructs the attack. By **Effect**:
  - *1–2:* Two ships landed and killed everyone, then blasted the warehouse and looted **recovered computer parts** (TL14+ salvage; the Florian Empire buys such scrap). The two ships were a **Type-S Scout** and a **Far Trader**, both refit with extra weapons — too weak to have done Torpol alone, so there must have been a **third, heavier ship**.
  - *3–4:* As above + the Psychopomps' fresh carbon monoliths include a victim revivable with a **Very Difficult (12+) Medic** check (1D×10 min, INT). If revived she reports the pirates were **led by a young man in an Imperial naval uniform**, and that the **scout filled its hold and left several hours ahead of the Far Trader**. (See Complications for failure.)
  - *5+:* The scavenger's radio logs recorded encrypted pirate chatter — a **Difficult (10+) Electronics(comms)** check (1D min, INT) breaks it, naming the leader **Ferrik**, a woman **Miria** commanding an orbiting ship, and a third captain **Krrsh**.
- **Complications:** deliberate killing is the cult's highest sin, so Malos is furious about the raid (cooperative toward the crew). **If the crew try to revive the monolith victim and fail the Medic check, the Psychopomps are enraged** — the crew "interfered with the sacred monolith" and are **no longer welcome outside Clarke's starport.** No other clues here; the trail continues on Torpol.

### 2. The Provosts of Torpol (0601)
- **Trigger:** crew go to Torpol (the raiders' second target; a watery "party planet" with a Highport for refuelling and a Downport). Directed to the southern polar port to meet **Provost Martial Haddo Farx**.
- **What's here:** Farx briefs them. The target was an **automated orbital chemical refinery**; its cameras transmitted images before destruction showing a **Far Trader** and a **400-ton Vulture-class salvage hauler**. Both refuelled at the orbital dock under false names (**"Argo 1" / "Argo 2"**), paid electronically, crews never disembarked.
  - **Difficult (10+) Electronics(sensors)** (1D min, INT) enhances the footage: the Far Trader has two double turrets (twin pulse lasers); the salvage hauler has two single + two double turrets (2 pulse lasers, 2 sandcasters, 2 missile launchers) — and computer analysis finds the **Imperial Navy starburst logo and registry** under fresh paint: she is the **ISS *Mercifuge*.**
  - **Admin / Electronics(computers)** or **Carouse** in the bar: per official bulletins the *Mercifuge* fled the Imperium ~30 years ago with renegade **"Admiral" Darokyn**, is listed **stolen**, and Darokyn is rumoured based on **Theev**. (Naval-background PCs/contacts already know Darokyn — point them at the *Trojan Reach* Library Data.)
  - **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics(comms)** (1D×10 min, INT) on comms logs: "Argo 1" sent one encrypted greeting while docked, to **a refitted scout landed at the Torpol Downport** → the **Sarcomond** lead.
- **Complications:** Farx is young, ambitious, ingratiating — he *wants* this solved to make his career and will push the crew forward. The scout (the *Misery's Company*) landed a day before the Argos; its Vargr captain **Krrrsh** sold the looted Clarke computer-scrap to a trader named **Holmer**, refuelled, left — and near the jump limit had an **incident with the *Sarcomond***. Only the *Sarcomond*'s crew know what happened. (If the crew already learned of the scout at Clarke, an **Average (8+) Investigate** (1D×10 min, SOC) jumps them straight to the *Sarcomond*.)

### 3. The Sarcomond incident (Torpol Downport)
- **Trigger:** following the scout/comms lead, the crew find **Captain Danel Vlaw** in the Downport bar; his battered Far Trader is docked under repair after a scout attacked it.
- **What's here:** Vlaw is a paranoid Asiman who **despises the Drinaxi nobles** (Asim was conquered 20 yrs ago). Getting him to talk needs **Persuade / Carouse / Deception**: **Routine (6+)** if he doesn't know the crew are from Drinax, **Very Difficult (12+)** if he thinks they work for "the conquerors." Win him over (or reveal the scout's murder of Clarke) and he recounts: a scout closed on a suspicious course, flipped, matched velocity, and demanded his cargo; his ship's **two pop-up turrets** ("she has surprises") burned the pirate amidships; over the radio he heard the (Vargr) captain order a jump for **Borite**.
- **Complications:** if the crew **fail to crack Vlaw**, fall back on the star chart: jump-2 from Torpol reaches Clarke, Blue, Drinax, Asim, Oghma, Marduk, **Borite**; eliminate Clarke/Drinax/Asim (no pirate activity there) and check the rest. **The trail continues at Borite.**

### 4. The slumbering horror (Borite, 0609) — the Krrsh rescue
- **Trigger:** scanning the Borite system picks up a **very weak distress call from the gas giant** → an ancient (2,000+ yr) **Sindalian High Guard station**, crumbling, slowly decaying into the atmosphere.
- **What's here:** **Average (8+) Electronics(sensors)** (1D×10 min, EDU) near the station reveals: signs of pirate repairs within the last century but no current occupancy; the distress call is a **boosted vacc-suit beacon** (someone wired a suit comm into the station's dish); ship debris consistent with the ***Misery's Company*** (docked here for repairs, didn't break up); and **at least one humanoid heat source — a Vargr in a vacc suit, in poor health.** Hailing gets no answer. **Docking is Difficult (10+) Pilot** (1D min, DEX) — the station is fragile; ramming/cutting/shooting it breaks pieces off.
- The Vargr is **Krrsh**, marooned here by his own crew after the *Sarcomond* fiasco. He wired his suit comm into the dish to boost his distress call **and** to lure the station's monster away from himself — meaning **his rescuers walk into the monster's hunting ground.**
- **The monster — a Hunter Chamax** (sterile, not a fecund Maternal; stats below). Awoken long ago by a pirate's pet "petrified Hiver" sculpture (actually a dormant Chamax from Foreven), it killed the old pirate crew and returned to slumber. It hunts by **heat, life-sense (Telepathy), and a radio-sense organ** (it can jam comms / mimic a distress signal, but not human voices). The station is mostly **dead** — no light/air/heat/gravity/computers; the crew need vacc suits and manual doors (1 min/zone to move, 10 min + **Average (8+) Recon** to search). A few zones are pressurised on nuclear batteries.
- **Station points of interest (map p.36):** 1 Manual Airlocks (gate to the warm zones; Chamax works them clumsily); 2 Deranged Ravings (a prior castaway's "it's coming" scrawls + an acid-dissolved human suit); 3 **Painting of the Sindalian Emperor in the lost crown jewels** (treasure seed, above); 4 Communications Dish (Krrsh's beacon); 5 **The Chamax's Lair** (resin-lined air vent; digging it turns up victims' cybernetics/teeth); 6 Krrsh's Hiding Place (he's stretching his air; refills at the nearest airlock).
- **Bug-hunt mechanics:** Chamax crosses **2 zones** per crew zone-move; max **6 min in vacuum** (then 1D dmg/round); targets the lone and unarmed; in vacuum uses acid to breach suits and drag corpses to the warm zones. Detect: **Difficult (10+) Electronics(sensors)** (needs a motion tracker). Track: **Very Hard (12+) Survival**. Hear: **Very Hard (12+) Recon**, **Formidable (14+)** in vacuum. **Station fragility:** any *missed* attack inflicting 10+ damage hits the station — pressurised zone decompresses, vacuum zone breaches/can shear off. **Krrsh has 10 min of suit air on arrival** (1 min in a warm zone buys 5 more).
- **mythic-gm keyed trigger (must-happen):** the Chamax awakens whenever living, warm prey enters the station — its **Life Sense** trips automatically. Don't roll *whether*; roll *when/where it strikes*: `dice.py keyed 1d10 8` each time the crew change zones or make noise/heat/radio (8+ → it closes this round). Reaching/rescuing Krrsh is the goal; the Chamax is the obstacle.
- **Complications:** killing the Chamax in vacuum is viable but risks venting the station; explosives/projectiles endanger Krrsh and the hull. The Chamax wants to **kill as many PCs as possible** (and would board the crew's ship if it could, but isn't smart enough to plan it).

### 4b. Krrsh's Tale + the Refuelling Dump (the gate to Theev)
- **Trigger:** rescue Krrsh. Over food and rum he confesses everything (rambling but covers the GM truths above): he captained the *Misery's Company* under **Ferrik Redthane**; was part of the Clarke raid; Ferrik was purged by **Darokyn** and now needs a gang's protection, which is why he raided Torpol/Clarke to show off; Ferrik dares not come to Theev so he sent **Miria Silverhand** to negotiate; Krrsh and Miria **hate each other** and he doesn't know which gang she'll approach or where she hides.
- **The payoff:** if offered passage to safety **or a berth on the crew**, Krrsh swears loyalty and **hands over the secret routes to Theev** — a **safe landing code** *and* the location of a hidden **refuelling dump ~1 parsec from Theev**, which lets a **jump-2 ship** reach Theev. (Krrsh stat block below; he is a recruitable, not-very-bright, broken NPC looking for a strong leader. The Harrier is jump-2, so this dump is the practical key.)
- **Refuelling Dump scene:** trusting astrogation to a drunken Vargr, the crew jump into **empty space** — a nerve-wracking blind jump (a bad jump strands them in deep space). On arrival **Electronics(comms or sensors)** detects a **weak beacon**; the dump is hundreds of 4-dton hydrogen drums, nearly invisible (same temp as space). **Krrsh's warning:** some drums are **booby-trapped with "poisoned" hydrogen** (heavy compounds that wreck a jump drive or force misjump) — **always run it through fuel purifiers** even though it looks refined. Then jump on to Theev.

### 5. Blacksand City, Theev (0506)
- **Trigger:** use Krrsh's landing **code** to pass **the Skull** and land at the **Theev Downport** (landing pads with bay doors that seal overhead to hide visitors). City = skyscrapers rising from canyons, linked by covered walkways.
- **The law:** met on arrival by a **Blacksand Widow** (genetically/cybernetically augmented enforcer; possibly a clone hive-mind — stats below). Two laws: in the **starport, Upper City, and any red-flag-marked building** = **Law of the Lords** (*no murder, no weapons fire, nothing disturbing the peace*); everywhere else = **Law of the Street** (anything goes except weapons that could breach the city's environmental containment). **Penalty for breaking either = death.** Red-flag zones **move** day to day.
- **Upper City** ("safe," corporate-enclave feel; everyone speaks in circumlocutions about piracy): the **Grand Hotel**, **Cliff District** (illegal weapons/mercs — "going off the cliff" = resorting to illegal methods), **Kallos Shipyards** (the Skull's front; builds/recycles pirate ships), the **Rose Pavilion** (exclusive restaurant, private booths — Darokyn's table), **Murghen Consortium** (~1,000 mercs, own ships, led by Johanna Murghen), **Asharden Camp** (refugee camp; many sink into Lower-City slavery).
- **Lower City** (dark, filthy, lethal; breather-mask outdoors in the canyon): **the Scrapheap** (junkyard of stolen goods/wrecks — Miria hides here), **Brain Dimming** (any drug imaginable), **Church of the Traveller** (God walks Humaniti incognito; some practice charity, some practice murder, hoping to find Him), **Tuk's** (a K'Kree-run bar, one of the safer dives), **the House Without Questions** (charity/assassin's guild hybrid — take you in *or* hire them, no questions), **Blood Pit** (lethal arena).
- **Investigations in Blacksand** (asking openly gets you stabbed; info scales with **Effect**): **Threats/Intimidation** Difficult (10+) Persuade (1D hrs, STR); **Gossip/Rumours** Difficult (10+) Carouse/Streetwise (1D days, INT) — slow but safe; **Bribery** Average (8+) Broker (1D hrs, SOC) ~Cr5,000/tip (fail = ripped off); **Krrsh's contacts** point the crew at **Darokyn** and tell them about **Miria**; wandering the Upper City long enough → **Darokyn contacts them.**
- **Complications (Blacksand Justice):** the Widows kill lawbreakers with perfect lethality (e.g., cut through a window, seal the breach, snipe a target on the street below). If the crew make trouble, **the gangs push back hard** — and there are many, with many guns. Plot encounters: a spy named **Gesh** quizzes them on their Drinax ties; a **Blood Gang** ambush (may flee if outgunned); a refugee **Vii Lon** begs for passage off-world; pirates plot to **steal the crew's ship** by kidnapping a crew member and dismembering him for the codes; a Contact in trouble. (Use page-263 patrons here.)
- **mythic-gm note:** treat Theev as an open sandbox node — drive scene-to-scene with mythic-gm; the "must-happen" beats are only **(a)** Miria arrives a few hours after the crew, and **(b)** if the crew stall, Miria closes her deal with Vallis (clock below).

### 6. The Girl & the Gangs (finding Ferrik via Miria)
- **Trigger:** the crew need Ferrik's location; **every route runs through Miria Silverhand**, who arrives on Theev a few hours after them and hides in the **Scrapheap**, working through paid beggar-boys/runners (the crew have **almost no chance** of finding her unaided). She's pitching Ferrik to **Petyr Vallis** and **Hroal Irontooth**.
- **Four ways to locate Miria:** (a) get her location from **Admiral Darokyn** (if he trusts them); (b) buy it from **Vallis or Hroal** (dearly); (c) infiltrate/spy on the gangs and **tail her runners** back to the Scrapheap; (d) pose as a new gang willing to ally with Ferrik and **lure Miria into contact.**
- **Negotiating the gang leaders (all want something — full NPCs below):**
  - **Admiral Darokyn** — meets at the Rose Pavilion. **Difficult (10+) Diplomat** (SOC) or SOC 10+ to impress. Will reveal Miria's location **if** the crew promise Ferrik dies "quietly and thoroughly" and that they **stay off Theev for a year**. (He never says outright that he wants Ferrik dead — he's hiding *why* Ferrik fled: see Darokyn's Secret.)
  - **Petyr Vallis** — borderline psychopath (Science(psychology)/high INT spots it); fanatical Aslan-hater. Wants the crew to **join his war on the Aslan**, or **money**. For Miria's location: **Cr50,000** or use of the crew's havens as bases vs. the Aslan. For help taking Ferrik: **MCr1** or joining his Aslan war. **Allying with Vallis costs the crew 1D Standing with the Aslan Hierate.**
  - **Hroal Irontooth** — Aslan loot-pirate who secretly **misses home / craves territory** but won't admit it. **Difficult (10+) Persuade** (STR) to impress. Gives Miria's location/help **if** the crew promise to get **King Oleb to grant the Aslan land when Drinax is restored** (he'll never ask directly) — otherwise something worth **≥Cr50,000.**
- **Once Miria is located, three handling options:** capture her in the Scrapheap and force her to talk; search her Scrapheap room / her ship's nav logs for Ferrik's location; or meet her in a Lower-City **red-flag zone** and **convince her to betray** Ferrik / trick her into revealing him.
- **Heading into the Scrapheap:** a maze of a dozen welded-together hull-wrecks (fake red flags out front to scare off thugs). Miria's lair is the upper deck of a downed **Fat Trader** (lower deck flooded with toxic chemical waste); she waits on the **bridge** with her rapid-fire machinegun, doors rusted open for a clear field of fire. **Sneak in disguised** and they can reach her; **arrive openly and she knows they're coming** and may ambush them. Ferrik's hideout location is among her possessions.
- **Sneaking onto the Skull (alt.):** go to the Highport and find Miria's ship, the **Mercifuge**. Security is light — **Average (8+) Stealth** (DEX) reaches it unseen — **but the whole asteroid is a red-flag (Widow) zone.** Three of five crew aboard on maintenance; ship is locked to Miria's codes (hack = **Formidable (14+) Electronics(computers)**, 1D min, INT) but the **nav logs are trivially easy** to pull.
- **The jump location:** every route yields the same answer — Ferrik is **one jump away, on the 4th moon of the gas giant in the Palindrome system.**
- **The clock (mythic-gm keyed must-happen):** if the crew **don't act**, Miria seals a place for Ferrik in **Petyr Vallis's** gang and Vallis leads them off to raid Aslan worlds spinward (Tliowaha/Goertal/Nora'a) — out of reach, trail cold. Track elapsed Theev-time; when the crew dither, advance the deal. (`dice.py keyed 1d10 <target>` to time Miria's negotiations if you want a hard countdown.)

### 7. Ambush! — capturing Ferrik (Palindrome, `540PD-5/4`)
- **Trigger:** the crew jump to Palindrome (0606) and approach the **4th moon** (icy methane wastes). Ferrik's **Janal Torsk** (200t Far Trader; stats below) is landed there with a **full crew of ten** (5 crew, 4 marines, Ferrik). If a ship approaches, the pirates lie low; if it then runs a **sensor scan of the moon**, they assume detection and either **attack or flee** depending on how well-armed the newcomer looks.
- **Killing Ferrik** is easy — the **Harrier outguns the Janal Torsk** (more so with Theev help); just keep firing until she breaks apart and scoop the corpse from space afterward. *But* if her hull is breached he makes one plea for clemency (Darokyn's Secret offer — below).
- **Capturing Ferrik alive** (harder, and what the bounty pays best for): best is to **trick him into surrendering** — lure him into an ambush if the crew captured/turned **Miria**, or pose as a damaged ship that misjumped in and let him come to them; otherwise land on the moon and storm the *Janal Torsk* on the ground, or cripple and board her.
- **Darokyn's Secret (keyed offer):** **mythic-gm keyed trigger — if Ferrik is about to be captured or his ship is breached, he contacts the crew with his last-ditch offer** (sell Darokyn's Imperial-Intelligence secret for his life; the proof is on Darokyn's flagship — see GM truths). Always play this beat when Ferrik is cornered alive. It's the crew's choice whether to take the deal.
- **Complications:** if the crew **promised Darokyn that Ferrik dies quietly**, keeping Ferrik alive (and learning his secret) breaks that promise and sours Darokyn. Taking Ferrik to the bounty worlds **alive** vs. **dead** changes the wrap-up (below).

## Rewards & Consequences
- **Bounties:** capturing/killing the raiders pays **Torpol MCr2 + Clarke MCr0.5 = MCr2.5** total — *proof required* that these are the actual raiders. (Darokyn/Vallis/Hroal each take a cut if the crew bought their help — Vallis up to MCr1, others ~Cr50,000 — netting against the bounty.) Remember **Oleb's 10% tithe** on takings.
- **Disposition of Ferrik (the wrap-up):** bring Redthane back (alive or his corpse) and the two worlds agree a **compromise sentence** — he is frozen in a **Clarke-style carbon tomb** and placed in **orbit of Torpol as an eternal warning** to lawbreakers. **Either outcome (alive-then-frozen, or killed-then-recovered) earns the full bounty and the political shift**, provided proof is delivered.
- **Recruited NPC:** **Krrsh** joins the crew if offered safety/a berth — a loyal (if dim) Vargr pilot/gunner who knows the Theev routes. Losing him to the Chamax forfeits that.
- **Darokyn's Secret:** if the crew spared Ferrik and pursue it, they gain a **major leverage thread** over Admiral Darokyn (blackmail or topple his gang; possible window into Imperial anti-piracy ops). The proof still has to be stolen from Darokyn's flagship later.
- **Sindalian treasure lead:** the Borite painting points toward the **lost Sindalian crown jewels** (Eagle of Sol, Thalassan Pearl, etc.) — fuel for *Shadows of Sindal* / treasure adventures.
- **Failure / costs (be honest):**
  - **Too slow on Theev** → Miria signs Ferrik into Vallis's gang and they vanish raiding Aslan space; the trail dies and the bounty is likely lost.
  - **Failing the Clarke revival** → barred from Clarke outside the starport (and a worse start with the Psychopomps).
  - **Breaking Theev's law** (murder/weapons-fire in a Lords zone, or any containment-breaching weapon) → **death** at Widow hands, no appeal; making general trouble → the gangs (and there are many guns) hunt the crew off-world.
  - **Allying with Petyr Vallis** → **−1D Standing with the Aslan Hierate.**
  - **Promising Hroal land / Aslan territory** → a promise that will bind King Oleb later (a political IOU to honour or break).
  - **Promising Darokyn that Ferrik dies + staying off Theev for a year** → then keeping Ferrik alive (for the secret) **breaks the deal** and makes an enemy of the best-organised gang in the Reach.
  - **Blind jump to the fuel dump going wrong**, or **using un-purified booby-trapped fuel** → misjump / wrecked jump drive; can strand the crew.
  - The **Chamax** can kill careless or isolated crew outright (acid breaches suits in vacuum); the **station can be vented** by stray fire.

## Empire / Reputation effects
*(Apply via empire-reputation.md → "Per-adventure effects.")*
- **Bring the raiders to justice in King Oleb's name** → **Torpol moves +2 steps toward Haven** and **Clarke moves +2 steps toward Haven** (both start **Unfriendly/Neutral** respectively; this is the adventure's headline payoff and the proof-of-concept for the privateer scheme). Anchor the shift to *who delivered the bounty in Drinax's name*.
- **Permanent campaign bonus:** crews who begin the campaign with *Honour Among Thieves* get **DM+1 to all Finale recruitment rolls** thereafter.
- **Theev** is **Friendly** at start; behaving inside its laws and dealing fairly with a gang keeps/strengthens that. **Picking a gang leader's side** (esp. Vallis vs. the Aslan, or pledging Hroal land) shifts later faction/Attitude work — record the promise.
- **Standing:** allying with **Petyr Vallis** → **−1D Hierate Standing.** Killing/robbing nobody outside the mission keeps Imperial Standing clean. (No automatic Standing gain here — the win is *world Attitude*, not great-power favour.)
- **Court standing at Drinax:** success validates **Rao's** scheme (raises her regard; needles **Lord Wrax**, who opposed the piracy plan). Note it on the court tracker.
- **Lost Worlds:** none expected (no WMDs / world destruction in this adventure).

## Threads & hooks onward
- **Into the sandbox:** the crew now have the **Harrier**, the **marque**, a **safe code + fuel route to Theev** (a recurring Friendly base for fencing, recruiting, intel), and first contact with the **four gang leaders** — the spine of every later pirate dealing.
- **Hroal Irontooth → *Treasure Ship* (Adv. 2):** Hroal independently plots to seize the Imperial Treasure Ship *Martin II*; prior dealings here (ally or enemy) carry straight in.
- **Admiral Darokyn → Imperial-Intelligence thread:** the proof aboard his flagship is a standing heist/blackmail hook; pulls toward Imperial anti-piracy operations and Darokyn's downfall or co-option.
- **Sindal / treasure threads:** the Borite station (a Sindalian High Guard outpost) and the painted **lost crown jewels** seed *Shadows of Sindal* and treasure-hunt adventures; the station's *Hiver-sculpture* origin nods to Foreven/Chamax lore.
- **mythic-gm List seeds:** *Threads* — "deliver proof + collect Torpol/Clarke bounty," "decide Ferrik's fate," "Darokyn's secret (steal proof from his flagship)," "honour/break promise to Vallis or Hroal," "recover the lost Sindalian crown jewels." *Characters* — Krrsh (crew), Miria (if spared/turned), Darokyn, Petyr Vallis, Hroal Irontooth, Provost Falx, Keeper Malos, Sal Dancet, the Widows. *Threats* — Aslan Hierate Standing (if Vallis-allied), Theev's lethal law, exposure of Drinax's hand.
