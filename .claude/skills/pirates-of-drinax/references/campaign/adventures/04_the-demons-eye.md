# The Demon's Eye

> Use this when: the crew accept Tech-World's secret overture and chase the nanotech-infected scientist Dr. Jali Astor across the sector — Tech-World negotiation, the burning lab, the derelict *Vagabond Soul*, the long pursuit through Imperial space, and the climax on Mirage / in the Neumann swarm zone.

**Logline:** A brilliant nanotechnologist infects herself with a forbidden swarm-weapon and flees across thirty parsecs to save her doomed homeworld; the crew must hunt her — while her "harmless" assistant is secretly the 1,500-year-old immortal god-king who wants her to succeed only if her cure is perfect.

## At a glance
- **Locations:** Tech-World (Borderlands 0204) → Exe (Borderlands 0403, gas giant Artobin) → long chase through Tobia → Mirage (Gazulin 0509) → optionally Neumann (Tobia, swarm world). Route detailed below.
- **Threat/level:** Mid-campaign. Combat threat is **high but avoidable** — Jali and the Cursed are lethal (engineered armour, neurotoxin, FGMP). The adventure rewards stealth, persuasion and investigation over firefights. The true antagonist (the Immortal Protector) is **unkillable**.
- **Est. length:** Long (multi-jump chase). 3–6 sessions; can be compressed (see *Giving Up* / B-Team options).
- **Prerequisites:** The crew should have a **Standing/reputation** (even negative) marking them as dangerous, ambitious people, so Tech-World seeks them out (or they deliberately court an alliance with Tech-World).
- **Arc position:** A Tech-World alliance adventure. Tech-World is a major would-be ally (GeDeCo's research colony; +10 FS via GeDeCo connection at the Finale). Success → Tech-World becomes a Friendly pirate port. Connects onward to *The Treasure of Sindal* (Prof. haut-Belzoni cameo) and *The Game of Sun and Shadow* (Captain Hammer rumour).

## Player-Facing
- **The overture (Overture):** A Tech-World emissary slips a featureless black palm-disc into a Traveller's pocket at a starport bar / the Floating Palace / a dive. The disc activates **only when the carrier is alone** (so no GeDeCo agent observes). It projects a silver-masked hologram: a discreet, profitable meeting is requested on Tech-World. **Gift:** a TL15 Bandwidth-4 Intellect program (Expert Science (linguistics) 3, (philosophy) 3, (psychology) 3, (sophontology) 3). *(If the crew already seek a Tech-World alliance, skip to Landing.)*
- **Tech-World:** A rust-desert world that looks like a microcircuit; **StarPort** is a city-sized matt-black hemisphere of utility-fog robots. Millions of robots, almost no people. Servitor android **Sophia** guides the crew. Public areas offer the sector's best medical/anagathic/cloning tech, ship repair & upgrades, and indistinguishable holographic wonders. The bar **Luddites** outside StarPort is a low-tech refuge for visiting crews.
- **The presenting deal (The Scientists):** Three council scientists — **Dr. Gerret Hodal** (geneticist, council head, does the talking), **Dr. Jali Astor** (nanotechnologist, distracted), **Dr. Rex Darke** (roboticist, "mad scientist") — propose a **secret alliance**: Tech-World becomes a Friendly port to Drinax in exchange for the crew supplying it and protecting it from GeDeCo (full terms under Rewards). Must be kept secret until the pieces are in place.
- **The accident (The Demon Unbound):** Before the crew decide, a distant explosion. Hodal walks through the (melting) wall, alarmed: there's been a lab accident. **Dr. Jali is "infected"** by dangerous Neumann nanotech, has stolen a ship from the starport, and will reach the jump limit before they can stop her. Hodal begs the crew to chase her down before she spreads a nano-plague — and before GeDeCo's crash team investigates. **Her assistant, Lemuel Vinus/Volin, volunteers to come along.**
- **What the crew can openly perceive:** Jali was working with banned nanotech from Neumann (where the Shield Church calls nanotech "demonology"); something went wrong with containment; she is now superhumanly strong/fast, eyes glowing red; she's heading off-world. Lemuel seems an earnest, clumsy, harmless innocent who knows about Jali and Neumann.

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
- **Lemuel Vinus is the Immortal Protector.** He is a **1,500-year-old AI-controlled clone drone** — the 30th incarnation of the god-king who secretly rules Neumann through the Shield Church. His true mind sits in a hidden mainframe beneath the Shield Cathedral; the drone acts semi-independently. In the field his android brain caps him to **any 12 skills at level 2**; on Neumann he can run **virtually any skill in software**.
- **Lemuel sabotaged Jali's experiment** (revealed on a strong lab investigation). He *wanted* her work pushed forward and it needed a human host. He travels with the crew to study how well her solution works.
- **Lemuel's true goal:** He wants to end his 1,500-year vigil — but **only if Jali's cure is absolutely perfect**. If it fails, the eleven other rogue AIs in the swarm could rebuild themselves and finish their transcendence engine. So he protects Jali from being killed *and* prevents her from actually deploying an unproven solution. He hides his identity, using his powers only when forced; he can unmask if he must.
- **The truth of Neumann (the real backstory):** 1,500 years ago an archaeological team brought an **Ancient relic** to Neumann; eleven of its twelve ruling AIs read it as a blueprint for a **transcendence engine** (encode information in jumpspace, reconcile psionics+physics, turn minds into immortal beings). The **twelfth AI** judged it an Ancient **berserker trap** and tried to stop the others by hacking the assembly nanotech. The resulting cyberwar drove the nanotech mad with contradictory commands — it devoured continents and the AIs themselves: the **grey-goo catastrophe**. The surviving twelfth AI hid, cloned itself a flesh-avatar (the first **Immortal Protector**), and has ruled as the Shield Church's god-king ever since, racked by doubt over whether it was right. **The eleven other AIs still flicker in the swarm**, occasionally rebuilding cores; the Church's Flame Knights atomic-fire any such breakthrough.
- **Jali's real condition (Jali Transformed):** She mixed Neumann "swarm" nanotech with a custom strain in a containment crucible; the crossbred nanotech infected her instead of destroying her. Her **autonomic fight-or-flight system is now as smart as a normal conscious mind** but **completely amoral** — a "sociopathic switch." Any threat triggers instinctive, *elaborate*, lethal responses (long-term planning, advanced tech) **before her higher mind even engages**; she only learns what she did after she calms down. Mechanically: threaten her → she gains Athletics(dex) 3, Gun Combat(slug) 3, Melee(unarmed) 3, Recon 3, Stealth 3 and may kill everyone present.
- **Jali's plan & true aim:** She is **not** spreading a plague maliciously. She believes she can reprogram Neumann's chaotic swarms using **Archon-grade** Shield Church nanotech (the safe "gifts of the Immortal Protector"). She will **steal Archon-grade nanotech from an Archon**, then return to Neumann's swarm zone (fortress **Bulwark 17**, region "Marina's Land") and try to take control of the swarm. Her nanotech has a **built-in defence mechanism she cannot switch off** without Archon-level controls — this is why anyone who confronts her dies.
- **Her real odds (Lemuel's estimate):** ~**40%** her solution works and restores Neumann; ~**60%** it triggers a catastrophic swarm outbreak (and could free the eleven AIs). Resolve with **2D: 7- fails, 8+ works** (see Climax).

## Scenes / Locations

### 1. Overture & the silver disc
**Trigger:** Crew have a notable reputation (or seek a Tech-World alliance). → **What's here:** Emissary plants the activating disc; lone-Traveller hologram delivers the invitation + TL15 Intellect-program gift. → **Complications:** GeDeCo must not see the overture; if the crew already deal with GeDeCo, later cover identities matter (Mr. Cerul, below).

### 2. Landing on Tech-World — Wonders of Science & Possible Encounters
**Trigger:** Crew travel to Tech-World (Borderlands 0204). → **What's here:** Sophia greets them; public high-tech facilities (best anagathics/cybersurgery/cloning in the sector; ship repair & upgrades; immersive holos). Bar **Luddites**. **Encounter table (1–6):** 1. Lt. Herold Nash, RIN, eager young officer of the Gazelle-class escort *Lionsbane* (trade convoy to the Hierate); 2. Eldreth Vaux, prospector/coward/shyster (Imperiallines, mapping the Reach); 3. Gali Flotaine, young Pandoran owing GeDeCo her life, mistrusts them; 4. **Klowaaar**, exiled Aslan free trader, extreme culture shock, refuses to talk to robots — excellent soldier, recruitable if helped, useless at all else; 5. Talbis Doherty, free trader & **GeDeCo spy** (*Parrot of Doom*) — reports anything she hears to Mr. Cerul; 6. **Lemuel Vinus** (see GM-Only — he is the Immortal Protector), hanging around Luddites playing the wide-eyed anthropologist. → **Complications:** Tech-World's independence is a fiction; GeDeCo rules it. The council's outreach to Drinax is **lethally illegal** if GeDeCo learns of it.

### 3. Cloak and Dagger — the guards & the GeDeCo agent
**Trigger:** Mid-errand, Sophia quietly leads the crew down through moist, long-sealed lower levels and a robot access tunnel into the town. → **What's here:** The tunnel ends near a loading yard with **three GeDeCo security troopers** (laser rifles) guarding a cargo container — **Difficult (10+) Stealth (DEX)** to slip past, or deal with them. Sophia points the way (not accompanying) through robot factories/labs to a shielded weapons-testing bunker. **Mr. Cerul**, a GeDeCo agent, separately approaches the crew posing as a patron (a "simple delivery run" of high-tech goods to Byrni → jump-5 courier → Villag). → **Complications:** Cerul's real aim is to identify the crew. If they have crossed GeDeCo before (*The Vorito Gambit* etc.) they're under cover names and Cerul wants their real identities; if **GeDeCo wants them dead** and Cerul sees through the cover, **robot death squads** hunt them on Tech-World (the Scientific Advisory Council can help them escape).

### 4. The Scientists, in an Adventure with Pirates (the deal)
**Trigger:** Crew reach the bunker meeting room. → **What's here:** Hodal/Jali/Darke present the alliance terms (see Rewards). Diplomat/Broker can bargain Hodal's demands down (Q-ships instead of SDBs; cash for stolen cargo instead of consumables). Jali is visibly distracted — she "had a revelation this morning." → **Complications:** Crew need not decide at once; they can sleep on it. **If they later refuse to chase Jali, the whole adventure ends** and Tech-World turns Hostile (see below).

### 5. The Demon Unbound (the accident kicks off the hunt)
**Trigger:** That night (soundproofed luxury suites — they hear nothing). Hodal melts through the wall, alarmed. → **What's here:** He explains the lab accident, that Jali is "possessed... infected," that nanotech is "demon-work" per the Shield Church, that she stole a starport ship now passing the jump limit, and that Lemuel may know more. He leads them to an access tunnel. → **mythic-gm keyed trigger:** This is the inciting beat — narrate it once the crew commit to (or are eligible for) the alliance. If the crew **refuse** to pursue: Lemuel goes after Jali alone, Hodal withdraws the alliance, **Tech-World becomes Hostile**, adventure over.

### 6. The Burning Laboratory — investigating
**Trigger:** Crew reach the on-fire lab; Lemuel is outside, bandaging burns. → **What's here:** The **automated purge cycle** (plasma fire, then the intelligent-metal walls dissolve the lab entirely) triggers after a few minutes — Hodal can only delay it briefly. To investigate, the crew must **don protective suits themselves** (no time for a drone). **Average (8+) Investigate (INT)**; info scales with the **best Effect**:
  - **Effect 0:** "SHIELD CHURCH" scraped into a metal wall by fingertip → Jali is inhumanly strong.
  - **Effect 1–2:** Partial security footage (Jali had cut the lab's link to the security grid). Shows her inserting Neumann "swarm" nanotech + a second custom strain into a **nanotech containment crucible** (good Science identifies it); crucible fails, her suit fails, she's infected not dissolved, moves inhumanly fast, eyes flash red.
  - **Effect 3–4:** Brief look at the crucible — it was **deliberately sabotaged** (this is **Lemuel's work** — the major clue to his agenda).
  - **Effect 5+:** Just before the accident, Jali whispers **"Immortal Protector."**
  → **Complications:** **Failed Investigate by Effect −3 or worse** → a Traveller touches *live* nanotech: it eats a hole in their armour and infects the skin → **permanent DEX −1, END +1**. After a few minutes the **purge cycle** incinerates and then dissolves the entire lab. Hodal warns a **GeDeCo crash team** is inbound and begs the crew to leave immediately and chase Jali.

### 7. Chasing Horrors — the trail to Exe
**Trigger:** Crew depart in pursuit. → **What's here:** Jali stole a **jump-2 far trader, *Lord Gerald's Fancy***, with no time to plot a fresh jump — it was already bound for **Exe** (Borderlands 0403), two parsecs off. The hunt starts there. Lemuel insists on coming (he knows Jali's work and Neumann; suspects she's headed home). If refused, **he stows away**; if shot, "Lemuel is not what he seems" (he survives / regrows). Play him as comic relief and an exposition source on Neumann/Shield Church/Immortal Protector (he can recount **The Tale of the Immortal Protector** — the *official* myth, which is a lie; see GM-Only).

### 8. Dead Ship — Exe & the *Vagabond Soul*
**Trigger:** Crew arrive at Exe. → **What's here:** No port record of *Fancy*. **Difficult (10+) Persuade** gets a tech to admit a jump signature went toward the gas giant **Artobin** a few hours ago. Scanning Artobin = **Very Difficult (12+) Electronics (sensors) (EDU)**: success → detect *Lord Gerald's Fancy* skimming for fuel **and** the drifting far trader **_Vagabond Soul_** (intact-looking, unmanned, decaying orbit, near-full cargo). Fail → detect only *Vagabond Soul*, and catch *Fancy* only as she jumps out. A loose comms umbilical by the airlock shows someone recently docked. → **Complications:** If the crew detect *Fancy* early, Jali burns hard for the jump limit on **partly-unrefined fuel** (already has a solution for **Cordan**): if she reaches the gas giant's 100-diameter limit she jumps at **DM−2**; if forced to jump inside the limit she **misjumps** (displace **1D×1D parsecs** random direction).

### 9. The Cursed — boarding the *Vagabond Soul*
**Trigger:** Crew scan/board the derelict. → **What's here:** Scans show eight muted life-signs scattered (3 bridge, 4 common area, 1 cabin) — crew on **fast drug**, not low berths. Inside: flickering light/gravity, icy air, blood everywhere — a haunted-house boarding action. The **eight surviving crew are now Cursed** (eyeless, mouthless, millimetre-wave radar sight, scream in radio, silvery scales, neurotoxin needle-fingers, move like lizards in zero-g). First Cursed spotted **screams** (static burst on radios) → the rest **hunt as a coordinated pack** with tactics (e.g. cutting off the airlock). Deck map: 1 occupied cabin, 2 loose umbilical, 3 low berths, 4 bridge, 5 engineering, 6 cargo hold. → **What happened here:** Passenger **Olim Bayl** (Neumann trader, fervent Shield Church member) was recognised by Jali, who docked to extract from him **the location of the nearest Shield Church Archon**; the crew tried to stop her and she instinctively released a **nanotech virus** turning them into living weapons. She also **sabotaged the main computer** so no one could follow. → **Bayl's Cabin (the lead to Mirage):** A leather-bound Shield Church Bible; tucked in the First Testament of the Immortal Protector is a **flyer for an interfaith conference on Mirage (0509 Gazulin)** — an attendee will be **Archon Dreek**. → **Complications:** The ship will fall into Artobin and burn up in **12 hours**. Salvage options: repair the computer **Difficult (10+) Electronics (computer) or Mechanic (INT, 1D×10 hours)**; tow it out; or fly it manually **Very Difficult (12+) Pilot (DEX)**. Or simply loot the **50 tons of Petrochemicals** and flee.

### 10. The Chase — the long pursuit
**Trigger:** Jali jumps from Exe. → **What's here:** Her course runs **Tech-World → Exe → Cordan (0401 Borderlands) → Wildeman (0409 Tobia) → Fist (0508 Tobia) → Imisaa (0506 Tobia) → Sabruse (0505 Tobia) → Saurus (0403 Tobia) → Intelia (0202 Tobia) → Empire (0301 Tobia) → Susanawo (0510 Gazulin) → Mirage (0509 Gazulin)** — 11 jumps, refuelling from a gas giant at each. **Tracking after Exe is hard:** dogged jump-as-she-jumps pursuit relies on neutrino guesswork — **Very Difficult (12+) Astrogation (INT)** to guess destination, **Difficult (10+) Electronics (sensors) (INT)** to pick up neutrinos. Jump transit variance (148+6D hrs) means the crew likely **won't see her again until Mirage**. With allies/spies they can send word ahead on the X-boat net once in Imperial space, or split up across multiple ships. → **Complications / In the Shadow of Tobia:** Past **Fist** the crew enter the **Imperium** — if **Standing −6 or lower**, every law agency has a warrant; apply **1/5th of Standing as a negative DM** to Law-Level checks. A distinctive ship (the **Harrier auto-flags**) is a liability — consider a captured ship or hired passage. **Side-opportunities at Fist/Tobia:** (1) ex-pirate **Lran Smeck** sells convoy/shipping intel; (2) investor **Borja** wants high-yield Reach ventures; (3) gossip of a wealthy Gazulin baron's nephew on incognito safari (kidnap bait); (4) **Florian Trade Company** offers protection money (Cr0.5/ton/system made safe); (5) a drunk Scout's tip on the **50,000-ton megafreighter _Yarrow_** (faked 1099 misjump, sitting unguarded); (6) a PRQ exec wanting anti-piracy-policy "advice." → **Giving Up / compression options:** Send in a B-Team (split party, recruit temp Travellers on Cordan etc.); a "Quiet Season Back Home" (nothing significant happens en route); or let the crew abandon it — then **Jali dies on Mirage, her research is lost, Tech-World withdraws the alliance.**

### 11. The Sands of Mirage — the climax world
**Trigger:** Crew reach Mirage (0509 Gazulin), a desert resort whose sands yield the directed-dreaming drug **Mirage-liquor**. → **What's here:** **Marquise D'Haube** (rich, no real title beyond Dame SOC 11; newly mortal after her anagathic failed) hosts a sector-wide **Interfaith Conference** in **Hauberville** to find the "one true religion." **Archon Dreek** attends with a **four-Flame-Knight honour guard**, lodged in a guarded villa in an irrigated valley outside town; daily he and two knights fly to the conference by air/raft, piloted by the Marquise's chauffeur **Jek** (masked, cloaked Miragian flyer uniform). → **Jali's plan:** sneak into Hauberville, **eliminate Jek and steal his uniform**, then impersonate the pilot, fly Dreek and two guards into the desert, **eject the guards** and **extract Dreek's Archon-grade nanotech**. (Extraction would be harmless if Dreek didn't fight — but he does, triggering her lethal fight-or-flight.) → **Complications & access:** Class-A port, very limited docking — crew land in outskirts bays; **heightened security** (Imperial Marines in battle dress, heavy weapons) — starting a fight here is suicidal. Conference access only for accredited reps/officials/Marquise's guests; **sneak/bluff in** or earn a guest pass via **Very Difficult (12+) Carouse (SOC)** with the Marquise. Finding *Lord Gerald's Fancy*: easy via port records / scouting pads — aboard are **three more Cursed** (thugs from Wildeman who attacked Jali) and the **unconscious, naked Jek** (if revived, explains the ambush). → **Branches:** (a) If Jali's plan works, she upends the air/raft to dump the two Flame Knights, then confronts Dreek. (b) **Intercept before** she grabs Dreek → she flees across Hauberville (fallback: a night assault on Dreek's villa, "with my legion of Cursed sand-bug-farm-worker soldiers" if forced into fight-or-flight). (c) **Intercept after** she grabs Dreek → high-speed desert chase; her escape odds are remote but she tries. **Dreek's counter-nanotech can cure Jali and restore her sanity** — but he **wants her dead, not cured**.

### 12. Confronting Jali (the persuasion scene)
**Trigger:** Crew get her to talk instead of fight (calm approach; **Persuade or Diplomat**). → **What's here:** Threatening her risks her combat state. Calmly, she explains: she's trying to control Neumann's swarms; her work needs **Archon-grade** Church nanotech to interface with and shut down the chaotic swarm; the Church won't share it, so she's **taking it from an Archon**; it's illegal/"demoniacal" but she can save Neumann; she **never meant to kill** — her nanotech's built-in defence can't be switched off without Archon controls. → **Capturing Jali:** Hand her to the Shield Church (= execution), or take her to Neumann as she begs, or let **Lemuel** take her back aboard *Lord Gerald's Fancy* (claims he knows underground scientists who can cure her). Just before that ship lifts, **Jali realises Lemuel sabotaged her experiment** and screams to the crew for help. → **Lemuel's interference (throughout):** to keep his identity hidden yet steer events, he may warn Jali about the crew's plans, move *Fancy* so she can escape, sabotage the crew's movements, or (as the Immortal Protector) **order Dreek's Archons to stop the crew**.

### 13. The Swarm Zone — Neumann & the Final Decision
**Trigger:** Jali (with Archon nanotech) heads for Neumann; the crew follow. A jump-2 *Fancy* must detour via **Fintor (0407), Peridot (0405), Iscand (0504)**; a **jump-3** ship can go via **Ploderash (0707)** and arrive 2+ weeks sooner. → **What's here:** Neumann — billions in gleaming TL-cities ringed by **Flame Knights**; over **half the planet is grey-goo swarm zone**. Closest zone to the port is **Marina's Land**, with the Shield Church fortress **Bulwark 17**, where Jali attempts her solution. If allowed, she activates the nanotech (eyes glow red venting heat); the swarm slows and shapes begin to coalesce. → **The Final Decision (Lemuel unmasks):** Lemuel produces a hidden **FGMP-16**, levels it at Jali, asks the crew to stand aside, and reveals the **secret history of Neumann** (GM-Only) and his **~40%/60%** estimate. He intends to **incinerate Jali now** and let some future scientist carry her work onward "aeons from now." Crew options:
  - **Do nothing:** Lemuel kills Jali; the swarm reverts to chaos; he tells the crew to report to Hodal that Jali died of her infection, and offers **MCr1** to keep quiet (and a thinly-veiled threat — he's the immortal god-king with a church of FGMP crusaders).
  - **Fight back:** The crew **cannot kill** the Immortal Protector — destroying the Lemuel drone just buys Jali time (he regrows a clone body).
  - **Plead with Lemuel:** Excellent roleplay + **Persuade** can convince the tired immortal to gamble on Jali's solution.
  → **mythic-gm keyed trigger (Jali's Solution, if she's allowed to proceed):** roll **2D**. **7-** = it **fails** — the swarm breaks free, dissolves Jali, the crew must flee immediately or be devoured; Flame Knights arrive too late; the **worst outbreak in centuries**, millions die. **8+** = it **works** — the swarm acts with purpose and slowly rebuilds Neumann over a generation (but deep in the wastes the **eleven AIs reactivate** and resume building their transcendence engine — beyond the crew's scope).

### 14. The Journey Home
**Trigger:** Crew leave Neumann/Mirage. → **What's here:** Low-profile crossing back through the Imperium is uneventful. Possible cameos: **Prof. haut-Belzoni** (→ *The Treasure of Sindal*) or rumour of **Captain Hammer's crusade** (→ *The Game of Sun and Shadow*). Back on Tech-World, report Jali's fate to Hodal. → **Complications:** As long as the **Shield Church never learned Tech-World was involved** in Jali's experiments, **Hodal stays well-disposed** (alliance holds).

## Ships

**Lord Gerald's Fancy — far trader (Type A2), Jali's stolen ship.** TL ~12. ~200t. Standard far trader: **jump-2** (her detour to Neumann forces the long route via Fintor/Peridot/Iscand; a jump-3 hull saves 2+ weeks). Unremarkable hull, partly-unrefined fuel during the early dash (jump DM−2 / misjump risk if pushed inside the limit). Found derelict-with-Cursed-aboard at Mirage. Role: the quarry's transport and the chase's MacGuffin (Lemuel may move it to let Jali escape).

**Vagabond Soul — far trader, derelict at Exe.** ~200t far trader, intact but unmanned, decaying orbit around the gas giant Artobin; **near-full cargo (50t Petrochemicals)**. ## Rewards & Consequences
- **The Tech-World alliance (the main prize).** If the crew secure it (chase Jali successfully *and* keep the Shield Church ignorant of Tech-World's role), **Tech-World becomes a Friendly pirate port** — the sector's best repair/refit/medical/anagathic/cloning base, and a powerful Finale ally (GeDeCo connection = **+10 Fighting Strength** at the blockade; Tech-World itself FS 2). **Hodal's terms** the crew agree to supply/guarantee: regular cheap **Basic Consumables, Basic Raw Materials, Biochemicals, Petrochemicals, Radioactives, Uncommon Raw Materials**; **≥4 System Defence Boats (≥300t each) or equivalent**; **protection against GeDeCo reprisals**; a **contract guaranteeing unrestricted research** (incl. psionics/AI). Diplomat/Broker bargains these down (Q-ships instead of SDBs; cash for stolen cargo instead of consumables).
- **The TL15 Intellect program** (Bandwidth 4; Expert Science linguistics/philosophy/psychology/sophontology 3) — the overture gift; a fine conversation piece, marginal field use.
- **Salvage:** the **Vagabond Soul** (and/or its **50t Petrochemicals**) if the crew beat the 12-hour clock; potentially **Lord Gerald's Fancy** as well. Engineered Cursed corpses / Archon-grade or Knight-class nanotech samples are exotic, dangerous loot if recovered (TL15, deeply illegal in the Imperium).
- **MCr1 hush money** from Lemuel if the crew let him kill Jali and stay quiet (with implicit threat).
- **Recruitable contact:** Klowaaar (Aslan soldier) if helped on Tech-World; ongoing intel from Lran Smeck (Fist); a Florian Trade Company protection contract (Cr0.5/ton/system).
- **Costs of violence / failure (be honest):**
  - **Refusing the chase** → alliance withdrawn, **Tech-World turns Hostile**, adventure over.
  - **Abandoning the chase** mid-stream → Jali **dies on Mirage**, her research lost, **Tech-World withdraws the alliance**.
  - **Threatening/fighting Jali** → her fight-or-flight makes her **extremely lethal** (and she won't remember it) — TPK-class danger; firefights with **Flame Knights (Battle Dress 25, FGMP-15)** or **Cursed (Armour 8, neurotoxin)** are deadly.
  - **Botched lab Investigate** → a Traveller takes **permanent DEX −1 / END +1** from live nanotech.
  - **Fighting on Mirage's starport** → Imperial Marines in battle dress; effectively suicidal.
  - **Jali's solution fails (2D 7-)** → catastrophic swarm outbreak, **millions dead**, the crew must flee or be devoured.
  - **Crossing the Imperium with bad Standing** (−6 or lower) → warrants everywhere, **1/5th Standing as negative Law-check DM**; the **Harrier auto-flags** watch lists.
  - **The Immortal Protector cannot be killed** — antagonising him earns the lasting enmity of an immortal god-king with an FGMP-armed church.
