## Premise (one paragraph)
**Drinax** is the last fragment of the **Sindalian Empire** that once ruled all of the Trojan Reach, now a single ruined world holding a fading TL15 Floating Palace, wedged in neutral space between the **Third Imperium** and the **Aslan Hierate**. **King Oleb** grants the Travellers the **Harrier** (a TL15 commerce raider) and a **letter of marque**, and sends them out to prey on Imperial–Aslan trade. His plan (his daughter **Princess Rao**'s idea): make the trade route so unsafe that the cheapest option for the great powers is to **recognise a resurgent Kingdom of Drinax** as the route's protector — rather than crush the upstarts and restore the status quo. The Travellers raid, build alliances, and either fight to **restore Drinax** as a real interstellar power, carve out **their own pirate empire**, get rich, or **swing from a noose**. (PoD1: Campaign Overview; Companion: Campaign Overview)

**Default start date 1105.** Major-war events brew in the Spinward Marches over the next decade; they draw off some Imperial Navy assets but barely touch the Reach's already-thin patrols — they mostly *lower the perceived Imperial threat*, which bold players can exploit (but a single cruiser squadron from Tobia could still flatten any minor Reach power). (Companion: Timing)

## The three-phase arc
The campaign has a fixed **start** and **ending**; everything between is sandbox. Three loose phases (no hard gates — phases overlap and are paced by player success, not a clock):

| Phase | Theme | What the crew do | Empire-track state |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Raiding & establishing** | A pirate's life for me | Learn the trade; raid shipping/installations; find safe ports to fence loot; survive the powers; first patrons. Based on Drinax, returning often for resupply/repair. | Only **Drinax** (Haven) & **Theev** (Friendly) are safe; building the first extra Havens. |
| **2 — Building alliances & the empire** | From pirates to a power | Win worlds over (shift Attitudes toward Haven), cultivate Factions/Assets, acquire ships & money, manipulate the political board, juggle court factions at Drinax. Strike out more independently. | Growing web of Tolerant+/Friendly worlds & Fighting Strength; Drinax begins to pose as a legitimate state. |
| **3 — Endgame & the throne** | The lion roars | A trigger event gathers the Reach; **recruit** worlds into the Kingdom; mount the **blockade**; **negotiate** recognition from Imperium & Hierate; **repel the final Aslan assault**. | The empire track is cashed out: recruitment rolls, Fighting Strength, Royal Policy → victory or annihilation. |

The set adventures are **threaded onto** these phases (see list). Phase 3 is essentially the single mandatory endgame adventure (*Finale*); Phase 1 essentially begins with the crew receiving the marque (optionally tutored by *First Prize* / *Honour Among Thieves*).

## Sandbox + set adventures: how they interleave
- **The sandbox is the default state.** Most of the campaign is the crew working their own plan — raiding, trading, traveling, politicking, upgrading ports. The **set adventures are punctuation**, not the spine. (PoD1: Running the PoD; Companion: Running the Campaign)
- **Order is mostly free.** With the exception of the start and the *Finale*, you do **not** have to run any episode, nor in order. Insert your own (mythic-gm-generated) adventures freely, drop episodes, retime them. (Companion: Running the Campaign)
- **Events happen when it makes sense.** The published episode numbering (below) is a *suggested* sequence and a place to foreshadow, not a railroad — "the sandbox concept works both ways." (Companion: Campaign Overview)
- **Watch the timed ones.** A few episodes have hard clocks (esp. *Ihatei!* = 25 weeks; the *Game of Sun and Shadow* sweep plays out over months). Don't trigger a timed adventure until the crew can realistically engage it. (Companion: Campaign Overview / Timing)
- **Place adventures where the crew already are.** The cleanest hook is to seat a scripted adventure in a system the crew are likely to visit (their hunting grounds: the Dustbelt, Borderland/Wildeman clusters, Clarke/Marduk groups, the Sindalian Main). (Companion: Astrographical / Episodes)

## Master adventure list (suggested sequence)

Every adventure has (or will have) a spoiler-gated file in **`references/campaign/adventures/`** (indexed at `adventures/00_index.md`). Most are optional and reorderable; only the bracketed start and the *Finale* are effectively fixed.

## Other content (patrons, side adventures, optional mini-campaign)
- **Patrons & Opportunities** — the campaign's bread-and-butter sandbox fuel: short, mission-sized jobs (many tied to specific worlds) whose rewards usually grant a **specific Attitude shift** toward Haven/Friendly. These are the *main engine* for moving worlds and for triggering set adventures. Use them constantly between (and to seed) the big episodes. → see **`references/campaign/adventures/`** (patrons file / index) and empire-reputation.md ("Per-adventure effects").
- **Further full-length adventures** (Companion: Further Adventures): *Marooned on Marduk*, *The Calixcuel Incident*, *Theories of Everything*, *The Borderland Run* — drop in whenever desired.
- **Optional: Shadows of Sindal** (3-part mini-campaign) — introduces a **rival claimant to the Sindalian throne** with plans like Oleb's but more sinister methods. Optional antagonist arc that can run in parallel to Phases 2–3. → its own file under **`references/campaign/adventures/`**.

## Solo pacing on mythic-gm
Run the campaign as **alternating turns**: free-form **sandbox pirate turns** punctuated by **set-piece adventures**. mythic-gm is the GM brain; this campaign is the content layer (per mythic-gm's adaptation guide).

**The sandbox pirate turn (the default loop).** Each turn = one chunk of pirate activity, resolved scene-by-scene:
1. **Decide the goal** — raid a hunting ground, trade, travel, upgrade a port, cultivate a Faction, answer a patron, return to Drinax. (Player choice drives this; don't assign it.)
2. **Run it on the engine.** Frame the scene; ask **Fate Questions** for anything uncertain (does prey appear? is the port suspicious today? did the spy report us?). Resolve the actual mechanics in the rules files: a raid → **piracy-raiding.md** (find/intercept/board/loot/fence); trade → **trade-commerce.md**; travel/jumps → **starship-operations.md**; combat → **space-combat.md / personal-combat.md**.
3. **Check a Random Event** at each new scene (per Mythic's Chaos Factor). Many results = an adventure hook or a heat consequence (see triggers).
4. **Apply the consequences to all three gauges** (below) and to the empire tracker.
5. **Advance time.** Move the in-fiction date for each jump/operation/refit; this matters for timed adventures, Standing decay, morale, and the tithe.

**Cutting to a set-piece.** When a trigger fires (below) and the crew choose to engage, **switch into the adventure's file** and run its scenes (Player-Facing first; reveal GM-Only only as earned; honor any `dice.py keyed` "must-happen" triggers). When it resolves, apply its **`## Empire / Reputation effects`** and **`## Threads & hooks onward`**, then return to the sandbox loop.

**Rhythm guidance (keep it from going stale).** "A repetitive pirate is a dead one." Alternate raids with politics, travel, and downtime; vary hunting grounds and targets; reward moving to fresh systems, punish camping one spot. (PoD1: The Pirate Life; Companion: Running the Campaign rules.) A healthy cadence is a few sandbox turns, then a patron job or scripted episode, then consequences/court time — not raid after raid.

**Three gauges always in play** (don't run the sandbox without them — they ARE the consequence system):
| Gauge | What it does | File |
|---|---|---|
| **World Attitude** (per world) | the empire through-line; Hostile…Haven | empire-reputation.md |
| **Standing** (Imperium / Hierate) | great-power tolerance of your piracy | piracy-raiding.md ("Heat 1") |
| **PRI** (Piracy Response Indicator) | regional law-enforcement reaction | piracy-raiding.md ("Heat 2") |

## How adventures get triggered
Set adventures and patron jobs enter play through the sandbox — surface them, then let the crew choose:
- **Patrons** — a contact at a port, the court at Drinax, or a Random Event offers a job. The most frequent trigger; many patron jobs *are* the seeds of the bigger episodes or directly shift a world's Attitude.
- **Travel / location** — the crew jump into the system where an episode is seated (e.g. arriving in the Clarke/Torpol cluster → *Honour Among Thieves*; near Tech-World → *The Demon's Eye*; a treasure-ship rumour reaching a port near Arunisiir → *Treasure Ship*). Place scripted content in systems they're heading to.
- **Reputation / standing** — as the crew climb the empire track or anger a power, scripted pressure arrives: enough raiding near Drinax or in one area summons the *Game of Sun and Shadow* sweep; growing ihatei pressure foreshadows *Ihatei!*; court intrigue ripens into *Blood of the Star Dragon*; reaching the empire's late milestones triggers the *Finale*.
- **Random Events (Mythic)** — roll on each new scene; interpret results against the campaign's Lists (NPCs, threads, factions). A "remote event / new NPC / move toward a thread" result is the natural moment to introduce a rumour, a patron, or the hook for the next episode. Rumours of the Reach (PoD1 D66 table) and the campaign's named hooks feed these interpretations.
- **GM clocks** — for timed episodes (*Ihatei!* 25 weeks; the *Game of Sun and Shadow* months-long sweep), once triggered, track the clock as a Mythic thread/clock and let it run regardless of detours.

## The empire track as through-line
The **per-world Attitude track + the recruitment/Fighting-Strength endgame** (empire-reputation.md) is the spine that ties the sandbox together and gives the campaign its ending. Treat it as the campaign's victory clock:
- Every raid, patron job, and episode should move *something* on it (an Attitude step, a Standing tick, a Faction advanced, a ship or ally gained).
- The **central tension** is piracy vs. empire: pirate income buys ships/money/leverage, but every raid risks the standing you need to *rule*. When the crew choose loot over goodwill (or vice versa), make the **other** gauge pay. (empire-reputation.md: Piracy ↔ empire tension.)
- The milestones (survival & a foothold → a power base → allies in place → the Declaration → show of force → negotiation → the final test) are the rungs from Phase 1 to Phase 3. Track them as a clock; when the late rungs are reached, fire the *Finale*.
- **Pay the tithe.** Oleb takes **10% of all piracy proceeds**; refusing forfeits the marque and makes an enemy of your only starting Haven. (piracy-raiding.md: Division of the Spoils.)

## Sandbox philosophy (player choice, not rails)
- **Follow the Travellers, not the plot.** If they want to take over a planet, start a war, or ignore Oleb's plan, run with it. Give them the freedom of open space. (PoD1: Building Alliances)
- **A true sandbox.** Once they leave Drinax they can go anywhere their jump drive reaches; their fate is their own. Don't fudge disasters — if they crash on a deserted world, let them struggle off it. Anything can happen. (PoD1: Refereeing the PoD)
- **No two campaigns are alike.** There's no right or wrong way to play — though there are ways it can fail to reach its intended conclusion. A group might never leave the Floating Palace (pure court intrigue), ditch Drinax entirely and build their own empire, or quietly rebuild in Drinax's wreckage. (Companion: Introduction)
- **Tone.** Technicolour swashbuckling: Shakespearean court drama at Drinax, an invincible-feeling Imperium, exotic Aslan, isolated island-worlds; lurid and weird; *Dune* meets *Firefly* with real-world pirate slang. (PoD1: Refereeing the PoD)
- **Solo corollary:** the engine must honor the crew's chosen goal each turn and the oracle's honest answers — never steer toward a "correct" episode. The episode list is a menu, not a script.

## Possible endings
Set by the crew's choices and the *Finale*'s recruitment/blockade/negotiation/battle (empire-reputation.md):
- **Restore Drinax** — the reborn **Kingdom of Drinax** survives the Aslan assault and is recognised as a Reach power. The crew retire as nobles/admirals; size of the Kingdom sets the reward tier (16+ systems → Oleb can style himself **Emperor of Sindal**). A marriage offer (Rao or Harrick) may stand.
- **A pirate empire of your own** — the crew use the same tools but cut Drinax out, ruling their own pocket polity (or moving into Drinax's wreckage to rebuild on their own terms). Even if the Floating Palace is destroyed and Oleb killed, the campaign continues if the crew want it to.
- **Personal wealth / go renegade** — skip the throne entirely; get rich raiding, take a big score and walk, or live the pirate life until it ends. A valid "win" by the crew's lights.
- **Failure** — the worst case is total: the Floating Palace destroyed, the crew dead in battle or executed as enemies of sophont-kind, the marque worthless, and the whole Reach falling under the Aslan claw. No middle ground in the final battle — reborn kingdom or annihilation.
