# Drinax — World, Court & Starting Situation

> Use this when: the crew is at/returning to Drinax, dealing with King Oleb or his court, asking about the Floating Palace, the letter of marque, the Harrier loan, or the kingdom's history/politics. The home base and the spine of the metaplot.

## At a glance
- **Drinax (Tliowaha 2223 / campaign hex 0603) UWP A43645A-E** — Starport A / Size 4 / Atm 3 / Hydro 6 / Pop 4 (tens of thousands) / Gov 5 / Law A / TL E (15). Trade: **Ni Ht**. Travel zone: none/green. Gas giant present. Allegiance: Kingdom of Drinax. Capital of the (broken) Kingdom of Drinax = "Star Dragon Empire of Sindal-in-Exile". **Haven** (the only Haven at campaign start).
  - *(verify — sources disagree on digits/hex. Companion subsector list & sector map: `A43645A-E`, hex 2223. Book 1 / Appendix campaign world list: `A33645C-F`, hex 0603, pop 6. Use `A43645A-E` as primary; the Class-A starport is the King's personal port, not a public one — Imperial charts are centuries out of date.)*
- **State:** The *planet's surface is a dead, radioactive wasteland* (Aslan orbital bombardment, 200 yrs ago). No surface settlements. **Everyone of consequence lives above it**, in the Floating Palace. Pop on the Palace ≈ **40,000**, all nobles by title; plus scattered Vespexer tribes on the surface.
- **Defences:** Minimal. The **Star Guard** (Drinaxi navy) has only a handful of ships, *none over 400 tons*; only the 100-ton scouts have jump drives. The recovered **Harrier is the closest thing Drinax has to a working warship** — which is why it is loaned to the Travellers. The Palace itself has the giant anti-grav generators (also used as gravity dungeons) but is *not* a fortress; a single Imperial cruiser or determined Aslan clan could destroy it.
- **Tech paradox:** Drinaxi retain **TL15 knowledge** (Scholar's Tower) but lack the minerals/industry to use it — "all the knowledge in the galaxy is useless when you have no copper or steel."

## The world of Drinax (the surface)
- A wasteland. The Aslan dropped rocks, plasma-bombed cities, and seeded disease bombs; an impact winter followed. Millions died.
- 200 years on it is *slowly* healing — green shoots, new forests in the ash — but full recovery is ~1,000 years away, and **Aslan bio-weapon spores still sleep in the ruined cities**; Drinax may never again be safe for human habitation.
- Ruined cities have been scavenged but still hold relics (a source of Vespexer income via Rachando).

### The Vespexers (surface tribes)
- Hardy human tribes: descendants of survivors + exiles from the Palace. Hunter-gatherers with a few sheltered farm valleys. Wear **hazard suits** (made by Palace craftsmen) against radiation/disease.
- Technically the King's subjects but **obey no law but their own tribe's**; kneel to no one. Trade food/raw materials to the Palace for hazard suits and worked goods.
- Lore hook: Vespexer prophecy of the **Hlax Kur Eaisa** — a messiah who will avenge them on the Aslan.
- Court contact: **Chieftain Galx** (visits the Palace when it passes over her territory; always in full hazard suit).

### Asim — the one remaining vassal (Tliowaha 0503, B867564-6, Ag Ni, Pop ~5M, TL6)
- Earth-like but lifeless-feeling; brown chlorophyll-equivalent → brown fields/forests. Conquered by Drinax **20 years ago** after a hydroponics failure threatened the Palace with starvation. Drinaxi plasma weapons crushed the rifle-armed locals.
- Former govt the **Foundation** (a sham "thousand-year plan" run by sages). Now ruled by Drinaxi nobles; peasants tithe grain to annual Drinax ships. Oleb privately regrets the conquest. Court rep: **Cleon Hardy** (elected ombudsman, ex-economist/broadcaster).

## The Floating Palace
A flying city — a mile-plus aerial citadel of plas-steel and carbosamite on a colossal grav platform, "a Gormenghast" of halls, towers and domes. Absurdly **overcrowded**: ~40,000 people, every ballroom home to a dozen families, washing strung between golden statues, ancient tapestries used as blankets. Holds ~20,000 artistic masterworks (loot + tribute from 30 worlds over 50 generations). Crammed with luxury yet short of vacc suits and engineering overalls.

### Layout & locations (map p.20, book 2)
- **Outer Ring:** 1 Royal Docks, 2 Bazaar, 3 Gallery, 4 University Grounds, 5 Life Support.
- **Inner Ring:** 6 Starport Control, 7 Starport Docks, 8 Palace, 9 Ambassador's Wing, 10 Great Hall, 11 Royal Apartments, 12 Star Guard Barracks, 13 Gardens, 14 Court Chambers, 15 Throne Room.
- **Places of note:** Repair Shops, Art Gallery, **Scholar's Tower**, **Rachando's Bazaar**, **Dragon Throne**, Underlinth Access, **Gravity Dungeons** (near Star Guard Barracks).

Key features:
- **Dragon Throne** — looted (legend says) from the Sindalian throne world Noricum; older than the Imperium's Iridium Throne. Built up over 3,000 yrs from fused hull-metal of ships the Star Guard defeated; a huge ugly "dragon's skull." Oleb only uses it for ceremony, preferring a grav-couch.
- **Throne Room** — the old one became a hangar; the current room is smaller, walls of laser-etched platinum. Long benches for "courtiers"; real advisers are chosen for competence, not blood.
- **Scholar's Tower** — the planet's scientific corpus + best academics were moved here weeks before the attack. Best university in the subsector; student fees are one of Drinax's few incomes. Also where Prince Harrick was rebuilt.
- **Rachando's Bazaar** — Rachando the merchant (no noble blood) sells Drinaxi treasures/tech offworld and imports vital supplies via his free trader *Invisible Hand*; the second-most powerful figure on the Palace. Oleb resents every treasure sold but has no choice.
- **The Starport** — the King's personal port (original was destroyed). No shipyard, but can repair almost any ship; luxurious fittings. Effectively Class-A in capability.
- **The Underlinth** — network of access corridors, drains and secret passages under the platform; the Palace's shadow side, used by spies, smugglers and conspirators.
- **Gravity Dungeons** — on the platform's underside near the anti-grav generators; cells held at 3G+; deepest cells under a bone-crushing **20G**.
- **Hanging Gardens** — once a sector wonder, now hydroponics bays (the Palace has no farmland).

## King Oleb XVI
**King Oleb XVI** (King of Drinax, Dragon Emperor of Sindal-in-Exile, Lord Admiral of the Star Guard). Age **105** (anagathics; looks mid-50s). Enormous girth — wears three grav belts to fly with his Hawk Warriors; jokes "I may be fatter than a gas giant, but I am still the godsdamned King."
- **Public persona:** loud, fat, impetuous; proclaims love of strong wine, red-haired women and glorious battle. Proud chiefly of his boxing and swordfighting trophies. Courtiers call him "Magnificent," enemies "a buffoon."
- **Reality:** a **subtle, cunning statesman**; can be diplomatic when forced — he simply enjoys blustering more. Spent his youth as a freebooting adventurer and sometime **pirate** before taking the crown.
- **Lifelong goal:** restore the Kingdom of Drinax to its former glory. "All he needs is a little leverage. All he needs is one good ship." (The recovered Harrier.) Conquest is the one pleasure denied him — Drinax is too weak to take more worlds and the Aslan threat grows.
- **Voice (for narration):** Shakespearean, swaggering, profane, theatrical; warm to those who prove useful; volatile. Treat the court as a Shakespearean drama of feuding nobles and conniving courtiers.

## The royal court & key NPCs
The court is the crew's **home base**; Oleb summons them to report at intervals and factions court them as they succeed. Stat blocks live in npc files; thumbnails:
- **Princess Rao** (age 28) — Crown Princess; **named heir** while Harrick lay dying. Oleb's foremost counsellor & diplomat. *She invented the privateer scheme* (false-flag piracy to pressure the powers). Dreams of a grand alliance / reborn kingdom and a Drinaxi-tech golden age. Does **not** want to marry an uncouth pirate. Critics: overambitious, blind to the danger, would only enrage the Aslan. If a Traveller takes Oleb's "marry my daughter" offer, they must prove worthy of her.
- **Prince Harrick** (age 54) — elder potential heir. Led the Asim invasion 20 yrs ago; his shuttle crashed and he was burned to a "charred husk," kept 20 yrs in a tank, **rebuilt cell by cell**, emerged only 6 months ago. Movements stilted, skin "too perfect"; most courtiers think he's more machine/cloned tissue than man. Sanity, loyalty and ambitions all uncertain — is he jealous of Rao, a pawn, or a hero in waiting?
- **Lord Wrax** — commander of the **Star Guard**; wanted the Harrier as his flagship and has **little time for Oleb's piracy scheme**. A standing obstacle for the crew at court (prove your worth or discredit his objections).
- **Scholar Voha** — elderly master of the Scholar's Tower, court sage. Guards Sindalian knowledge; **wary of anything that endangers the Palace**. The campaign's exposition-dispenser for Sindal/Reach lore.
- **Imperial Consul Thao Poloc** — the Third Imperium's man at court; comic-relief. **Must never learn the Travellers are pirates.**
- **Rachando** — merchant of *Invisible Hand* (see Bazaar above). [GM secret below.]
- **Kasiyl of the Ahroay'if** — exiled Aslan prince/warrior & ace pilot living at court as bodyguard/interpreter; refuses to discuss why he fled the Hierate. Wrax refused him a Star Guard post. (Patron of *The Prodigal Outcast*.)
- **Sal Dancet** — free trader/smuggler using the Palace for repairs and cover; superb source of rare goods, rumours and info; can find the crew anywhere. (Key NPC in *Ihatei!*)
- **Cleon Hardy** — Asim's elected ombudsman at court (above).
- **Chieftain Galx** — Vespexer representative (above).
- **Lady Hil** — courtier who hints she can help the crew at court — for a price (what does she want?).

### Political situation
- The Trojan Reach is "every world for itself" between an overstretched Third Imperium and the expanding, aggressive **Aslan Hierate**; the dust-spice trade route (Fist→Tyokh) threads between them. The sector is on a knife edge.
- Oleb's plan exploits this: privateering to bleed merchants + winning over independent (ex-Drinaxi) worlds, then offering the Imperium a deal — *piracy stops if Drinax is recognized*. Drinax claims, by Sindalian inheritance, half the sector.
- Court factions all have agendas advanceable via the Travellers (Asimi vs. nobles, Vespexers, Lady Hil, etc.). Keep Standing and port attitudes in the foreground; every world won over is a victory toward surviving the campaign's final storm.

## History & timeline
- **Sindalian Empire:** barbarian raiders who crowned themselves kings; rose **-2000**, ruled ~600 yrs over dozens of worlds; revolts from **-1600**; capital **Noricum bombed to oblivion ~-1400**, ending the Empire. Several dukes claimed the title; **Drinax's claim lasted longest** (kings of Drinax = self-styled heirs of Sindal).
- **Drinax's golden age:** From -1400 to ~902 the Kings of Drinax ruled their subsector for over two millennia. Wiser than their Sindalian cousins — "the iron fist of orbital bombardment behind a velvet glove of trade and protection." Drinax itself became a fat **garden world**, an oasis of culture and TL15+ wonders; the Floating Palace a galactic wonder. Core worlds (Solomani-named for Terran SF authors): **Banks (now Khusai), Stross (now Kteiroa), Asim, Pourne, Hilfer, Paal, Torpal, Clarke, Blue**, plus ~2 dozen more.
- **Decline & fall:** ~700, **Glaco IX "the Vengeful"** revived Sindalian terror-tactics and bombed vassals — the kingdom grew unruly as Aslan pressure rose. **884: King Oleb XIV** imposed a **20% tax** on all Imperium↔Hierate trade through Drinax; **Harrier-class ships built to enforce it. 885 (≈200 yrs ago): the Aslan, led by the Ahroay'if clan, bombed Drinax from orbit** in a war lasting under a year. Subject worlds were conquered or broke away; **only the Floating Palace was deliberately spared** (nobles and art left untouched while millions of commoners were exterminated). "One palace… and a few ships."
- **The long decline → now:** ~20,000 survivors adapted (gardens → hydroponics; nobles forced to labour). Pop grew to ~40,000. **1085: reconquest of Asim. 1105 (current year):** the derelict Harrier is recovered and rebuilt; Oleb launches his gambit.
- **Timeline crib:** -2000 Sindal rises · -1600 revolts · -1400 Noricum falls, Drinax claims the title · 500 Aslan reach Tliowaha · 700 Glaco bombs rebels · 884 Drinax tax · 885 Fall of Drinax · 1085 Asim retaken · 1105 Harrier rebuilt (start).

## The Travellers' starting situation
The campaign opens with **King Oleb offering the crew a letter of marque** in the throne room (read-aloud text, book 2 p.16+). The deal:
1. **The Harrier** (TL15 commerce raider, original name *V'Hurg*; J-2, 6G, turret + particle barbette, 8 staterooms, 8 low berths, 29.7t cargo — full stats in ship file) is **loaned, not given** — it stays the King's property and "comes home at the end"; the crew pays for repairs/upkeep.
2. **Letter of marque & reprisal:** legal right to prey on shipping within the old borders of the Empire of Sindal that lacks "proper documentation" — i.e., *every ship in space* (no one's carried such papers in centuries). Oleb wants **thieves, not murderers**: "Don't kill anyone you don't have to" — bleed merchants until they beg the Imperium for terms, don't start a war.
3. **The plan (two parts):** (a) raid shipping, starports and fuel depots across the Reach; (b) win over the independent ex-Drinaxi worlds (sell loot there, defend them, recruit, spread wealth) into a renewed Kingdom. Then offer the Imperium a deal: recognition for an end to the attacks.
4. **Terms:** keep the marque **secret** from the Imperium until a peace is signed; **10% of all takings to Oleb**; spend freely on colony worlds to curry favour. Reward for success: **made dukes of the court** — and Oleb dangles **his daughter's hand** (Rao slips in, exasperated, reminding him it's supposed to be *clandestine*; "if they succeed, you're marrying one of them").

### What good is the marque?
Piracy is punishable by **death** everywhere (Imperium: trial then firing squad; Aslan: the local lord executes you personally with his dewclaw). **The letter will NOT save a captured crew — unless the Kingdom of Drinax is restored.** If Drinax's flag flies again over a dozen worlds, the crew's crimes are **retroactively negated** (they were always licensed privateers). So **saving Drinax literally saves the crew's necks** — the throne/kingdom is the ultimate prize.

### Sandbox note
This is a true sandbox: the crew may follow Oleb, go rogue and carve out their own pocket empire, even seize the Harrier. **Follow the Travellers, not the plot.** Only Drinax counts as a Haven at the start. Don't let them raid the same hunting ground repeatedly (success breeds danger; the 7th identical victim is an Imperial q-ship).

## GM-Only — Truth & Secrets
*Reveal only as earned in play.*
- **Oleb is not a buffoon.** The bluster is partly a mask; he is a genuinely cunning statesman with a lifelong, patient plan. He is also an ex-pirate himself — he knows exactly what he's unleashing.
- **The scheme was Princess Rao's idea**, not Oleb's; she frames it (privately) as *manipulating subsector politics*, not a crude pirate order. Her long game is a grand alliance that may **eclipse** a simple restored monarchy — potentially sidelining Oleb's vision. She has **no intention of actually marrying a pirate**; the betrothal dangle is leverage.
- **The throne is the real prize, and it is contested.** Succession is unstable: Rao is named heir, but **Prince Harrick** has returned, rebuilt and uncanny — possibly insane, possibly a pawn, possibly more machine than man. This rift detonates in **Episode 9 (*Blood of the Star Dragon*)**: a coup attempt, Oleb missing and gravely wounded (survival and which faction wins both hinge on the crew). *Foreshadow Harrick/Rao tension now.*
- **Character hook:** a Travellers' background result can reveal a PC is the **bastard child of King Oleb** — a live claim to the Dragon Throne.
- **Rachando is a deep-cover agent of GeDeCo** (General Development Corporation). GeDeCo manipulates Reach worlds as an anti-Aslan buffer and **believes Drinax's fall is inevitable** — in **Episode 7 (*The Vorito Gambit*)** they offer the crew aid in exchange for *ensuring Drinax falls to the Aslan*. Confronted, Rachando begs for his life and can turn coat. (His resentment-laden trade with Oleb quietly bleeds the kingdom's treasures either way.)
- **The Palace is indefensible and the spores never died.** The grav platform can be brought down; the surface bio-weapons mean Drinax can't simply be recolonised — both constrain any "just move everyone down to the planet/Asim" plan (which some courtiers whisper about, but no one dares say to Oleb).
- **The campaign's spine = the fate of Drinax.** Whether the crew save or doom it, the last days/rebirth of the Kingdom drive the metaplot to **Episode 10 (Finale)**: an external assault (Aslan invasion) the crew must meet with the alliances they've built. Allied worlds won during play directly determine survival. A rival heir to Sindal with similar but darker aims appears in the *Shadows of Sindal* mini-campaign.
- **Underlinth & Gravity Dungeons** are the Palace's hidden-action zones — use for intrigue, infiltration, prisoner scenes, and the coup.
