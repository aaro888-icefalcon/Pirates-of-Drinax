## How to read these blocks (prize vs threat)
- Format per ship: **TL · tonnage/config · Hull (HP) · Armour · M-Drive(Thrust) · J-Drive(Jump) · Power · weapons (damage) · key systems · crew · cargo** → 1-line role/tactics. Damage values from space-combat.md weapon tables (beam laser 1D, pulse laser 2D, missile 4D, sandcaster special, particle barbette 4D, fusion bay/gun 4D, etc.). Turret weapons add **+1 per damage die per extra same-type weapon** (double/triple).
- **PRIZE / AVOID tag:** PRIZE = worth taking (cargo, low fight). AVOID = out-guns or out-runs a lone raider; flee or never engage. MIXED = beatable but bites back.
- **Hull Points (HP)** is the damage track; 0 = wrecked (salvage only). Each 10% lost = a Severity-1 critical. Disable, don't destroy a prize — ion / called-shot M-Drive or Power, then board (space-combat.md).
- **MOR** = ship morale where the encounter book gives one (merchant 1D+3, armed 1D+6, naval 1D+8 by default; piracy-raiding.md). Demand Surrender at DM = −(current MOR).
- Tonnage drives "to-hit" bonus for the *attacker*: **+1 per full 1,000 t of target (max +6)**. Big freighters are easy to hit but slow to loot; small ships are nimble but light.
- Many garbled source numbers reconstructed from the markdown tables; mechanically-key fields (Thrust, Jump, Armour, weapons, HP, crew) are reliable. `(verify)` marks anything uncertain.

## Merchants & traders (the prey)
The bread and butter. Most are PRIZES; a few bite. Cargo value baseline ≈ **Cr50,000/ton** (loot at fence %, piracy-raiding.md). Hold is **1D×10 + 40%** full.

### Fast Trader (Type-A3) — PRIZE (also a pirate favourite)
TL12 · 200t streamlined · **HP 80** · Armour 2 (Crystaliron) · **Thrust 4** · **Jump 2** · Power 135 · *Weapons:* none · Fuel scoop+processor, cargo crane, aerofins · Crew 5 (pilot, astrogator, engineer, medic, steward) · **Cargo 36t**.
Stripped-down Free Trader trading cargo for speed/agility. Unarmed but **Thrust 4** — can outrun a slow raider; intercept before it reaches the 100-D limit. Light prize; popular among pirates as a hull too.

### Subsidised Merchant (Type-RQ "subbie") — **AVOID — Q-SHIP**
TL12 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · **Armour 8** · **Thrust 4** · Jump 1 · Power 315 · *Weapons:* **4× pop-up triple turrets, pulse lasers** (3D, +2) · Fire Control/4, Evade/2, Anti-Hijack/2, breaching tube, booby-trapped airlock, grav-screen, **4× light fighter** in concealed bays · Crew incl. **16 marines** · Cargo 76t.
Looks exactly like a helpless subsidised merchant — **a pirate's nightmare**. Lures a raider into range, drops the disguise: heavy armour, concealed lasers, fighter swarm, boarding marines. If a "soft" 400t subbie has Thrust 4 and acts confident, suspect Q-ship.

### Buccaneer-class Blockade Runner — MIXED (prize if you can catch+crack it)
TL15 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · **Armour 5** · **Thrust 3** (+ high-burn thruster = combined **6G for 2 hrs**) · Jump 2 · Power 300 · *Weapons:* 2× double pulse-laser turrets (3D,+2), 2× double sandcaster/pulse turrets · Improved sensors, 40 sand barrels, launch · Crew incl. 4 gunners · Cargo 62t.
Built to punch through blockades with small high-value loads (weapons, drugs) — so its cargo is worth far more than its tonnage suggests. Hard to catch (6G burst) and fights back; militaries and pirates both fly it.

### Vulture-class Salvage Hauler — PRIZE (fat hold, near-toothless)
TL15 · 400t standard · **HP 160** · Armour 0 · **Thrust 1** · Jump 2 · Power 135 · *Weapons:* 1× double pulse-laser turret (3D,+2) · Two prow grappling arms (one heavy), cargo scoop, probe drones, launch · Crew 5 (1 gunner) · **Cargo 199t**.
In-system derelict-pickers; doubles as a trader. **Thrust 1 = can't run**, one weak turret = can't fight. Huge cargo bay. Excellent prize; often found alone near wrecks/belts.

### Queen Elizabeth-class Liner — MIXED (rich pax, but you'll be outnumbered)
TL12 · 1,200t streamlined · **HP 480** · Armour 0 · **Thrust 1** · Jump 3 · Power 600 · *Weapons:* none (flying hotel) · Medical bay, gaming space, 95t passenger shuttle, library · Crew incl. 3 stewards · **78 std + 10 high + 4 luxury staterooms**; Cargo 5t.
A leased luxury hotel-ship — passengers carry valuables (personal treasure: Cr10,000/high pax). **Thrust 1, unarmed = easy to stop**, but boarders are badly outnumbered by passengers/crew. Liner result on Prey table.

### Galoof-class Megafreighter — **AVOID solo (capital-scale prize, always escorted)**
TL12 · **30,000t** standard · **HP 15,000** · Armour 0 · Thrust 1 · Jump 3 · Power 15,000 · *Weapons:* none itself · 6 cargo cranes, full hangar (475t), 5 shuttles, 170 re-entry capsules · Crew ~165 · **Cargo 15,248t**.
A corporate capital-ship trader — capturing one is a pirate's lifelong dream and **essentially impossible alone**: it travels with an escort that grows with the danger of the route. Note as a long-game target, not a tonight target.

### Reach Freighter — PRIZE (cheap bulk hauler, weak guns)
TL11 · **4,000t** light/close-structure · **HP 1,600** · Armour 0 · Thrust 1 · Jump 2 · Power 2,100 · *Weapons:* 4× single beam-laser turrets (1D,+4), 4× single sandcaster turrets · Cargo crane, 3 airlocks, medbay, 100 low berths · Crew ~24 (2 gunners) · **Cargo 2,273t**.
No-frills COTS bulk hauler of the mains; never lands (orbital ports only). Token defences, vast slow hold, **Thrust 1**. Great bulk prize if you have time and hands to move 20-ton blocks.

## Patrol & Navy (the law)
Hunters, escorts, and warships. **Almost all AVOID for a lone raider** — destroying a navy ship guarantees extermination; surrender at least means survival (piracy-raiding.md, Getting Caught). SDBs have **no jump drive** — a clever pirate evades and jumps out.

### Watchdog-class Fleet Picket — AVOID (the eyes; sensor + EW)
TL13 · 500t standard · **HP 200** · **Armour 8** · Thrust 3 · Jump 4 · Power 330 · *Weapons:* **5× triple beam-laser turrets** (1D, ×3 dice → effectively heavy laser fire, +4) · Computer/20 fib, Enhanced Signal Processing, Countermeasures Suite, Extension Net, **Electronic Warfare/1** · Crew 11 (5 gunners) · Cargo 16.8t.
Stands at a fleet's edge filtering incoming traffic — the sensor/EW screen of a jump-in force. Sees you coming and jams you. Don't be detected by one.

### Patrol Tender — AVOID (Imperial Navy resupply, well-armed)
TL15 · 1,000t standard · **HP 400** · Armour 4 · Thrust 2 · Jump 4 · Power 600 · *Weapons:* 3× triple beam-laser turrets (1D,+4), 3× triple sandcaster turrets (100 barrels) · Slow pinnace, cargo crane, heavy grappling arm · Crew ~15 (6 gunners) · Cargo 238.5t.
Resupplies front-line/patrol ships; decommissioned ones become civilian long-haul traders (those are PRIZE). On naval duty: jump-4 reach, six laser+sand turrets — leave it alone.

### Ritchey-class Escort — **AVOID (8,000t fleet escort)**
TL13 · 8,000t standard · **HP 800** · **Armour 6** + radiation shielding · Thrust 6 · Jump 4 · Power 10,770 · *Weapons:* **10× small missile bays, 10× particle barbettes (4D, Radiation), 40× triple beam-laser turrets**; 2,400 missiles · **5 meson screens, 5 nuclear dampers**, Point Defence/2, EW/2, Adv Fire Control/1, 2 pinnaces · Crew ~290 (70 gunners) · Cargo 83.7t.
Screens fleets/convoys against fighters, missiles and lighter attackers. A wall of point-defence and barbettes. Catastrophic to engage; if one escorts a convoy, the convoy is off the menu.

### Planet-class Heavy Cruiser — **AVOID utterly (75,000t spinal-mount capital ship)**
TL15 · **75,000t** reinforced · **Armour 15** · Thrust 6 · Jump (capital) · *Weapons:* **spinal mount**, small bays, meson screens, nuclear dampers, command bridge. Crew in the hundreds.
Political/strategic overkill — appears only to **pound a discovered base to dust**, never for routine hunting (piracy-raiding.md). If you see one tasked at you, the campaign-level mistake is already made.

### Gazelle-class Close Escort / Patrol Corvette (referenced) — AVOID (the bane of piracy)
Named in the encounter book as the standard small navy hunter (see *Emuar*, *Templeton* below). Outguns/outruns most pirates and **chases across systems** with high-accel fighters. Treat any "Naval Patrol" Prey result as: disengage.

### System Defence Boat (generic) — MIXED→AVOID (fast, heavy, **no jump**)
Faster and better-armed than any pirate ship, but cannot jump and crews may be less skilled than a hardened band (piracy-raiding.md). Largest SDBs are hollowed-asteroid supermonitors — never engage. Stat the encounter SDB *SOAAD* (below) as the type exemplar. SDB result on Prey table: **roll 1D, 6 = Q-ship.**

## Pirates, raiders & rivals
What you ARE and who you compete with. Built/sold mostly at **Theev** (the only Friendly pirate port). Rivals are PRIZE-as-hull but poor cargo and dangerous.

### Corsair — the iconic pirate warship (rival; AVOID one-on-one if hostile)
TL15 · 600t streamlined · **HP 240** · **Armour 0** · Thrust 3 · Jump 2 · Power 660 (large surplus) · *Weapons:* **3× triple beam-laser turrets** (1D,+4; hull readily accepts more turrets/barbettes/bays) · **Clamshell cargo doors swallow a 100t ship**, cargo scoop, breaching tube, forced linkage, repair drones, Auto-Repair/1, Fire Control/2 · Crew 14 incl. **5 boarding party** · **Cargo 279t**.
The Pirate Lords of Theev's signature raider/pocket-warship. **Reconfigurable**: retractable fins, deployable modules, variable transponder/IFF — disguises itself in minutes. Unarmoured → relies on speed + hitting power, NOT a stand-up brawler. The crew may well meet (or buy) one.

### Star Ray-class Interceptor — rival/Theev-built (MIXED)
TL12 · 200t streamlined · **HP 80** · **Armour 4** · **Thrust 3** · Jump 1 (×2 tanks) · Power 140 · *Weapons:* 2× double beam-laser turrets (1D,+4) · Heavy grappling arm, cargo scoop, forced linkage, **1× light fighter** in 10t docking space · Crew incl. 6 marines, fighter pilot · Cargo 44t.
Cheap Theev-built interceptor: well-armed and cargo-grabbing but **low Thrust 3** — struggles to catch anything sprightlier than a merchant. Good against soft prey; a peer threat to the Harrier.

### Indigo-class Pirate Carrier — rival (AVOID the fighter swarm)
TL15 · 300t dispersed · **HP 180** · Armour 0 · **Thrust 1** · Jump 2 · Power 135 · *Weapons:* 3× triple turrets (2× beam laser + missile rack) · **10× light fighters** clamped to hull, **cargo net**, heavy grappling arm · Crew incl. 10 fighter pilots, 3 gunners · Cargo 65t.
A tiny light carrier beloved of raiders: its **ten clamped fighters** swarm a target into surrender or wreck it, then the Indigo trawls the debris with its cargo net. The carrier itself is slow and fragile — kill it and the fighters lose their base; but ten fighters first.

### Fiery-class Gunship — pirate/criminal heavy hitter (AVOID solo)
TL12 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · **Armour 6** · **Thrust 6** · Jump 2 · Power 525 · *Weapons:* **small fusion gun bay** (4D, ×10), **particle barbette** (4D, Radiation), double beam-laser turret, triple sand/beam turret · Holographic controls, Computer/20 fib, breaching tube, armoury, Fire Control/3, Evade/2 · Crew incl. **30 marines** · Cargo 6t.
A small-navy fast-attack boarding ship — race in, blast, board. "Send ripples of fear through a system." In pirate hands it's a serious raider; against the Harrier it has the bay + barbette + Thrust 6. Avoid a stand-up fight.

### The Ghost of the Reach — unique stealth raider (AVOID; signature threat)
TL15 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · **Armour 6** · Thrust 3 · Jump 3 · Power 360 · *Weapons:* 4× double beam-laser turrets (1D,+4) · **Superior Stealth + Emissions Absorption Grid**, Advanced sensors + Enhanced Signal Processing, Evade/2, Fire Control/3, probe drones, medbay, lab, **ship's boat** for flanking · Crew incl. **6 marines (teleporting boarders)**, 4 gunners · Cargo 36.8t.
A sensor-baffled **Zhodani heavy scout**. Closes unseen — the target gets no distress call off before teleporting marines arrive; fast prey gets flanked by its boat. A bespoke ambush predator that hunts the way the crew wishes they could.

### Magenta-class Repair Ship — support/rival auxiliary (PRIZE; lightly armed)
TL12 · 700t streamlined, radiation-shielded · **HP 280** · Armour 1 · Thrust 1 · Jump 3 · Power 240 · *Weapons:* 1× double pulse-laser turret (3D,+2) · **Full hangar (100t) swallows small ships**, Type IV docking clamp, repair drones, tow cable, workshop, Auto-Repair/1 · Crew ~12 (1 gunner) · Cargo 19.75t.
Dispatched to patch crippled hulls and tow them home; can land to repair planetside. Barely armed — a prize, and a tempting one to *donate* to a friendly port or fold into your own fleet for the Harrier's endless repairs (the-harrier.md RU system).

### Ulfhednar-class Escort Carrier — AVOID (2,000t squadron carrier)
TL13 · 2,000t standard · **HP 800** · Armour 4 + armoured bulkheads · Thrust 4 · Jump 4 · Power 2,205 · *Weapons:* 10× triple beam-laser turrets (1D,+4) · **12× light fighters**, recovery deck + launch tube + full hangar, Countermeasures Suite, Battle System/1 · Crew ~70 (10 gunners, 12 fighter pilots) · Cargo 7t.
Escorts convoys with a full fighter squadron plus its own laser turrets. A convoy with this is untouchable by one raider.

## Aslan ships (clan vessels, ihatei craft)
All Aslan ports are **Unfriendly** to the crew (piracy-raiding.md); Hierate Standing starts at **−5**. Hitting Aslan ships pleases the Imperium but enrages the Hierate. Note: Aslan controls are gendered (male simplified / female complex / dual / reconfigurable); ships >100t carry a **Shrine to Heroes** (4t, no occupancy). The **Glorious Empire** (Aslan splinter) flies the slaver and raids for captives.

### Hraye-class Scout — PRIZE (light, cheap, common)
TL13 · 100t streamlined · **HP 40** · Armour 4 (Crystaliron) · Thrust 2 · Jump 2 · Power 45 · *Weapons:* 1× double turret (pulse laser/missile rack) · Probe drones, shrine, fuel processor · Crew 2–3 · Cargo 15t.
Exploration scout; often a courier/transport in small colonies. Thin crew, one mixed turret. Soft target.

### Ihateisho-class Scout — MIXED (the iconic ihatei wanderer)
TL13 · 100t streamlined · **HP 40** · Armour 0 · Thrust 2 · **Jump 4** · Power 60 · *Weapons:* 1× **triple pulse-laser turret** (2D, ×3 dice, +2) · Air/raft, shrine, Fire Control/3, automated controls · Crew 2–3 · Cargo 4.5t.
Given to a noble's **ihatei** to roam (2–3 yr) seeking territory to conquer. Highly automated so a lone male can fly it. **Jump-4** reach and a real triple-laser turret — bites for a 100-tonner; little cargo. Tied to the ihatei-invasion plot of the campaign.

### Ktiyhui-class Courier — MIXED (armoured dignitary carrier)
TL13 · 200t streamlined · **HP 88** · **Armour 12** (very heavy) · **Thrust 4** · Jump 3 · Power 135 · *Weapons:* double turret (pulse/missile), double turret (sandcaster/missile); 12 missiles · Luxury stateroom (harem / holo hunting ground), shrine, Fire Control/2 · Crew 5 (2 gunners) · Cargo 3t.
Fast long-range clan-business courier carrying **nobles** (ransom value!). **Armour 12** shrugs off light fire — disable via ion/called shots, don't trade laser taps. Little cargo; the value is the passenger.

### Kteiroa-class Seeker — PRIZE (asteroid prospector)
TL11 · 200t streamlined · **HP 80** · Armour 0 · Thrust 2 · Jump 2 · Power 90 · *Weapons:* none · **20 mining drones**, shrine, fuel processor · Crew 4 · Cargo 61t.
Belt prospector / isolated-system transport. Unarmed, decent hold. Easy prize; loot the mining drones (equipment).

### Iyeliy-class Messenger — PRIZE (Aslan X-boat)
TL14 · 200t · **HP 80** · Armour 0 · **Thrust 1** · **Jump 5** · Power 150 · *Weapons:* 1× triple pulse-laser turret (2D,+2) · **Mail Distribution Array**, shrine, Intellect software (astrogation) · Crew 3–4 (1 gunner) · Cargo 8t.
Aslan equivalent of the Imperial X-boat — **jump-5** but **Thrust 1** (can't run, can't dock-evade). Skeleton crew. Mail = political heat if interfered with, but a quick light prize.

### Aoa'iw-class Light Trader ("Pouncer") — MIXED (armed trader/raider)
TL11 · 300t streamlined · **HP 120** · Armour 4 · **Thrust 1** · Jump 2 · Power 130 · *Weapons:* 2× double beam-laser turrets (1D,+4), double missile/sandcaster turret; 12 missiles · Air/raft, launch, shrine · Crew 5 (2 gunners) · Cargo 86t.
Popular clan-owned trader in the Trojan Reach that doubles as a **raider/self-defender**. Decent cargo but real teeth (4 laser dice + missiles + sand). Thrust 1 = can't flee; will fight. Good prize if you can weather the bite.

### Eakhau-class Trader — PRIZE (family tramp, fat hold)
TL12 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · Armour 0 · **Thrust 1** · Jump 2 · Power 165 · *Weapons:* none as built (~11t spare → owners add weapons/vehicles) · Re-entry capsules (rare Aslan escape pods), shrine · Crew 4 (one family) · **Cargo 173.5t**.
Aslan tramp trader crewed by a single family in a nomadic pride-fleet of ~12 ships. Often unarmed with a big hold = strong prize — but the pride may avenge it, and warriors view fleeing (the escape pods) as shameful, so the crew may fight to the end.

TL12 · 400t streamlined · **HP 160** · Armour 0 · Thrust 1 · Jump 3 · Power 240 · *Weapons:* none · **Laboratories (10 scientists)**, pinnace, G-carrier, Computer/25, Intellect · Crew 6 · Cargo 28t.

Large clan freight/passenger hauler on major routes. Unarmed; needs no streamlining (uses the shuttle). Excellent fat prize — but rarely truly alone in dangerous space.

Military tender / armed freighter for hostile regions; often refits cargo for auxiliary fighters. Three laser+sand turrets give real cover; Thrust 1 = can't run. Big hold if you crack it.

Clan space-patrol/combat escort, found guarding Aslan freighters. Eight triple turrets + pinnaces + fighters. A convoy-killer for the *pirate*. Disengage.

Small-scale clan troop transport + warship — deploys **two full platoons** from orbit (pinnaces each carry 2 ATVs). Ten triple turrets. Often paired with an Owatarl tender (low cargo). Don't engage.

### Hkisyeleaa-class Slaver — MIXED (Glorious Empire raider; ethically loaded)
TL12 · 1,000t streamlined · **HP 400** · Armour 0 · Thrust 2 · Jump 2 · Power 510 · *Weapons:* 3× triple turrets (beam laser/missile/sandcaster); 36 missiles · **750+ low berths** (up to 1,500 crammed — double-occupancy = **DM-4 to revive**), 8 ATVs, shrine · Crew ~12 (3 gunners) · Cargo 114t.
Converted agricultural transport: low berths built to freeze livestock, repurposed by the **Glorious Empire** to freeze **slave-raided humans**. Comparatively lightly armed — usually escorted. Liberating a slaver is a Heroic Deed (+Standing) and great fiction; the "cargo" is people.

### Halaheike-class Pocket Warship — AVOID (1,200t jack-of-all-trades)
TL14 · 1,200t reinforced · **HP 528** · **Armour 10** · Thrust 3 · Jump 3 · Power 720 · *Weapons:* **2× small particle beam bays** (4D, Radiation), 4× triple beam-laser turrets, 6× triple missile/sandcaster turrets; 360 missiles · **10 light fighters**, 2 launches, Fire Control/5 · Crew ~40 (12 gunners) · Cargo 65t.
Smaller clans' do-everything warship — carrier + assault cruiser + SDB + troop transport in one, **sub-par at all of them** and prone to maintenance/jump-drive failures. Still heavily armed (particle bays + 10 fighters). Avoid; its weakness is reliability, not firepower.

### Sakhai-class Assault Carrier — **AVOID (2,000t invasion ship)**
TL12 · 2,000t reinforced · **HP 880** · **Armour 8** · Thrust 3 · Jump 3 · Power 1,500 · *Weapons:* **medium fusion gun bay** (4D, ×20), 2× small missile bays, **3× particle barbettes**, 6× triple beam-laser + 5× triple sandcaster turrets; 600 missiles · Armoury, 160 marines + 200 low berths, Fire Control/5 · Crew ~40 + **160 marines** · Cargo 6t.
Heavy invasion carrier — "if a Sakhai appears over a world, it's under the claw in days." Distinctive horseshoe hull rakes both flanks while shielding the central fusion bay. Many far-flung Aslan outposts are built around a downed Sakhai (salvage hooks). Never engage.

## Sindalian / signature named ships
Ancient Drinaxian/Sindalian designs — the Harrier's kin. Found in service, derelict, or as salvage caches (the-harrier.md repairs need exactly these materials; Sindalian wrecks are strong plot bait). Exotic-material hulls run **+10% HP** like the Harrier.

### Rorix Command Vessel — the Harrier's command ship (signature; AVOID if crewed/hostile)
TL15 · 600t streamlined · **HP 240** · **Armour 4** (Bonded Superdense) · **Thrust 5** (size-reduced) · **Jump 2** (Early Jump, Energy Efficient, **Stealth Jump**) · Power 600 · *Weapons:* **4× missile barbettes** (4D each, ×3 dice; 240 missiles), 2× dual pulse-laser turrets · **Detachable bridge** (escape/anti-mutiny), Military Countermeasures Suite, Advanced sensors, **breaching tube + forced linkage + Type III docking clamps ×2** (docks 2 Harriers), UNREP system, **armoured captain's suite + 12-prisoner brig**, medbay, workshop · Crew ~16 + **12 marines** · Cargo 36t.
Designed as the **Harrier's partner**: standard Sindalian formation = **3 Harriers + 1 Rorix**. Stands off and bombards with missile salvoes (Harriers give mid-course guidance) while the Harriers close; then moves in to deliver boarding marines and "retrieve" prisoners. A symbol of Sindalian oppression. Lighter-armoured than a Harrier but a prestige flagship — if the crew can restore/capture one, it transforms their squadron.

### Cestion Strike Boat — Sindalian interface-superiority craft (AVOID; tiny but vicious)
TL12 · 100t streamlined, reinforced · **HP 30** · **Armour 15** (!) · **Thrust 9** · no jump · Power 140 · *Weapons:* **fusion gun barbette** (4D, ×3, Radiation), single pulse-laser turret · **Nuclear damper**, Advanced sensors, holographic controls · Crew 3–4.
A "miniature Sindalian starship" for atmosphere/orbit defence — **Thrust 9, Armour 15, a fusion barbette and a nuke damper** on a 100t hull. Lurks in ambush or orbit. Punches absurdly above its weight; the low HP is its only weakness. Treat as a heavy fighter that bites back.

### Balleyn Frontier Transport ("tough trader") — PRIZE/MIXED (versatile survivor)
TL12 · 300t standard · **HP 120** · Armour 2 · **Thrust 3** · Jump 1 · Power 210 · *Weapons:* dual pulse-laser turret (3D,+2), dual beam-laser/sandcaster turret · **2× 30t cargo/fuel modules** (extra jumps — can run Dustbelt/Imperial space → Theev without fuel caches), cargo crane, holographic controls, Computer/20 · Crew 5 (2 gunners) · Cargo 61.5t (+modules).
Centuries-old flexible hauler on a Sindalian "wings-and-neck" layout; used as a naval auxiliary by minor powers. Good safety/survival record, can outrun many threats and reach places a subbie can't. Light teeth; prize if caught, but it's built to wriggle away.

### ECITS modular system (Embleton Commercial Interstellar Transport System)
Standardised 100t modules clipped to a jump-capable or in-system **prime mover**; **very common in the Reach** and easy to adapt into a raider or Q-ship.
- **Gig / single-module Tug ("Penguin")** — TL12 · 50t · **HP 20** · Thrust 5 (2 w/ module) · no jump · in-system hauler/salvage tug. PRIZE (trivial).
- **6-Module Prime Mover** — TL11 · 300t · **HP 180** · Thrust 5 (2 w/ 6 modules) · Jump 1 · 2× dual beam-laser turrets · remote-controls modules from extra piloting stations · hauls 6 modules (or swap modules for 100t fuel tanks to cross between clusters). PRIZE if module-laden.
- **Cargo / Passenger / Crew Module** — TL9 · 100t light · **HP 36** (cargo/pax) / 20 (crew) · reaction Thrust 2 (cargo/pax) · battery power · cargo 73t / 13 staterooms / 8-berth crew. Cargo module = pure prize.
- **Salvage Module** — TL9 · 100t · 4 cargo cranes + 4 heavy grappling arms, tow cable, repair drones, breaching tube, forced linkage, workshops · "forcible salvage" rig. (Watch: pairs with the torpedo module for piracy.)
- **Torpedo Module — Q-SHIP (AVOID/SUSPECT)** — TL9 · 100t, **outwardly identical to a cargo module** · **small torpedo bay** (6D, behind blow-out panels; 60 torpedoes) · armoured bulkheads · lets a tug/prime-mover sucker a pirate close then "let them have it." If a ratty ECITS rig acts like bait, assume torpedoes.
- **Fighter Module** — TL11 · 100t · **4× light fighters** + dual pulse-laser turret · turns an ECITS ship into a patrol/escort. MIXED.
- **Base Module** — TL11 · 100t light · single pop-up beam-laser turret, armoury, lab, medbay, workshop · dig-in planetside base (mobile clandestine base — note for hidden-base play, starship-operations.md).

## Quick-pick by Prey Encounter result (piracy-raiding.md)
- **Small Freighter (100–300t)** → Type-A3 Fast Trader, Hraye, Aoa'iw, ECITS module/prime-mover, Balleyn; named Type-A/A2/Y/S above. *Ideal prey.*
- **Medium Freighter (400–1,000t)** → Eakhau, Vulture, Khtukhao, Reach Freighter (small block); named Type-R above. *Good, slow to loot.*
- **Heavy Freighter (1,000t+)** → Galoof, plus escorts. *Usually too much for one raider.*
- **Rich Freighter** → roll 1D size; cargo value twice, take better. Callsign Alpha, JCV Arym Essden, Uekh Etasieaheari fit.
- **Liner** → Queen Elizabeth, Princely Challenger, JCV Arym Essden, Khtukhao. *Outnumbered boarders.*
- **Unusual Vessel** → Hkiyrerao researcher, Trojan Ranger safari, Starlight Voyager / ECITS yacht, Mendina (rival), Hero (merc). *Useless or rich — or a rival pirate.*
- **System Defence Boat** → SOAAD (1D: 6 = Q-ship). *Fast, no jump; evade.*
- **Naval Patrol** → Emuar / Templeton (Patrol Corvette), Kerrelblossom (Destroyer Escort), Patrol Tender, Watchdog, Gazelle. **Disengage.**
- **Convoy** → DW-744 template (freighters + Liner + escort corvette). **Disengage if escorted.**
- **Traveller** → scouts/couriers/another pirate (Blank Slate, Red Adder, Noseforgold, Hraye, Iyeliy, Star Ray). *Loot for parts.*
- **Q-ship dangers everywhere:** Type-RQ subbie, Ikurdagur, ECITS torpedo module, Handow's Ship, SOAAD. A confident "soft" target with surplus Thrust = suspect.
