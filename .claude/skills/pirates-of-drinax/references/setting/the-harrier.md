## Snapshot & identity
- **Class:** Harrier-class Commerce Raider. 200-ton streamlined strike ship, an ancient Sindalian/Drinaxian design (TL15).
- **Original name:** *V'Hurg* (PCs may rename). Loaned to the PCs by King Oleb as transport + instrument of policy. Oleb wants her restored as a *symbol* of Drinax's revival — she is a tool, not a goal (PoD2 p.8; Supp. p.18).
- **Appearance:** bird-of-prey hull, swept-forward "down-swept" wings, long thin nose ("neck"), stern prow. Interior is baroque — gilding, oak panelling, engravings; now faded/mismatched from 2 centuries of scavenging. Currently bare grey hull, no markings (was Star Guard heraldry).
- **Recognition risk:** ship-recognition DBs across the Trojan Reach flag the configuration + EM emissions as a commerce raider unless **disguised** (holographic hull). Carrying such a ship invites suspicion even when operating legally (Supp. p.2).
- **Role tagline:** fast strike raider — sweeping particle-beam gun pass, then escape or hard-decel and board. Forward-arc weapons; built to attack, not to brawl defensively.

- **Hull Points: 88** as-built (vs 80 conventional, thanks to exotic materials). **Currently 40** as gifted — see defects.

- **Weapon arcs:** both mounts are **forward-arc only** when using direct-fire weapons — the ship is a strike ship, not a defensive turret platform (Supp. p.12).
- **Notable gap:** no point-defence / laser → vulnerable to fighters and incoming missiles. No medical bay (common room doubles as casualty station).

## History / what's special
- Built at Drinax during the late **Sindalian Empire** era; the class was developed specifically for commerce raiding in Sindalian internal wars (Supp. p.4). Sindal/Drinax kept advancing in the Long Night (much of empire TL13, a few worlds TL15) while the rest of Humaniti declined.
- **Drinax fell ~2 centuries ago** (Aslan-led sack): fleet wrecked, dockyards and the spares/materials industry destroyed, and the *knowledge* of forging the exotic hull materials died with its engineers. Those materials are "all but lost to history" (Supp. p.18).
- **Unique features:** exotic smart-material hull (88 HP vs 80); advanced TL15 jump drive that jumps *closer to a gravity well*, with *low emissions* and *smaller size* (early jump + size reduction + **stealth jump**); Superior Stealth coating + Holographic Hull for disguise; Military Countermeasures Suite with a holotank intel centre; full fuel self-sufficiency (gas-giant skim or water/ice cracking — endless fuel, water, air, even ice cubes); cargo scoop/capture system for grabbing containers and escape pods (ransom).
- Other Harriers may exist derelict or in service; the class could be put back into production by a capable shipyard.

## Damage & Defects (the ship starts broken)
Repairs come in three flavours: **Structural (SRU)**, **Technical (TRU)**, **Cosmetic (CRU)** — see Repair Units below. Initial defects when the PCs receive her (Supp. p.18-21 / Companion p.59-61):

1. **Hull — Reduced Hull Points.** HP is 40 (should be 88). Fragile. **Repair:** each **SRU** → +2D HP, max 88.
2. **General Systems — wear & tear.** Running costs are double. **Repair:** each **TRU** → −20% extra cost (max −100%, i.e. back to normal). ~5 TRU clears it.
3. **Ventral Hardpoint — destroyed.** Turret mechanism shot away by a fusion hit; currently storage only. **Repair:** 1 SRU + 1 TRU → fires **forward arc only**; +1 SRU + 2 TRU more → fires **any arc**.
4. **M-Drive — intermittent failure.** On any **double-1 on a Pilot check**, the drive cuts out (no manoeuvre that round). **Repair:** diagnostic = 3-4 days + partial teardown, then **D3 TRU** + 3 days rebuild.
5. **J-Drive — wayward (misjump risk).** **DM-2 on all jump-associated checks.** **Repair (2 stages):** (a) replace obvious-defect parts → 1 day + 1 TRU → reduces to DM-1; (b) full teardown D3 days + catalogue + **D3 TRU** + 1-2 days reassembly → clears it.
6. **Cosmetic defects** (no mechanical effect, but matter for diplomacy on Drinax's behalf):
   - Bridge: D3 TRU + 12 CRU
   - Crew Accommodation: 16 CRU
   - Lower Deck: D3 TRU + 12 CRU
   - Outer Hull: 20 CRU

**Full restoration** ≈ **6-10 SRU, 12-16 TRU, 60 CRU** (Supp. p.20).

### Diplomacy DMs from ship condition (cosmetic only)
When doing diplomacy on Drinax's behalf and the ship's state shows (in person or by video from the bridge):
- DM-2 if ship has unrepaired *new* damage
- DM-1 if in the state first received
- DM+0 mixed impression (e.g. hull a mess but bridge gorgeous)
- DM+1 good impression / seen in port with restored hull / video from restored bridge
- DM+2 fully restored AND shown off
A jump drive that *looks* baroque but doesn't work still makes a good impression if its internal faults aren't revealed.

### Quality of Repair (emergency / improper repairs)
Emergency battle repairs (and any work done without matching the exotic materials/procedures) may fail later. After things calm down, **GM secretly rolls 2D**:

| 2D | Result |
|---|---|
| 4- | Repair begins to fail; some warning, then the damage re-applies after **3D minutes**. Needs a complete new repair. |
| 5-8 | Holds for now (alarming creaks / intermittent faults). **Roll again after 1D days** (can recur). |
| 9+ | Good repair, holds indefinitely. |

DMs: **−4** rushed emergency repair without proper materials; **−2** emergency repair *with* correct materials; **+ highest skill level** working on it (usually the Engineering Officer's skill — if he directs properly from the bridge it applies to *all* concurrent repairs).
- A repair done **with correct procedures or correct materials** takes **3× as long** but is **not** subject to this failure roll.
- Use this for drama: a patched hull blowing out on landing approach; a jury-rigged drive needing a second emergency fix *mid-jump* as the field collapses.

## Repairing the Harrier (Repair Units)
- **Why it's hard:** the exotic materials behave differently under stress/heat; mundane repairs come undone under acceleration, weapons fire, or even heat/cool cycling toward a star. The fix is to obtain **original materials** (a cache, or — better — a machine that makes them) or **strip a wrecked Drinaxian/Sindalian ship** for components. Such rumours are strong plot bait.
- **Costs to manufacture RU** from purchased/looted/plundered goods (Supp. p.20):

| RU type | Cost / source | Buys |
|---|---|---|
| Structural (SRU) | MCr0.25 std starship components or heavy-industry produce | +2D Hull (max 88) |
| Technical (TRU) | MCr0.75 starship components or light-industry produce | −20% running cost; drive/turret fixes |
| Cosmetic (CRU) | MCr0.1 raw materials; must be custom-fitted at **1 RU per 8-hr shift** | appearance only |

- **"Proper" cosmetic restoration:** to restore *appearance* alongside function, spend **5 CRU per SRU or TRU** used (on top of listed cosmetic work).
- **Ship Shares (campaign start):** 1 Ship Share → 1 SRU **or** 1 TRU **or** 2D CRU. Spend before her first cruise or bank for later.
- RU can also be found in supply caches or salvaged from wrecks during play.

## Crew requirements & shipboard operations
- **Absolute minimum: 2** — a pilot/astrogator + an engineer can get her star to star, but with **no margin**; maintenance/routine ops get skipped → within **weeks** she suffers breakdowns/incidents. Short-handed = constant defect/Quality-of-Repair exposure.
- **Official full crew: 12** (large for 200t). Stat-block roster: **Captain, Pilot, Astrogator, Engineer, Maintenance, Gunner x2, Medic, Marines x3** (+ in narrative an assistant engineer / 2nd pilot).
- **Four officer positions** (seniority order): Captain → Executive Officer (usually First Pilot / "first lieutenant") → Navigation Officer (astrogation + sensors, "second lieutenant") → Engineering Officer (managerial; normally runs the dept from the **bridge**, only visits drive rooms for inspections/hands-on damage).
- **Ratings:** Drive Chief (runs machinery spaces when Eng Officer is on the bridge), 2 Gunners (one missile, one beam; bridge stations in action, maintain turrets/missiles), Medic (part-time — usually chief steward; duty station common room/galley), 3 Marines (groundside combat + vacc-suit, but mostly act as techs/damage control; each trained to basic level in Engineer/Electronics/Gunner/Mechanic), 2nd Pilot (often a cadet; the ship's odd-job-man / damage-control lead in action).
- **Action stations:** bridge = Captain, First Pilot, Nav Officer, Eng Officer, both Gunners. Engineering spaces = Drive Chief + 2nd Pilot. Medic in common room. Marines muster at main airlock then scatter to damage control / inspection / engineering.
- **Routine watch:** one bridge officer/pilot on sensor watch + one qualified person in engineering (rarely the Eng Officer) + an Officer of the Watch.
- **Hard to run short-handed:** damage control (needs bodies fast — the distributed "split machinery" means the Eng Officer being *off* the bridge has doomed ships, while having an engineer *outside* the drive spaces has also saved them); manning two forward turrets + bridge; prize crews (carry spare specialists in low berths rather than stripping the crew); fuel/cargo handling down the long nose corridor.

## Deckplan overview (compartments)
Two-deck main section aft; long forward nose holds fuel/sensor/utility systems. (Isometric deckplan in Supp. p.7 / PoD2 p.9 / Companion p.50. NOTE: deckplan image is **not present** in `assets/maps/` — describe from this layout.)

**Upper Deck (Deck 2) — command + accommodation, the "show" deck (most-restored):**
1. **Bridge** — upper (captain's railed walkway, command chair w/ sword receptacle) + lower (crew stations). Any console configurable for any role (pilot/astro/sensors/eng/gunnery). Forward iris valve runs in **secure** mode (officer biometric); valves can go to locked/crew/access modes.
2. **Ship's Locker** — odd layout (was for decorative spares); now full of trophies, needs sorting.
3. **Officers' Staterooms** (x4) — luxurious double beds, worn; captain's is best, junior's worst.
4. **Crew Staterooms** (x4) — double-occupancy bunks, mismatched salvaged fittings.
5. **Armoury.**
6. **Common Area** — (6A) common room with oak table that lifts into ceiling storage; doubles as **emergency casualty station** (no med bay). (6B) Galley, over-cluttered with salvaged gadgets.
7. **Stores / utility rooms** — superfreeze food lockers, larder.
8. **Dorsal Barbette (Particle)** — bridge-controlled or local workstation; the working main gun.
9. **Avionics / Sensors** — far forward in a demountable pod (sensor upgrades retrofit by removing hullplate); neural-activity sensors, standoff densitometer, radar/lidar/jammer; primary comms (backups distributed).

**Lower Deck (Deck 1) — functional; drives/weapons/cargo, surrounded by fuel tanks:**
10. **Cargo Scoop Airlock** — magnetic scoop grabs containers / escape pods (ransom), winch + cable; battered.
11. **Fuel Processors** — gas-giant skim or water/ice cracking; can purify drinking water (few hrs to adjust). Self-sufficient.
12. **Countermeasures Suite** — distributed secondary computer nodes; holotank intel centre.
13. **Main Airlock** — belly exit, fits 4 (or 2 in combat armour); not built for hostile boarding.
14. **Low Berths** (x8) — survival margin / extra crew / **prisoners**; short walk from cargo-scoop receiving chamber, bypassing control areas.
15. **Magazine** — turret ammo; vents blast outward; doubles as secure locker / prisoner cell.
16. **Ventral Turret** — standard missile mount; **currently destroyed/disabled**, crudely sealed, storage only.
17. **Engineering Spaces** — drives + power **split between two near-identical bays** (survivability; can lose half drives and limp out); multifunction consoles incl. backup piloting station.
18. **Cargo Bay** — (18A) subdivided, low ceiling, cluttered with Drinaxian busts; 29.7t. (18B) aft cargo hatch + retractable ramp (can deploy troops).
19. **Landing Gear Access** — retractable legs; hydraulics maintainable in flight at need.

Survivability notes: split machinery + distributed comp nodes mean she can take hits and still escape; losing the whole forward nose (sensors/scoop) is survivable in theory (can't land) but never tested.

## Upgrade path
Blueprints exist at the Floating Palace; install is trial-and-error (Supp. p.22-27). Restore first (defects above), then:

**Rebuild the ventral turret** (1 SRU+1 TRU forward arc; +1 SRU+2 TRU any arc) for a working missile/torpedo/beam mount. OR repurpose the turret space without a full rebuild:
- **Line Gun** — towing cable/grapple/harpoon; tether to ships or asteroids (hide / mine). Tethered to a big target → enemy DM-2 to detect. Cr50,000 (+~Cr50,000 fit, ~2 days). Core-sampling version Cr200,000.
- **Anti-personnel weaponry** — up to 4 weapons <250kg for strafing/ground attack (limited arc). +Cr1000 mounting/power per weapon, +Cr5000 turret mod per weapon, +1 CRU each (appearance).
- **Assault Pod** — pressurised ready chamber for 4 armoured marines + light line gun for hull transfer; magazine = boarding-suit storage. Cr200,000 + 1 CRU.
- **Fixed Torpedo Mount** — 2 torpedoes on cradles in turret space (manual reload) + 3 spares in the 1t magazine; forward arc only. 1 SRU + 3 CRU. (Torpedoes: High Guard p.31.)

**Hull/mobility upgrades:**
- **Specialised Atmospheric Manoeuvre Kit** — rebuild wings into control surfaces w/ flight computer → **DM+2 Pilot in Thin (atmo 4)+ atmospheres** (also gas giants/underwater, not deep). 1 week+ in dock; 1 SRU + 2 TRU (+2 CRU appearance); uses 1t cargo; flight computer Cr250,000 (without it, DM+1 only).
- **Underwater Operations Kit** — *Partial:* to 300m + 10kn drive; 2 SRU + 2 TRU (+2 CRU), 2t cargo. *Full:* to 1000m + 20kn + lights/sensors/strengthened airlocks; 5 SRU + 3 TRU (+4 CRU), 6t cargo. (If atmo kit also fitted, its Pilot DM applies in any fluid.)

**Cargo / utility:**
- **Cargo Capture Drone** — grabs containers/debris/ice/drifting crew; 4G for ~4 min on a vacc-suited payload. DM-1 Electronics (remote ops) to control. Cr70,000 — **one is already aboard.** Negligible space (10 = 1t).
- **Configurable Mission Pod** — converts the cargo area's central section into a swappable pod: **−10t cargo**, and the forward ~10t of cargo becomes inaccessible from the aft hatch. Build: 1D days, Cr250,000. Reconfigure in ~1 hour; pod-built facilities cost **+20%**. Configurations:
  - Command Suite — EW DM+4; multi-spectral scan through ≤4 armour at ≤50km (track marines inside a target); Tactics DM+1 for forces under the commander (incl. own crew). MCr7.5.
  - Diplomatic Suite — luxury briefing room (Diplomacy DM+1) + common area. MCr0.7.
  - Emergency Fuel Store — +10t fuel (not enough to jump; extends powerplant endurance). Free.
  - Marine Barracks — 4 extra marines + armoury. MCr0.8 + Cr500/occupant/week.
  - Medlab — up to 10 casualties / life-sciences lab (disease/chem/bio/rad). MCr4.
  - Prisoner Holding — 10 prisoners in comfort. MCr0.75 + Cr300/prisoner/week.
  - VIP Accommodation — 1 High Stateroom + recreation. MCr1 (+1 CRU to match décor).
  - Weaponry Workshop — armoury to maintain/fabricate weapons & ammo. MCr0.5.

**Distinctive gear** (ship's locker / Sindalian, Supp. p.28-30): Officer's Sword (TL15, melee 2D+2, Cr300 replica); Officer's Revolver (TL15, 8mm, Red 3D-2 AP3 / Blue 3D-4 Zero-G / Mutiny 3D+3 Blast 2); Breaching Gun (TL15, rng 25, 4D, AP6 Auto5 Blast1, Cr2400); Boarding Gun (TL15, rng 50, 3D / dart 6D Blast2, Auto5 Bulky, Cr2100).

## Commerce-raiding tactics
Commerce raiding ≠ piracy — declare a transponder/allegiance *before* firing or be tried as a pirate. Goal: damage the enemy economy, not max profit (taking fuel/supplies/ammo/components to sustain the raider is legitimate) (Supp. p.3-4).
- **Signature move:** sweeping particle-beam gun pass (forward arc, DM+1, re-roll low damage), then either a hard-decel **board** or a fast escape. Follow the barbette strike with a missile salvo to deter pursuit or keep the target busy for a second pass; or run in *behind* your own salvo, forcing the enemy to choose point-defence or engaging you.
- **Small-hull doctrine:** can't take well-armed freighters except *en masse* (heavy losses) — instead pick soft targets: **in-system traffic, outsystem assets (mining/ore ships), and backwater refuelling points** on major runs. A single raider ties down many escorts/guardships even after it stops operating.
- **Avoid:** highport defence zones, guardships, convoys, patrols. Counters to expect: Q-ships (bait), naval sweeps, and intelligence collating where you strike/refuel to find your **base**. Defeat this by varying tactics and concealing identity (holographic hull) — predictability is a death sentence.
- **Self-sufficiency = range:** skim gas giants / crack ice for fuel-water-air so you can operate far from friendly ports; use low berths for prize crews and prisoners; cargo scoop for ransom-worthy escape pods.
- **Disguise vs power:** running the Holographic Hull costs **Power 100** — you usually can't disguise *and* fight at full power, so drop the disguise the moment you commit to the attack.
