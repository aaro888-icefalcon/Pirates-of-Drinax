## What & where
- The **Trojan Reach** is a 16-subsector sector on the **spinward/rimward frontier of the Third Imperium**, beyond the Spinward Marches. It is a narrow ribbon of wild, mostly-lawless space between two great powers: the **Third Imperium** (coreward/trailing, "the great red expanse") and the ever-advancing **Aslan Hierate** (rimward/spinward, "the golden glow"). Also called the **Outrim Void**.
- The First Imperium (Ziru Sirka) never conquered it; the Second left isolated colonies; the **Third Imperium claims no more than ~1/6 of the systems** — three subsectors (Pax Rulin, Gazulin, Tobia). The Aslan hold the rimward half and push outward every generation.
- A **dust-spice trade route** links the two empires, running the perilous crossing from **Fist (Tobia 3215)** to **Tyokh (Tlaiowaha 2226)**. Between them lies "a sea of lawless, dangerous planets." (PoD2: Welcome to the Reach; Exploring the Trojan Reach)

## The feel
Treacherous opportunity. A whole sector of settled, living, conquerable worlds — and almost every venture here founders violently. Empires "run aground and mortally wound themselves." Two-thirds of the Reach is small, lightly-defended worlds; half survive only because the Aslan have not yet bothered to take them. The sector "stands on a knife edge": the right pressure could push it into Aslan claws, force the Imperium deeper in, or let **a new pirate kingdom be carved out in blood and steel**. Now is the time for corsairs, privateers, rogues and empire-builders. (PoD2 p.14, p.121)

## Where Drinax & the action sit
- **Drinax** = hex **0603, Tlaiowaha subsector**, UWP **A43645A-E** (dead world; the Floating Palace orbits above it). Tlaiowaha is Aslan-held space, dominated by the trade-minded **Ahroay'if** clan; the surviving independent worlds of the **old Kingdom of Drinax** lie scattered through it (the Solomani-named arc: Asim, Banks/Khusai, Stross/Kteiroa, Clarke, Pourne, Hilfer, Paal, Torpal, Blue).
- The campaign's playground is the wedge between **Fist (Tobia)** and **Tyokh (Tlaiowaha)** — the lawless independent worlds either side of the trade route — plus the old Sindalian Main running through **Sindal subsector** (Theev, Noricum). This is where pirates prey, where Drinax can rebuild alliances, and where the two empires jockey.

## History in brief
- **Ancients** active here; little physical trace. Vilani (First Imperium) barely settled it.
- **Terran/Solomani explorers** (Second Imperium era) aggressively colonised 2+ dozen overspecialised worlds. When the Second Imperium fell, those colonies couldn't survive alone and turned to **raiding their neighbours** — raider culture, the sector's lasting curse, took hold (≈10% of raiders Vargr corsairs, the likely seed).
- **The Sindalian Empire** (the "old empire"): rose ~**-2000** from a Noricum-led defence pact, grew to cover **9 of 16 subsectors**, drove raiders to the fringe — but became autocratic, bombing vassals from orbit (an empire founded to stop raiders became the worst raider). Rebels loosed engineered plagues; it collapsed; **Noricum fell ~-1400**, sinking member worlds into barbarism. Remembered (wrongly) as a golden age.
- **Petty successor kings** arose; the longest-lasting was **Drinax** (the "Star Dragon Empire of Sindal in Exile"). Drinax's kings ruled their arc for ~2 millennia, hiding the iron fist behind trade and protection — Drinax itself became a garden world with the wondrous **Floating Palace**.
- **Aslan expansion:** Aslan crossed the **Great Rift ~-1000**; the **Cultural Purge (Kinstrife)** and the **Peace of Fthair** (which blocked Aslan growth on the Solomani border) drove waves of **landless-male ihatei migration** into the Reach (~500 onward into Tlaiowaha). A dozen colonies became hundreds of worlds. The Aslan formed a local council, the **Ya'soisthea**; the slaver **Glorious Empire** broke away (declared independent 650).
- **Drinax's fall:** Drinax revived the old Sindalian tactic of taxing the Imperium–Hierate trade through its space. **884: King Oleb XIV's 20% tax. 885: the Aslan (Ahroay'if clan) bombed Drinax from orbit**, sparing only the Floating Palace. Drinax's worlds were conquered or revolted.
- **Imperium:** expanded spinward under Cleon I but stalled (Zhodani threat); built fleet bases at **Pax Rulin and Tobia** and declared it would go no further. Fixed borders; expands now only via client states.
- **Current era (~1105 Imperial):** Imperium and Hierate "pretend to be friends while they jockey for position." Menorial surveyed only in 1105; the Drinaxi Harrier is recovered and rebuilt — the PCs' ship. The sector is on a knife edge.

## Major powers' footprints (summary — detail in factions.md)
- **Third Imperium:** 3 subsectors (Pax Rulin = military bulwark; Gazulin = old cultural core; Tobia = ducal seat & sector fleet, cosmopolitan). Borders fixed; expands by client states & defence pacts. Sees the Reach as a poorly-regarded backwater.
- **Aslan Hierate:** dominates the rimward half. **No central authority** — opaque clan politics via the toothless **Ya'soisthea** (12-clan grand council); real drivers are corporations (trade) and **ihatei** (migration/conquest). Holdings projected to grow 10–20% this century. Key Reach clans: **Ahroay'if** (Tlaiowaha, trade-rich, pro-peace), **Tlaiowaha** (Tlaukhu patrons of Ahroay'if), **Aroaye'i** (hold the jump-5 Rift crossing), **Hrakoea** (aggressive, Borderland), **Ikhtealyo** (dust-spice traders), **Tiykhisto** (pro-war).
- **Glorious Empire:** breakaway Aslan slaver state (Goertal/Nora'a); 30–60% human-slave population; xenophobic, always at war, economy stagnating; feared bogeyman, no real allies. Losing ground to the Hierate.
- **Florian League:** oldest extant human civilisation (Floriani: brute **Feskals** + brainy **Barnai**, Ancient-engineered). Seat at Floria/Yggdrasil; ultra-conservative, lottery government, amoral-utilitarian diplomacy, closes borders in times of surplus. Will use overwhelming force in war.
- **Minor states & raiders:** Strend Cluster (Solomani, Menorial), Belgardian Sojournate (pirate, Egyrn), Senlis Foederate (Pax Rulin), Dustbelt worlds (Sindalian Main), Oghman Clans (Sindal). **50–100 raider bands**, many based at **Theev**; raider mix ≈60% human / 30% Aslan / 10% Vargr.
- **Megacorps & the Zhodani:** **GeDeCo** (General Development Company) is the most important non-state actor — builds starports, signs development worlds; HQ at Vorito. Others: Company of Grandmotherly Kindness, Florian Trade Company, PRQ, Tyeyo Dustspice Importers. **Zhodani**: no bases, but 2–5 cruisers patrol the spinward Reach yearly and court independent worlds.

## Travel realities
- **The two trade routes (both run through neutral space):**
  - **Hierate Route** (busiest, ~5× the traffic; shorter/safer): Fist→Wildeman→Cordan→(jump-3 Acrid→Tech-World, or slow via Argona/Sperle)→Tech-World→Paal→Tyokh. **~7 jumps / ~55 days** for a jump-2 freighter. Imperial escorts usually Fist→Acrid; **Ahroay'if** escorts take over at Tech-World. **Argona & Sperle = prime pirate-ambush spots** (escorts skip them).
  - **Florian Route** (to the League): Fist→…→diverge at Arunisiir→through Tlaiowaha (Blue, Torpol, Oghma)→into the Sindalian Main (Thebus…)→Yggdrasil. **~14 jumps / ~140 days** at jump-2. Largely unescorted past Arunisiir; the Florian League gives no protection.
- **Jump distances:** the Aslan crossed the Great Rift via a **jump-5** route (Aroaye'i clan holds it); **Binary** at the Rift edge needs **jump-6, TL15**. Faster **jump-4** shortcuts exist from Pax Rulin/Glisten but are "extremely hazardous." Convoys move at the slowest ship's speed (usually **jump-2**).
- **Lawful vs lawless:** **Imperial space (Pax Rulin, Gazulin, Tobia) is patrolled** — raiding there gets you hunted by the Navy. Everywhere else is the **Outrim Void**: no law. Convoys (Imperial or Aslan escort, for a fee) are the only protection; <half of traders bother, and convoys are slow to assemble.
- **Where pirates thrive:** independent worlds astride the trade routes (ambush just before a convoy jumps; raid right after escorts jump out), low-tech backwater systems (a hidden free trader + a winter raid), the **Sindalian Main / Dustbelt**, **Sindal subsector** (Theev), and the gaps where Imperial escorts hand off to Aslan escorts. Traditional plunder: precious metals, gems, electronics, radioactives, art, **starship parts**, and slaves.
- **Refuelling:** gas-giant skimming is common (worlds like Hadara are bypassed because of it); note dedicated refuelling stations on long routes (e.g. station 291-540 on the Florian route). Most independent worlds offer at best a Class C/D port — and the Imperial charts (which still list Drinax as Class-A) are centuries out of date.
