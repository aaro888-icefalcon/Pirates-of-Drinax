## How to use this file (playing powers honestly)
1. Each power has **goals it pursues whether or not the PCs are present.** When the crew act, ask "what does this power *want*, and does this help or threaten it?" — then react.
2. **Powers are not monoliths.** The Imperium is a lazy giant; the Hierate is dozens of feuding clans. Pick *which* faction reacts, not "the Aslan."
3. **Piracy heat is a clock** (SKILL.md sandbox clocks). Hitting small independents draws little heat; hitting a megacorp, an Aslan clan, or repeating a hunting ground draws a fleet. Track Imperial heat and *ihatei* pressure in state.
4. When a power decides to retaliate, **use the actual force listed** and roll it via mythic-gm — never fudge a navy into incompetence.

## The strategic picture (the Reach in one breath)
The Trojan Reach is wild space between the **Third Imperium** (spinward/coreward) and the **Aslan Hierate** (trailing/rimward), linked by the **dust-spice trade route** from Fist (Tobia 3215) to Tyokh (Tlaiowaha 2226), ~7–8 jump-2 jumps / ~60 days. Both great powers control well under half the sector; the rest is independents, ruins of the **Empire of Sindal**, the **Florian League**, the renegade **Glorious Empire**, and pirate haunts (Theev). The sector "stands on a knife edge": the right pressure could tip half the Reach into Aslan claws, drag in the Imperium, or let **Drinax** rise again (PoD2: Welcome to the Reach). *Imperial date 1105; Aslan date 3637.*

## THE THIRD IMPERIUM (Domain of Deneb / Spinward Marches border)
*The lazy giant. It will not chase the crew — until they cost a megacorp real money.*

- **Territory / reach:** Controls **parts of four subsectors** in the Reach — roughly a sixth of all systems — behind the "**rampart worlds**" of **Pax Rulin** and **Tobia**. Three Imperial-dominated subsectors, each distinct (PoD2: Powers of the Trojan Reach; The Imperium):
  - **Pax Rulin** — military subsector; "watchmen on the walls," fiercely anti-Aslan-expansion; **fleet base** here.
  - **Gazulin** — oldest, most cultured; the Reach's bastion of civilisation.
  - **Tobia** — the **ducal seat**; cosmopolitan, grown fat on Aslan trade.
- **Domain context:** The Reach borders the **Domain of Deneb** (which also holds the Spinward Marches). Deneb is contentious — its duchies squabble, the Marches feel neglected by Capital, and the Domain currently has **no Archduke** (The_Third_Imperium). Capital is *weeks to months* away; even the governor at Pax Rulin is many weeks from Drinax.
- **Navy & Scout presence:** Imperial **convoy escorts run only as far as Acrid**, then hand off (Aslan pick up convoys at Tech-World; mercenary/corporate escorts cover the whole route). Anti-pirate detachments sortie from the **Pax Rulin fleet base** into wild space (PoD1). Scout Service charts of the outer Reach are officially "**questionable**"; Drinax is still listed (wrongly) as Class-A starport on centuries-old charts. An **Imperial Consul** sits at Drinax's court (currently Thao Poloc — see personalities file).
- **Law & attitude to piracy:** **Tolerates a low level of piracy** so long as it hits small independents. The moment losses dent a **megacorporation's bottom line**, "the fleet is sent out to scour the stars" and pirates are driven from the Borderlands **for a few months** (PoD2). Imperial law = standard high-law; a captured pirate faces an Imperial firing squad.
- **Reaction to a Drinax privateer / letter of marque:** The Imperium does **not** recognise Drinax's authority to issue letters of marque. To the Imperial Navy a "privateer" of Drinax is simply a **pirate**; the letter of marque buys nothing with them. (It only matters with worlds that still honour Drinax.)
- **Goals / wants:** Hold the line cheaply; **cultivate client/buffer states** against the Hierate (often via GeDeCo, below); avoid the expense of expansion.
- **Attitude to Drinax:** Indifferent affection — Emperor Strephon sends greetings to his "beloved cousin" every ten years. Drinax is a low-priority backwater unless it becomes a useful buffer or a nuisance.
- **Attitude to the crew:** Ignores them while they're small fish on the trade fringe; hunts them once they're attributed to megacorp or escorted-convoy losses, or once a consul/agent fingers the Harrier.
- **Military shorthand (Grave of Empires endgame):** A late-campaign **Imperial Punitive Fleet = 35 Fighting Strength, 6 Capital Ships** (PoD1: The Grave of Empires) — use this scale only when the Imperium finally moves to crush a risen Drinax.

## THE ASLAN HIERATE
*To humans it looks like a frozen tsunami poised to break over the Reach. In truth it is a loose alliance of warring clans that rarely act in unison — and that is exactly why it is more likely to act than the Imperium: each clan has its own navy and reacts with alarming speed.* (PoD2: Powers of the Trojan Reach; The Aslan Hierate)

### Clan structure (the chain of fealty)
| Tier | Aslan term | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Family | *ekho* | 2–12 persons under a patriarch (head, wives, children, blood relatives). Patrilinear; females outnumber males 3:1. |
| Pride | *ahriy* | Several families; one family dominant, its head leads the pride. |
| Clan | *huiha* | Several prides; one pride dominant. Clan leader protects, provides, settles disputes. |
| Council | *Tlaukhu* | The **Twenty-Nine** pre-eminent clans of the homeworld Hierate. Most clans are *independent* of the Tlaukhu. |
| Reach council | *Ya'soisthea* | The Reach's **local** Tlaukhu (12 clans on the grand council), founded ~-802 because of Great-Rift comms lag. Far more **fractious and divided** than the Tlaukhu; includes exiled clans; moves world-to-world yearly. Standings here are fluid. |

- **Only males hold land (TER).** SOC derives from Territory; landless males are the lowest class. **Females own and run all corporations, finance, and most logistics** — upper-class males often can't function in a tech society without a wife/mother/employer.
- **Tech ~TL slightly below Imperial**, made up for by weight of numbers.

### The *ihatei* (the migration pressure — a sandbox clock)
- ***Ihatei*** = "second/non-inheriting son." Only the eldest inherits; the rest must **win their own territory.** With jump drive, the favoured route is to **expand the Hierate into new space** — i.e. *into the Reach* (PoD2: Land / Ihatei).
- They **flock to border regions, warzones, and unexplored space.** A poor sire gives an *ihatei* a weapon, armour, and a one-way ticket; a rich one gives a scout ship or even a warship and retainers. Following them come outcasts, low-class males seeking work, and mercenaries.
- **Border camps** are the most dangerous Aslan gathering: thousands of armed, competitive young males around bonfires, each *ihatei* boasting of a target (a virgin world, an isolated human world, a weak neighbour, "a crusade to the heart of the Imperium"). Warriors rally to the most promising — bands run from a few warriors/one ship to **hundreds of thousands** strong. "Advanced and well-defended worlds have fallen to the locust swarm of *ihatei*."
- **No formal political power**, but inciting or restraining the *ihatei* is "a key element of any Aslan clan's foreign policy." A clan's expansion = the support it funds to its *ihatei* sons.
- **Surge trigger:** war years (the looming Fifth Frontier War) and clan defeats push *ihatei* outward. Drives the **Ihatei!** adventure: an *ihatei* warlord may unite the swarm and pour into Drinax space — a **25-week** clock the crew must beat (see `adventures/`). The **Peace of Fthair** barely brakes this — it binds only the clans that fought the Border Wars (under 40% of Reach clans).

### Honour culture (run every Aslan NPC through this)
**Three pillars of honour** (PoD2): **respect** (for *territory* — which means everything another holds: land, money, wives; *conquest is honourable, theft is not* — Aslan do not work in secret), **tradition** (ways of ancestors/heroes; obey clan elders and the poets), **harmony of action** (*khoafteirleao* — zen-like perfect action). An honourable Aslan "takes everything he can hold and keep, but does not overreach"; his boasts honestly map the limits of his strength.

**Offence ladder:** *Familiarity* (treating a superior as an equal; touching without permission) → *Impoliteness* (bad manners, wrong honorifics, losing temper) → *Discourtesy* (deliberate insult). Failing to apologise escalates one rung. **Discourtesy is grounds for a duel,** and an unaddressed offence makes a permanent enemy. Non-Aslan who never duel are *tahiwihteakhtau* (barbarians); willingness to challenge/duel earns respect.

**Duelling:** strictly same-gender; bare claws + dewclaws, fought to first wound (or unconsciousness for grave insult). A **formal** duel needs the clan head's sanction. **SOC effect:** win a formal duel vs an equal/higher-SOC Aslan → **SOC +1**; lose → **SOC -2.** Victor may scalp (or geld, in breeding-challenge duels) the loser but may not kill outside a death-duel. Even fleet engagements and fighter battles can be settled by an agreed duel.

### How the Hierate treats humans, pirates & Drinax
- **Humans:** chattels-or-respected by *territory* logic. A conquered human world's people become a land-owning male's property — and are then *protected* as his (an Aslan may bomb a city, then tend its survivors). Outside conquest, humans are tolerated, often condescended to.
- **Being raided by pirates:** raiding **Aslan** shipping is a grave matter. The wronged clan will **pursue and avenge** by its own navy and by *honour* — pirates who provoke the Hierate alarm even *other* pirates (cf. Peytr Vallis stirring up Aslan wrath). But the Hierate is **quietly pleased** when pirates bleed the **Imperium's** trade. So: raid Aslan prizes → expect retaliation; raid Imperial prizes → Aslan shrug or smile.
- **Drinax:** the Hierate **bombed Drinax in 885** (led by the Ahroay'if) to keep the trade route's tolls in check. A *risen* Drinax threatens that arrangement; an ambitious clan will move to crush it again (see Grave of Empires fleets below).
- **Endgame clan fleets (Grave of Empires Punitive Fleets, PoD1):** if Drinax rises and the crew lack a treaty with a given clan, that clan attacks with — **Ahroay'if 30 FS / 3 Capital Ships; Tokofeahr 25 / 2; Yerlyaruiwo 25 / 2; Tlaiowaha 20 / 1; Syoisuis 20 / 1.** A generic Aslan punitive horde = **40 FS / 4 Capital Ships**, and *any* Aslan attack brings ≥1 capital ship — often the antique heavy bomber **Eikhoifiruah** (the very ship that burned Drinax), wheeled out as a deliberate provocation.

## THE GLORIOUS EMPIRE (renegade Aslan slavers)
*A breakaway Aslan state built on human slavery — feared and hated by every human world that knows of it.* (PoD2: The Glorious Empire)

- **Origin / government:** The **Tokouea'we** clan, driven from Hierate space by the Yerlyaruiwo, fled to the Reach, rebuilt, and **broke from the Hierate entirely (~650 Imperial)**. Ruled by a single **Emperor** (centralised autocracy); retains a Ya'soisthea seat but few real allies or trade partners.
- **Territory:** **Goertal subsector** (spinward-trailing fringe) — **too far from the Imperium for either to threaten the other.**
- **Economy / population:** Founded on **mass human slavery** (estimates of the enslaved run very high); slaves range from third-class citizens to "food animals." Stagnating — too few skilled technicians among the slave caste. Its **only real export is mercenaries.**
- **Military / status:** **Slowly losing ground** to Hierate clans (~6 parsecs lost coreward since founding). Defeats push it to **raid human colonies for fresh slaves** to fuel the war.
- **Goals / wants:** Survive the Hierate's pressure; seize human worlds for slaves and strategic depth.
- **Attitude to Drinax / crew:** A **distant slaver threat**, not a trade rival. Its **slave ships menace weak human worlds Reach-wide** — a ready antagonist and a foe even other powers despise. The Florian League cynically uses it as a **bogeyman** ("accept our terms or the Aslan monsters get you").
- **Reaction to piracy:** Itself a raider state; will fight to keep prizes/slaves and answer raids with force.

## THE FLORIAN LEAGUE (the Florani)
*A xenophobic island of genetically-engineered humans on the far (trailing) side of the Reach — never aggressive first, utterly ruthless if threatened.* (PoD2: The Florian League)

- **Who they are:** The **Florani**, engineered long ago by the **Ancients**, in two castes: **Feskals** (huge, ~2m+, strong labourers) and **Barnai** (small, large-headed, highly intelligent administrators). After 300,000 years of stasis they recovered jump drive from a **crashed Aslan scout (~-225)**.
- **Territory:** **Yggdrasil subsector** and the far trailing Reach; homeworld **Floria** (riddled with Ancient structures); expanding into **Beyond** sector. One of the **most stable governments in the Reach.**
- **Government:** A **lottery democracy** — randomly drafted Barnai serve fixed council terms, never re-eligible, so no power base forms; result is **extreme conservatism.** Justice "learns what happened, then decides if it was a crime."
- **Tech / military:** Effectively **TL15** via Ancient manufacturing; defensive but **uses nuclear weapons and overwhelming force without hesitation** when at war (e.g. crushing rebellious worlds). Naval academy + mobile shipyards. **Has never attacked another interstellar power** — but has absorbed degenerate human colonies.
- **Goals / wants:** Preserve the **status quo** and the privacy of the Florani race; trade **only** for resources it lacks (closes its borders entirely in times of surplus); diplomacy exists solely to safeguard the League. **Guided by amoral, purely utilitarian logic.**
- **Trade & access:** A second trade route runs Imperium ↔ League (lighter than the Hierate route, also **pirate-plagued**). Outsiders must register at border worlds before using the jump-1 **"Florian Main"** — ostensibly anti-piracy, really to preserve Florani isolation. Refuses to trade with most Aslan corporations.
- **Attitude to Drinax / crew:** **Indifferent and transactional.** Won't aid or oppose Drinax; treats pirates on its route as a border-control/convoy problem, not a casus belli — but a crew that *threatens the League itself* meets overwhelming force.

## Vargr corsairs & the Zhodani Consulate (other neighbours)
- **Vargr:** No Vargr state borders the Reach; the **Two Thousand Worlds / Vargr Extents are not a neighbour here.** Reach Vargr are **descendants of corsairs** who migrated in during the First Imperium's decline (via Corridor, skirting the Domain of Deneb and the Great Rift) — they may have started the raiding tradition that blights the sector. A **few Vargr corsairs** still operate (e.g. **Zuekhvi the Corsair**, below). Treat as freelance raiders, not a polity.
- **Zhodani Consulate:** A "**constant if unseen presence**" — **no worlds or known bases**, but **2–5 Zhodani cruisers patrol the spinward Reach each year**, and Zhodani agents hold **secret talks with independent worlds**, hinting at a long game to build a network of friendly worlds. Use them as shadowy wildcards (espionage, quiet offers to independents), not open belligerents.

## The Sindalian remnants & independent worlds (pocket empires)
- **Empire of Sindal (history):** Barbarian raiders crowned themselves kings; the Empire **rose ~-2000, ruled ~600 years, fell ~-1400** when its seat at **Noricum was bombed to oblivion** (PoD2). Half a dozen regional dukes then proclaimed themselves kings — the longest-lasting pretender was **Drinax**, the "Star Dragon Empire of Sindal in Exile," which ruled much of Tliowaha (Drinax) subsector for two millennia until the Aslan burned it in 885.
- **Sindal today:** A **ruined world of a few hundred dirt farmers** whose ancestors once ruled the night sky; the Empire survives only in confused legends of a golden age.
- **Sindalian relics:** Lost **bunkers, vaults, supply caches and access codes** seeded across many worlds, and TL15-era knowledge (Drinax still *knows* TL15 but lacks the minerals to build it). These relics — sought by rulers chasing legitimacy and tech — drive the **Shadows of Sindal** material (see `adventures/`).
- **Independent worlds / pocket empires:** Most Reach independents were settled by First-Imperium exiles or Second-Imperium colonists; during the Long Night they fell under Sindalian influence. Today they are **isolated, lightly trafficked, and individually weak** — no single named multi-world pocket empire rivals Drinax, the Hierate's clans, the Glorious Empire, or the Florian League. They are the **prizes** of the campaign: the worlds Drinax (and its rivals) court, raid, or annex. The **Borderlands subsector** (the rough stretch of the trade route) sees the most piracy and the most independent-world turbulence. **King Oleb's whole project is to win these worlds back under Drinax's banner.**

## Pirates, Theev & mercenaries
- **Theev** (Sindal subsector): the Reach's **pirate world** — a haven of tens of thousands of criminals and the base for many of the **50–100 raider bands** Imperial intelligence estimates operate in the sector. Pirates are safe here because it sits in lawless wild space beyond reliable Imperial reach. (PoD2)
- **Named free pirates (as of 1105)** — one line each; full stats/personalities in `personalities-and-factions.md` and `ships-of-the-reach.md`:
  - **Admiral Darokyn** — ex-Imperial-Navy officer; rules a fleet **said to top 50 ships** out of Theev; rumoured Imperial Navy protectors.
  - **Peytr Vallis** — newcomer with a vendetta, preys on **Aslan** traders along the border (and so stirs up Hierate wrath, to other pirates' annoyance).
  - **The Ghost** — legend; more thief than pirate; ships vanish **without a shot fired.**
  - **Hroal Irontooth** — Aslan **outcast**; fled the Hierate with ~a dozen warships including a **Halaheike-class pocket warship**; cybernetic jaws bite through battle dress.
  - **The Dread Pirate Ashan** — perhaps the most feared; raids **independent worlds** with a band of cutthroats, rarely entering the Borderlands.
  - **Zuekhvi the Corsair** — Vargr; knows the trade-route worlds better than anyone; repeatedly slips Imperial patrols.
- **Mercenaries:** Widely hired as **convoy escorts** along the full trade route (where Imperial escorts stop at Acrid). The Glorious Empire **exports mercenaries**; female-run Aslan mercenary companies are common. Treat as buyable muscle for any side, including the crew.

## GeDeCo (General Development Company)
- An Imperial **megacorporation** — not the biggest in the Reach, but the **most widespread.** Founded to improve colonies/independent worlds, it has become a **major political player**: its advisors have the ear of many independent rulers, and it acts as the Imperium's quiet instrument for **building a buffer of client worlds against the Aslan.** It runs spies (including on Drinax), brokers treaties, and plays sophisticated destabilisation games. **Goal:** profit + Imperial influence. **Attitude to Drinax/crew:** a rival empire-builder by other means — sometimes a patron, often a competitor for the same worlds, and a leak risk for the crew's secrets.

## Quick reaction-to-piracy table
| You raid… | Likely responder | Heat |
|---|---|---|
| Small independent merchant | Nobody much; local grumbling | Low |
| Megacorp / escorted convoy cargo | **Imperial Navy** sortie from Pax Rulin; pirates swept from Borderlands for months | High |
| **Aslan** clan shipping | The wronged **clan's own navy** + honour vendetta (assassins for Yerlyaruiwo/Syoisuis) | High, personal, lasting |
| Imperial shipping (generally) | Imperium hunts; **Hierate quietly pleased** | Medium (Imperial) |
| Glorious Empire slave ship | Glorious Empire reprisal raids; humans cheer | Medium; earns goodwill with human worlds |
| Florian-route shipping | Florian **border-control / convoy** clampdown (not war) | Low–medium |
| Same hunting ground, repeatedly | Whichever power owns the lane concentrates force there | Escalating |
