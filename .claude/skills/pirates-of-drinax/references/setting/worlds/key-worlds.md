# Key Worlds of the Trojan Reach

> Use this when: the crew arrives at, plots a course to, raids, hides on, or negotiates with one of the campaign-critical worlds and you need its rulers, economy, defences, attitude to Drinax/pirates, and hooks at a glance. For the full UWP list of every world see `00_world-index.md`; for the sector picture see `../trojan-reach.md`; for powers/clans see `../factions.md`; for named NPCs see `../personalities-and-factions.md`; for Drinax itself see `../drinax.md`.

## How to use this file
1. Each profile: **UWP line** → who runs it → economy/features → factions/power players → **attitude to Drinax / to pirates** → hooks → set-adventure cross-ref (spoiler-light; the adventure file holds secrets).
2. **UWP key codes** in the header line: Bases (N=Navy, S=Scout, A=other/depot per gazetteer), trade codes, Travel Zone (Amber/Red), and any flag.
3. **Two published sources disagree on a few TLs** (PoD2 gazetteer vs Drinaxian Companion). Where so, the line shows both as `PoD2 X / Companion Y` — pick PoD2 unless the table later says otherwise. Never "correct" a number silently in play.
4. **Defences are real.** When the crew raid a defended world, run the listed force honestly via mythic-gm (see `../factions.md` "Quick reaction-to-piracy table" and `../../rules/piracy-raiding.md`). Imperial-trade-route worlds can summon a fleet; backwaters cannot.
5. Attitudes shift with play — track them in campaign state (`../../campaign/empire-reputation.md`).

## DRINAX — the home stage (brief)
**Drinax (0603 Tlaiowaha) A43645A-E** — Ni Ht / Kingdom of Drinax / gas giant. Starport A (charts only; see below), TL E(14), pop ~hundreds (palace only).
- **Full detail lives in `../drinax.md`.** Summary only here.
- The surface is a **bombed, lifeless wasteland** — the Aslan (Ahroay'if clan) glassed it in 885 after King Oleb XIV taxed the Imperium–Hierate trade at 20%. The only thing left is the **Floating Palace**, a vast grav-platform citadel of beauty and art hanging above the dead world, fed by the repurposed **Hanging Gardens of Drinax**.
- **Who runs it:** **King Oleb XVI** — boxer, swordsman, lover of strong reds; a buffoon's manner over a cunning statesman. He wants the **Kingdom of Drinax reborn** and loans the crew the **Harrier** (see `../the-harrier.md`) as his instrument of policy.
- The Imperial charts still list Drinax as **Class A**; in truth the port is a shadow. This out-of-date listing is a recurring plot lever.
- **Attitude to pirates:** Drinax sponsors the crew's privateering as statecraft. The court is the campaign's patron hub. **Role in the set adventures:** the campaign spine; see `../../campaign/00_overview.md` and `../../campaign/adventures/00_index.md`.

## TLAIOWAHA INDEPENDENTS — the old Kingdom of Drinax arc
*Aslan-held subsector; the trade-minded **Ahroay'if** clan controls/influences >1/3 of worlds and wants peace, but rimward clans (**Hkaaiheir/Hkaaiheir**) push to conquer the neutrals. The surviving Solomani-named human worlds — Asim, Clarke, Blue, Torpol, Paal, Pourne, Hilfer (plus lost Khusai/Kteiroa) — are Drinax's old vassals and the natural targets of Oleb's alliance-building.*

### ASIM — Drinax's breadbasket and first reconquest
**Asim (0503 Tlaiowaha) B867564-6** — Ag Ni / Kingdom of Drinax / gas giant. Starport B, **TL6**, pop ~5 million. Non-industrial agricultural world, near-Earthlike mass/atmosphere/water but "lacks the vitality of a true garden world."
- **Who runs it:** **Drinax** — Oleb's forces **invaded Asim ~10 years ago** ("jump over, blast the Asimen from orbit, keep shooting until they hand over food"), mainly for its produce. Before that it was ruled for ~200 years by **the Foundation**, a psychohistorical cabal claiming a plan to rebuild civilisation; life for the peasants improved only marginally after their fall.
- **Economy/features:** grain and foodstuffs — the larder that keeps the Floating Palace fed. Low-tech peasantry.
- **Attitude to Drinax:** subject/occupied; resentful peasants, a recently displaced elite. To pirates: defenceless backwater, but it's Drinax's own — raiding it is raiding the patron.
- **Threat:** the **ihatei staging at Kteiroa** name Asim as their most likely invasion target.
- **Role in set adventures:** **The Demon's Eye** (Drinax→Asim; palace intrigue, the missing Prince Harrick, the Foundation) — see `../../campaign/adventures/`. Spoilers gated there.

### CLARKE — the death-cult world born in nuclear fire
**Clarke (0702 Tlaiowaha) B899753-8** — (no trade codes) / Companion lists **NI** / gas giants. Starport B, **TL8**, pop ~80 million. Dense tainted atmosphere; was TL14 garden world before the bombing.
- **Who runs it:** the **Psychopomps** ("soul-guides") — a priest-hierarchy who are also the political and economic leaders. Theocracy, but **low Law Level** (citizens self-police; the religion implies consequences beyond death).
- **History/features:** bombed by Drinax's **Star Guard** in the Kingdom's last years — the old capital **Hiewad** destroyed, millions starved in years-without-summer. The survivors froze their dead hoping for revival; this became a **religion of preservation**. The dead are sealed in **black carbon monoliths used as building material** — the frozen dead massively outnumber the living. Goal: the **"Day of Healing"** when lost tech is rebuilt and all are revived. Progress is glacial; cynics note the priests **lose power if they ever succeed**. Ecosphere still slowly dying from fallout.
- **Economy:** mid-tech backwater, chronically poor. Small-scale, high-quality handbuilt small craft at the highport (10–15% premium). Belters in two thin asteroid belts. **More Aslan traffic than most human-side worlds** (jump-3 clan trade/passenger service stops here en route to Arunisiir). Cluster trade links to Drinax and Pourne.
- **Defences:** highport built to withstand attack (missile + beam + a handful of SDBs around the mainworld); the port operators **don't care what happens elsewhere in-system**. No gas giant — fuel from port or ice (some run secret wilderness refuelling spots on charted comets/planetoids).
- **Attitude to Drinax:** Drinax did this to them — deep, scarred grievance. To pirates: heavy traffic of small ships near the highport = targets, but defence boats respond there; outsystem is poor pickings unless you murder crews (draws a harder response). **Salvage** in the dead offworld colonies = desecration of graves to the locals unless sanctioned + accompanied by a preservation team; rival scavenger gangs have fought over troves out there.

### BLUE — the strategic crossroads with an open system
**Blue (0801 Tlaiowaha) B543487-B** — Po Ni Ht (Companion: Po) / gas giants. Starport B, **TL B(11)** (Companion treats society as TL10), pop ~70,000. Small world, thin tainted atmosphere, little water; was a high-tech jewel of Old Sindal that **strip-mined itself** to ruin (the Killai Depression may be a colossal abandoned mine).
- **Who runs it:** an **insular world government** that can enforce law **only at the starport** — the rest of the system is, by interstellar convention, **open/ungoverned territory**. Population focuses on **art and religion** (many faiths, amicable "preach-offs"; profanity fined as "cussing for charity"). Complex, firmly-enforced speech laws trip up offworlders.
- **Strategic value out of all proportion to its economy:** a **jump-2 hub** linking Arunisiir/Exocet (→Imperial space) to Torpol/Marduk (→Sindalian Main) and to Clarke/Torpol (→Hierate). The **Imperium courts it** — flag-showing visits are frequent; a full anti-piracy task force (light carrier + destroyers) recently stayed two weeks with officers going planetside, suggesting **the Navy wants a forward base here** (huge Credits + shifts the local balance; not everyone wants that).
- **Power players / complications:**
  - **Bulhai Freeport** — independent spaceport on a moon of the outer gas giant Bulhai (~20,000 pop, growing). Sells **military-grade weapons and high-end starship electronics openly** but runs a *just-barely-legal* business (careful not to fence pirated parts). Alarms Blue's government, which fears losing trade income to it.
  - **Aslan ihatei** have **toeholds**: a couple of gas-giant moons and part of a rockball are now Aslan territory — secure bases for deeper incursions.
  - **Unified Enforcement Service** — offworlder-crewed customs/police force Blue stood up to assert system control; backed by **locally-built cheap fighters** (Blue now exports fighters, crewed by mercenaries with Imperial advisors).
- **Attitude to Drinax:** old Drinax claimed Blue but never held it tightly; a potential ally if approached. To pirates: highport raids risk Imperial response; Bulhai Freeport's small-but-rich traffic = opportunistic targets that "hopefully won't trigger a large-scale response."
- **Rumours:** a secret **Psionics Institute**, hidden pirate bases, an Ancients site, a surviving Sindalian underground city.

### TORPOL — the water-world liberty port (Torpol Cluster capital)
**Torpol (0601 Tlaiowaha) B55A77A-B** — Wa (PoD2 **TL B/11**; Companion **TL8**) / gas giants. Starport B, pop ~60 million. Small water-world (ocean-covered), poor in resources; **prosperity is built on trade and facilitating others' trade.**
- **Who runs it:** the **Provosts** — merchant-prince city councils; officially **balkanised** but "unusually harmonious" (an advisory council meets at the **highport**, which doubles as de-facto planetary capital). **High Law Level, light/subtle enforcement** — kept pleasant for visitors; the **EPS (Enforcement & Protection Service)** is the only real combat force (sea/ice/air/aerospace units, paramilitary heavy hand in reserve).
- **Astrographic linchpin of the cluster:** jump-2 access between the **Sindalian Main (via Marduk)** and the **Hierate (via Drinax or Asim)**, plus jump-2 to **Blue→Arunisiir/Exocet→Imperial space**. Far more traffic than Clarke or Blue. **Torpol = the cluster's stopover and liberty port.**
- **Economy/features:** polar **floating raft-cities** on the ice caps; equatorial **stilt-city pleasure ports** (tourism, vice, surreal themed resorts; **Esafen** has the **Museum of Sindal** and pushes Torpol as the cultural heart of the Sindalian Renaissance). Inner asteroid belt mining (some Provost-owned; **Sternmetal Horizons** has a base); outer belt belters. Second inhabited world **Traefar** (cold rockball, ~2M pop, basic spaceport, the **Traefar Flotilla** of a few armed craft) — a good place to **recruit experienced-but-unqualified crew**, culturally hostile to pirates.
- **Defences:** Torpol Highport is **well defended** — missile bays (off-limits zones) + beam turrets across the hull, a fighter flotilla, and a single **(very) old Imperial Gionetti-class light cruiser** (non-jump, locally patched, still outmatches most regional threats). Heavy missile capability reaches near-station space but not far out-system.
- **Imperial interest:** strategically important to the Imperium, which **sometimes keeps a guardship here** and treats trade-route disruption as a threat — it "could flood the surrounding systems with cruisers until the pirates are dead." **Provosts post bounties** on those who attack their shipping.
- **Attitude to Drinax:** old Drinax claimed Torpol; the Provosts are pragmatic traders — court them with profit, not flags. **To pirates:** attacks **near the port carry severe risk**; out-system (inner-belt mining ships, Traefar trade) is a softer prospect unlikely to be read as a trade-lane threat. An **all-cargoes licence** is dear; "**middle-brokers**" wriggle restricted cargo through for 10–15%.

### PAAL — rich, jealous, important trade hub
**Paal (0805 Tlaiowaha) B564679-6** — Ag Ni Ri / gas giant. Starport B, **TL6**, pop ~ (mid). Rich agricultural world.
- **Who runs it:** dukes/kings of Paal — an **old association with Drinax** (Paal once supplied Drinax's produce). They **covet Drinax's technology** still.
- **Stance:** Paal (with Pourne and Hilfer) hopes to **negotiate equal-partner entry into the Imperium or the Hierate** rather than rejoin a revived Drinax — a key tension for Oleb's alliance pitch. One of the **most important trade hubs** in the subsector.
- **To pirates:** a prize but a populated rich world — expect defences and that raiding it sours alliance prospects.

### POURNE — paranoid, fortified, vital
**Pourne (0704 Tlaiowaha) A9B2887-A** — Fl (fluid/exotic-atmo) / gas giant. **Starport A**, **TL A(10)**, high pop. Named for the writer Pournelle.
- **Who runs it:** a **calcified, paranoid bureaucratic caste** convinced all offworlders are saboteurs. **Customs officials are notoriously aggressive and heavily armed**; the world bristles with **SDI satellites**.
- **Why it matters:** despite the hostility, its **stability + tech level make Pourne one of the subsector's most important trade hubs**. Trade links to Clarke and onward.
- **Stance:** like Paal/Hilfer, leans toward negotiating into the Imperium or Hierate as a trading partner. **To pirates:** very dangerous to approach hostile; the SDI net and trigger-happy customs make this a hard target. Threatened by the **Kteiroa ihatei** buildup.

### HILFER — the parched ex-vassal
**Hilfer (0804 Tlaiowaha) B55077A-6** — De Po / no gas giant. Starport B, **TL6**, high pop, desert/poor-water.
- Hot desert world, **once part of the Kingdom of Drinax**; the rebellion cost it the **water-reclamation tech** that held the desert back — a world in slow decline.
- **Stance:** among the worlds (with Paal, Pourne) hoping to negotiate Imperial/Hierate partnership. To Drinax: bitter that the Kingdom's fall ruined it. To pirates: poor pickings.

### KHUSAI (formerly Banks) — lost to the Aslan
**Khusai (0403 Tlaiowaha) A576655-C** — Ag Ni Ht / **Hierate** / Bases ACS / gas giant. Starport A, TL C(12).
- **Who runs it:** the **belligerent Hrakoea clan** — Khusai hosts a **major Aslan naval fortification** (orbiting base), commonly used by **pirate-hunters**. Pleasant world of blue fields and purple forests; Aslan legends of underground rock-monsters likely cover **weapons testing**.
- Former northern anchor of the Kingdom (lost with Kteiroa, which unified the surviving human worlds somewhat). **To pirates:** a fortified Aslan naval world — do not raid lightly.

### KTEIROA (formerly Stross) — the ihatei muster
**Kteiroa (0505 Tlaiowaha) C560164-8** — De Lo Ni / **Hierate** / Bases AIC. Starport C, TL8, low pop. Cold tundra/CO₂-glacier world; valuable **phosphorus** in the **Land of White Bones** (unexploited).
- **Who runs it:** the port is kept by a small family of **Outcastes** — a **haunt of pirates, smugglers and free traders** seeking a back door into Hierate space.
- **The clock that matters:** Kteiroa is the **last Aslan refuelling stop before the human border**, and **ihatei numbers are exploding** — a dozen five years ago, hundreds three years ago, **500+ ihatei and ~2,000 retainers/mercenaries** mustering this year. If the trend holds, an **ihatei invasion launches from here** — **Asim** the likeliest target; **Drinax and Pourne** should not rest easy. A live campaign threat (see `../factions.md` ihatei pressure clock).

## THE PIRATE HAVEN — Theev Cluster
*Three systems (Theev 0506, Vume 0505, Palindrome 0606) in the centre of Sindal subsector, forming a **jump-3 link** between the Sindalian Main and Imperial space. Notorious **piracy hotspot**; generally **anti-Imperial** (fostered by Theev's lords). The Imperial **Realgar** naval base (3 parsecs away) is too thin-stretched to clean it out. See `../factions.md` "Pirates, Theev & mercenaries."*

### THEEV — the pirate kings' refuge (the important one)
**Theev (0506 Sindal) A434500-F** *(PoD2)* **/ A434500-E** *(Companion)* — Na Ni Ht / **Travel Zone: Amber** / Base N (Navy, per Companion table) / gas giant. **Starport A**, **TL F(15)** (PoD2) or **E(14)** (Companion), pop ~500,000.
- **The fabled thieves' port — and it is real.** A dry, thin-atmosphere dustball (Mars-like); biosphere virtually nonexistent; ruined Sindalian cities buried under centuries of dust.
- **Who runs it:** the **Pirate Lords of Blacksand** — not rulers but a cabal who **preside over the chaos and profit from it**. Most command several ships plus semi-independent captains; identities often hidden behind "blinds"; assassination among them is routine. Many keep **legitimate fronts** (merchant lines, broker positions) and use piracy to **manipulate markets and ruin rivals**. **One iron rule: raids happen well away from Theev** (minor independents are even pushed to raid dangerous, Navy-patrolled systems to keep the Navy looking elsewhere).
- **Law = the Widows.** Black-robed, cybernetically-linked **vat-grown assassin enforcers** (possibly one entity in many bodies) impose the **"Law of the Lords"** in the Upper City and red-flagged zones. Elsewhere, the **"Law of the Streets"**: the *only* rule is that **possessing a weapon that can breach the city's environmental seals is death**; everything else is unregulated. Widow stat block below.
- **The Skull (highport):** a tunnelled moonlet in forced geostationary orbit, bristling with **missile bays + turrets** from fortified control rooms — **no warning shots**; arrivals must dock at the highport or present a valid downport clearance code or be **fired on**. Hosts **Kallos Shipyards** (builds up to ~2,000 tons; specialises in overhauls, converting merchants to disguised corsairs/pirates, and fencing **stolen ship components**). Clearance to the surface is rarely granted to outsiders.
- **Blacksand (downport, the only settlement):** canyon-maze city, dispersed clamshell landing bays.
  - **Upper City** — clean, "respectable" business district where piracy, murder and genocide are arranged in euphemism. The **Grand Hotel** has a deal with the Imperial Navy (cheap shore-leave packages) that conveniently keeps visiting crews distracted from investigating. Districts: **Cliff District** (mercenaries, arms dealers, even WMDs — "trained mercenaries" vs. Lower-City thugs), **Industrial Zone** (Kallos's on-planet outlet fencing traceable/illegal parts).
  - **Lower City** — lawless ramshackle warren under sealed roofs. **Asharden Camp** (refugee recruiting ground for desperate, controllable talent — assassins, spies), the **Scrapheap** (wrecked-ship dwellers selling rock-bottom stolen parts), the **House Without Questions** (shelter + will-do-anything-for-a-fee, don't ask how), the **Blood Pit** (arena), **blood gangs**.
- **The patron behind Theev:** **GeDeCo** (General Development Company). The corp's influence is considerable and can **redirect Imperial attention**. Why GeDeCo backs Theev is murky — harm rivals via piracy, control markets, access illegal naval hardware, balance the Imperium/Hierate powerplay, even (speculation) **engineer a Sindalian revival**; most likely simply **a tool to grow/protect GeDeCo profits**. (Keep this a slow-burn GM secret.)
- **Attitude to Drinax:** indifferent-to-hostile to a *kingdom* (order is bad for business), but Theev is where the crew **buy upgrades, fence cargo, recruit, and repair the Harrier** — a vital, dangerous service hub, not an ally. **To pirates:** safe haven *if* you obey the Law of the Lords and never foul your own nest.
- **Role in set adventures:** the campaign's pirate-world keystone; multiple adventures route through it. Truths/secrets stay in the adventure files — see `../../campaign/adventures/00_index.md`.

> **Blacksand Widow (enforcer/assassin)** — STR 10 DEX 14 END 12 INT 7 EDU 10 SOC 9. Skills: Gun Combat (energy) 3, Gun Combat (slug) 3, Melee (unarmed) 3, Athletics (dex) 2, Flyer (grav) 2, Investigate 2, Streetwise 2, Electronics (comms) 1, Deception 1, Medic 1, Persuade 1, Steward 1, Diplomat 0. Armour: Lightweight Polycarapace (+12) + Subdermal (+3). Weapons: Gauss Rifle (4D), Laser Pistol (3D+3), Stunner (3D). Gear: Enhanced Vision, Neural Comm (TL14). Vat-grown, cyber-linked to the hive. *Play her: never speaks; smiles like light off a razor; one sentence — death.* (Companion: Theev Cluster.)

### VUME — the alien-station system, GeDeCo highport
**Vume (0505 Sindal) B100477-C** — Ni Va An (**An = Ancients/alien site**) / gas giant. Starport B, **TL C(12)**, pop ~10,000 (in the station only). Tiny airless rockball orbiting far out from an **expanding K7 II orange giant** (frequent solar flares; the inner system was swallowed long ago).
- **Who runs it:** **Vume Highport** is a wholly-owned **GeDeCo** subsidiary (built to foster trade and to base exploration of the ruins; never sold for lack of a buyer). Run-down, geared to large jump-3 ships; revenue mostly from **fleecing passing Imperial crews** behind a façade of friendliness. The **groundside population is not part of the port** and has no stake in it.
- **Vume Station ("the alien complex"):** a huge modular habitat on/under the surface, built **after** the star became a giant by an **unknown alien race** (officially listed Ancients, but unconfirmed). Its ~10,000 human "indigenes" are **four mutually-incomprehensible factions**, each warped by a **different strain of an alien bacterial infection** that altered their brain chemistry — they cannot understand each other or outsiders; violence is sporadic. Vast derelict (airless) sections + pockets kept alive by **ancient machinery of unknown function**.
- **Salvage = cursed treasure:** scrapping components or finding working alien devices could set a crew up for life, **but** illicit salvage has triggered **disease outbreaks**, most expeditions **don't return**, and parties report finding earlier explorers' remains before going silent. Heavy GM-secret potential — keep the truth withheld.
- **Defences:** ramshackle but lethal — trigger-happy mercenaries, salvaged missile racks/beam turrets, and **two particle-accelerator bays from a scrapped Imperial destroyer** (the reason no pirate fleet has ever taken the station; a couple tried). A **"Trojan horse" boarding from docked ships** worked best for would-be raiders; shooting their way in did not.
- **Attitude:** least anti-Imperial of the three, but mercenary and unfriendly. **To pirates:** few good targets (jump-2/3 freighters here are well-armed and big); cooler pirate heads **discourage** raiding them (draws Imperial cruisers nobody wants overhead).

### PALINDROME — Lady Yemar's fief and clearing-house
**Palindrome (0606 Sindal) B433334-B** — Po Ni Lo / gas giants. Starport B, **TL B(11)**; on-planet town runs a **solid small TL12 base**, pop ~5,000 (Astrogo). *(Companion table truncates UWP to "B43334-B"; the PoD2 gazetteer gives **B433334-B**.)* Most habitable of the three cluster worlds — thin atmosphere, little water, sparse plant-based biosphere.
- **Who runs it:** **"Lady" Yemar**, a **retired pirate** crippled by an experimental bioweapon (all organs but her brain replaced repeatedly; she travels to **Theev several times a year** for astoundingly expensive life-support/medical care). Her one domed town, **Astrogo**, is her personal fief; ~5,000 followers/employees/hangers-on, comfortable, safe, insulated. Her former pirate associates form the ruling elite, police and military.
- **Economy/role:** a **clearing-house for stolen goods** and a waystation. Yemar profits by **buying/selling shares in cargoes** passing through the highport (mostly virtual — Astrogo residents own a slice of a cargo and take a cut of the sale, never touching it). The town has a **thriving weapons market and a weapons fair**, and sells hard-to-get illegal electronics (missile guidance, military sensors). Pirate cargoes clear through Astrogo if you know who to ask.
- **Highport:** one of GeDeCo's "central three," the **least successful — mostly abandoned/cannibalised** (officially Class B, "the port at Blue" of its day). Yemar **won't sink money into it** (buys cargo shares instead). The Thebus↔Palindrome run is worked by a **flotilla of jump-2 traders with doubled fuel tankage** (slow, heavily armed) — **pirates routinely pose as this flotilla**, so attacking "a freighter" here may mean attacking a fanged corsair.
- **Attitude to Imperium:** the **highport is openly hostile** (anti-Imperial propaganda fed by Theev's lords); **Astrogo is milder** — locals take individuals on their merits and host many **fugitives from Imperial justice** (Yemar wants no agents/investigators in her town). **To pirates:** dangerous to *raid* (the ships here bite back; Yemar's connections see attackers "turn up destroyed as a warning"), but friendly *to* pirates with the right contacts.

### Torpol Cluster note
Torpol (above) is the **capital of the Torpol Cluster** (Torpol + Clarke + Blue, all within jump-1), the longer-but-safer **jump-2** alternative to the Theev jump-3 transit — which is *why* Theev declined. Small **Imperial presence** here (intelligence assets, occasional warships "showing the flag"; Navy will hunt pirates with local permission). A more powerful **Drinax could be the barrier** that slows Aslan expansion up the Sindalian Main — Oleb's strategic pitch. (Companion: Torpol Cluster, ch.5–7.)

## THE SINDALIAN MAIN — Sindal subsector ruins
*The old imperial heartland, now a graveyard. The **Sindalian Empire** ruled from **Noricum** until engineered plagues and orbital bombing brought it down (~-1400). The **Oghman Clans** (rimward) are barbaric slaver-raiders who have reduced Marduk and Borite to pre-spaceflight tech. The NE corner is Imperial (**Realgar** naval base, **Albe**, **Cordillon**). "Most ships avoid the subsector if they have any sense."*

### NORICUM — the fallen capital
**Noricum (0408 Sindal) D8867BB-1** *(PoD2)* — Ag Lt (Low-tech) / **Travel Zone: Amber** / Base A / gas giant. Starport D, **TL1**, agrarian. *(World index/Companion give variant **D886... ** strings; treat as a wrecked TL1 agrarian world — confirm digits in `00_world-index.md`.)*
- The **former seat of the Sindalian Empire**, bombed back to the dark ages in the wars that ended it (~-1400) and never recovered. Today: **entirely agrarian, wooden huts, people deliberately burying the past.**
- **Death zones:** >30% of the planet is a **viral death-zone** — open your airlock without a vacc suit and weaponised plagues kill you in days. Ruined cities hold **mutated animals and last-ditch Sindalian security systems.** The Imperial Palace and Ministry of Justice are radioactive craters.
- **Rumour/hook:** the **Sindalian treasury** was supposedly moved and buried in the **catacombs beneath the old capital** in the final days — no grave-robber has returned.
- **Who runs it:** the **"Emperor of Noricum"** — heir to a hundred-world dynasty, now tending a vegetable garden in a hut, content. Men over 40 are sent into the blight to maintain firebreaks (a death-ritual; none survive). To pirates: nothing to steal but legends — and the danger zones.

### MARDUK — the drowned, regressed islander world
**Marduk (0510 Sindal) C577436-5** — Ni Lt / gas giants. Starport C, **TL5**, pop (low). Habitable, breathable, Earth-like — civilisation **wrecked in the late Sindalian wars** and finished off by **Oghman raids**.
- **Who runs it:** no real government — the population **broke into several dozen island clans**, each fearing outsiders, each with **its own language** and a **taboo against learning a neighbour's tongue** (a defence against slaver-raiders identifying useful captives). Lost the tech for ocean-going ships, so the enclaves are isolated.
- **Features:** the **only civilised haven is the GeDeCo-run downport** on an **artificial island** far from native archipelagos (large fishing fleet feeds it; recruits some locals). Pervasive **sea-god legends** — colossal squid-like creatures, benefactors in some tales, monsters in others.
- **Astrography:** the **jump-2 stepping-stone** between Torpol Cluster and the Sindalian Main / Thebus (via Marduk + Torpol). To pirates: poor pickings; the value is positional. Preyed on by **Oghma**.
- **Role in set adventures:** the patron-quest **"Sea Gods of Marduk"** (anthropologist Maris Enar hires a sub crew; deep dives, hostile islanders, an ancient temple) — see `../../campaign/adventures/`. Spoilers gated there.

### OGHMA — the slaver raider-state
**Oghma (0410 Sindal) B534754-9** — Ic / gas giant. Starport B, **TL9**, high pop. Iceworld that **retains an industrial base able to build jump-capable ships.**
- **Who runs it:** **tribal warrior-kings.** The **Oghman Clans** are savage xenophobes who deliberately **conquer neighbours by raiding their scientists, teachers, engineers and craftsmen** as slaves — kneecapping rivals' tech/industry over generations (this is why **Marduk and Borite are pre-spaceflight**). A warrior's worth = how many jump-ships he "rides" and how many soldiers he commands.
- **Defences/behaviour:** **Oghman Raiders** are a constant Sindal-subsector threat, despised by all civilised worlds — but they **know not to attack interstellar shipping** (the GeDeCo-run starport stays comparatively safe); they prey on **weak backworlds.**
- **Attitude:** hostile to everyone; no friend to Drinax or to "civilised" pirates. **To the crew:** a slaver power and a recurring enemy/foil rather than a haven.

### BORITE — the locked-down mining backwater
**Borite (0609 Sindal) E655796-4** — Ag Lt / gas giant. Starport **E**, **TL4**, several million pop. A yellow-brown stain of a world (boron compounds).
- **Who runs it:** the **Borite Continuity Authority (BCA)** — a decaying remnant of the old **Drinaxian bureaucracy** controlling all trade and on/off-world travel.
- **Features:** repeatedly **raided by the Oghman Clans**, who target scholars — so **writing is forbidden on Borite** except for a few heavily-guarded BCA members ("lest the Oghman Clans decide you are a scholar and steal you away"). Class-E port sees only a handful of ships a year, mostly traders dodging pirates on the **Florian route**. To pirates: almost nothing to take.

### THEBUS — the lion-hunt wilderness
**Thebus (0309 Sindal) B534320-7** — Po Ni Lo / gas giant. Starport B, **TL7**, pop ~ (low; ~8,000 in the one town + outlying farms). Bombed-but-not-quite-destroyed in the Sindalian collapse; **very thin atmosphere** (offworlders suffer altitude sickness/confusion at sea level — a real, sometimes lethal hazard the safari guides "forget" to mention).
- **Who runs it:** effectively no enforceable law outside the single settlement. The **highport is a private concern** — the **Thebus Highport Corporation** (GeDeCo-built, sold to investors) — which ignores the planet and lives off **Sindalian-Main trade** (Thebus is the **jump-2 entry/exit to the Main via Marduk + Torpol**). Ships that go straight to the surface are barred from re-docking (commercial leverage).
- **The draw:** **Thebun Lions** — big, skinny, thick-pelted mutant predators (from the war's mutagens) that **resemble Aslan from a distance**. Locals run **lion-hunting safaris**; a lion pelt is a "must-have Grand Tour souvenir," and **hunting popularity tracks rising anti-Aslan sentiment** (some hunt them *because* they look Aslan). Imperial cruisers bound for the Florian League now stop to hunt. Real threats (radiation/mutagens) are mostly **overplayed by guides** to justify their fees; the **thin atmosphere** is the genuine killer.
- **To pirates:** little to steal; useful as a wilderness bolthole / rendezvous / hunting-ground cover.
- **Role in set adventures:** the patron-quest **"Hunting Party"** (Aslan lord Ftahkaiw hires the crew to quietly rescue his ihatei son Ftahteas, downed on Thebus) — see `../../campaign/adventures/`. Spoilers gated there.

> **Thebun Lion** — Hits 30, Speed 16m. Skills: Survival 2, Recon 2, Melee (unarmed) 2, Athletics (end) 1. Attack: Jaw (3D). Traits: Camouflaged, Heightened Senses, IR Vision. Behaviour: Hunter/Killer. *Wary of humans; slinks away unless threatened — the "ambush" reputation is safari hype.* (PoD2 Sindal.)

## THE TRADE-ROUTE WORLDS
*The Imperium–Hierate dust-spice route and the Florian route run through neutral space; the worlds astride them are where pirates feed and where the great powers jockey. See `../trojan-reach.md` "Travel realities."*

### TOBIA — the sector capital
**Tobia (0805 Tobia) A544A55-F** — In Hi / Base N / gas giant. **Starport A**, **TL F(15)**, **pop A (tens of billions).** A world-girdling city; the **ducal seat and the sector fleet.**
- **Who runs it:** the **Duke of Tobia**, who is also **Sector Duke** — so isolated from Capital that he **effectively runs the whole Trojan Reach** unsupervised; all subsector nobility are kin. The Imperium's answer to "what is the Reach?" is "Tobia."
- **Economy/features:** high-tech, high-industry, **cosmopolitan** (welcomes outside traders, unlike paranoid Pax Rulin); earns **trillions/year** from trade taxes and escort fees — much of it squandered on luxuries or invested coreward rather than on the fleets or developing the Reach. The main Imperium–Hierate route runs through here.
- **Concerns:** critics question Tobia's **readiness for a prolonged siege**, especially with **Simok's civil war and Ayldem** threatening supply routes deeper in-subsector. Locals scoff — "this world could never fall."
- **Attitude to Drinax:** a far-off curiosity; the Imperium shows little interest in Drinax (the Pax Rulin governor barely thinks of it). **To pirates:** **patrolled Imperial space — raid here and the Navy hunts you.** A place for business, fencing legitimate goods, and information, not plunder. Key linked Tobia-subsector worlds: **Imisaa** (B520867-7, "Trader's World," last safe stop before the Hierate crossing, Border-Watch jump-6 couriers), **Fist** (B789430-C, the Imperium-end gateway of the Hierate route, asteroid monuments), **Empire** (B679334-C, the mothballed **"Trojan Reserve"** fleet + a plotting cabal of retired admirals).

### HRADUS — the Aslan-Imperial world
**Hradus (0304 Tobia) B54699B-7** — In Hi / Base S (Scout) / **Travel Zone: Amber** / gas giant. Starport B, **TL7 (capped)**, **pop 9B+ (~80%+ naturalised Aslan).**
- **Who runs it:** the **Esuaheakhahryoyulr clan** — *tsekho* (Progressive) Aslan who **bred the territorial urge out of their males** (status from contribution, not conquest), fled the Cultural Purge across the Rift, were savaged by the Glorious Empire, and finally settled here; **Hradus joined the Imperium** within a century. Now the **most Aslan-populated world in the Imperium.**
- **The Treaty of Hradus restrictions (~400 years, still in force):** technology **capped at TL7** (the clan can build to TL12); **all clan vessels must register at Tobia**; a portion of clan military forces is **always seconded to the sector fleet** — born of old Imperial paranoia that the clan was a trojan horse. Many citizens feel they've proven loyalty repeatedly (they've served as warriors and spies and built Aslan ships for the Imperium).
- **Attitude:** loyal-but-chafing Imperial Aslan; a unique bridge culture. **To pirates:** Amber zone, Imperial-aligned, scout base — not a target; potentially a source of Aslan ships/contacts.

### WILDEMAN — the asteroid trade port
**Wildeman (0409 Tobia) B431674-C** — Ic Na Ni Va / gas giant. Starport B, **TL C(12)**, pop ~ (mid). "Less a planet, more a big asteroid."
- **Who runs it:** essentially a **trade port** — its only real importance. On the main Hierate route (Pandora→Wildeman→**Fist**). Anchors the **Wildeman Cluster** transit (the busy route alternative to the Theev jump-3).
- **Reputation:** a **bad one among spacers** — wild tales of **ghost ships, mysterious breakdowns, psionic phenomena, even space-kraken sightings.** Atmosphere of unease; good for rumor-hooks. To pirates: traffic exists but it's a watched route segment near Imperial space.

### CORDAN — the baronial garden world
**Cordan (0401 Borderland) A895347-9** — Ni Lo / gas giant. **Starport A**, **TL9**, official pop ~2,000 (households only). A **pristine garden world** — breathable air, rich ecology, farmlands — "a real prize for whoever controls it."
- **Who runs it:** **three feudal barons** (officially a "representative democracy" — a creative fiction). Two-tier society: a few thousand **citizens** (baronial households, who hold all high-tech manufacturing) over a far larger mass of low-tech (~TL5) **subjects**. Was a vassal of **Arunisiir** until the Aslan overran that world; now stands "alone" — meaning **tacit Imperial backing so long as the starport stays open.**
- **Power players:** **Baroness Lux** — popular (she fought off local ihatei and bought goodwill with TL9 medical gifts she can't really sustain), now scheming to **destabilise her two rival barons** and dominate the world. The Imperium sends ships through Cordan regularly and **doesn't care how it's run** as long as the port works — but would act against anyone upsetting the status quo.
- **Astrography:** a stop on the Imperium–Hierate route (Tobia→**Cordan or Acrid**→Tech-World→Byrni). **To pirates:** Imperial-watched trade world — raiding the route here risks a fleet response; the *internal* baronial conflict is the real opening (intrigue, not plunder).
- **Role in set adventures:** **"The Cordan Conflict"** (Baroness Lux hires the crew to trigger a small, nasty civil war among the barons) — see `../../campaign/adventures/`. Spoilers gated there.

### ACRID — the company hell-world
**Acrid (0302 Borderland) AAC1388-D** — Fl Lo Ni Ht / gas giant. **Starport A**, **TL D(13)**, pop ~1,000 citizens + ~25,000 debt-slaves. "Makes the surface of Venus look like a balmy resort" — an **insidious atmosphere** that leaches into everything (constant seal-replacement and decontamination).
- **Who runs it:** the **PRQ megacorporation** (also runs nearby **Argona**). Mining + industrial-chemical extraction, profitable only by **crushing wages**: nominal pay gutted by "clean air/water" surcharges and company-store debt, so **few workers can ever afford to leave** (~25× as many debt-bonded workers as full citizens — slavery in all but name, propped up by a co-opted overseer/security buffer class).
- **Powder keg:** the situation is **explosive**. **Gera Hollis** — a demoted ex-citizen industrial chemist with a gift for leadership/manipulation — leads a workforce movement to **seize the world and become an independent state**, which means finding **orbital defences** to survive PRQ's retaliation. On the route (Tobia→Cordan or **Acrid**→Tech-World). GeDeCo has facilities here too.
- **To pirates:** Imperial-trade-route proximity; the leverage is the **labour revolt** and PRQ's grip, not casual raiding.
- **Role in set adventures:** the **Acrid worker-revolt** patron arc (help Gera's workers arm and free their world) — see `../../campaign/adventures/`. Spoilers gated there.
