# Genre Pack — Pirates of Drinax

> Use this when: setting tone and what "maximal honest consequence" means for mythic-gm in this campaign.

- **Genre:** space-opera **piracy** on a contested frontier — swashbuckling, opportunistic, and lethal.
- **Tone:** freedom, ambition, and hard choices. Lean into the romance of the pirate life — the score, the chase, the crew, the dream of a crown — but its brutality always has a price.

## Maximal honest consequence (genre-mapped)
Harshness scales to the fiction; honesty never relaxes.
- A lost boarding fight → **capture, maiming, or death**; prisoners spaced or ransomed.
- A botched raid → a **crippled Harrier**, a bounty on the crew, a hunted ship.
- Brutality and atrocity → worlds turn **Hostile**; the empire game slips away.
- Greed and noise → the **Imperial Navy** comes; Heat rises into a hunt.
- Betraying an ally → **lost reputation and Fighting Strength**.
- *Skill changes HOW the crew survive, never WHETHER danger comes.*

## Reward earned safety
When the player scouts, picks soft targets, wins with Tactics or Persuade, or talks a prize into surrender — let honest dice spare them, and don't claw it back. Good piracy is patient.

## Stakes vocabulary (what's on the line here)
**The ship** (the Harrier is life and livelihood) · **the crew** (NPCs with loyalty and morale) · **reputation & Attitudes** (the empire game) · **Heat** (Imperial and Aslan response clocks) · **the marque** (legitimacy that can be revoked) · **the throne** (the long dream of Drinax restored).

## Doctrine reminders
- **Disable, don't destroy** — a dead prize pays nothing.
- NPC captains, navies, and rivals **act to win**; roll their competence.
- The oracle's answer stands; a bad Random Event mid-raid is not rescued.
