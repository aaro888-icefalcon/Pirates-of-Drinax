## Three forms of commerce (Core p.237)
Travellers earn via three channels, each independent of the others:
1. **Passengers** — fixed fares, need staterooms/low berths + Steward.
2. **Freight** — fixed rate per ton per parsec, low-risk, paid on delivery.
3. **Speculative trade** — buy low / sell high; biggest profit and biggest losses. Smuggling is speculative trade in illegal goods.

A pirate crew uses 1–2 as cover income and a paper trail; 3 (esp. the black-market branch) is how plunder gets converted to cash. Carrying real freight/passengers also gives a legitimate reason to be in a system.

## SPECULATIVE TRADE — procedure (Core p.241–243)

### Trade Checklist
1. Find a supplier or local broker.
2. Determine goods available.
3. Determine purchase price.
4. Purchase goods.
5. Travel to another market.
6. Find a buyer or local broker.
7. Determine sale price.

### 1. Find a supplier / buyer (Core p.241)
Searching costs time and there is **DM-1 per previous search attempt on the same planet in the same month**. Use one of:
| Goal | Check | Time | Char |
|---|---|---|---|
| Find legal supplier/broker (and buyer) | Average (8+) **Broker** | 1D days | EDU or SOC |
| Find **black-market** supplier/broker of illegal goods | Average (8+) **Streetwise** | 1D days | EDU or SOC |
| Find online supplier/broker (TL8+ worlds only) | Average (8+) **Admin** | 1D hours | EDU |

Find-supplier DMs (Core p.241):
- Travellers' ship is armed: **+2**
- World TL 5 or less: **-4**
- + Travellers' highest Naval or Scout rank
- + Travellers' highest SOC DM

**Starport size bonus to find a supplier:** Class A +6, B +4, C +2 (D/E/X: no bonus).
On worlds regularly visited by trade vessels, finding a supplier is essentially automatic; low-TL worlds force you into markets/bazaars.

### Brokers (Core p.242 + Broker skill p.137)
- **Broker skill checks:** Negotiate a deal — Average (8+) Broker, 1D hrs, INT. Find a buyer — Average (8+) Broker, 1D hrs, SOC.
- **Local broker (legal):** Broker **2D ÷ 3**; flat fee **10% of gross**; grants **DM+2** on the negotiation (local knowledge).
- **Local fixer (illegal goods):** Streetwise **2D ÷ 3**; fee **20% of gross** (higher for very illegal/dangerous cargo); **DM+2** on negotiation. If the skill roll is a **natural 2**, the fixer is an informer / government agent / double-crosser — referee springs consequences.
- The crew may use **their own Broker skill** instead of hiring (no fee, no +2). The supplier/buyer on the other side is assumed **Broker 2** (may vary) and that skill is subtracted from your roll.

### 2. Determine goods available (Core p.242)
- Goods split into **Common Goods** (buyable on any world) and **Trade Goods** (need a matching Trade code).
- A supplier carries: **all Common Goods** + the **Trade Goods matching the world's Trade codes** + a number of **randomly rolled** goods equal to the world's **Population code** (roll on the Trade Goods table; re-roll 61–65 unless dealing with a black-market supplier; duplicates = extra quantity of that good).
- **Quantity available** = the Tons column of the table (roll the dice shown), modified: **Pop 3- → DM-3** to the quantity roll (can hit zero = nothing available); **Pop 9+ → DM+3**.

### 3 & 4. Purchase price + buy (Core p.243)
Roll **3D** and apply:
- **+ Traveller's Broker skill** (or the hired broker's skill)
- **+ the largest applicable Purchase DM** (from the good's Purchase DM column / world Trade codes)
- **− the largest applicable Sale DM**
- **− the supplier's Broker skill** (assume 2)

Cross-reference the total on the **Purchase** column of the Modified Price table → that % of Base Price is the buy price. Reject the deal and you **cannot deal with that supplier again for ≥1 month**.

### 6 & 7. Sale price + sell (Core p.243)
Selling mirrors buying: find a **buyer** (same checks/DMs). Roll **3D** and apply:
- **+ your Broker skill** (or broker's)
- **+ the largest applicable Sale DM** (for the destination world's Trade codes)
- **− the largest applicable Purchase DM**
- **− the buyer's Broker skill** (assume 2)

Cross-reference on the **Sale** column → that % of Base Price is the offer. Reject it → find another buyer or **wait a month**.
Rule of thumb: sell where the good's **Purchase DMs are low and Sale DMs are high** (i.e. a world that does NOT produce it and wants it).

> When multiple Purchase or Sale DMs apply, use **only the single largest** from each column.

## Illegal goods & black markets (Core p.242–243)
- **Universally illegal** goods (D66 61–65) are marked illegal and can only be sourced/sold via a **black-market supplier** (found with **Streetwise**, not Broker). They carry high built-in Sale DMs → very profitable, very risky.
- A black-market supplier stocks the **illegal goods matching the world's Trade codes** plus randomly rolled illegal goods: roll **1D** on the Trade Goods table and use **'6' as the leading digit** (→ rows 61–66). May include "illegal exotics" of questionable providence.
- **Locally illegal** goods (legal elsewhere): determined by **Law Level** (Core p.255). Their **Sale DM = (Law Level the good is banned at − the world's Law Level)** as a positive bonus when smuggled into a stricter world. Example: Military Weapons are banned at Law 3; smuggled onto a Law 9 world they get **Sale DM+6**. If a good is both universally and locally illegal, **use the higher Sale DM**.
- **Getting caught** smuggling → fines or imprisonment per Law Levels (Core p.255).

## FREIGHT (Core p.239–241)
Safer than spec trade: pays a **fixed rate per ton (per parsec)** from the Passage & Freight table, **paid on delivery**. A lot **cannot be broken up** — haul it whole or not at all. Late delivery cuts pay by **1D+4 × 10%**.

Lot sizes:
- **Major** = 1D × 10 tons
- **Minor** = 1D × 5 tons
- **Incidental** = 1D tons

Roll **2D on Freight Traffic three times** (once each for Incidental, Minor, Major) and apply DMs:
- Effect of an Average (8+) **Broker or Streetwise** check: add the **Effect**.
- Rolling for **Major** cargo: **DM-4**; rolling for **Incidental**: **DM+2**.
- Source AND destination world Pop: **1 or less -4**, **6–7 +2**, **8+ +4**.
- Starport: **A +2, B +1, E -1, X -3**.
- TL: **6 or less -1**, **9 or more +2**.
- **Amber Zone -2, Red Zone -6**.
- Each parsec of destination past the first: **-1**.

## MAIL (Core p.241)
Mail = sealed data drums + physical packages, **flat-rate, does not scale with distance**. To check availability, roll **2D** and add the **Mail DM**:
| Freight Traffic total DM | Mail DM |
|---|---|
| -10 or less | -2 |
| -9 to -5 | -1 |
| -4 to +4 | +0 |
| +5 to +9 | +1 |
| +10 or more | +2 |
| Travellers' ship is armed | +2 |

On a result of **12+**, the crew may carry mail: there are **1D containers**, each takes **5 tons** and pays **Cr25,000**. Must take **all or none**. (Armed ships are favoured for mail — relevant to a raider with cover.)

## PASSENGERS (Core p.238–240)
Fares are **per single jump**, scaling with distance and class. Need berths + Steward (see staterooms below and starship-operations.md). Number available cannot normally exceed the world's population.

Classes & requirements:
| Class | Accommodation | Steward needed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High** | Stateroom + 1 ton cargo each | 1 Steward level / 10 passengers | First-class luxury |
| **Middle** | Stateroom each + 100 kg cargo | 1 Steward level / 100 passengers | Standard class |
| **Basic** | Shared stateroom (≈2/room) or set-aside space; **2 tons spare room** | — | Steerage; 10 kg baggage |
| **Low** | Low berth; 10 kg baggage | — | Cryo; risk of death on revival |

**Seeking passengers:** roll **2D on Passenger Traffic four times** (Low, Basic, Middle, High) and apply DMs:
- Effect of an Average (8+) **Broker, Carouse or Streetwise** check: add the **Effect**.
- **Chief Steward:** DM = highest Steward skill on ship.
- Rolling for **High** passengers: **DM-4**; rolling for **Low**: **DM+1**.
- Source AND destination Pop: **1 or less -4**, **6–7 +1**, **8+ +3**.
- Starport: **A +2, B +1, E -1, X -3**.
- **Amber Zone +1** (people flee), **Red Zone -4**.
- Each parsec of destination past the first: **-1**.

## SHIP FINANCES (Core p.149, 154)

### Mortgage (standard)
- **Monthly (per Maintenance Period) payment = total purchase price ÷ 240**, repaid every Maintenance Period (4 weeks) for **40 years** (240 payments). This is the default driver of Traveller cashflow pressure.
- **Ship as career Benefit:** each time a ship Benefit is rolled, **25% of the original mortgage is already paid off**. The owner may either keep paying the full rate (finishing 10 years sooner per Benefit) or **re-mortgage**: deduct 25% of purchase price before computing the ÷240 payment, lowering each installment.
- **Ship Shares:** each worth **MCr1**; subtract MCr1 from purchase price per share *before* calculating the mortgage.

### Running costs interplay (Core p.154 — full detail in starship-operations.md)
Every Maintenance Period the crew owes, in addition to the mortgage:
| Item | Cost per Maintenance Period |
|---|---|
| Mortgage | purchase price ÷ 240 (varies by ship) |
| Life Support | Cr1,000/stateroom + Cr100/low berth + Cr1,000/extra person |
| Fuel | Cr500/refined ton, Cr100/unrefined ton |
| Repairs & Maintenance | (0.1% of purchase price per year) ÷ 12 |
| Crew salaries | Pilot 6,000 · Astrogator 5,000 · Engineer 4,000 · Medic 3,000 · Steward 2,000 · Gunner 1,000 · Marine 1,000 |
| Berthing | varies by starport (see Starports, Core p.257) |

Skipping maintenance: each subsequent period roll **2D + (periods skipped)**; on **8+** the ship takes a critical hit (Poor Maintenance table). Cover income (freight/passengers/mail) typically must at least clear mortgage + life support + fuel + salaries to keep a hull flying — the core economic squeeze that pushes crews toward piracy.

## FENCING STOLEN CARGO (pirate note)
Plundered cargo — especially contraband — is hard to move on the open market: it is often **coded and traceable**, so selling it legitimately invites arrest even when the goods are intrinsically valuable. Practical handling:
- Treat captured legal goods as ordinary spec-trade cargo, but the crew has **no provenance/paperwork** — sell through a **fixer/black-market buyer** (Streetwise to find; **fees ~20%+ of gross**, doubled-up because the buyer knows it is hot), or at a **friendly/lawless port** that does not ask questions.
- Captured **illegal** goods can only be offloaded via a **black-market buyer** (as above) — high Sale DMs make them lucrative if you survive the deal.
- A **fence** (a respectable-seeming broker willing to launder the take) pays a **fraction of open-market value** and takes a cut of the eventual resale. Canonical example: in *The Cicendai* score, a fence pays **MCr1 (up to MCr1.5)** for four containers of traceable pharmaceuticals theoretically "worth millions" (Companion: Selling the Cargo) — i.e. expect a deep discount vs. base value.
- Cultivating fences/contacts in many ports (as Assets) is core to a sustainable pirate operation; sometimes pass up short-term profit to build the relationship.

> **The detailed piracy economics — raiding income, prize/cargo value rolls, danegeld, splitting the take, and the Drinax cut — live in piracy-raiding.md.** This file covers only the legitimate trade machinery and the fence-discount principle.
