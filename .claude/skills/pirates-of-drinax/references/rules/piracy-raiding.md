## The letter of marque & the law
- King Oleb of Drinax grants the Travellers a **letter of marque**, permitting them to prey on "illegal" trade within the borders of the vanished Kingdom of Drinax. Oleb's aim: disrupt Imperial shipping for leverage to rebuild Drinax. (PoD1: A Pirate's Life)
- **Commerce raiding ≠ piracy.** Commerce raiding = attacking an enemy's *economy* (capture/destroy ships, cripple ports) as an instrument of policy; piracy = theft for profit. Legally, on most worlds, there is a difference — but a captured raider may still be **spaced as a pirate**, especially if no declared war/dispute is in progress. (Companion: Commerce Raiding)
- **Legitimacy ruse:** Raiders who want combatant (not criminal) status operate **in uniform** and **display a transponder code declaring allegiance before attacking**. It is a legal *ruse de guerre* to pose as a merchant/escort until the moment of attack. **A ship that opens fire without first declaring its true nature is treated as a pirate.** Anonymous raiders use a **blank ID code** (the spacefaring "black flag"). (Companion: Commerce Raiding)
- **Imperial penalty for piracy = death** (spacing). Elsewhere penalties vary but tend to be stiff. A letter of marque is **of little use to the captured** — the Imperium and Aslan Hierate are unimpressed by them; a single-world state may honour one, especially if a powerful backer stands behind the crew. (Companion: Getting Caught)
- **Who honours the marque:** Drinax (Haven) and pirate-friendly worlds. The Imperium and Hierate do **not** — to them the crew are pirates. The Imperial ambassador at Drinax (Thao Poloc) must never learn the crew are pirates.

## The core raid sequence (quick reference)
Run a raid as these steps (PoD1: Pirate Attacks). Each leg has a skill check (see tables below). Once you open fire, a **clock starts** — the longer you take, the likelier hunters arrive (see Distress Response).
1. **Pick a system & locate prey** — roll Prey Encounter (frequency set by traffic).
2. **Intercept** — close the range, match velocity (opposed Pilot at Short range).
3. **Attack / prevent escape** — cripple M-drive or dump fuel; warning shots; demand surrender.
4. **Confrontation** — hail and intimidate (Electronics comms vs target MOR), or fight.
5. **Board** (optional) — breach airlock, fight crew in zero-g.
6. **Loot** — supplies, cargo, treasure, equipment, vehicles, prisoners, prize ship.
7. **Escape** — reach the 100-diameter limit and jump out (jumping inside the limit = DM-4).

## Choosing a hunting ground
The **Imperium–Aslan Hierate trade route** is prime territory: cargo-heavy ships, few civilised worlds, fewer SDBs/hunters. Most merchants travel in **escorted convoys**; the prey are the unlucky few without escort or who lost it to a mistimed jump. Off the routes, pickings are thin (free traders, vagabonds). (PoD1: Finding Prey)

Trade vs risk by target type (Companion: Commerce Raiding Operations):
- **Targets:** outsystem assets/traffic (asteroid miners, ore transports — often unprotected), refuelling points on major runs (1+ jumps from support, hard to protect), backwater ports, lured naval forces.
- **Dangers:** highport defence zones (missile batteries, fighters), guardships on duty, escorted convoys, patrols, naval sweeps, Q-ship bait.
- Major routes run **highport → jump point → next highport**; navies concentrate there. Beyond the highport defence zone, raiding is much safer. In-system traffic is often softer than interstellar trade.
- **Stay mobile.** A repetitive pirate is a dead one. Hit a system too often and the next "freighter" is a Q-ship full of marines. Reward moving on; punish sticking to one hunting ground. (PoD1: The Pirate Life)

**Target types:**
- **Traveller:** non-cargo ship (scout, small military, courier, another pirate). Poor prey — little cargo; loot for parts.
- **Small Freighter (100–300t):** ideal prey — quick to loot, light defences.
- **Medium Freighter (400–1,000t):** good, but a big hold takes time to sort.
- **Heavy Freighter (1,000t+):** travels with escorts/fighters/guns; often more than one pirate can handle.
- **Rich Freighter:** roll 1D for size (1-3 Small, 4-5 Medium, 6 Heavy). Carrying especially valuable cargo — **roll cargo value twice, take the better**.
- **Liner:** passenger/colony/troop/tourist vessel. Passengers carry valuables, but boarders will be **outnumbered**.
- **Unusual Vessel:** yacht, science ship, X-boat, merc transport, survey ship — useless or a rich prize (could be a rival pirate).
- **System Defence Boat:** roll 1D — 1-5 classic SDB (fast, heavily armed, no jump drive); **6 = Q-ship** (disguised honey trap).
- **Convoy:** 2–12 ships split among Heavy/Medium/Rich Freighters and **armed escorts**.
- **Naval Patrol:** heavily armed navy ship, escort to full warship. Disengage.

## Encounter geometry & timing
The attack window is slim. A ship needs only a few hours to lift off and pass the 100-diameter limit, or to reach atmosphere. Within that window the pirate must close, cripple/cow the prey, match velocity, dock, loot, and escape. (PoD1: Encounter Distance & Timing)

1. **Initial distance:** referee rolls **3D × the mainworld's diameter** (use 1,000 km for Size 0). Arriving ships emerge outside the 100-diameter limit.
2. **Prey's heading:** roll 1D — **1-3** inbound to land; **4-5** outbound to jump limit; **6** stationary / other destination (moon, another ship). A ship elsewhere in-system with fuel can **jump out immediately** when threatened.
3. **Transit times:** use the **Jump Limit Transit table** (by planet Size 0–A and Thrust 1–6) for minimum up/down times — leaving (full-thrust burn) is faster than landing (needs a turnover/aerobrake to zero velocity at the surface). Sample anchors: Size 0 Thrust 1 = 1 hr up / 1.5 hr down; Size A (1,600,000 km) Thrust 1 = 5 hr up / 7 hr down; Size A Thrust 6 = 2 hr up / 3 hr down. (Full table: SOM / PoD1 p.15 — values scale with Size and inverse-square with Thrust.)

## The interception (chase)
The pirate must close range **and match velocity** for boarding. The prey evades; evasion lengthens its transit time. **Opposed Pilot checks** begin when range drops to Short or less. **If the pirate's Thrust > target's and range ≤100,000 km, escape is very difficult.** (PoD1: Intercept the Target)

| Action | Check |
|---|---|
| Locate a target (find it on sensors) | Average (8+) Electronics (sensors), 1D minutes, INT |
| Estimate a prize's value | Difficult (10+) Broker, 1D seconds, INT |
| Plot an intercept course | Average (8+) Pilot, 1D×10 seconds, EDU |
| Dock with a crippled target | Routine (6+) Pilot, 1D minutes, DEX |

**Masking identity (legal cover):** to keep combatant status, declare allegiance/uniform before firing; to stay anonymous, run a **blank ID code** or a doctored transponder (pose as escort/merchant until the strike). Best ambush approach (per Margaret Blaine, FIRST PRIZE): minimal drive & sensor emissions, hide behind a moon, catch prey while it is fuel-skimming a gas giant or with dry tanks so it cannot jump. (Companion: Commerce Raiding; FIRST PRIZE)

## The confrontation (hailing, surrender, morale)
Pirates want to **cripple, not destroy** (a breached hull risks the cargo). Most challenge the prey as soon as the first shot is fired, demanding it surrender and heave to. (PoD1: Attack the Target)

**Ideal damage:** critical hits to the prey's **M-drive** (cuts its Thrust, easing interception) or **fuel** (dump fuel to space → can't jump). Missiles/torpedoes "soften up" but stop once the hull is breached.

**Enemy Morale (MOR):** every ship has MOR like the crew does:
- Merchant: **1D+3** · Armed ship: **1D+6** · Naval/escort: **1D+8**.
- MOR drops **-1 per critical hit**, **-1D per critical of Severity 4+**.
- **MOR 0** → the victim **jumps out** (if able) or **surrenders**.

**Demanding Surrender:** Average (8+) Electronics (comms), 1D×10 seconds, SOC, **DM = minus the target's current MOR**. Surrenders are rarely unconditional — the merchant typically demands **safe passage for ship & crew** in exchange for handing over part of the cargo. Many merchants keep a few tons of **"sacrificial" cargo** to buy pirates off. A damaged-MOR ship may sacrifice cargo for safe passage even before reaching 0. (PoD1)

**Warning shots / called shots:** at Short range or less, direct-fire weapons (not missiles/torps) may make **called shots at DM-2**; on a successful **critical**, attacker chooses the location hit (aim for M-drive or fuel). (PoD1: Called Shots) — see space-combat.md.

**Fight-or-flee for the prey:** a cool merchant surrenders; a brave one resists at the airlock; some quirks override MOR entirely (No Surrender, Self Destruct, Deceitful — see Prey Quirks). Against a Naval Patrol or SDB the *pirate* should flee; destroying a navy ship guarantees extermination — surrendering at least means survival.

## Disabling & boarding
Board by **docking** (best — fast cargo transfer) or sending boarders across via thruster pack, grav belt, grapple line, or psionic teleport. Boarders force an airlock; most pirates **warn the target to open up or lose atmosphere** first — all but the bravest comply. Defenders who fight will be in vacc suits behind sealed internal doors; both sides then battle in vacuum. **Weapons of choice: cutlasses, snub pistols, short-ranged zero-g weapons.** (PoD1: Board the Target) — resolve crew fighting in personal-combat.md; ship damage in space-combat.md.

| Action | Check |
|---|---|
| Set a breaching charge | Average (8+) Explosives, 1D minutes, EDU |
| Move in zero-g | Average (8+) DEX, 1D seconds |
| Find the cargo hold | Average (8+) Recon, 1D minutes, INT |
| Open a ship's locker | Very Difficult (12+) Electronics (computers), 1D×10 minutes, EDU |
| Find the right cargo | Average (8+) Recon, 1D minutes, INT |
| Move a cargo container manually | Very Difficult (12+) STR, 1D minutes |

## Looting the prize (loot tables, times, values)
Loot in priority order; **each item type takes time**, and the clock is running. Times assume **one** crewman; more crew reduces time but **never below the listed minimum**. Estimate value first (Broker, above). (PoD1: Pirate Loot)

**Pirate Looting Times & Values:**

| Item | Value | Time (1 crew) | Skill |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supplies (spares, components, food, O₂) | — (keeps you flying) | 1D×10 min | None |
| Cargo, **by hand** | ≈Cr50,000/ton | 1D min/ton | Athletics (DEX) |
| Cargo, **by robot** | ≈Cr50,000/ton | D3 min/ton | None |
| Cargo, **by grappling arm** | ≈Cr50,000/ton | 1 min/ton | Electronics (remote ops) |
| Ship's safe (treasure) | Cr20,000 / 100t of ship | 1D hours | Recon (Difficult 10+, 1D hrs, INT, per 100t) |

| Equipment | Cr1,000 / crew member | 1D min/crewman | None |
| Prisoners | Varies (ransom) | 1D min/passenger | Melee (or stunner) |
| Vehicles | Varies (see below) | 1D×10 min/vehicle | Electronics (computers) + Pilot |

**Cargo composition (per freighter):** hold is **1D×10 + 40%** full. **Half** the cargo is a single type — drawn from goods available at the world it left, goods that sell well at its destination, the **Imperium/Hierate trade-goods list** (below), or one of the six basic cargo types. Cargo handled in containers: hatch is **one container wide per 50 tons** of bay; with no cargo-handling gear, each container is moved individually. (PoD1)

**Treasure / equipment / vehicles notes:**
- **Treasure** (cash, gems, metals, art, software, alien gear): in a hidden safe; ≈Cr20,000/100t of ship. **Cash/easily-sold treasure does NOT need fencing.**
- **Equipment** (armour, weapons, computers): Cr1,000/crew member; **must be fenced** — only a fraction of real value.
- **Vehicles:** a 20-ton launch = **MCr5** (≈Cr250,000/ton-equivalent!) — must be fenced but prime loot. Stow in your bay (fly it across: Average 8+ Pilot, 1D min, DEX; override security: Difficult 10+ Electronics computers, 1D×10 min) or dock it externally (risky: **DM-2 to jump per 10 tons** without a proper mount).
- **What's not nailed down:** fully stripping a ship = 1D×10 hours/100t; yields **10 tons of spares worth Cr5,000 each per 100t stripped** (Cr50,000/100t).

## Prisoners, prize ships & the fate of the crew
**Disposition of the captured ship & crew** drives both reward and **reputation cost** (see Heat below). Brutality raises Standing penalties, PRI, and victim morale against you; restraint extends a career.

- **Ransom:** take prisoners (esp. a **Noble** — worth far more) into low berths for "storage." Forcing a prisoner aboard = Melee, 1D min (or stunner). Ransoming is an adventure in itself. (PoD1)
- **Recruit:** a charismatic pirate may convince merchant crew to **turn pirate** (needs crew to fly a captured ship anyway). (PoD1)
- **Maroon / safe passage:** leaving ship and crew unharmed (only cargo taken) keeps you under the great powers' "must-react" threshold — the ideal low-heat outcome.
- **Spacing / massacre:** murdering prisoners = **Atrocity** (Standing -1D with the offended power) and pushes PRI up sharply; it also makes future victims **less willing to surrender**. Avoid unless you want the navy.
- **Prize ship:** to escape *with* a captured hull you need crew to fly both ships. **Selling a stolen ship is nearly impossible** — IFF beacons and serial numbers are woven into the hull's molecular structure, and few brokers have the cash. Options: keep it (cargo store, legit front, add to your fleet) or **donate it to a friendly system** to curry favour. (PoD1: Prize Ships)
- A pirate **admiral** with subordinate ships takes **10% off the top** of any loot those ships take. (PoD1: Upgrading)

## Fencing plunder & friendly ports
Pirates can't trade freely. They use **lawless worlds** or networks of **fences** at otherwise-lawful ports. In the Trojan Reach the Imperium does **not** control the starports; each world's port is its own affair. (PoD1: A Pirate's Life)

**Port attitudes toward the crew** — Haven, Friendly, Tolerant, Neutral, Suspicious, Unfriendly, Hostile:

| Port | Fence % (you receive) | Recruit | Risk Arrest | Risk Spies | Protection |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Haven | 30% | 3+ | — | 12+ | 3+ |
| Friendly | 25% | 5+ | — | 12+ | 7+ |
| Tolerant (high) | 20% | 7+ | 12+ | 10+ | 11+ |
| Tolerant (low) | 10% | 9+ | 12+ | 10+ | — |
| Suspicious | 10% | 11+ | 10+ | 8+ | — |
| Unfriendly | — | 12+ | 10+ | 8+ | — |
| Hostile | — | — | 2+ | 2+ | — |

- **Fence %:** share of *normal* value you receive for stolen goods sold there (e.g. Haven = 30%). Cash/easily-sold treasure needs no fence; identifiable goods (serial-numbered TL15 robots, etc.) and equipment/vehicles **must** be fenced. Bulk anonymous goods (wood, ore) sell anywhere.
- **Risk Arrest** (2D on arrival ≥ target → authorities try to detain). **Risk Spies** (2D on departure ≥ target → a spy reports your heading to your enemies). **Protection** (2D ≥ target → port defences help if you're attacked in its space). **Recruit** = chance willing replacement crew are present.
- **Hostile** ports fire on the pirate ship on arrival; faces are on wanted holograms, ship profile logged with every listening post and SDB.
- **Starting attitudes:** **Drinax = only Haven. Theev = only Friendly** (its shipyards build Corsairs; SDBs and locals defend pirates there). **All Aslan ports = Unfriendly.** Other ports by **Law Level**: 2- Tolerant · 3-5 Neutral · 6-9 Suspicious · 10-11 Unfriendly · 12+ Hostile.
- **Upgrade a port's attitude** by: making contacts / completing patron missions; spending money on improvements/upgrades/repairs/bribes; donating captured ships & equipment. (See empire-reputation.md.)
- **Laundering:** run a **"clean" tender** (a legit-looking merchant) to make supply runs and carry off cargo/prisoners while frontline ships attack — far lower risk than landing a known pirate hull at a regulated port. Operate from a hidden base/tender to avoid being sighted in local ports after a raid. (Companion: Tender Operations — full base/tender rules in starship-operations.md.)

## Heat 1: Standing with the Imperium / Hierate
Two great powers shadow the Reach. **Standing** tracks how each reacts. Both ignore minor larceny that doesn't disrupt trade, and each quietly *welcomes* attacks on the other. Push too far and they respond with violence. (PoD1: Standing)

- **Starting Standing:** Imperium **0**; Aslan Hierate **−5**.
- **Stealing Cargo:** **−1** per incident stealing >Cr100,000.
- **Infamous Incident** (big cargo/ship taken, "rock the boat"): **−1D**.
- **Atrocity** (destroy ships, murder prisoners, breach the code): **−1D**.
- **Interference** (attack Imperial scouts/couriers/registered convoys, damage starports/refuelling stations, disrupt trade): **−1D**.
- **Heroic Deed** (attack pirates, or hurt the *other* power): **+1D** with the pleased power.
- **Drift:** Standing moves **1 point toward its start (0 / −5)** per month with no consequential action.
- Rolls **combine**: e.g. steal millions + destroy an escort + gun down escape pods = Infamous + Atrocity + Interference = **Imperium −3D−1**; meanwhile the **Hierate +1D** (pleased by the Imperial setback).

**Effects of Standing:**

| Standing | Effect |
|---|---|
| 20+ | **Ally** — all that power's ports become Friendly; crew gain **SOC+2** in its territory |
| 6 to 20 | **Tolerated** — that power's ports become **Tolerant** of the crew |
| −5 to +5 | **Ignored** — no effect |
| −6 to −20 | **Irritant** — bounty of **Cr1,000 × 1D × Standing** (treat Standing as positive) |
| −21 to −40 | **Infamy** — a patrol is dispatched to hunt trouble (incl. the crew) |
| −40+ | **Enemy of the State!** — hounded by assassins to naval ships; any port not already Friendly+ drops **one level** |

**The Code of the Stars (unwritten):** take only cargo (or accept bribes to let a trader pass) and leave ship/crew unharmed → the powers turn a blind eye for months. **Kill crew, steal ships, or hit Imperial convoys** → you breach the code and *must be destroyed*. (PoD1)

## Heat 2: Piracy Response Indicator (PRI) — regional reaction
PRI is a single regional gauge of how seriously local governments take the piracy problem (tracks the Travellers' own activity; tracking other bands is optional book-keeping). **Starts at 0.** Decays **−1 every 2D weeks** to 0. (Companion: Responses)

**On each attack, check for a response:** roll **2D + Response DM + Circumstance DM + current PRI**. On **12+**, consult the Action table.

**Response (by starport class):** X −4 · E −2 · D 0 · C +2 · B +4 · A +6.

**Circumstance DM:**

| Circumstance | DM |
|---|---|
| Very 'polite', nobody harmed, only cargo taken | −4 |
| Minimal force, only cargo taken | −2 |
| Little force, life preserved, only non-essential cargo, ship left serviceable | −1 |
| Typical cargo raid, some violence/threats | 0 |
| Modestly violent, or important components taken leaving ship badly damaged/out of service | +1 |
| Violent raid, or ship taken, or hostages taken, or disregard for life | +2 |
| Significant casualties, ship and/or hostages taken | +4 |
| Appallingly violent or callous | +6 |
| Atrocity (e.g. nerve gas on passengers) | +8 |
| Agent in place downplaying the incident | −2 |
| Reputation as 'good guys' / justified (e.g. hitting a hostile faction's ships) | −1 to −4 |
| Reputation for violence & wanton destruction | +2 |
| Extremely bad reputation | +4 |

**Action table (2D + current PRI):**

| 2D+PRI | Action |
|---|---|
| 2-3 | Vague statement of reassurance/condemnation. **PRI +1** |
| 4-5 | Bounty **Cr2D×2,000** on "any proven pirate" (alive or lawfully killed). **PRI +1** |
| 6-7 | Bounty **Cr2D×5,000**. **PRI +1** |
| 8-9 | Loud condemnation + intelligence agents assigned to nearby worlds. **PRI +D3** |
| 10-11 | **1 small warship** (patrol corvette/merc) hunts the pirates **2D months**. **PRI +D3** |
| 12-13 | Ships totalling **2D×100 tons** hunt **4D months**. **PRI +D3** |
| 14-15 | Ships totalling **2D×200 tons** hunt **4D months**. **PRI +D3** |
| 16+ | Ships totalling **2D×500 tons** hunt **4D months**; a nearby world/shipping company becomes an **enemy** and petitions the Imperium. **PRI +1D** |

**Reducing & manipulating PRI:**
- **Stop attacking:** −1 every 2D weeks (responses get withdrawn).
- **Relocate** ≥6 parsecs from the last attack: on the next attack roll **2D + parsecs from last attack**; **12+ → PRI −D3** (problem "moved on").
- **Gambits** (frame another ship, set up an amateur pirate, decoy attacks): roll **2D + 1 per 100t of pirate vessels captured/destroyed**; **12+** starts a reduction. During reduction PRI drops **−1 immediately and −1 every D3 weeks** while no new attacks are made; max **−1D** per gambit. A *played-out* "we got them" gambit (selling the authorities a fall guy) gives automatic reduction if it succeeds.

**Response Force behaviour (1D, re-roll every 2D weeks):** 1 Half-hearted faffing (no real hunting) · 2 Escort duty (convoys) · 3 Search last attack site · 4 Logical search (centre-point between attacks) · 5 Random search (1D parsecs, 1D direction) · 6 Off doing something else entirely. Clever misdirection (false info, decoy raids) can send a force chasing a phantom.

## Pirate hunters & distress response
Any system with resources keeps SDBs and customs vessels; primitive worlds hire contractors or call for Imperial aid. (PoD1: Pirate Hunters)
- **Customs ships:** small, fast, lightly armed; first to respond; not built for a shooting match but can hurt a small pirate; call in SDBs.
- **System Defence Boats:** faster and better-armed than any pirate ship, but **no jump drive** and crews may be less skilled than a hardened band — a clever pirate can avoid/escape. (Largest SDBs are hollowed-asteroid supermonitors — avoid entirely.)
- **Q-ships:** disguised merchants with concealed weapons/fighters, hauled out when piracy spikes; jump into dangerous systems as bait. Variant: freighters with **booby-trapped cargo** (explosives, hidden marines, toxins, even nuclear mines) that triggers when stolen.
- **Naval vessels** (e.g. Gazelle-class close escort): outgun/outrun most pirates and **chase across systems**; may carry high-accel fighters. The bane of piracy — flee to a less-patrolled subsector. **Destroying a naval ship is the worst move** (guarantees extermination); surrender at least means survival.
- **Capital ships:** too valuable for routine hunting; appear only to **pound a discovered base to dust** or as political overkill.
- **Bounty hunters:** when all else fails, a price on the pirate's head/hull — usually collected by **another pirate**.
- **Imperial navy** hunts pirates rarely (training crews, or a truly troublesome nest) but then uses overwhelming force. **Subsector navies** hunt actively with small agile warships; pirates exploit subsector borders (bureaucracy delays cross-border pursuit).

**Distress Response (roll 2D when the attack begins; tracks how fast a hunter arrives):**

| 2D | Result |
|---|---|
| 5- | No response; roll again in one hour |
| 6-7 | A vessel launches from the starport to investigate |
| 8-9 | A vessel launches from starport **or** the 100-diameter limit, whichever is closer |
| 10 | A vessel in orbit responds; arrival in **1D+1 hours** |
| 11 | Vessel in orbit; arrival in **1D hours** |
| 12 | Vessel in orbit; arrival in **1D×10 minutes** |
| 13+ | Vessel in orbit; arrival in **1D×5 minutes** |

(Same DMs as the Prey Encounter table: Backwater/Dangerous −1; High-traffic/Secure +1; Capital/Naval base +2. Rapid Reaction quirk: **DM+4**. A **Screamer** prey broadcasts to *every* system it visits, spreading heat beyond the current one.)

## Getting caught: trial, the airlock pitch
Imperial penalty is death, but capture isn't automatically the end. (Companion: Getting Caught)
- **The airlock pitch:** to avoid immediate spacing, shout a **couple of sentences** (long speeches get cut short by escaping air) that make the captors **stop and think**. Requires an **Average (8+) Deception or Persuade** check (or Diplomat depending on the pitch). Approach sets the skill: claiming bystander status / noble protection = **Diplomat**; offering to reveal loot = **Persuade/Deception** (Deception if there's no loot). Last resort: offer to turn informer / give evidence. Referee adds DMs for reputation, etc.

**Airlock Pitch Factors:**

| Factor | DM |
|---|---|
| Hunters have ferocious hatred of pirates | −4 |
| Hunters have greater-than-usual hatred of pirates | −2 |
| Pirates have a bad reputation for atrocities | −4 |
| Pirates have a reputation for violence | −2 |
| Pirates have a reputation for leniency | +2 |
| Pirates have public sympathy (gave away loot / hit disliked groups) | +2 |
| Pirates have powerful connections that could trouble the hunters | +2 |
| Relevant skill | + skill level |

**Airlock Pitch Outcome (2D + modifiers):**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 0- | Death, with extra humiliation or torture |
| 1-5 | "Whatever." Out the airlock you go |
| 6-10 | Taken back to base for a formal trial |
| 11+ | **Offered a deal** |

- A **deal** is a new problem, not safety: lead hunters to the loot, help ambush another pirate band, etc. — ripe for betrayal; use the bought time to plot an escape.
- **Commutation:** death is commonly commuted to a long jail term for **information**; a pardon/immunity is possible for *really* good info (a pardon from one world doesn't make you a citizen elsewhere, and won't protect an informer from other pirates).
- **Other exits:** a captured trained crew may be **offered a job** (turn pirate-hunter or become an ambitious ruler's ready-made navy) — betraying such a deal earns no second chance. Beating a **show trial** (via bribery/intimidation, or genuine acquittal) is a clean way out of the game. A formal trial can be abstracted or played as an adventure — but capture **must carry penalties/losses**, not just a free adventure lead.

## Watch lists (Advisories / Warnings / Alerts)
The most likely reason a band gets caught (short of a dedicated task force) is the **watch lists** circulated to ports — out of date and often ignored in backwaters, but auto-downloaded when a ship enters port (so the crew *see* what's on them). (Companion: Watch Lists)

- **Advisory** — "might be a pirate." Carries **DM +1 to +4** (applied **against** the Travellers, i.e. −1 to −4 to their attempts to explain/avoid notice; positive for everyone else). Triggers: ship is a piracy-suited design (corsair, merc cruiser, **Harrier auto-counts**), frequents lax ports, or recently drew suspicion. Each occasion **+1, max +4**. Lasts **1D months**. A flagged ship is more likely to be boarded/searched.
- **Warning** — "known/strongly-suspected pirate" (includes ferrying booty to port). Carries **DM+6** (usually against the crew). May still berth if it hasn't caused local trouble, but expect to be **watched, escorted, or barred from leaving without escort**. Normally triggers an **arrest-and-question attempt**; avoid with **Average (8+) Deception/Diplomat** (DM−6 if the Warning cites clear evidence). Lapses after **D3+3 months** (sometimes permanent). Arrest ≠ conviction — insufficient evidence may mean release or a minor charge; you can be arrested almost anywhere, beat the questioning, and keep going... but not forever.
- **Alert** — specific to an incident/circumstance (e.g. a world seeking a ship's movements, a missing freighter reward). **No DM**; used by the referee to feed info or throw a curve ball.

**Staying off the lists:** engage in (or be seen to engage in) legit operations; suborn port personnel to **doctor** the lists (delete/drop entries in the mail relay); intimidation/bribery of the "you didn't see us" sort; or use a **different ship / clean tender** while pirate hulls do the attacking. Sloppy or careless crews get spotted faster.

## Random events (every 2D weeks)
Roll **2D every 2D weeks**; on **10+** an event occurs — roll **2D** on the Events table (Companion: Events):

| 2D | Event |
|---|---|
| 2 | A major power sends a task force: **2D×500 tons** deployed for **2D months** |
| 3 | A rival pirate band's successes make the Travellers look ineffective |
| 4 | Audacious raids a subsector away draw attention there (nearby region grows safer) |
| 5 | A "pirate scare" — **PRI +D3** whether or not attacks occurred |
| 6 | A major world redeploys naval forces — gaps appear, but other systems grow more dangerous |
| 7 | Rumours of a big score (e.g. hard-currency shipment between two major worlds) |
| 8 | A "gold rush" of small merchants heads to a nearby world — good pickings for weeks |
| 9 | A small merchant crew turns pirate out of necessity — clumsy, but draws patrols/warships |
| 10 | A minor pirate leader captured — **PRI −D3**; some merchant captains grow complacent |
| 11 | A rival pirate band takes heavy losses in a bungled raid/interception |
| 12 | A shipping trade-war erupts — both sides post bounties; authorised attacks won't count as piracy |
