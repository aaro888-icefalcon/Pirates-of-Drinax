## The seven characteristics (Core p.8)
Roll 2D per characteristic; assign in any order (or in order STR→SOC). Max for an unaugmented Traveller is 15. Characteristics can change during careers/play.

| Char | Name | Type | Governs |
|---|---|---|---|
| STR | Strength | Physical | Physical strength, fitness, forcefulness; brute-force tasks, melee damage DM. |
| DEX | Dexterity | Physical | Co-ordination, agility, reflexes; shooting, melee, balance, sneaking, Initiative. |
| END | Endurance | Physical | Stamina, determination, ability to sustain damage; long exertion, holding still. |
| INT | Intellect | Mental | Intelligence, quickness of mind; inspiration, improvisation, reasoning, unfamiliar problems. |
| EDU | Education | Mental | Learning & experience; trained responses, known solutions, recalling facts/trivia. Sets # of background skills (EDU DM +3). |
| SOC | Social Standing | Mental | Place in society; wealth/position, dealing with nobility, celebrity. High SOC may carry a noble title (see below). |
| PSI | Psionic Strength | Mental | Latent/active psychic potential; powers all psionics. **Not rolled at creation without referee permission** (rare). See Psionics note. |

## Characteristic DM table (Core p.9)
Apply to EVERY check using that characteristic. PSI uses the same table but its DM falls as PSI points are spent.

| Score | DM |
|---|---|
| 0 | −3 |
| 1–2 | −2 |
| 3–5 | −1 |
| 6–8 | +0 |
| 9–11 | +1 |
| 12–14 | +2 |
| 15+ | +3 |

## Characteristic checks (Core p.8, 58)
Roll **2D + characteristic DM + other DMs**, beat the target (Average = 8+). Referee picks the characteristic by fiction:
- Balancing on a beam → DEX. Brute force → STR. Long desert trek → END.
- Decipher an alien puzzle-box → INT. Recall trivia/common knowledge → EDU. Impress a guard with wealth/position → SOC.
- INT vs EDU overlap: INT = inspiration/improvisation/pure reasoning & totally unfamiliar problems; EDU = trained responses, known solutions, learned facts. Some checks allow either.

## How skills work (Core p.42, 58)
- **Skill level** = proficiency; add it to skill checks. Untyped careers grant level 1 (or +1) unless a level is stated.
- **Level 0**: trained at a basic level — adds +0 to the roll but **avoids the untrained penalty**. Background skills are all level 0.
- **Untrained −3**: no levels at all in the needed skill → **DM−3** on the check (reduced/removed by Jack-of-all-Trades; see skill list).
- **Specialities**: at level 1 in a skill with specialities, pick one speciality at level 1. Each further level may raise that speciality or take a new one (at level 1). Having a skill at level 0 covers all its specialities at 0; **Profession and Language are exceptions** — each speciality is learned separately. Most skills have no specialities.
- Creation caps: skill max 4 during creation; total skill levels ≤ 3 × (INT+EDU).

## Skill check procedure (Core p.59–61)
1. Referee sets **Task Difficulty** (target number) — Simple 2+ / Easy 4+ / Routine 6+ / Average 8+ / Difficult 10+ / Very Difficult 12+ / Formidable 14+ / Impossible 16+. If none stated, assume Average (8+).
2. Roll **2D + skill level + characteristic DM + other DMs**.
3. Apply one **Boon** (roll 3D drop lowest) or **Bane** (roll 3D drop highest) if circumstances warrant; they cancel and never stack.
4. **Effect = total − target.** −6 or less Exceptional Failure; −2..−5 Average Failure; −1 Marginal Failure (scrape a success with a cost); 0 Marginal Success (success with a condition); +1..+5 Average Success; +6 or more Exceptional Success.
- **Untrained = −3** (JoT reduces). Opposed checks: highest Effect wins; tie = standstill. (Full task rules — timeframes, going faster/slower, multiple tasks, task chains, working together — see `task-system.md`.)

Examples: Sneaking with Stealth 0 & DEX +1 → 2D+1 vs 8+ (no skill DM but no penalty). Stealth 2 & DEX +0 → 2D+2. No Stealth & DEX +2 → 2D−1 (DEX +2 then untrained −3). Carouse 1 & INT 9 on a Difficult (10+) bar-rumour check → 2D+2 vs 10+.

## Complete skill list (Core p.64–72)
Format: **Skill** — one-line use. *Specialities:* list. (default characteristic noted where one dominates).

- **Admin** — bureaucracy, paperwork, inventories, ship manifests; navigating officialdom (EDU/SOC).
- **Advocate** — legal codes (esp. interstellar law), oratory, debate, court (EDU/SOC/INT).
- **Animals** — care, riding, taming animals (rare on high-tech worlds). *Specialities:* Handling, Veterinary, Training.
- **Art** — creative arts; impersonation/forgery via performer. *Specialities:* Performer, Holography, Instrument, Visual Media, Write.
- **Astrogation** — plot starship courses & jump calculations (EDU). Standard jump = Easy 4+, DM− = jump distance.
- **Athletics** — augments physical chars; the gravity-environment skill. *Specialities:* Dexterity (climb/jump/throw, sprint, low/zero-G), Endurance (running/swimming/hiking), Strength (feats, high-G).
- **Broker** — negotiate trades & fair deals (Trade chapter); Find Buyer (SOC), Negotiate Deal (INT).
- **Carouse** — socialising, having/spreading fun, social awareness & subterfuge at parties (SOC/END/INT).
- **Deception** — lie, disguise, sleight of hand, cheat; oppose with Recon (INT/DEX/SOC).
- **Diplomat** — formal negotiation, peaceful contact, high-society protocol & addressing nobles (SOC/EDU/INT). More formal than Persuade.
- **Drive** — control ground vehicles (DEX). *Specialities:* Hovercraft, Mole, Track(ed), Walker, Wheel(ed).
- **Electronics** — operate (and at higher levels repair/build) electronics & ship systems. *Specialities:* Comms, Computers, Remote Ops, Sensors. (TL advantage = DM+1 per TL in opposed e-war.)
- **Engineer** — operate/maintain/repair spacecraft & advanced-vehicle systems (simpler gear = Mechanic). *Specialities:* M-drive, J-drive, Life Support, Power.
- **Explosives** — demolitions, breaching, assemble/disarm bombs (EDU/DEX). Breaching damage ×Effect; failed Effect −4 or less may detonate early.
- **Flyer** — atmospheric flying vehicles only (orbit-capable craft use Pilot) (DEX). *Specialities:* Airship, Grav, Ornithopter, Rotor, Wing.
- **Gambler** — gambling games; grasp of odds. Gambler 1+ gives DM+1 to Benefit/cash rolls (INT).
- **Gunner** — ship-mounted weapons in space combat. *Specialities:* Turret, Ortillery, Screen, Capital (DEX or INT by mount).
- **Gun Combat** — ranged personal weapons (Combat chapter) (DEX). *Specialities:* Archaic, Energy, Slug.
- **Heavy Weapons** — portable/large high-damage weapons (DEX/INT). *Specialities:* Artillery, Portable, Vehicle.
- **Investigate** — observation, forensics, detailed analysis; search scenes (INT).
- **Jack-of-all-Trades** — reduces the untrained −3 by 1 per level (−2 at JoT 1, −1 at JoT 2, none at JoT 3). No benefit at JoT 0 or 4+. No specialities.
- **Language** — read/write a specific language; Language 0 = a few phrases in common tongues (EDU). *Specialities:* one per language, e.g. Galanglic, Vilani, Zdetl, Oynprith, Trokh, Gvegh. (Each learned separately.)
- **Leadership** — direct, inspire, rally allies; Leadership combat action (SOC).
- **Mechanic** — maintain/repair most equipment (not build/alter; advanced gear needs Engineer) (INT/EDU). Covers all equipment types.
- **Medic** — first aid, triage, diagnosis, surgery, long-term care (EDU). First Aid restores characteristic points = Effect; see Injury & Recovery.
- **Melee** — hand-to-hand combat & melee weapons (STR/DEX). *Specialities:* Unarmed, Blade, Bludgeon, Natural.
- **Navigation** — planetside courses & finding directions (ground counterpart of Astrogation) (INT/EDU).
- **Persuade** — informal fast-talk, bargain, wheedle, bluff, bribe, intimidate (INT/SOC/STR). Casual version of Diplomat.
- **Pilot** — fly spacecraft (Spacecraft Operations) (DEX). *Specialities:* Small Craft (<100t), Spacecraft (100–5,000t), Capital Ships (>5,000t).
- **Profession** — produce useful goods/services; earn Cr250 × Effect per month on a supporting world (varies). *Specialities (examples):* Belter, Biologicals, Civil Engineering, Construction, Hydroponics, Polymers — each learned individually; no free level-0 spread.
- **Recon** — scout dangers, spot threats/out-of-place people; opposes Stealth/Deception (INT).
- **Science** — knowledge + practical application. *Specialities:* Archaeology, Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry, Cosmology, Cybernetics, Economics, Genetics, History, Linguistics, Philosophy, Physics, Planetology, Psionicology, Psychology, Robotics, Sophontology, Xenology.
- **Seafarer** — watercraft & ocean travel. *Specialities:* Ocean Ships, Personal, Sail, Submarine.
- **Stealth** — stay unseen/unheard/unnoticed; opposed by Recon (DEX).
- **Steward** — serve/care for nobles & high-class passengers; cooking, tailoring, address; required for high passage (EDU/SOC).
- **Streetwise** — urban environments, power structures, criminal contacts/fixers (INT).
- **Survival** — wilderness survival, shelter, hunt/trap, recognise plants & animals (wild counterpart of Streetwise) (INT/EDU).
- **Tactics** — tactical planning from squad to fleet (Combat chapter). *Specialities:* Military, Naval.
- **Vacc Suit** — wear/operate vacc & environmental suits and battle dress (EDU). Usually no roll needed; lacking the required level = DM−2 to all suit checks per missing level.

## Psionics quick note (Core p.228)
- **PSI** is the characteristic; uses the standard Characteristic DM table. PSI 0 = no psionic potential. Determine with referee permission: roll 2D − (terms served).
- Psionic powers cost PSI points (temporary reduction); the PSI DM for psionic skill checks uses **current** PSI, so powers get harder as PSI drops. Recover 1 PSI/hour, starting 3 hours after last use.
- Talents (each a separate skill): Telepathy, Clairvoyance, Telekinesis, Awareness, Teleportation. (Full subsystem out of scope here — see Psionics chapter.)
