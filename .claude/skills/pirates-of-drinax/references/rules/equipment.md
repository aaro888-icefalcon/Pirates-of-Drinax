## How to read the tables (Core p.76)
- **TL** = Tech Level item first appears; buying below that TL is unusual.
- **Range** = effective range. Short range (≤¼ Range): DM+1. Long range (>Range, <2×): DM−2. Extreme (>2×, <4×): DM−4. Beyond 100 m is auto-Extreme unless the weapon has **Scope** and the shooter aims. Melee = within 2 m.
- **Damage** in dice (e.g. 3D). On a hit, add the attack's Effect to damage; melee also adds attacker's STR DM. `DD` (e.g. 2DD) = **Destructive** → multiply total ×10 (crushes small vehicles).
- **Mag / Mag Cost** = shots per load / cost of a spare magazine or power pack.
- Damage always inflicts ≥1 point regardless of Protection. **AP X** ignores X points of Protection.

## Weapon traits glossary (Core p.79)
- **AP X** — ignores X points of Protection. Spacecraft-scale targets ignore AP unless the weapon is also spacecraft-scale.
- **Auto X** — fires 3 modes (aiming gives no benefit): **Single** = normal; **Burst** = +X to damage, uses X rounds; **Full Auto** = X separate attacks (targets within 6 m of each other), uses 3X rounds. Cannot combine with Scope/aim in the same action.
- **Blast X** — hits every target within X metres; no Dodge (may dive for cover); cover between target and blast centre helps.
- **Bulky** — needs STR 9+; else attack DM = (STR DM − 1), i.e. negative.
- **Very Bulky** — needs STR 12+; else attack DM = (STR DM − 2).
- **Destructive (DD)** — multiply total damage rolled ×10.
- **Radiation** — target takes 2D×20 rads (×3 = 2D×60 if spacecraft-scale); if Destructive, everyone within 10 m also takes 2D×20 rads.
- **Scope** — vision-enhancing sight; ignores the auto-Extreme-beyond-100 m rule if the shooter aims first. Can't be used with Auto/aim conflicts.
- **Smart** — guided rounds; attack DM = TL difference vs target's TL (min +1, max +6); can home on armour, vehicles, comms, computers, weapons.
- **Stun** — non-lethal; damage to END only (after Protection); END→0 = incapacitated for (damage − END) rounds; fully healed by 1 h rest.
- **Zero-G** — little/no recoil; usable in low/zero gravity with no Athletics (dexterity) check. (Most energy weapons, snub/accelerator slug guns.)

## Armour (Core p.100)
Protection reduces damage by its value. Normally only one armour at a time (resolve outer→inner where layering is allowed). Some armour needs a **Required Skill** — each missing level = DM−1 to all checks in it (no skill at all = the usual −3). **Rad** = rads subtracted per exposure. ## Armour upgrades & options (Core p.101–105)
- **Eye Protection** (TL6, Cr50) — auto-included TL9+; vital vs laser-blinding at TL9.
- **Self-Sealing** (TL11, Cr2000) — seals minor breaches/rips (not battle damage); any armour except ablat.
- **Computer Weave** — adds Computer/0 (TL10, Cr500), /1 (TL11, Cr1000), /2 (TL13, Cr5000) to armour lacking a computer.
- **Medikit (internal)** (TL10, Cr5000) — auto first-aid at END 0 (Medic 3), can inject Fast Drug; TL11 (Cr10000) also injects combat drugs/metabolic accelerators. Fits combat armour, battle dress, vacc suit.
- **Extended Life Support** (TL10, Cr10000) — 18 h oxygen on any life-support suit.
- **Magnetic Grapples** (TL8, Cr100) — walk on a hull without artificial gravity. Boarding staple.
- **Thruster Pack** (TL9, Cr2000) — manoeuvre in microgravity (Athletics check); long-range versions TL12 Cr14000 / TL14 Cr20000 give 0.1g for 48 h.
- **Chameleon IR** (TL12, Cr5000) / **Vislight** (TL13, Cr50000) — DM−4 to detect (sensors / naked eye).
- **Grav Assist** (TL12, Cr110000) — adds grav-belt flight to combat armour/battle dress only.
- **Vacc Suit Accessory Pack** (CSC) — helmet + semi-rigid torso, **Protection +6** to suits with little armour (micrometeorite/rad).

## Vacc suits & boarding suits
- Combat armour, battle dress, vacc suits, HEV suits all give **6 h life support** (extendable to 18 h). HEV suit handles toxic/underwater/extreme-rad/temperature.
- Protective suits at TL8+ include a built-in **Electronics Suite** (comm, scope/HUD, sensors scaling with TL) — see CSC table; the suite's Scope applies to any weapon the wearer fires.
- A sword opening a self-sealing vacc suit vents the wearer to vacuum — the pirate's argument for blades over guns in a hull-pressurised fight.

## Comms (Core p.108)
- **Commdot** (TL10, Cr10) — tiny hands-free relay, range a few metres; doubles as improvised bug/throat mic.
- **Mobile Comm** — handset/computer/camera; short-range, rides planetary networks. TL6 Cr50 (audio) → TL10 Cr500 (multi-data, Computer/1).
- **Transceiver (stand-alone two-way radio)** — works without a network. Needs **500 km range to reach orbit reliably**. Radio TL8 50 km Cr75; TL9 500 km Cr500; TL12 10,000 km Cr1000 (Computer/0). Laser transceiver TL9 500 km Cr2500 (tight-beam, hard to intercept).
- **Bug** (TL5+) — hidden mic/camera; TL options add Audio/Visual/Data/Bioscan/Computer; smaller = shorter range. TL5 audio Cr50 → TL15 full Cr500.
