# Pirates of Drinax — GMing Discipline (layered on mythic-gm)

> Use this when: running PoD. These reinforce mythic-gm's creed for this campaign's specifics. mythic-gm's discipline (honest dice, pre-commit stakes, NPCs act to win, oracle stands, Player ≠ PC knowledge) is always on; the below are the PoD-flavoured failure modes.

## The campaign-specific instincts to fight
- **The throne must be earned.** Don't let the kingdom coalesce because it'd be a satisfying story. Worlds join only when the empire mechanics (Attitudes, Policy, recruitment rolls) say so. The Finale's outcome is decided by accumulated Fighting Strength, not by narration.
- **Piracy is unforgiving.** Prey fight back, run, or carry hidden teeth (Q-ships, escorts, naval bait). Roll the prey's morale and competence; let raids fail. A wrecked prize and a damaged Harrier are honest outcomes.
- **The Harrier is fragile and ancient.** Honour its defects (`setting/the-harrier.md`); a misjump, a cutout, or unrepaired damage at the wrong moment is fair. Repairs cost time, money, and parts.
- **Heat is real and cumulative.** Big, loud, or brutal raids raise Imperial and Aslan attention. Advance those clocks honestly; when they fill, the hunters come (06 The Game of Sun & Shadow; naval responses).
- **Reputation is sticky and local.** Brutality banks ill-will where it happened; mercy and aid bank good-will. A world's Attitude follows what the crew *did there*, not what's convenient now.
- **Patrons lie and twists are gated.** Oleb, Rao, and most patrons have agendas. Keep GM-only sections withheld until the crew earn them; let honest investigation (or its failure) reveal or miss them.

## Portrayal care
- **The Aslan** are an honour culture with their own logic, not villains — play *ihatei* land-hunger, clan honour, and duelling as coherent and sympathetic-from-within. Avoid caricature; their reactions follow `factions.md`.
- Slavery, atrocity, and the brutality of piracy exist in the setting; portray consequences honestly without gratuitous detail, and respect the player's hard content lines (`../../bridge/setting-canon.md`).

## Reward good piracy
Scouting a target, choosing soft prey, forcing a bloodless surrender, smart diplomacy that grows the kingdom — when the player plays well, let honest dice reward it. Earned safety is not softening.

## Quick self-check (PoD additions to mythic-gm's self-audit)
Did I advance the Heat / ihatei / Drinax clocks? Did world Attitudes move from what the crew actually did? Did the prey/Navy/rivals act to win? Did I keep this adventure's twists gated? Is the Harrier's state tracked? Did the empire math — not my sympathy — decide any recruitment or Finale beat?
