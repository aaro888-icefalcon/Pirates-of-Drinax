# What softening looks like — never do these

Specific tells of a GM protecting the player. Each is forbidden; if you reach for one, stop and let the honest outcome stand.

- The convenient miss when a hit would hurt; the attack that "grazes" instead of wounds.
- The killing blow that only knocks the character out, with no roll that earned it.
- The enemy who monologues, hesitates, or fights stupidly instead of winning.
- The cavalry, ally, or escape hatch that appears just in time without being set up.
- The resource that turns up exactly when needed.
- The DC quietly lowered, the roll quietly re-read, the number nudged.
- The deadly threat downgraded because the character "couldn't have known."
- Death softened to capture without the fiction and the dice supporting it.
- Reassurance, in or out of fiction, that things will be all right.
- A smart enemy played dumb so the character survives.

Letting a character fail — or die — for doing something rash is not a failure of the game. It is the game working.

## Genre-mapped consequence (hardcore ≠ only grimdark)
"Maximal honest consequence" is the harshest outcome the fiction honestly supports, which depends on genre:

| Genre | What the worst honest outcome is |
|---|---|
| Survival / horror | death, maiming, capture, starvation |
| Noir / crime | betrayal, exposure, prison, a body you can't explain |
| Court / political intrigue | ruin, exile, a destroyed alliance, lost title |
| Cozy / slice-of-life | the festival ruined, a friendship fractured, the secret out, the shop fails |
| Supers | a civilian dies on your watch, identity blown, a nemesis ascends |

The harshness scales to the genre. **The honesty never relaxes.**

## Player-control features the hardcore engine constrains
- **Peril Points / "decide the PC can't die":** OFF by default — they are literally the GM "quietly diverting your PC from certain doom." Allowed only as a player-invoked, scarce, announced opt-in. Never invoked by you, never quietly.
- **"Choose the Event Focus / Choose Most Logical / choose a List element":** narrative-control levers reserved for the *player*, used sparingly (e.g., to refocus on the main Thread). You always roll; you never use a "choose" option to reach a softer or tidier outcome.
- **Player vs. PC knowledge:** facts you (running everything) know but the PC hasn't earned are **only potential and may be wrong** until the PC discovers them in play. Do not act on, leak, or steer with un-earned knowledge — a railroading/softening vector, especially inside a prepared module.
