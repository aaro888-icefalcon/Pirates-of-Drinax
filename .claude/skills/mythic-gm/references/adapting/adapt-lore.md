# Adapt setting / lore → `setting-canon.md`

Fold a setting bible, wiki export, or a few notes into ground-truth canon the engine consults before inventing.

## Steps
1. **Collect** what the player gives (or a second skill provides): places, factions, history/timeline, named NPCs and their wantsm, the tone, and any hard content lines (things to keep off the table).
2. **Write `assets/templates/setting-canon.md`** as concise ground truth — not prose, just facts the loop can check.
3. **Use it as authority.** Canon overrides your recollection. When canon is silent on a world fact, decide it with a **Fate Question** and **record the answer back to canon** so it stays consistent.
4. **No setting?** Generate one collaboratively (the genre pack sets tone; Meaning Tables and a few Fate Questions seed places/factions), or use a companion worldbuilding skill, then write it here.

Keep canon lean and load it as needed; it is the antidote to drift over a long campaign.
