# Setting Canon — Pirates of Drinax (Trojan Reach)

> Ground truth. Overrides recollection. The engine consults this **before inventing**; when canon is silent, decide by a **Fate Question** and **record the answer back here** so it stays consistent. Lean by design — drill into the detailed reference files (see **Pointers**) for crunch, full UWPs, secrets, and stat blocks.

## Premise
- The crew are **privateers of Drinax**: King Oleb XVI loans them the **Harrier** (TL15 commerce raider, orig. *V'Hurg*; J-2, Thrust 6, particle barbette) and a **letter of marque** to prey on Imperium–Aslan trade through the Reach.
- The plan (Princess Rao's idea): make the trade route so unsafe that the great powers find it **cheaper to recognise a reborn Kingdom of Drinax** as the route's protector than to crush the upstarts. Raid → win worlds over → restore Drinax (or carve your own pirate empire, get rich, or hang).
- **The marque saves no one if captured — unless Drinax is restored.** Restore the kingdom and the crew's crimes are retroactively legal. Saving Drinax literally saves their necks. Oleb takes **10% of all takings**; refusing forfeits the marque.

## The Reach (where this happens)
- **Trojan Reach** = a 16-subsector wild frontier (the "Outrim Void") between the **Third Imperium** (coreward/trailing) and the advancing **Aslan Hierate** (rimward/spinward). Both powers hold under half the sector; the rest is weak independents, Sindalian ruins, the Florian League, the renegade Glorious Empire, and pirate haunts.
- **The lifeline:** the **dust-spice trade route**, Fist (Tobia) → Tyokh (Tlaiowaha), ~7 jump-2 jumps / ~55–60 days. Imperial escorts run only as far as **Acrid**; Aslan (Ahroay'if) escorts take over at **Tech-World**. Argona & Sperle are prime ambush spots. A second, longer route runs to the **Florian League**.
- **Key regions:** **Tlaiowaha subsector** (home; Drinax + the old Kingdom's independent worlds, Aslan-held), **Sindal subsector** (the Sindalian Main ruins + Theev the pirate haven), the **Borderland** (the contested trade corridor + GeDeCo). Imperial bloc = Pax Rulin, Gazulin, Tobia.
- **Current year ≈ 1105 Imperial** (Aslan date 3637). The sector "stands on a knife edge." Major war brews in the Spinward Marches over the next decade but barely touches the Reach's thin patrols — it *lowers the perceived* Imperial threat (exploitable), but one cruiser squadron from Tobia could still flatten any minor power.

## Powers / Factions (one line each — want + stance toward Drinax/pirates)
- **Third Imperium** — the lazy giant; holds 3 subsectors behind Pax Rulin & Tobia. WANTS: hold the line cheaply, cultivate buffer client states (often via GeDeCo). Stance: indifferent to Drinax; **does NOT recognise its marque (a "privateer" = a pirate)**; tolerates low-level piracy until a megacorp/escorted convoy bleeds → Navy sortie from Pax Rulin sweeps the Borderlands for months.
- **Aslan Hierate** — not a monolith; dozens of feuding clans, each with its own navy, reacting fast (honour culture; *territory* = SOC; run NPCs through the honour ladder + reaction table). WANTS: territory/expansion (clan by clan). Stance: **bombed Drinax in 885** (Ahroay'if-led) and will crush a risen Drinax again; **raid Aslan shipping → relentless honour vendetta**; raid *Imperial* shipping → Aslan quietly pleased.
- **Aslan *ihatei*** (the migration pressure — a clock) — landless second-sons who must **win their own land**, flocking to the border to conquer. WANTS: a world to take. Stance: a swarm a charismatic warlord could point at **Drinax/Asim** (mustering at Kteiroa; the *Ihatei!* 25-week clock). The campaign's standing existential threat.
- **Florian League** (the Florani — Ancient-engineered Feskals + Barnai) — TL15 via Ancient tech, lottery government, ultra-conservative. WANTS: preserve the status quo and Florani isolation; trades only for what it lacks. Stance: **indifferent/transactional** to Drinax; uses overwhelming nuclear force only if the League itself is threatened; treats route pirates as a border-control problem.
- **Vargr corsairs** — descendants of corsairs who drifted in long ago; likely seeded the Reach's raiding curse. **Not a polity** — freelance raiders (e.g. Zuekhvi the Corsair). WANTS: easy prizes, freedom of the lanes. Stance: opportunists, no side.
- **Sindalian remnants & independent worlds** — the dead Empire of Sindal (Noricum) + isolated, individually-weak pocket worlds, many old Drinax vassals. WANTS: survival; the ex-vassals (Paal/Pourne/Hilfer) lean toward negotiating into the Imperium/Hierate as equals. Stance: **the prizes** — the worlds Oleb must court, raid, or annex; relics/vaults seeded everywhere (drives the *Shadows of Sindal* hooks).
- **Theev pirates** (Pirate Lords of Blacksand) — the Reach's pirate world; ~50–100 raider bands operate from the wider Reach. WANTS: profit, anonymity, Theev's survival (they deliberately steer Navy heat to *other* systems; one iron rule — never raid near Theev). Stance: **a vital, dangerous service hub** (fence, repair, recruit, upgrade), not an ally; Friendly at start.
- **GeDeCo (General Development Company)** — Imperial megacorp, the Reach's **most widespread** non-state actor; builds starports, advises rulers, runs spies, brokers treaties as the Imperium's quiet anti-Aslan instrument. WANTS: profit + Imperial influence; **believes Drinax's fall is inevitable**. Stance: sometimes a patron, often a rival empire-builder for the same worlds, and a **leak risk** (its agent sits in Drinax's court — *GM secret*).

## Key places (hex + UWP + one phrase)

- **Drinax** (0603 Tlaiowaha) **A43645A-E** — Ni Ht — the home base: a dead, glassed world with the **Floating Palace** (grav-city, ~40,000 nobles) hanging above it; only **Haven** at start. *(Appendix list variant A33645C-F.)*
- **Theev** (0506 Sindal) **A434500-F** — Na Ni Ht, Amber — the pirate haven (Blacksand + the Skull); ruled by the Pirate Lords, policed by the **Widows**; only **Friendly** port at start.
- **Asim** (0503 Tlaiowaha) **B867564-6** — Ag Ni — Drinax's occupied breadbasket; resentful TL6 peasantry; the *ihatei*'s likeliest invasion target.
- **Tobia** (0805 Tobia) **A544A55-F** — In Hi, Navy base — the **sector capital**; the Sector Duke effectively runs the Reach; patrolled Imperial space (Navy hunts you).
- **Tyokh** (0606 Tlaiowaha) **B466AH7-E** — Hi Ht — the Aslan end of the dust-spice route; high pop, Hierate-aligned.
- **Torpol** (0601 Tlaiowaha) **B55A77A-B** — Wa — water-world **liberty port** & cluster capital; well-defended highport (a patched Imperial light cruiser); old Drinax vassal, pragmatic traders.
- **Pourne** (0704 Tlaiowaha) **A9B2887-A** — Fl — paranoid, fortified (SDI net, armed customs), but a vital high-tech trade hub; leans toward Imperial/Hierate partnership.
- **Paal** (0805 Tlaiowaha) **B564679-6** — Ag Ni Ri — rich agricultural trade hub; old Drinax association, covets Drinax's tech; would rather join an empire as an equal.
- **Kteiroa** (0505 Tlaiowaha) **C560164-8** — De Lo Ni, Hierate — Outcaste-run smuggler back-door; the **ihatei muster** (500+ this year) — invasion launch point.
- **Clarke** (0702 Tlaiowaha) **B899753-8** — death-cult theocracy (Psychopomps) born of Drinax's bombing; the frozen dead outnumber the living; deep grievance against Drinax.
- **Blue** (0801 Tlaiowaha) **B543487-B** — Po Ni Ht — strategic jump-2 crossroads the Imperium courts; insular gov (port-only law); Aslan *ihatei* toeholds in-system.
- **Noricum** (0408 Sindal) **D8867BB-1** — Ag Lt, Amber — the **fallen Sindalian capital**, bombed to TL1; viral death-zones; rumoured buried treasury (no robber returns).
- **Cordan** (0401 Borderland) **A895347-9** — Ni Lo — pristine baronial garden world on the trade route; tacit Imperial backing; ripe for internal intrigue (Baroness Lux).
- **Acrid** (0302 Borderland) **AAC1388-D** — Fl Lo Ni Ht — PRQ megacorp hell-world of debt-slaves on the trade route; a powder-keg worker revolt is the lever.

## Key NPCs (what they WANT — act on it)
*Mirror the active ones onto the Characters List. Full stats/secrets in personalities-and-factions.md.*
- **King Oleb XVI** — King of Drinax; the crew's patron. WANTS: **rebuild the Kingdom** via piracy; enjoy drink/women/food/flying. A bellowing buffoon who is secretly a **cunning ex-pirate statesman**. (*GM: the bluster is a mask.*)
- **Princess Rao** — Crown Princess & chief diplomat; **invented the privateer scheme**. WANTS: a **grand alliance** / reborn kingdom and a TL15 golden age; favours subtle empire-building over crude piracy. (*GM: won't actually marry a pirate — the betrothal is leverage; her alliance may eclipse Oleb's vision.*)
- **Prince Harrick** — rebuilt rival heir, emerged from a regrowth tank 6 months ago, uncanny ("more machine than man"). WANTS: to **reclaim his place, honour, and the throne** Rao now stands to inherit. (*GM: stability/loyalty a wildcard; detonates the succession in Episode 9.*)
- **Lord Wrax** — commander of the **Star Guard** (Drinax's handful of sub-400t ships). WANTS: the **Harrier as his flagship** and a real fleet; **little time for the pirate scheme** — a standing court obstacle.
- **Scholar Voha** — court sage, master of the Scholar's Tower. WANTS: **preserve Sindalian knowledge** and keep the Palace safe; obstructs reckless schemes. The exposition source for Sindal/Reach lore.
- **Kasiyl of the Ahroay'if** — exiled Aslan prince, ace pilot, court bodyguard/interpreter. WANTS: **honour, territory, a place** (refused a Star Guard berth); won't say why he fled the Hierate. A potential loyal Aslan ally.
- **Sal Dancet** — court free trader/smuggler/fixer. WANTS: profit and rare goods to move; a ready **Contact** for goods, rumours, and info anywhere.
- **Admiral Darokyn** — urbane ex-Imperial-Navy officer running the most disciplined pirate fleet (~30 ships) from Theev. WANTS: a professional operation and to keep his **secret** buried. (*GM: still a deep-cover Imperial Intelligence agent — provable, blackmailable.*)
- **Petyr Vallis** — charismatic, borderline-psychopath Theev gang leader. WANTS: **bloody vengeance on all Aslan** (Glorious Empire killed his family) + money; wants the crew's havens as anti-Hierate launch pads (allying drops Hierate standing).
- **Lady Yemar** — retired-pirate sovereign of Astrogo (Palindrome); chronically ill (Theev keeps her alive). WANTS: comfort, security, and to launder her fortune; clears pirate cargoes. (*GM: cure her dependence on Theev and you own a world.*)
- **Imperial Consul Thao Poloc** — mild historian-consul at Drinax's court. WANTS: to study Sindal in peace + "a little light spying." A **leak risk** — what he reports shapes Imperial heat; **must never confirm the crew are pirates**.

## Timeline / history (relevant)
- **~-2000:** Sindalian Empire rises from a Noricum-led defence pact; grows to ~9 of 16 subsectors, then turns tyrant (bombs its own vassals).
- **~-1400:** rebels' engineered plagues + war end it; **Noricum bombed to oblivion.** Successor kings arise — **Drinax's claim ("Star Dragon Empire of Sindal-in-Exile") lasts longest**, ruling its arc ~2 millennia behind a velvet glove of trade.
- **~-1000 → 500:** Aslan cross the Great Rift; waves of **ihatei migration** pour into the Reach; a dozen colonies become hundreds of worlds; the **Glorious Empire** later breaks away (independent ~650).
- **884–885:** King Oleb XIV taxes Imperium–Hierate trade at **20%** (Harriers built to enforce it) → the **Aslan (Ahroay'if) bomb Drinax from orbit**, sparing only the Floating Palace. Subject worlds conquered or revolt.
- **1085:** Drinax reconquers **Asim**. **1105 (now):** the derelict **Harrier is recovered and rebuilt**; Oleb launches his gambit (campaign start).

## Tone
**Space-opera piracy with hard consequences** — technicolour swashbuckling: Shakespearean court drama at Drinax, an invincible-feeling Imperium, exotic honour-bound Aslan, weird isolated worlds (*Dune* meets *Firefly* with pirate slang). **Honest dice; no rescues.** Play every power and NPC to **win, not to lose to the PCs**: run the actual force listed, honour the oracle, let disasters land. A repetitive pirate is a dead one — exposure, heat, and the *ihatei* clock are always ticking.
