## The premise

The crown of **Drinax** is a ruin pretending to be a kingdom. Once a hundred-world
empire, it was bombed flat by the Aslan in 885 and has limped on as a single
floating palace ever since. Old **King Oleb XVI** — ex-pirate, all Shakespearean
bluster over a patient mind — and his sharp heir **Princess Rao** have a scheme:
hand a privateer crew a loaned warship and a **letter of marque**, let them raid
the great powers' trade lanes, and use the chaos to court the Reach's scattered
independent worlds back under a reborn Drinax. Make the lanes so unsafe that the
Imperium and the Aslan find it cheaper to *recognise* a restored Drinax as the
route's protector than to keep crushing it.

The crew got the ship: the **Harrier** (ex-*V'Hurg*), a gifted wreck of a
patrol cruiser — TL15 bones, a glorious baroque hull, and a list of defects as
long as your arm. The marque came with a string attached: **a 10% tithe** to
Oleb on everything they take.

**The PC:** **Abhishek "Shake" Rao** — captain and ship's surgeon, a former
Imperial Navy officer turned privateer. Medic 3, with a soldier's eye for naval
tactics, a growing diplomat's tongue, and a brother, **Akhil**, back in the
Imperium he keeps meaning to return to. His crew:

- **Reyna Voss** — XO / pilot / Face; Shake's old Navy comrade, and a Drinaxi
  noble who opens court doors.
- **Dak Surrow** — pilot / astrogator; quiet, precise, superstitious about the
  ship's wayward jump drive.
- **Mira Kell** — chief engineer; blunt, earns loyalty by honesty.
- **Brakk** — lead gunner / boarder; big, hot-tempered, fiercely loyal.
- **Sal Quist** — quartermaster / fence / second gunner; works every room.
- **Doc Hadrian** — medic / steward; a fellow exiled Imperial surgeon, wry.
- **Krrsh** *(joined later)* — a Vargr pilot/gunner rescued at Borite; not
  bright, badly broken, utterly devoted to the man who saved him. Knows Theev.

## Act I — *Honour Among Thieves* (the opener)

**The wager.** Before launch, at a dinner in Sindal, Star Guard captain **Sir
Coriol Tashan** (client of the anti-privateer **Lord Wrax**) sneered at the whole
scheme. Shake called the bluff into a wager — Tashan's **Sindalian heirloom
blade** against Shake's own officer's blade that the crew would bring back their
raider. Tashan took the bet.

**The hunt — and the misjump.** Laying in the jump to chase the raiders, the
ship's still-wayward J-drive threw the throw. The astrogation failed badly and
the Harrier **misjumped** — flung five parsecs coreward into the lawless **Sindal**
subsector, limping out at **Borite (0609)** with cracked frames and empty tanks.
Off-mission, deep in pirate country. There the crew found and destroyed a **Star
Ray raider** preying on Borite's granary run, rescued free-trader captain **Edda
Korr** of the *Larkspur* (a grateful local contact), and pulled a half-feral
Vargr, **Krrsh**, out of a derelict — who swore himself to Shake.

**Theev, the Chamax, and Admiral Darokyn.** Refitting at the pirate haven of
**Theev**, the crew tangled with a **Sindalian station** infested by a **Hunter
Chamax** — a brutal fight that mauled the boarding party and forced a retreat
before they cleared it. Theev is ruled in part by **Admiral Darokyn**, an
apparent renegade. The crew learned his real secret: **Darokyn is still a deep-cover
Imperial Naval Intelligence agent**, running Theev as the Imperium's eye on the
Reach. He wanted the raider boss **Ferrik Redthane** dead and silent to bury it —
and the **proof** of his true allegiance sits on a secure core aboard his flagship
(a dormant blackmail/heist hook the crew chose not to pull).

**Miria, then Ferrik.** Chasing the bounty, the crew captured Ferrik's lieutenant
**Miria Silverhand** alive — a stun attempt fizzled, so they took her by force,
and disarmed the **suicide-bomb hidden in her cyberarm** at the last second. Her
nav logs led to Ferrik on Palindrome's fourth moon; broken in interrogation, she
agreed to **bait him into a trap** for a price. The trap worked. Shake took
**Ferrik alive** first — then, reading the bounty's fine print (dead or alive paid
the same, and a live Ferrik could talk and expose Darokyn), **executed him** and
delivered him frozen. Miria was paid off and released. Darokyn's secret stayed
buried; his conditional goodwill was kept.

**The payoff.** The **MCr2.5 bounty** was collected and delivered in Oleb's name —
proving Rao's privateer scheme in the field. Back home, Shake **declined to
humiliate Tashan**, trading swords instead (Shake now carries the Sindalian
heirloom blade), which turned the rivalry into grudging respect and won Tashan's
public endorsement. At court: **Oleb's favour is strong, Rao is vindicated, Lord
Wrax was publicly bested.** Completing the opener earned a permanent **+1 DM to
all Finale recruitment rolls.**

## Act II — Building the Kingdom (Phase 2, in progress)

With the Harrier **fully refit at last** — hull restored to 88/88 with salvaged
Sindalian alloy, every defect cleared, a **dorsal particle barbette** as the main
gun, an **all-arc triple pulse-laser turret** for point defence, and a bolted-on
**Diplomatic Suite mission pod** — the crew set out to court the independent worlds
of the old Kingdom. Doctrine, per Rao: **subtle / saviour** — win worlds as
protector and partner, deeds over intimidation, keep the raiding hand deniable.

**Torpol (0601) — first true ally.** Delivered their raider, then out-pitched
**GeDeCo** (the megacorp rival empire-builder) on a partnership. Now **Friendly**,
FS 3 pledged, with **Provost Farx** as a firm champion inside its government.

**Clarke (0702) — held against poison.** Won over in the opener; GeDeCo then sent
an agent to poison the well by reviving Clarke's old anti-Drinax grievance. Shake
**out-honoured the lie** and struck a **protector pact** — Drinax defends Clarke
from raiders, Clarke becomes a friendly refuge. **Friendly, held**; delivering the
defence promotes it to **Haven**. The death-priest **Keeper Malos** is now a
personal ally who holds Drinax to "actions, not words."

**Pourne (0704) — cold, but a key in the lock.** A paranoid, Imperial-leaning,
high-tech ocean fortress with no diplomatic angle (scouted, cold reception). But
bar-gossip revealed it's bracing for an attack: **a gathering corsair warlord is
uniting raider bands into a fleet to sack Pourne.** Irony — Pourne fears pirates,
and the crew fly privateer colours. The saviour angle is obvious: **break that
fleet and Pourne could be won.** (The warlord is also a rival empire-builder-by-
the-sword and a live danger.)

**Paal (0805) — where we are now.** A rich low-tech farm world with deep old ties
to Drinax, a hunger for Drinax's TL15 tech, and a stated willingness to ally **"as
equals."** The strongest prospect yet — but its council is a **fractious oligarchy**.
The crew worked it from the inside:

- Allied freely with **Matron Sera Avelline**, a pro-Drinax old-blood matron — a
  foothold in the council.
- Mapped the factions and identified the man who holds the balance: **Magnate Ivo
  Sarrel**, leader of the **centrist swing-bloc**. Hosted him aboard the Harrier;
  a successful social read drew out his **two fears** — that Paal becomes a
  **prison** (a vassal, a possession) or a **waste** (dragged into an empire's war
  and bombed to slag again, as it was during Oleb XIV's tax-war).
- Shake made his pitch, aimed straight at both fears: *war is coming whether anyone
  wants it or not — the ihatei massing in the corner, the Glorious Empire, the
  frontier war brimming between the Aslan, Zhodani, and Imperium. I can't promise
  it won't come. I can promise we meet it together, as equal partners, back-to-back
  — not as sacrifices. And ask yourself: wouldn't a corporation write Paal off as
  acceptable losses in a second?*

**That pitch is delivered but NOT yet rolled.** It is the cliffhanger.
